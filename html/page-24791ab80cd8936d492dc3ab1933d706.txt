                <h1>Luxury vs. Economy Cars &#8212; Can Hyundai Really Beat Out Mercedes?</h1>
                <span class="author"><a title="Jennifer Calonia on GoBankingRates" href="http://www.gobankingrates.com/author/jenniferc/" rel="author">By Jennifer Calonia</a></span> &bull;

					<p style="text-align: center;"><small><img class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-202588" style="text-align: center; width: 310px; border-width: 1px; border-style: solid; margin: 0 auto;" title="Hyundai" src="http://www.gobankingrates.com/system/uploads/Hyundai-300x169.jpg" alt="Hyundai" width="300" height="169" />Photo: Orlando Hyundai</small></p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><strong>Car comparison </strong>shopping can be bittersweet for car buyers who yearn for luxury features but own economy-sized wallets. Often, consumers resort to <a title="Auto Loan" href="https://www.gobankingrates.com/r/4ea061348b/?subid=gbr_EconomyLuxury" rel="nofollow">auto loan</a> financing at exacerbated interest rates to feed the desire for premium engineering and design.<span id="more-202586"></span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Economy brands, however, are stepping up with new vehicle models that attempt to rival the luxury options of German juggernauts like Mercedes-Benz, BMW and Audi, but at a considerable value.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">As the auto industry develops new and existing product lines, brands like Hyundai and Kia are redefining their image and blurring the line between <strong>luxury vs. economy</strong> cars. Redesigns and the heightened features of these cost-conscious industry names are leaving car enthusiasts with eyebrows raised.</p>
<h2 style="text-align: left;">Luxury vs. Economy Car Comparison</h2>
<p>Conducting a preliminary car comparison between luxe car models and their more affordable counterparts can drastically reduce long-term debt from <a title="Auto Loans" href="http://www.gobankingrates.com/auto-loans/">auto loans</a>. While economy brands tout their plush attributes and reasonable price tags, the question remains: Are affordable cars with luxury features really comparable to their high-end competitors?</p>
<h3>2013 Hyundai Equus vs. 2013 Mercedes-Benz S-Class S550</h3>
<p><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-202590" title="Hyundai-Mercedes-Car-Comparison" src="http://www.gobankingrates.com/system/uploads/Hyundai-Mercedes-Car-Comparison-257x300.png" alt="" width="257" height="300" />Among Hyundai&#8217;s developments is the 2013 Equus. This yacht-sized sedan originally found its fame in the Korean consumer market as a &#8220;business class&#8221; vehicle used to transport businessmen, politicians and other notable figures to and from their daily appearances.</p>
<p>What has piqued curiosity about Hyundai&#8217;s recent attempt at fusing luxury with affordability is that the brand goes so far as to pair the Equus with elite ranks as the Mercedez-Benz S-Class S550.</p>
<p>In 2011, Hyundai upgraded the Equus&#8217; original 4.6-liter Tau V8 drivetrain at 385-horsepower, in an effort to keep at the heels of the Mercedez S550. The 2013 Equus boasts an 8-speed automatic transmission, 5.0-liter Tau V8 engine with 429-horsepower under the hood. In terms of design, the Equus does a decent job at mimicking Mercedes&#8217; classic appeal.</p>
<p>Hyundai also provides Equus owners with a first-class &#8220;At Your Service&#8221; perk, wherein owners simply schedule maintenance appointments on a mobile app or the iPad included with the car and a customer care representative picks up the car and leaves another Equus loaner in its place. With this complementary service, Equus owners never bear the burden of stepping into the dealership.</p>
<p>On the other hand, the Mercedes S550 offers a 4.6-liter V8 turbo engine at an equal 429-horsepower as a Hyundai Equus. The interior upholds Mercedes-Benz&#8217;s reputation with soft leather and burnt walnut wood trim details.</p>
<p>While the Equus costs a great deal more than Hyundai&#8217;s low to mid-level models at about $59,250, it still beats out the painful $95,000 price tag of the Mercedes S550. The Equus&#8217; low sticker price, compared to the S-Class, is its primary pull among consumers seeking a luxury vehicle option and easy-to-digest <a title="Auto Financing" href="http://www.gobankingrates.com/auto-loans/">auto financing</a> terms.</p>
<p>According to the 2013 Car Brand Perception Survey conducted by Consumer Reports, consumers identified Hyundai as being among the best car brands overall along with the likes of Mercedes-Benz.</p>
<p>Despite Hyundai&#8217;s lavish list of upscale options, there are still reservations about deeming Hyundai worthy enough to undergo a car comparison against Mercedes-Benz. Unquestionable branding as a luxury vehicle and seamless execution thanks to decades of success in the industry are main factors keeping Mercedes-Benz on its velvet throne.</p>
<h3>2013 Kia Optima vs. Audi A4</h3>
<p><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-248434" title="Kia-Audi Car Comparison" src="http://www.gobankingrates.com/system/uploads/Kia-Audi-Car-Comparison-286x300.png" alt="Kia-Audi Car Comparison" width="286" height="300" />Kia is another brand that has stealthily soared as an easy competitor among the Honda and Toyota markets. This achievement, however, is not enough for the company as it begins tackling its next challenge &#8212; leveling up to top-tier luxury brands.</p>
<p>In 2006, Kia decided to turnaround its bland design by enlisting the expertise of luxury automobile design insider, Peter Schreyer. With years of experience designing for the likes of Volkswagen and Audi, Kia reinvented its brand image through their new design strategy.</p>
<p>Schreyer determined that the Korean automobile company needed a new &#8220;face&#8221; that consumers would immediately recognize as the Kia brand. As a result, the &#8220;tiger nose&#8221; was created and is now the signature facade of their entire product line.</p>
<p>The 2013 Kia Optima showcases the company&#8217;s updated styling strategy. Thoughtful European touches that challenge the design aspects of Audi&#8217;s A4 are present throughout the Optima&#8217;s redesign, including wrap-around headlights and tail lights, a lower &#8212; and sleeker &#8212; roof line and standard 17&#8243; alloy wheels for the EX trim.</p>
<p>With regard to performance, the Kia Optima EX does a good job for its class with a 2.4-liter I4 engine which produces 200-horsepower and 186-pound-feet of torque.</p>
<p>Its opponent in this car comparison, the Audi A4 premium trim, performs at 211-horsepower and 258-pound-feet of torque thanks to its 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine.</p>
<p>While the Audi A4 out-performs the Kia Optima under the hood, the Optima EX offers a stylish value starting at $23,650 compared to the A4&#8242;s $32,500 MSRP. Additionally, the Kia provides drivers with greater fuel economy at 24 mi./gal. city and 35 mi./gal. on the highway &#8212; a definite plus for car buyers looking to <a title="Save Money on Gas" href="http://www.gobankingrates.com/savings-account/4-lesser-known-tips-to-save-on-gas/">save money on gas</a>.</p>
<p title="Leasing of Buying a Car">In this luxury vs. economy car comparison, however, ardent auto buffs may be inclined to sacrifice the added $10,000 cost of an Audi A4 in exchange for its superior driving capabilities and the tenured brand swagger. Car buyers who are unwilling to part with Audi&#8217;s quality, performance and luxury appeal may have to consider <a title="Car Leasing" href="http://www.gobankingrates.com/auto-loans/the-real-difference-between-leasing-and-buying/">car leasing</a> over buying if facing budget constraints.</p>
<h2>Affordable Cars with Luxury Features</h2>
<p>The rise of affordable cars with luxury features is a clear statement of how economy underdogs are earning their place within the competitive automotive industry. However, European brands like Merdedes-Benz, BMW and Audi have maintained their prestige and stronghold over the luxury market due to their extensive experience and engineering prowess &#8212; a head start that is unlikely to falter in the near future.</p>
<p>Hyundai and Kia are commendably on the right track in relinquishing their low-level brand image and are gaining their footing as a mid-level brand &#8212; arguably pacing alongside Lexus &#8212; but for now are just out of arms reach in the luxury market.</p>
