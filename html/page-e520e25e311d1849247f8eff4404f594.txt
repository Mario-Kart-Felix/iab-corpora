                <h2>Sierra Club pushes electric vehicles</h2>
				
                
					
		                <figure class="wide">
		                <img src="http://www.trbimg.com/img-51dc505f/turbine/chi-sierra-club-pushes-electric-vehicles-20130-001/620" /></img>
		                    
		                    <figcaption>
		                        The Sierra Club's EV Guide uses the EPA's national electricity grid to calculate emissions by region. The region encompassing Chicago and West Virginia uses 69.9 percent of coal-powered electricity. The national average is 44.5 percent.   (EPA)
		                    </figcaption>
		                    
		                </figure>
					
				
				<time>July 9, 2013, 12:54 p.m.</time>
				
                    <p id="author">By Robert Duffer, Chicago Tribune</p>
                
                
                <article>
				
                <p>The Sierra Club got on the emission-free electric vehicle bandwagon last month by launching its <a href="http://content.sierraclub.org/evguide/" target="_blank">Electric Vehicle Guide</a>. The guide features the typical range, price, MPGe and carbon- and cost-saving metrics. The blog section will report positive news, says Jesse Prentice-Dunn, a transportation policy analyst for Sierra Club. &ldquo;We'll be publicizing success stories of communities going electric and doing what they can to reduce their dependence on oil,&rdquo; Prentice-Dunn says.</p>
<p>&nbsp;One of the more interesting components of the site is the state index. Entering your zip code will turn up your annual oil savings, CO2 emission savings, and state incentives from rebates to reduced vehicle registration fees. The oil and CO2 savings are based on the <runtime:topic id="ORGOV000048">EPA</runtime:topic>&rsquo;s national breakdown of <a href="http://www.epa.gov/cleanenergy/documents/egridzips/eGRID2012_eGRID_subregions.jpg" target="_blank">26 electrical grids</a> and the estimated emissions in each of those grids. Numbers were last calculated in 2009.</p>
<p>&ldquo;This does take into account the full lifecycle emissions of the electricity generated to power the EV, based on the region you're in,&rdquo; Prentice-Dunn explains.</p>
<p>In the Chicago region, which includes Indiana, Ohio, West Virginia, even western Pennsylvania, but does not include most of Illinois, our Com Ed power is dirtier than the national average. 69.9 percent of electricity in the region is powered by coal. The national average is 44.5 percent. CO2 emissions are correspondingly higher. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>Back to the fun parts of the Sierra Club EV Guide. It features a &ldquo;fun to drive&rdquo; category and a &ldquo;will it be around in 10 years&rdquo; note for each vehicle. That should make for interesting prognosticating and updating, considering that the first generation, first mass-produced hybrid, the Toyota Prius, is at that milestone now.</p>
<p>The EV Guide focuses on electrics or plug-in hybrid electrics only. The Sierra Club is known for its environmental activism, and the EV Guide is a way to generate more awareness to reduce dependence on foreign oil, says Prentice-Dunn. &ldquo;The guide supports our "Go Electric" campaign, which aims to increase electric vehicle charging infrastructure and get more EVs on the road.&rdquo;</p>
<p>Which, we hope, will lead to a cleaner power grid charging all these EVs.&nbsp;</p>
