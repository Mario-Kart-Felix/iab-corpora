<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">
Its no secret that the name&nbsp; Allison is synonymous with world-class design 
and manufacturing of heavy-duty automotive systems worldwide.&nbsp; Their name is 
associated with the world-renowned heavy-duty automatic transmissions, which 
reliably power trucks and buses of all descriptions.</span></b></font></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">&nbsp;</span></b></font></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">
Now Allison Electric Drives has released its newest product for the transit bus 
market.&nbsp; The EP-50 System was designed specifically for articulated and 
suburban coaches. This system is referred to as Split Parallel Hybrid 
Technology, and is a design patented by Allison. It uses power from Allisons 
advanced energy storage system that contains or uses no Lead Acid Batteries.</span></b></font></p>
<p class="MsoNormal">
<img border="5" src="images/allison%20ep50%20hybrid%20top.jpg" width="600" height="400" align="left" hspace="5" vspace="5" alt="Allison EP50 Hybrid"></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">&nbsp;</span></b></font></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">
The EP-50 system combines the Allison automatic transmission with an integrated 
electric drive unit. This hybrid system uses Concentric AC Induction Motors 
with an electrically infinitely variable gear ratio and torque amplification 
transmission controlled by Allisons 1000 / 2000 system controllers.&nbsp; Combined 
with General Motors Corporations advanced inverter technology, the dual power 
inverter module (DPIM) provides 430 - 900 VDC 160KW 3-phase AC power.</span></b></font></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">
&nbsp;The EP-50 system also uses full regenerative braking to recover energy from 50 
mph.&nbsp; The unit is cooled by a common oil cooling and lubrication system that 
takes care of the motors, drive unit, and DPIM.&nbsp; No water / Ethylene Glycol 
support pumps are required.</span></b></font></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">
&nbsp;The Allison buss EP system engine generates the power that keeps its 
propulsion batteries charged.&nbsp; The engine also couples to a drive unit through 
an infinitely variable transmission to power the wheels.&nbsp; The drive unit has 
electric motors that run off its batteries. When the vehicle accelerates from a 
stop, it use the superior torque of electric motors, eliminating the excessive 
exhaust cloud that usually results when a diesel powered bus accelerates.&nbsp; The 
bus then maintains its speed with its diesel engine- the same one that charges 
the batteries.&nbsp; The buses regenerative brakes capture energy normally spun off 
as heat and return it to the batteries for reuse.&nbsp; </span></b></font></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">
&nbsp;What does all of this mean?&nbsp; Glad you asked!&nbsp;&nbsp; Take a typical transit bus, 
weighing in the neighborhood of 55,000 pounds.&nbsp; They are normally powered by a 
large diesel engine with displacements of 10 to 14 liters.&nbsp; These engines can be 
run on diesel or even on compressed natural gas.&nbsp; But as you probably know 
already, these large diesel engines produce a large amount of emissions as they 
swallow large amounts of fuel.</span></b></font></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">
&nbsp;The EP-50 Allison Electric drive used in the same size coach is coupled to a 
much smaller displacement diesel.&nbsp; Most use under 6 liters, combined with the 
hybrid electric drive, and deliver terrific power to operate the bus.&nbsp; Heres 
the good news!</span></b></font></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">
This hybrid system <i>REDUCES </i>exhaust emissions up to 90 %.&nbsp; Thats 
correct!&nbsp; 90 %!&nbsp;&nbsp; The emission reductions break down this way:&nbsp;</span></b></font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">
Remarkably, all of this is achieved while <i>IMPROVING FUEL ECONOMY</i> up to 60 
%.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Sixty percent is a whopping number!&nbsp;</span></b></font></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">
Consider that there are approximately 13,000 transit buses in service in the 
nine largest U.S. cities.&nbsp;&nbsp; If they were replaced with buses featuring hybrid 
technology, the nations diesel fuel consumption would decrease by nearly 40 
million gallons a year- for an equivalent fuel savings of 584,000 small hybrid 
cars.&nbsp;</span></b></font></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">
Thats right, it would only take these nine cities to save 40 million gallons of 
diesel annually.&nbsp; How about the top fifty cities?&nbsp; &nbsp;</span></b></font></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">
Hybrid bus systems developed by GM and its Allison Transmission Division are 
already in service by the Orange County Transit Authority in Anaheim, California 
and in Portland, Oregon.&nbsp; About a dozen systems are scheduled for delivery this 
year (2002) and will be deployed in key sites in North America.&nbsp;</span></b></font></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">
The typical EP-50 system performance with energy storage is 298 KW or 400 
horsepower.&nbsp; This is pretty incredible technology.</span></b></font></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><font color="#00000B"><b><span style="font-family: Arial">
&nbsp;I had the privilege of riding on one of these buses around Georgias Stone 
Mountain Park, and also on the road.&nbsp; It performed very well, and they are 
already in use in major cities throughout the United States.&nbsp; We here at Sams 
Garage feel that GM and Allison have a sure winner with the EP-50 Hybrid 
System.&nbsp;</span></b></font></p>
