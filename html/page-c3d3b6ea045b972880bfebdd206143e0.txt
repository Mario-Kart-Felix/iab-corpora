  <h1 style="margin-top: 0;"><font size="4">Towing Car with 
    Roadside Assistance Service May Increase Your Auto Insurance Rates</font></h1>
  <p style="margin-bottom: 0;">When 
  you <strong><a href="index.phtml">buy cars</a>, <a href="index_2.phtml">trucks</a>, 
  <a href="index.phtml">vans</a>, or <a href="index.phtml">SUV</a></strong>, you 
  may decide to get towing and roadside assistance services coverage, to help 
  you in the event your car breaks. This peace of mind <em>may</em> cost you more 
  by way of auto insurance rate increase.</p><p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0;"><BR> 
  </p><p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">According to Cosumer Reports' 
  &quot; <strong><strong>A tow could boost your car insurance premium&quot;, </strong></strong>your 
  auto insurance rates could be affected if you use roadside assistance or towing 
  service of an insurance company too often.</p><p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0;"><br> 
  </p><p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0;">The article stated that some 
  auto insurance companies view calls for roadside assistance negatively, in the 
  same way they view accidents.&nbsp; Here is the article <a href="http://www.consumerreports.org/cro/cars/tires-auto-parts/car-maintenance/roadside-assistance-7-06/overview/0607_roadside-assistance_ov.htm?view=Print">&quot; 
  <strong><strong>A tow could boost your car insurance premium&quot;</strong></strong></a>. 
  <br></p><p style="margin-top: 0;"><br><em>As always, AutoCrisis.com 
  suggests that you check with your Insurance Agent or provider first before you 
  make a purchase decision, as to the amount, type, and level of auto insurance 
  coverage you will need. </em></p>
