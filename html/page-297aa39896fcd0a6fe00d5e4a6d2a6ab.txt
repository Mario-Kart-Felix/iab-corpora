<h2>User Guide To 4WD and Off Road Vehicles</h2>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><p>A big number of  people are enthusiasts of off-road vehicles because of its many uses,  versatility, and to some, the social status it represents. Off-road vehicles  are any type of vehicle that usually has higher traction and higher clearance  and capable of driving on and off roads. It generally has large tires, flexible  suspensions, or even caterpillar tracks. The most common use of off-road  vehicles is for areas without pavement such as in sight seeing activities,  trails and forest roads. <br />
 Common  commercial off-road vehicles include pickup trucks and light trucks with  station wagon-like bodies. Examples are Mitsubishi Pajero, Chevrolet Blazer,  Land Rover Defender, Range Rover, and Toyota Land Cruiser. A number of off-road  vehicles are also luxury SUVs but with reduced off-road capability.<br />
<br />
 Most off-road  vehicles are four-wheel drive vehicles which allow them to manually switch  between two-wheel mode for pavements and four-wheel mode for low traction  conditions. This ability to switch from two different modes and lock  differentials distinguishes 4WD from AWD (All-wheel drive) vehicles. However,  other examples of off-road vehicles include dirt bikes, dune buggies and  sandrails.<br />
 Military  vehicles such as the Jeep CJ and the AM General Hummer have also been converted  for commercial use and civilian specifications.<br />
<br />
 4WD and off road  vehicles have a great number of enthusiasts because of its many uses and  versatility. In fact, many types of motorsports have been established that  involves racing 4WD and off road vehicles. Competitions include rally, desert  racing, and rock crawling which are considered to be the three largest sports  for 4WD vehicles. On the other hand, All Terrain Vehicles and motorcycle  competitions include Motocross, Enduro and also desert racing such as Dakar  Rallye and Baja 1000. <br />
 Since most 4WD  and off road vehicles are built with higher ground clearance and have a higher  centre of gravity, the risk of a rollover is higher than ordinary passenger  cars. <br />
<br />
 4WD and off road  vehicles have also been criticized for the impact of illegal trail and  off-trail activities on natural vegetation and wildlife. Erosion and habitat  loss are known to have been caused by the drivers testing their vehicles  against natural obstacles. The illegal use of 4WD and off road vehicles is an  environmental and land management problem that is being addressed by  governments. To support acts of maintaining the environment, many organisations  encourage only the use appropriate trail ethics.<br />
 The Australian  National Four-Wheel Drive Council is the peak recreational 4WD organisation in  Australia and its membership is comprised of all 4WD associations in each State  and the Northern Territory. <br />
<br />
<strong>Four-drive advice and tips</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Replace all worn parts on your  vehicle before you set out on a trip and have it checked by an expert 4WD  repairer for a safe and enjoyable trip. </li>
<li>Bring a UHF radio to contact  other people for information and assistance if needed.</li>
<li>Carry as little as possible on  your roof rack; use a heavy duty alloy to take light but bulky items. </li>
<li>Carry a first aid kit and learn  how to use it in case of emergencies.</li>
</ul>
<p>Bring a tool kit to help you with minor repairs  and maintenance. </p></p>
