<strong>Toyota Camry Hybrid</strong> concept was unveiled at the 2008 Los Angeles Auto Show about three years back, and now its official that the <a href="http://www.latestautoreviews.com/pictures-of-2012-toyota-camry-hybrid">2012 Toyota Camry Hybrid</a> will be another battery-electric vehicle (BEV), which will be launched in 2012.<br />
<br />
The 2011 Camry will continue with two conventional gas powertrains and a gas-electric hybrid system, but if you&#8217;re worried about the frequent recalls, you&#8217;d be better off waiting for the <a href="http://www.latestautoreviews.com/">new 2012 toyota camry hybrid</a>, rather than putting your money on <strong>2011 Camry</strong>.<br />
<br />
Toyota Camry has been a top selling sedan, and the new hybrid will improve its capabilities, including power levels, and especially the fuel efficiency.<br />
<br />
New Camry incorporates throttle-pedal modifications, and is mostly a carry-forward of 2010 Camry.<br />
<br />

<br />
<span id="more-995"></span><br />
Coming back to Toyota&#8217;s plan of releasing 10 more hybrids this year, its pretty evident that Toyota is now getting serious about scope of its advanced alternative-fuel vehicle development, and expanding its Camry lineup.<br />
