				<h3 class="entry-title"><a href="http://cyrilhuzeblog.com/2013/05/10/spectro-explains-motorcycle-versus-automotive-oils/" rel="bookmark" title='Permanent Link to "Spectro Explains Motorcycle Versus Automotive Oils."'>Spectro Explains Motorcycle Versus Automotive Oils.</a></h3>
				
				<small class="entry-meta">
					<span class="chronodata">
						Published by <span class="vcard author"><a href="http://cyrilhuzeblog.com/author/cyrilhuze/" class="url fn">Cyril Huze</a></span> <abbr class="published" title="2013-05-10T08:00:06+0000">May 10th, 2013</abbr>					</span>

					<span class="entry-category">in <a href="http://cyrilhuzeblog.com/category/editorial/" title="View all posts in Editorial">Editorial</a>.</span>
									
									
									</small> <!-- .entry-meta -->
			</div> <!-- .entry-head -->

			<div class="entry-content">
				<p><a href="http://cyrilhuzeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/HD-Cast-Away.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-119363" alt="HD-Cast-Away" src="http://cyrilhuzeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/HD-Cast-Away.jpg" width="322" height="263" /></a>Lots of riders, and even some “in-the-know” magazine journalists, say all oils are the same. The FACTS are: engines, drivetrains and demands on motorcycles are different and so are the lubricants required.</p>
<p>Take power: maximum engine output per liter for motorcycles is typically 1.5 to 1.8 times that of automobile engines. Similarly, R.P.M.’s at maximum output are 1.3 to 2 times that of most automobiles. Further, motorcycle engines, being small and lightweight, have a smaller thermal capacity &#8211; the result is that motorcycle engine oils can reach temperatures as high as 320° F.<span id="more-119354"></span></p>
<p><a href="http://cyrilhuzeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/Spectro-Oils.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-119359" alt="Spectro-Oils" src="http://cyrilhuzeblog.com/wp-content/uploads/Spectro-Oils.jpg" width="540" height="175" /></a>Motorcycle oils: 1- MUST contain higher quality shear stabilizers because many run the same lubricant in both the engine and gearbox. 2- MUST have higher volatility to control consumption because they do run hotter. 3- MUST have special additives to better prevent cam wear and oxidation in their extreme heat environment.</p>
<p>So does it make sense to skimp on your motorcycle’s “health” to save a few dollars per oil change? No Way! An average Motorcyclist changes their oil twice a year, using a less expensive oil to save only a few dollars -vs- using high quality motorcycle specific oils. That just doesn’t make sense. Written by <a href="http://www.spectro-oils.com" target="_blank">Spectro Oils</a>. <em>(engine photo @ harley-davidson)</em></p>
