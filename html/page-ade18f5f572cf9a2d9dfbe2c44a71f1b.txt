	<div id="story_content"><div class="col_480"><div class="col_460"><div id="storycontent" class="para18"><div id="page1"><p>Many of today's generation and even a few from yesterday are living an active lifestyle that encompasses a broad range of year-round outdoor activities that includes mountain biking, kayaking, hiking and cross-country skiing.</p><p>They are looking for vehicles that can not only haul their gear, but are capable of taking them well off the beaten track and beyond the end of the road - no road, no worries. Several manufacturers have vehicles targeting these buyers including Jeep's venerable TJ, Nissan's award-winning Xterra, and Toyota's new 2007 FJ Cruiser.</p><p>Jeep TJ</p><p>Jeep's TJ series - two wheelbase versions - is a two-door go-anywhere vehicle that has a long history of outdoor ruggedness. And rugged is perhaps the best word to use when comparing it with the others.</p><p>To begin with, the collapsible canvas soft top will remind some of being in a tent (there is an optional removable hardtop), and then there is entering the interior, which tends to still have the look of General Patton's Second World War Jeep. However, that is much of the appeal of the TJ. The charm of the TJ attracts all ages and genders.</p><p>What it lacks in creature comforts compared to the others it makes up for when you hit the road, or I should say no road. And you know it is serious about going off road when the halogen fog lights are submersible.</p><p>My tester was equipped with the optional 4.0-litre in-line six-cylinder that produces adequate torque to get you through the mud and water. However, it produces the slowest acceleration and just does not have a lot of grunt for the highway. The six-speed manual enables solid shifts with the gear ratios matching well to the power band. There is an optional four-speed automatic with overdrive and a lockup torque converter. However, the manual is your best bet.</p><p>Handling is interesting to say the least and you can get caught if you do not focus. Braking tests also resulted in the longest stopping distances of the trio. However, the TJ's forte shows up off road and is reflected with the only four-wheel drive system of the trio that includes front and rear locking differentials, a part-time two speed transfer case (Rubicon only), skid plates constructed of three-millimetre steel, live solid axles and gas-charged shock absorbers.</p><p>The interior is more basic than the others tested, with comfortable seating up front and limited space in the rear. The rear seats are of the fold and tumble variety and can be removed, which is best if you plan to haul much gear.</p><p>Nissan Xterra</p><p>The Nissan Xterra has been marketed to buyers who want to play outdoors and are serious enough about it that they may sustain a few cuts and bruises - hence the standard first aid kit.</p><p>The Xterra is the only full four-door tested, but it is a long way from your family sedan. Power comes from a 4.0-litre V6 that produces the most power of the trio and that is reflected in the fastest acceleration times. My tester was equipped with the optional five-speed automatic (a six-speed manual is standard) that worked well, producing smooth shifts while enabling some manual override for off-road manoeuvres.</p><pagebreak /><p>A fully boxed ladder frame, available Bilstein off-road performance shocks, locking rear differential, hill descent control and four-wheel limited slip all contribute to making the Xterra capable of some serious off road work. The Xterra also struts its stuff when the road gets slippery due to ice and snow. Handling feels the best of the trio, although braking distances were not as short as the Toyota FJ.</p><p>Interior space is inviting with comfortable seats up front and ample leg room. The driving position and outward vision are good with easy to reach and use controls. Rear cargo area is the most of the trio with 10 tiedown hooks, including four in the ceiling.</p><p>Toyota FJ Cruiser</p><p>Toyota's new 2007 FJ Cruiser is a unique blend of retro based on the rugged, go-everywhere Toyota FJ 40.</p><p>This FJ is ultra-modern in everything from the bumpers to the integrated lights, and even a white top that is standard no matter which of the five colours you choose. And whether you are 7 or 77, you will do a double take the first time you see the vehicle.</p><p>My road test with the FJ included a blend of highway with plenty of twisty two-lane hard top and a few off-road challenges. Included was a rock wall that seemed to reach to the sky and a water hole that was deep enough to send a wake over the hood.</p><p>The FJ was more than up to the challenge, taking on all conditions and then sitting like a dog panting and tail wagging, begging you to throw something else. Needless to say, the FJ handles all this off-road activity as if it were child's play. The highway ride is surprisingly smooth, although there is a fair amount of wind noise at the high speeds.</p><p>Even though the FJ's 4.0-litre V6 is not a power house, this vehicle punches well above its weight class. Neither the six-speed manual or optional five-speed automatic are the smoothest shifting devices on the planet, although they get the job done. There is a solid rear axle with a locking differential and A Trac that seems to enable the FJ to get traction even with two wheels hanging in the air.</p><p>The interior is simple, with good ergonomics, legible gauges including an Inclinometer, and easy to reach and use controls. Good stowage and lots of useable cargo capacity with a very durable and washable floor.</p><p>Summary</p><p>The Jeep TJ is a tough and ready image vehicle that is a little rough around the edges when compared with the other two. Its off-road capability is solid, however, it is dated in other areas and is being replaced with an all-new Wrangler in 2007.</p><p>Nissan Xterra has all the key ingredients for off and on road. It is an all-weather vehicle that is good at many things and, if you need four doors, offers good value.</p><p>The Toyota FJ Cruiser does so many things well and has one of the highest vehicle fun quotients that I have experienced. When combined with Toyota's DNA for dependability, its competitive pricing and that retro look - it is a grand slam home run.</p><pagebreak /><p>- - -</p><p>Jeep TJ</p><p>Price: $23,230 (base); $33,260 (as tested)</p><p>Engine: 4.0-litre in-line 6 cylinder</p><p>Power: 190 horsepower; 235 pound-feet of torque</p><p>Fuel economy: 16.8 litres per 100 km (city); 11.6 litres per 100 km (hwy.)</p><p>More specifications are listed on www.driving.ca</p><p>Nissan Xterra</p><p>Price: $33,569 (base); 	// When user selects a make populate the dropdownboxes
	function filterRoadTest(keyword) {
		var	make = jQuery("#ddRoadTestMake");
		var	model = jQuery("#ddRoadTestModel");
		var	year = jQuery("#ddRoadTestYear");

		if (keyword=='make' && make.find("option:first")[0].selected!=true) {
		    adSync.TriggerAdSynch();

			populateDropdown("ddRoadTestModel", "Select a Model", "/scripts/listattributevalues.aspx?Output=json&catalog=drvg&tags=storytype|review&tag=Model&tags=Make|" + make.val(), "");

			year.find("option:first")[0].selected = true;
			year.attr("disabled","true");
		}
		else if (keyword='model' && model.find("option:first")[0].selected!=true) {
			populateDropdown("ddRoadTestYear", "Select a Year", "/scripts/listattributevalues.aspx?Output=json&catalog=drvg&tags=storytype|review&order=desc&tag=Year&tags=Make|" + make.val() + "&tags=Model|" + model.val(), "");

			year.removeAttr("disabled");
		}

		// Enable/Disable Model
		if (make.find("option:first")[0].selected==true) {
			model.find("option:first")[0].selected = true;
			model.attr("disabled","true");
		}
		else
			model.removeAttr("disabled");

		// Enable/Disable Model
		if (model.find("option:first")[0].selected==true) {
			year.find("option:first")[0].selected = true;
			year.attr("disabled","true");
		}
		else
			year.removeAttr("disabled");
	}

	// Ensures selections have been made and sends user to results page
	function findRoadTest() {
		var make = jQuery("#ddRoadTestMake");
		var model = jQuery("#ddRoadTestModel");
		var year = jQuery("#ddRoadTestYear");
		var columnistname = jQuery("#ddRoadTestWriter");

	}
