            <td class="main"><p><STRONG>Making the correct choice when buying a car lift?</STRONG></p>
              <p>This is a common delima our customers ask when looking to purchase an   <a href="index.php">automotive lift</a> (<A href="index.php">car lift</A>). They are usually   deciding between a <A href="2post_car_lifts.php">2 post lift</A> or a <A href="4post_car_lifts.php">4 post lift</A>. The main factor to consider when   deciding on your lift purchase is "What am I using this lift for".</p>
              <p>Well to help with your decision, below we have highlighted a few questions to   ask yourself?</p>
              <UL>
                <LI>Are you looking to store vehicles on your auto lift?<BR>
                  Answer: <A href="4post_car_lifts.php">4 Post Car Lift</A><BR>
                <LI>Are you making frequent repairs or service on your vehicle?<BR>
                  Answer: <A href="2post_car_lifts.php">2 Post Car Lift</A>
                <LI>Are you on a limited budget for your purchase?<BR>
                  Answer: <A href="2post_car_lifts.php">2 Post Car Lift</A>
                <LI>Are you looking for portability?<BR>
                  Answer: <A href="4post_car_lifts.php">4 Post   Car Lift</A>
                <LI>Are you to save space?<BR>
                  Answer: <A href="4post_car_lifts.php">Storage   Lifts</A> </LI>
              </UL>
              If storing a car or portability sounds like the task you are   most likely to be using a lift for, then the four post lift is highly   recommended. A four post lift is able to support an entire car or truck much   easier than a two post lift. The four post lifts are more like a bed being able   to support the entire vehicle. A four post lift is also designed for portability   and most come with a  caster kit. Not only is the four post lift portable   with the use of the casters, but you can also permanently anchor the lift to   your floor if you choose to. As for power, four post lifts generally feature a 110 volt motor and   easy installation because no special wiring is required.&nbsp;
              <p>If repair and maintenance is what you are looking to do with your lift, then   the 2 post lift is for you. The two post car lifts are the most inexpensive lift   for raising vehicles with heavy weights, whereas the four post lifts require an   auxiliary lift at an extra cost. To free up wheels, these lifts allows you to have access to all   parts of the vehicle for repairs and service including easy access to your   wheels. The two post lifts will raise your car higher in a low ceiling garage.   If you have a 10 foot ceiling, a two post lift will raise most vehicles to about   4-1/2 feet off the ground.&nbsp;Not  portable as the four post <a href="car-lift-s.php">lifts</a>, the 2 post lifts   MUST be anchored to the ground. And as for power, two post lifts come with a 220   volt single phase motor.</p>
