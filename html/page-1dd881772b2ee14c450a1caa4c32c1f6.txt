			<h1>Best Luxury Small Cars</h1>
			<small>Saturday, June 12th, 2010</small>

			<div class="entry">
				
<!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
<div style="float:left;margin:10px 10px 10px 0;">

<p>If you’re looking to move to upscale  wheels, the best bet is to check out Best Luxury Small Cars in the  $20-35K  price range. These are entry-level <span class='wp_keywordlink'><a href="http://www.iseecars.com/cars/small-cars " title="small cars list
" target="_blank">small cars</a></span> that are filled with lots  of luxury features that are sure to please. One thing is certain: Once  you’ve had a taste of luxury, there’s really no going back. And  you can always move up to the next level from the luxury brands later  on.</p>
<p>Here are our top picks for Best Luxury  Small Cars $20-35K:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://www.iseecars.com/Used_Cars/used-Audi_A3"  target="_blank"><strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Audi     A3</span></strong></a><strong> – </strong>Unique to its class, the <span class='wp_keywordlink'><a href="http://www.iseecars.com/car/2010-audi-a3" title="2010 Audi A3
" target="_blank">2010 Audi A3</a></span> delivers the performance    of a sports car in a luxury small <a href="http://blog.iseecars.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/Audi-A3.jpg" ><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-1406" title="Audi A3" src="http://blog.iseecars.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/Audi-A3-300x199.jpg" alt="Audi A3 300x199 picture" width="300" height="199" /></a>hatchback. Starting manufacturer’s    suggested retail price (MSRP) fits the criteria, too, with the range    from $27,270 to $29,950. A3 is available with gasoline or diesel  engines    and offers plenty of Audi’s trademark features, such as Quattro  all-wheel    drive and S-tronic direct-shift gearbox. <a href="http://www.kbb.com/new-cars/audi/a3/2010/review" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.kbb.com']);" target="_blank"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Kelley    Blue Book</span></a> says:  “Hungering    for a European sports sedan like an Audi A4, BMW 3-Series or  Mercedes-Benz    C-Class, but can’t quite justify the expense? If so, the 2010 Audi’s    taut handling, sophisticated design and hatchback versatility, at a    price lower than those worthy machines, could make this car a keeper.”</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://www.iseecars.com/Used_Cars/used-Acura_TSX"  target="_blank"><strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Acura     TSX</span></strong></a><strong> – </strong>With a base MSRP starting at $29,310, the <span class='wp_keywordlink'><a href="http://www.iseecars.com/car/2010-acura-tsx" title="2010 Acura TSX
" target="_blank">2010 Acura TSX</a></span> is    a real bargain for an entry-level luxury small car. Of course, you can     go up to the top-end base MSRP of $37,950 and adding in options will    cost even more. Remember, however, that you never pay invoice, so work     your best deal to get what you want. Consumers searching for a  fun-to-drive    luxury small car should really check out the TSX, especially with the    optional V6 engine. High-quality cabin, lots of standard features,  seamless    shifting with the <span class='wp_keywordlink'><a href="http://www.iseecars.com/cars/manual-transmission-cars " title="manual cars list
" target="_blank">manual transmission</a></span> and good fuel economy with the    automatic are just a few of the plusses. <a href="http://www.roadandtrack.com/tests/impressions/2010-acura-tsx-v-6" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.roadandtrack.com']);" target="_blank"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Road    and Track</span></a> comments: “When     driven in a more responsible manner – meaning day-to-day commuting    and the occasional on-ramp Grand Prix, the TSX V-6 is a smooth-riding,     quiet and powerful machine that’s wanting for very little. Combine    all that with a very-cool and well-built interior (including terrific    front seats), and it seems Acura has another winner on its hands.”</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://www.iseecars.com/Used_Cars/used-BMW_1%20Series"  target="_blank"><strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">BMW    1-Series</span></strong></a><strong> – </strong>Long considered the benchmark for luxury cars, BMW offers an    entry-level small car in the <a href="http://blog.iseecars.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/bmw_1series_2010.jpg" ><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-1407" title="bmw_1series_2010" src="http://blog.iseecars.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/bmw_1series_2010-300x300.jpg" alt="bmw 1series 2010 300x300 picture" width="300" height="300" /></a>1-Series with a 2010 128i model base MSRP     of $29,150. Top-end MSRP for the 135i convertible is $40,350. BMW  1-Series    is only offered as a coupe or convertible, though, so if you’re in    the market for a small luxury sedan, check out the other picks. The    rear-wheel drive 1-Series is powered by a 3.0-liter inline 6 that gets     230 horsepower in 128i and 300 horsepower with twin turbos in the  135i. <a href="http://www.edmunds.com/bmw/1series/2010/review.html" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.edmunds.com']);" target="_blank"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Edmunds</span></a> says: “The styling won’t suit everyone,    but the 2010 BMW 1 Series undeniably offers a delectable combination    of performance and manners in either coupe or convertible form.”</li>
</ul>
<p>Other Best Luxury Small Cars $20-35K  include the <a href="http://www.iseecars.com/Used_Cars/used-Volvo_C30"  target="_blank"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Volvo  C30</span></a> (base MSRP starting  at $24,600) and the <a href="http://www.iseecars.com/Used_Cars/used-Volkswagen_GTI"  target="_blank"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Volkswagen   GTI</span></a> (base MSRP range of  $23,664 to $24,269, for 2-door and 4-door, respectively).</p>
