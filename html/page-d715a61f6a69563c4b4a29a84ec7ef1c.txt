				<h2><a href="http://autotruck.wordpress.com/" rel="bookmark" title="IAM&nbsp;Union">IAM&nbsp;Union</a></h2>

				<div class="entry">

					<p style="text-align:center;">
<p align="left">Auto Truck Transport has a contract with the IAM Union, also known as the <em>International Association of Machinists and Aerospace workers</em>. Like many organizations, the guys at the bottom are the ones doing the most amount of work.</p>
<p align="left">Our <a href="http://autotruck.wordpress.com/about-the-iam-union/district-112/">Districts and Locals</a> put a large amount of effort on recruiting members, but unfortunately have little time to represent them. On the flip side, the International is mainly in charge of &#8220;maintaining&#8221; it&#8217;s ($50,000/mo) member base.</p>
<p align="left">Basically, there is a fine line on how much representing a member can get, due to the amount of politics within the ranks. With this being said;</p>
<p align="center">&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;-</p>
<p align="left">After 8 years with this company, I (we) have been gouged over <strong>$35,000 in lost wages</strong>. <a href="http://autotruck.wordpress.com/auto_truck_transport/" target="_self">Auto truck</a> has a history of shorting our mileage pay and the IAM has only allowed it to happen with a weak/manipulative contract. To make matters worse, driving is only <a href="http://autotruck.wordpress.com/health-and-wages/lost-wages/">half of our job</a>!</p>
<p style="text-align:center;"><img src="http://autotruck.files.wordpress.com/2007/07/508979960_aadedd36af.jpg?w=468" alt="508979960_aadedd36af.jpg" /><br />
&#8230;. The IAM International&#8217;s Private Jet &#8230;</p>
<h2 style="text-align:center;"><a href="http://unionfacts.org/unions/unionOfficers.cfm?id=107&amp;sort=total&amp;year=2006" target="_blank"> [[ Click here to see a list of Executive Salaries! ]]</a></h2>
<p style="text-align:center;">
<p><strong>After years of pursuing the </strong><a href="http://autotruck.wordpress.com/health-and-wages/2006-miles/"><strong>mileage issue</strong></a> (and payroll issues), I finally get the IAM, (<a href="http://autotruck.wordpress.com/2006/11/22/response-to-unions-decision/#more-34">Anderson</a>) to come to our terminal to resolve the matter. I had stacks of documentation showing him how much Auto Truck had been screwing us out of our mileage/wages.</p>
<p>The end result? The amount of my time gathering all this paperwork, the harrasment, the forced loads, the stress, the intimidation &#8230; all ended up something like this;</p>
<ul>
<li>Boysen showed up a half hour before the meeting.</li>
<li>Called me a computer geek outside, after coming to him for suggestions on how to present our case.</li>
<li>Walked away while trying to show him three years worth of documentation proving <a href="http://autotruck.wordpress.com/health-and-wages/2006-miles/">the company was shorting us miles</a>.</li>
<li>Showed him an article showing <a href="http://www.everytruckjob.com/press-releases/0506/barr-nunn-transportation.html" target="_blank">a non union company paying their employees <em>Practical Miles</em></a>. He tells me to go work for that company.</li>
<li>And then the meeting ends with him going to lunch, paid for by the company representatives!</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Not only was this all a waste of time</strong>, but a punch in the gut for all that I have done for these guys. Ever since I became a shop steward for the IAM, my career has done nothing but go downhill and add more stress to my life.</p>
