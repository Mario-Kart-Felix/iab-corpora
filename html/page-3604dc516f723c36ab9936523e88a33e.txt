   <h2 itemprop="alternativeHeadline" class="article-promo">Getting Ready for and Repairing Paint Damage</h2><div id="article-page-0"><p>Running narrow trails and having oversized tires that kick up every passing stone is hard on your truck's paint job. What sounds like fingernails scraping a blackboard as you squeeze through some brush may be tough on your ears, but its even worse on your paint.<br>And that loud crack you hear when a rock is hurled from your tire, smacks your paint, and proceeds into a low-earth orbit isn't exactly good for your truck's color either. While not much short of staying off the trails can be done to prevent this kind of damage, there are ways to make your paint less susceptible to it. There are also ways to fix the damage if it does occur, without having to repaint the whole truck. Check out the sidebars below for some tips on how to keep your rig's exterior in shape.</p><div class="article-photos"><h2>Photos</h2><a href="/how-to/3283/photo-gallery/" title="Photo Gallery" class="photos-viewall" onclick="s_objectID='SeeAllPhotos';">View Gallery</a><ul class="photo-list clearfix"><li class="photo-item"><span itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject"><a href="/how-to/3283/photo-01.html" itemprop="url" title="131 9907 Pain 01 Z" onclick="s_objectID='Photo01';"><img src="http://image.fourwheeler.com/f/8745464+w270+h180+cr1+ar0/131_9907_pain_01_z.jpg" itemprop="thumbnailUrl" alt="131 9907 Pain 01 Z" title="131 9907 Pain 01 Z" width="100%" onerror="this.src='/i/noimage.jpg'" /></a></span></li><li class="photo-item"><span itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject"><a href="/how-to/3283/photo-02.html" itemprop="url" title="131 9907 Pain 02 Z" onclick="s_objectID='Photo02';"><img src="http://image.fourwheeler.com/f/8745470+w270+h180+cr1+ar0/131_9907_pain_02_z.jpg" itemprop="thumbnailUrl" alt="131 9907 Pain 02 Z" title="131 9907 Pain 02 Z" width="100%" onerror="this.src='/i/noimage.jpg'" /></a></span></li></ul></div><p><b>Chip Fix</b><br>OK, those big meats are cool, but they throw every rock they can get their hands on at your paint job. Don't ignore those chips. They make easy entrances for moisture, which causes unwanted rust. Luckily, this fix is an easy one.</p><div class="article-photos"><h2>Photos</h2><a href="/how-to/3283/photo-gallery/" title="Photo Gallery" class="photos-viewall" onclick="s_objectID='SeeAllPhotos';">View Gallery</a><ul class="photo-list clearfix"><li class="photo-item"><span itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject"><a href="/how-to/3283/photo-03.html" itemprop="url" title="131 9907 Pain 03 Z" onclick="s_objectID='Photo03';"><img src="http://image.fourwheeler.com/f/8745491+w270+h180+cr1+ar0/131_9907_pain_03_z.jpg" itemprop="thumbnailUrl" alt="131 9907 Pain 03 Z" title="131 9907 Pain 03 Z" width="100%" onerror="this.src='/i/noimage.jpg'" /></a></span></li><li class="photo-item"><span itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject"><a href="/how-to/3283/photo-04.html" itemprop="url" title="131 9907 Pain 04 Z" onclick="s_objectID='Photo04';"><img src="http://image.fourwheeler.com/f/8965180+w270+h180+cr1+ar0/131_9907_pain_04_z.jpg" itemprop="thumbnailUrl" alt="131 9907 Pain 04 Z" title="131 9907 Pain 04 Z" width="100%" onerror="this.src='/i/noimage.jpg'" /></a></span></li><li class="photo-item"><span itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject"><a href="/how-to/3283/photo-05.html" itemprop="url" title="131 9907 Pain 05 Z" onclick="s_objectID='Photo05';"><img src="http://image.fourwheeler.com/f/8745512+w270+h180+cr1+ar0/131_9907_pain_05_z.jpg" itemprop="thumbnailUrl" alt="131 9907 Pain 05 Z" title="131 9907 Pain 05 Z" width="100%" onerror="this.src='/i/noimage.jpg'" /></a></span></li></ul></div><p><b>Scratch Science</b><br>If you take your vehicle off road you are going to get scratches. Besides being ugly, scratches give moisture easy access to metal, causing rust. Fixing scratches is a little more involved than fixing a paint chip.</p><div class="article-photos"><h2>Photos</h2><a href="/how-to/3283/photo-gallery/" title="Photo Gallery" class="photos-viewall" onclick="s_objectID='SeeAllPhotos';">View Gallery</a><ul class="photo-list clearfix"><li class="photo-item"><span itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject"><a href="/how-to/3283/photo-06.html" itemprop="url" title="131 9907 Pain 06 Z" onclick="s_objectID='Photo06';"><img src="http://image.fourwheeler.com/f/8965207+w270+h180+cr1+ar0/131_9907_pain_06_z.jpg" itemprop="thumbnailUrl" alt="131 9907 Pain 06 Z" title="131 9907 Pain 06 Z" width="100%" onerror="this.src='/i/noimage.jpg'" /></a></span></li><li class="photo-item"><span itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject"><a href="/how-to/3283/photo-07.html" itemprop="url" title="131 9907 Pain 07 Z" onclick="s_objectID='Photo07';"><img src="http://image.fourwheeler.com/f/8965225+w270+h180+cr1+ar0/131_9907_pain_07_z.jpg" itemprop="thumbnailUrl" alt="131 9907 Pain 07 Z" title="131 9907 Pain 07 Z" width="100%" onerror="this.src='/i/noimage.jpg'" /></a></span></li><li class="photo-item"><span itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject"><a href="/how-to/3283/photo-08.html" itemprop="url" title="131 9907 Pain 08 Z" onclick="s_objectID='Photo08';"><img src="http://image.fourwheeler.com/f/8965243+w270+h180+cr1+ar0/131_9907_pain_08_z.jpg" itemprop="thumbnailUrl" alt="131 9907 Pain 08 Z" title="131 9907 Pain 08 Z" width="100%" onerror="this.src='/i/noimage.jpg'" /></a></span></li></ul></div></div>
