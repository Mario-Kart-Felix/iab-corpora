									<h1 class="post-title"><a href="http://www.werksauto.com/keep-it-flowing-with-a-fuel-filter-replacement-at-werks-auto-diesel-repair/">Keep it Flowing With A Fuel Filter Replacement At Werks Auto &#038; Diesel Repair</a></h1>
			 
		    

		    
		
				
			<p><object id="video_player" width="400" height="320" classid="clsid:d27cdb6e-ae6d-11cf-96b8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" name="video_player"><param name="flashvars" value="file=http://autonettv.com/media/FuelFilter_Terry_LM.flv&amp;image=http://autonettv.com/media/thumbs/FuelFilter_Terry_LM.jpg&amp;autostart=false" /><param name="src" value="http://autonettv.com/media/mediaplayer.swf" /><param name="AllowScriptAccess" value="always" /><param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always" /><embed id="video_player" width="400" height="320" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" src="http://autonettv.com/media/mediaplayer.swf" flashvars="file=http://autonettv.com/media/FuelFilter_Terry_LM.flv&amp;image=http://autonettv.com/media/thumbs/FuelFilter_Terry_LM.jpg&amp;autostart=false" AllowScriptAccess="always" allowscriptaccess="always" name="video_player" /></object></p>
<p>The function of the fuel filter is pretty self-explanatory. It filters your fuel. The fuel filter is in the fuel line somewhere in between the fuel tank and the engine. Both gas and diesel vehicles around Gypsum CO use fuel filters.</p>
<blockquote><p>For more information about your fuel filter, visit <strong><a title="Werks Auto &amp; Diesel Repair" href="http://" target="_blank">Werks Auto &amp; Diesel Repair</a></strong> or come by our shop located at 695 Lindbergh Dr in Gypsum, CO 81637.<br />
Please call <strong>970.328.9000</strong> to make an appointment.</p></blockquote>
<p>Generally speaking there&#8217;s not a lot of dirt in our Gypsum CO auto fuel supply, but there is enough that you want to screen it out. The problem actually gets worse the older your vehicle becomes. That&#8217;s because dirt, rust and other contaminants will settle out of the fuel and onto the bottom of the fuel tank. After your family car is five years or older, it can actually have a fair amount of sediment built up.</p>
<p>That just means that the <strong>fuel filter</strong> has to work harder as your family car ages. It&#8217;ll get clogged sooner and need to be replaced more often.</p>
<p>A symptom of a <a title="Gypsum clogged fuel filter" href="http://" target="_blank">clogged fuel filter</a> is that the engine sputters at highway speeds or under hard acceleration. That&#8217;s because enough fuel is getting through around town, but when you need more fuel for speed, enough just can&#8217;t get through the filter. Obviously, that could be dangerous if your car or truck can&#8217;t get enough power to get you out of harm&#8217;s way.</p>
<p>For just that reason, fuel filters have a bypass valve. When the filter is severely clogged, some fuel can bypass the filter all together. Of course that means that dirty, unfiltered fuel is getting through to be burned in the engine.</p>
<p>This dirt can then clog and damage your <a title="Gypsum fuel injectors" href="http://" target="_blank">fuel injectors</a>. Now injectors are not cheap to replace, so you don&#8217;t want to cause them damage just because you didn&#8217;t spend a few bucks to replace a fuel filter.</p>
<p>You know, in a way, the fuel filter can be the poster child for preventive maintenance. It&#8217;s a little part, it&#8217;s simple and it&#8217;s cheap to take care of. But if it&#8217;s neglected, it could lead to thousands of dollars of repair bills.</p>
<p>Those <a title="Gypsum auto service schedules" href="http://" target="_blank">auto service schedules</a> in your owner&#8217;s manual are there for a reason. If ever you don&#8217;t understand a recommended service, just ask your Gypsum service advisor at Werks Auto &amp; Diesel Repair. We&#8217;ll be happy to explain.</p>

									<h1 class="post-title"><a href="http://www.werksauto.com/e-85-fuel-safety-advice-from-werks-auto-diesel-repair/">E-85 Fuel Safety Advice From Werks Auto &#038; Diesel Repair</a></h1>
			 
		    

		    
		
				
			<p><object id="video_player" width="400" height="320" classid="clsid:d27cdb6e-ae6d-11cf-96b8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" name="video_player"><param name="flashvars" value="file=http://autonettv.com/media/FuelSafety_MichaelEmily_TX.flv&amp;image=http://autonettv.com/media/thumbs/FuelSafety_MichaelEmily_TX.jpg&amp;autostart=false" /><param name="src" value="http://autonettv.com/media/mediaplayer.swf" /><param name="AllowScriptAccess" value="always" /><embed id="video_player" width="400" height="320" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" src="http://autonettv.com/media/mediaplayer.swf" flashvars="file=http://autonettv.com/media/FuelSafety_MichaelEmily_TX.flv&amp;image=http://autonettv.com/media/thumbs/FuelSafety_MichaelEmily_TX.jpg&amp;autostart=false" AllowScriptAccess="always" name="video_player" /></object></p>
<p>Today at Werks Auto &amp; Diesel Repair we want to talk with you about a very important safety issue. This <a title="Gypsum automotive safety warning" href="http://" target="_blank">automotive safety warning</a> is coming from a very unusual source: <strong>fire fighting experts</strong>. You’ve probably heard of <strong>E-85 gasoline</strong> being offered in the Burns area. Some newer vehicle models are specifically built to run on E-85 – the rest are not.</p>
<blockquote><p>Does your vehicle use E-85 gasoline? Bring it down to <a title="Werks Auto &amp; Diesel Repair in Gypsum" href="http://" target="_blank">Werks Auto &amp; Diesel Repair</a> at 695 Lindbergh Dr in Gypsum, CO 81637 to find out, or give us a call to make an appointment by calling <strong>970.328.9000</strong>.</p></blockquote>
<p>E-85 gasoline has been developed to fight air pollution and reduce oil consumption. E-85 fuel is a mix of 85% ethanol, a grain-base alcohol, and 15% gasoline. So-called <strong>Flex-Fuel</strong> vehicles are designed to use either normal gasoline or E-85 gas. The result is lower harmful exhaust emissions to our local Burns community.</p>
<p>All gas engines can run with up to 10% ethanol and in fact a lot of gasoline sold in the Gypsum CO area does contain some ethanol. The problem comes when well meaning people without Flex Fuel certified vehicles put E-85 into the tank.</p>
<p>At a 15% concentration, the potential for problems arises. Because of the chemical differences between ethanol and gasoline, special seals and gaskets are needed for Flex Fuel vehicles in Gypsum CO. In a normal engine, the ethanol in E-85 gas will eat away important seals and gaskets. This leads to gas and oil leaks.</p>
<p>You can imagine the fire hazard caused by leaking gasoline. Fire fighting experts caution consumers to only use E-85 if they know they vehicle is certified to handle it. They expect vehicle fires to increase because of using the wrong fuel in non-Flex Fuel vehicles.</p>
<p>So are people who don’t have a Flex Fuel vehicle but use E-85 to help the environment actually putting themselves at risk? Yes. In fact, <a title="service and repair centers in Gypsum CO" href="http://" target="_blank">service and repair centers in Gypsum CO</a> are being warned to purchase fire extinguishers that use the special foam needed to extinguish ethanol fires as a precaution in case starting-up an ethanol damaged engine leads to a fire.</p>
<p>Obviously, this isn’t something you want to have in your Gypsum CO garage at home, either. If you have a Flex-Fuel certified vehicle, feel free to protect the environment by using E-85 gasoline. If not – please do not use E-85 in an engine that’s designed to run on unleaded gasoline only. At <strong><a title="Werks Auto &amp; Diesel Repair" href="http://" target="_blank">Werks Auto &amp; Diesel Repair</a></strong> we are concerned about your safety. If you have questions or want to find out how to determine if your vehicle can safely use E-85, go to <a title="http://www.e85fuel.com" href="http://www.e85fuel.com" target="_blank">www.e85fuel.com</a> for more resources.</p>

<p>It would be very tempting to skip the 30,000 mile service if you knew you’d be turning in your leased vehicle in a couple of months.</p>
