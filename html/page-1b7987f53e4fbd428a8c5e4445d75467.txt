								<h1 class="single-title post-title" itemprop="headline">Women&#8217;s Motorcycle Helmets</h1>
                                
								<p class="meta"> <time datetime="2012-02-16">02/16/2012</time>, <a href="http://www.automotivetips.org/author/marj-licos/" title="Posts by Marj Licos" rel="author">Marj Licos</a>, <a href="http://www.automotivetips.org/womens-motorcycle-helmets/#comments" title="Comment on Women&#8217;s Motorcycle Helmets">9 Comments</a></p>
													
							</header> <!-- end article header -->
					
							<section class="post_content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
	                        									<p style="text-align: justify;"><a href="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/Nolan_N42E.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-277" title="Nolan_N42E" src="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/Nolan_N42E.jpg" alt="" width="296" height="300" /></a>It’s been proven that among the different modes of transportation, motorcycle ride is the more dangerous way to travel. When a motorcycle rider meets an accident, he may experience hospitalization, long-term disability, or even death. In 2008, statistics reveal that there were more deaths that resulted from motorcycle accidents over car accidents.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Thus, the need for all kinds of motorcycle protective apparel made available to the riders. There are helmets, shades/goggles, jackets, pants, vests, and full riding suits, rainwear, riding underwear, gloves and socks. Among these, the motorcycle helmet is considered the most important personal protective riding gear.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">There are motorcycle helmets that are made for unisex. However, as women motorcycle riders have significantly increased over time, more attention is now given to women’s motorcycle helmets. A woman’s motorcycle helmet is a lot like a man’s motorcycle helmet. Both helmets are made with safety of the rider in mind. The main difference is just in the sizing and in the graphics, colors and design. In terms of size, women’s motorcycle helmets are slightly smaller than men’s motorcycle helmets.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Women’s motorcycle helmets come in more feminine and attractive colors. It could range from bright pink to loud purple. Neutral colors like black, greys, blues and whites are also available. Femininity and sexual appeal are considered in the design of motorcycle helmets for women.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">When buying a motorcycle helmet, you must give weight to protection and safety features on top of expression of personal style. The following factors must also be considered in choosing a helmet,</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">• the outer shell and inner liner</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">• proper sizing and fit</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">• approval/sticker from the concerned government agency</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><strong>Helmet Construction</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Helmets have two protective components: the outer shell and inner liner. The hard outer shell provides skull protection from puncture of pointed objects. Also, this is designed to spread the kinetic force from the impact across itself prior to conduction into the helmet. The shock-absorbing inner liner acts as the second layer of defense when the force of impact can no longer be contained by the outer shell.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><strong>Helmet Sizing and Fit</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">When choosing a motorcycle helmet, refer to the manufacturer’s fitting chart as a guide. Women’s motorcycle helmet sizes come in extra small, small, medium, large, extra large (XL) and 2XL. It is still best to try on the motorcycle helmet when buying. Outwardly pull to widen the chin strap and place the helmet over the head. It should have comfortable, snug fit and its tightness should not allow you to lodge a thumb between the helmet and<a href="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/TSG-SkateBMX-Gloss-Black.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-280" title="TSG SkateBMX Gloss Black" src="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/TSG-SkateBMX-Gloss-Black-300x300.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="300" /></a> your forehead.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Adjust and fasten the chin strap but make sure that this will not restrict your neck movements. Shake your head to ensure that the helmet doesn’t move unnecessarily or fall off. In addition, press down on the helmet to check on the overall comfort and snug fit onto your head.Your field of vision, front and both sides, must not be obstructed. For riders who don corrective eyewear, make certain that the helmet can comfortably accommodate your pair of eyeglasses.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">If your have long hair, and wish to “wear” it inside the helmet while riding, do so while trying on the helmet to properly accommodate the bulk.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><strong>Approval Sticker</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">The motorcycle helmet must be approved or have a sticker issued by the Department of Transportation (DOT) of the United States of America (U.S.A.) or the Snell Memorial Foundation. A DOT or Snell sticker signifies that the helmet meets U.S. or international safety standards</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><strong>Helmet Types</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Generally, there are five principal types of motorcycle helmets;</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">• full face helmets</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">• modular helmets</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">• half helmets</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">• open face helmets</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">• motor-cross helmets</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><a href="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/Arai_SZc_Helmet.jpg"><img class="alignleft  wp-image-279" title="Arai_SZc_Helmet" src="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/Arai_SZc_Helmet.jpg" alt="" width="220" height="220" /></a>The Lady Bikers’ Den enumerates the different motorcycle helmets available for women. These types range from shorty skid lid biker helmets, German motorcycle helmets, carbon fiber motorcycle helmets, full-face sports bike or street bike helmets, open-face ¾ shell retro biker helmets and flip-up modular helmets. There is a particular style to suit your ride.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Aside from protection, a motorcycle helmet can be a fashion statement.  If you prefer to stick out and set yourself apart while riding your motorcycle, then you can choose a custom painted, designed or personalized helmet. You may visit your nearest local shop or the internet websites to check out the vast selection of custom designed motorcycle helmets.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">As motorcycle helmets are designed to break or crack in a crash in order to absorb the impact, they become easily damaged.  Replace the helmet when it has been damaged in an accident or after any impact. Ideally, it is recommended to replace the motorcycle helmet every three years or so even if it has not suffered any impact or appears to be in top condition.</p>
