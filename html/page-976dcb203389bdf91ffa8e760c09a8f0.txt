<p>The <b>Mazda3</b> or <b>Mazda 3</b> (known as the <b>Mazda Axela</b> in Japan) is a <a href="/wiki/Compact_car" title="Compact car">compact car</a> manufactured in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Mazda" title="Mazda">Mazda Motor Corporation</a>. It was introduced for the 2004 model year,<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> replacing the <a href="/wiki/Mazda_Familia" title="Mazda Familia">Familia/323/Protegé</a>. A more performance-oriented version of the Mazda3 is marketed as the <a href="/wiki/Mazdaspeed3" title="Mazdaspeed3">Mazdaspeed3</a> in North America and the Mazda3 MPS in Europe.</p>
<p>A <a href="#Second_generation">second generation</a> Mazda3 for the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Model_year" title="Model year">model year</a> was unveiled in late 2008, with the sedan premiering at the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Auto_Show" title="Los Angeles Auto Show" class="mw-redirect">Los Angeles Auto Show</a> and the hatchback at the Bologna Auto Show. For the 2012 model year, Mazda began offering the Mazda3 with their newly developed <a href="/wiki/SkyActiv" title="SkyActiv">SkyActiv</a> technology, including a more rigid body, a new direct injection engine, and a new 6-speed transmission.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>The Mazda3 has been generally well received by the automotive press for its performance, handling, styling and interior, with some describing it as feeling like a more expensive sport sedan despite its value-oriented price.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Some criticisms have included fuel economy and crash test results (<i>only receiving four out of a maximum five stars from the EURO NCAP Safety Testing Programme)</i> the latter of which was rectified by making six airbags standard.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2006 the Mazda3 was the second best-selling car in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and the best selling car in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> during 2005–2007.<sup id="cite_ref-cars.com_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cars.com-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mazda3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mazda3&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Mazda3 is based on the <a href="/wiki/Ford" title="Ford" class="mw-redirect">Ford</a> global <a href="/wiki/Ford_C1_platform" title="Ford C1 platform">C1 platform</a>, shared with the latest European <a href="/wiki/Ford_Focus_(international)" title="Ford Focus (international)" class="mw-redirect">Ford Focus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volvo_S40" title="Volvo S40">Volvo S40</a>. Based on the styling of the MX-Sportif <a href="/wiki/Concept_car" title="Concept car">concept car</a>, the Mazda3 is available in two body styles, a four-door <a href="/wiki/Sedan_(car)" title="Sedan (car)" class="mw-redirect">sedan</a>, marketed as "coupé style" in Europe, and a five-door <a href="/wiki/Hatchback" title="Hatchback">hatchback</a>, branded the Sport version in Canada, Japan, and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The lead stylist for this model generation was <a href="/wiki/Moray_Callum" title="Moray Callum">Moray Callum</a>.</p>

<p>The front suspension comprises <a href="/wiki/MacPherson_strut" title="MacPherson strut">MacPherson struts</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Coil_spring" title="Coil spring">coil springs</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Anti-roll_bar" title="Anti-roll bar" class="mw-redirect">anti-roll bar</a>. The rear suspension is a Ford-designed "E-link" <a href="/wiki/Multi-link_suspension" title="Multi-link suspension">multi-link suspension</a>, with four locating links per wheel and an anti-roll bar, suspended on coil springs that are mounted inboard of the <a href="/wiki/Shock_absorbers" title="Shock absorbers" class="mw-redirect">shock absorbers</a> to reduce suspension intrusion into the cargo area. Four-wheel <a href="/wiki/Disc_brakes" title="Disc brakes" class="mw-redirect">Disc brakes</a> are fitted, with 300&#160;mm (11.8&#160;in) discs in the front and 279&#160;mm (11&#160;in) discs in the rear; <a href="/wiki/Anti-lock_braking_system" title="Anti-lock braking system">ABS</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_brake_force_distribution" title="Electronic brake force distribution" class="mw-redirect">electronic brake force distribution</a> are available or standard, depending on the model. Wheel and tire sizes vary with model, from 15" on base models to optional 17" wheels on upper-level models.</p>
<p>When first introduced, United States-market Mazda3 models were available in only two trim levels, i and s, with the 2.0&#160;L and 2.3&#160;L engines, respectively. Since then Mazda has introduced additional models under the Touring and Grand Touring labels. <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> Mazda3s are offered in S, TS, TS2, Sport, and a top end 2.3ltr turbocharged Mazda3 MPS (<a href="/wiki/Mazdaspeed" title="Mazdaspeed">Mazda Performance Series</a>) models. Since April 2008, when there was a mainly cosmetic facelift of the Mazda3, there have been some changes to the trim designations for UK cars, with the models now being the entry level S, then Takara (which replaces TS &amp; TS2), the Tamara Special Edition and the Sport and MPS as before.</p>
<p>All 3 models use the <a href="/wiki/Inline-4" title="Inline-4" class="mw-redirect">inline-4</a> <a href="/wiki/Mazda_MZR_engine" title="Mazda MZR engine">Mazda MZR engine</a>, with various types, displacements and outputs including the <a href="/wiki/Mazda_Z_engine#MZ-CD" title="Mazda Z engine">MZ-CD</a> <a href="/wiki/Turbodiesel" title="Turbodiesel" class="mw-redirect">turbodiesel</a>, depending on model and market. Transmissions are a five-speed <a href="/wiki/Manual_transmission" title="Manual transmission">manual transmission</a> and a four-speed <a href="/wiki/Automatic_transmission" title="Automatic transmission">automatic transmission</a>; since the 2006 model year, a five-speed automatic is optional on models with the 2.3 L engine. This transmission has now been made standard on the 2.0 L engine in Japan (FWD models only), as part of a minor facelift in early 2008 which includes different front/rear bumper designs, new wheel designs and body colors, stiffened chassis, and better interior materials. The MPS / Mazdaspeed version is only available with a six-speed manual.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Performance">Performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mazda3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Performance">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mazda3&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Performance" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Official performance figures for the European Mazda3 1.4 S, the lowest-powered model, are 0–100&#160;km/h in 14.3 seconds, with a maximum speed of 170&#160;km/h (106&#160;mph). <i><a href="/wiki/Wheels_magazine" title="Wheels magazine" class="mw-redirect">Wheels magazine</a></i> reported an 8.7-second 0–100&#160;km/h time for the Australian 2.0 model in its May 2004 issue.</p>
<p>The 1.6 CiTD 80&#160;kW diesel (as sold in Europe) with a five-speed manual does 100&#160;km/h in 11.6 seconds and has top of 182&#160;km/h (113&#160;mph) according to the official Mazda specifications.</p>
<p>In test results for the 2012 Mazda 3 Maxx Sport five-door 2.0-litre engine, it has been reported as having an acceleration time of 9.2 seconds (0–100&#160;km/h) and a top speed of 190&#160;km/h.</p>
<p>In its test results for the 2004 Mazda3 five-door with the 2.3-litre engine, <i>Car and Driver</i> magazine reported a 0–60&#160;mph (0–97&#160;km/h) acceleration time of 7.4 seconds and a <a href="/wiki/Governor_(device)" title="Governor (device)">governor-limited</a> top speed of 190&#160;km/h (118&#160;mph).</p>
<p>The fuel consumption of these models averages in the 10&#160;L/100&#160;km (24&#160;mpg<sub><small>-US</small></sub>; 28&#160;mpg<sub><small>-imp</small></sub>), with the 2-litre 2008 Mazda3 automatic-transmission model scoring a 10&#160;L/100&#160;km (24&#160;mpg<sub><small>-US</small></sub>; 28&#160;mpg<sub><small>-imp</small></sub>)/7.6&#160;L/100&#160;km (31&#160;mpg<sub><small>-US</small></sub>; 37&#160;mpg<sub><small>-imp</small></sub>) city/highway <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">United States Environmental Protection Agency</a> (EPA) rating.</p>

<p>For the 2006 model year, Mazda added <a href="/wiki/Variable_valve_timing" title="Variable valve timing">variable valve timing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Variable_Length_Intake_Manifold" title="Variable Length Intake Manifold" class="mw-redirect">variable-length intake runners</a> to the 2.0 L engine resulting in a power increase to 150&#160;bhp (112&#160;kW; 152&#160;PS). The automatic transmission used in Mazda3 S trim with the 2.3 L engine was changed from a four-speed to a five-speed design. The larger 2.3 L engine was PZEV-certified (Partial Zero Emissions Vehicle) for vehicles sold in California and other states that have adopted California automotive emission standards. The 2.0 L engine had already been PZEV-certified. The colour palette was also simplified in 2006, with the deletion of Canary Yellow and Lava Orange Mica.</p>

<p>The 3 received a minor cosmetic facelift for the 2007 model year with minimal exterior, interior and mechanical changes. On base models, the black plastic at the top of the grille became body-coloured. The front fascia and bumper were changed with a floating foglight design and the lower air intake opening was reshaped to better resemble the typical "Mazda five-point face." All Mazda3 sedans and five-doors gained the same "Axela" clear-lens style rear tail-lights as the SP23 model. Which in 2007 Mazda brought out the Mazda Axela similar to the Mazda6 Atenza . In addition, the Grand Touring trim also featured LED brake lights. The LED brake lights were added to compete with the growing trend of higher-end vehicles using LED tail lamps for more visible light output. The range of alloy wheels were redesigned, featuring a 17&#160;inch alloy wheel for the Grand Touring version. The rear fascia was slightly changed adding a notch on the bumper cover of the sedan. Several new exterior colors were added, phantom blue, a copper red metallic (April 2008), Aurora Blue and dark cherry. The Titanium Gray color was replaced with a darker Galaxy Gray color.</p>
<p>The interior of the Mazda3 was offered with several new color choices and an audio jack in the centre console, allowing the use of digital music players. Also, Takara models, which were introduced in the 2008 upgrade, added climate control and 6&#160;cd autochanger to the TS specification.</p>
<p>The 2007 model also includes less visible mechanical changes. The keyless entry system was improved. Reinforcements to the body shell improve overall chassis rigidity. The front dampers of the <a href="/wiki/MacPherson_strut" title="MacPherson strut">MacPherson strut suspension</a> have been re-tuned to quicken steering response and reduce understeer. The hydraulic dampers of the multi-link rear suspension were also re-tuned to match the front suspension changes. Mazda engineers and designers addressed concerns regarding cabin noise level by redesigning or changing multiple systems and adding sound-deadening material to the roof lining and hood panel.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The bumper-to-bumper warranty was reduced to 36 months / 36,000&#160;miles while the powertrain warranty was increased to 60 months / 60,000&#160;miles in most markets. In the UK all Mazda3s have a 3 year unlimited mileage warranty, 3 year roadside assistance and 12 year anti perforation warranty as standard.</p>
<p>Canadian Mazda3s received standard seat mounted side-airbags and body shell mounted side curtain airbags across the model range. Previously both airbag types were not available.</p>

<p>In Canada, the 2008 model year was replaced in Spring 2008.</p>
<p>An additional trim level included the Sport GX with a 2.0L engine, previously all Sport hatchbacks had a 2.3L engine and came only in GS or GT trims. Compared to the GX sedan, the GX hatchback has body-coloured door handles, and fog lamps. Options for the Sport GX include air conditioning, a convenience package containing power accessories and 15-inch alloys, and a rear trunk spoiler.</p>
<p>The GS sedan received standard 16-inch alloy wheels, previously only available with the moonroof option, while the GX had 15-inch alloy wheels added to the convenience package (which includes steering-wheel mounted audio controls, power windows, power locks, and remote entry). The GS hatchback now comes with factory-installed air conditioning. GT models received standard xenon headlights, previously part of the leather package.</p>
<p>In the U.S., the i Touring trim level was replaced by the i Touring Value model. This new model added a body-color front grille, fog lights and 17" alloy wheels, giving it a very similar appearance to the more expensive s models. The interior also received leather-covered steering wheel and shift knob from the s model. Anti-lock brakes and side-impact airbags, previously optional, were now standard.</p>

<p>In October 2008 Mazda released pictures and video showing the next generation design of the Mazda3. Mazda has restyled the exterior to reflect its then design language and a dual exhaust. Two engines are offered in the US and Canadian markets, the 2.0L petrol engine offered in the previous generation and a new 2.5L <a href="/wiki/Inline_4" title="Inline 4" class="mw-redirect">inline-4</a> that is shared with the second-generation <a href="/wiki/Mazda6" title="Mazda6">Mazda6</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ford_C1_platform" title="Ford C1 platform">C1</a> architecture, a collaboration of Ford, Mazda, and Volvo,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> from the previous generation is carried over.<sup id="cite_ref-C.26D2010_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-C.26D2010-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> The new model is marginally wider, longer and lighter than the previous generation Mazda3. The <a href="/wiki/Mazda_MZR_engine#2.3L_.28L3-VE.2C_L3-DE.29" title="Mazda MZR engine">2.3L</a> engine was replaced with a <a href="/wiki/Mazda_L_engine#2.5L_.28L5-VE.29" title="Mazda L engine">2.5L</a> engine that produces 167&#160;bhp (125&#160;kW; 169&#160;PS) and 167&#160;lb·ft (226&#160;N·m) of torque. In other markets, more engines are offered including a new 2.2L turbodiesel engine.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Mazda unveiled the 2010 Mazda3 sedan at the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Auto_Show" title="Los Angeles Auto Show" class="mw-redirect">Los Angeles Auto Show</a> on 19 November 2008. The new Mazda3 is slightly larger than the older Mazda3 and is available in "i" and "s" versions. The "i" is powered by a 148 horsepower (110&#160;kW) <a href="/wiki/Mazda_MZR_engine#2.0" title="Mazda MZR engine">2.0L</a> engine while the "s" is powered by a 167 horsepower (125&#160;kW) <a href="/wiki/Mazda_MZR_engine#2.5" title="Mazda MZR engine">2.5L</a> engine adapted from the <a href="/wiki/Mazda6" title="Mazda6">Mazda6</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cars.about.com_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cars.about.com-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> Both engines are also available in PZEV versions, the 2.0L producing 144&#160;hp (107&#160;kW) and the 2.5L producing 165&#160;hp (123&#160;kW).<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> The 3s Grand Touring model adds leather seats, power driver's seat with memory, rain-sensing windshield wipers, dual-zone automatic climate control, and steerable active bi-xenon headlights as standard equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-cars.about.com_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cars.about.com-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> The hatchback was unveiled a few weeks after the sedan at the <a href="/wiki/Bologna_Motor_Show" title="Bologna Motor Show">Bologna Motor Show</a>.</p>
<p>The European model includes the i-stop feature for the 2.0L DISI engine with an estimated increase <a href="/wiki/Fuel_economy_in_automobiles" title="Fuel economy in automobiles">fuel economy</a> in city cycle by roughly 12%.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> This feature stops the engine each time the car comes to a complete stop, it quickly starts up again without the need of a starter when the vehicle proceeds.</p>
<p>Full details and images of the high-powered Mazdaspeed3 (aka Mazda3 MPS) were released at the <a href="/wiki/Geneva_Motor_Show" title="Geneva Motor Show">Geneva Motor Show</a> in March 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> Like the previous generation, the Mazda3 hatchback variant has been selected as the basis for the 2nd generation <a href="/wiki/Mazdaspeed3" title="Mazdaspeed3">Mazdaspeed3</a>.</p>

(January 2012)">where?</span></a></i>]</sup> to the features and options of the Mazda3 midway through 2010. Features such as side mirrors with integrated turn signals, footwell lighting, and power seat memory functions were removed.</p>

<p>The 2012 Mazda3 with <a href="/wiki/SkyActiv" title="SkyActiv">SkyActiv</a> powertrain was unveiled at the Canadian International Auto Show in Toronto, February 2011. It comes with Mazda’s SkyActiv-G 2.0-liter, direct-injection gasoline engine, and SkyActiv-Drive 6-speed automatic or SkyActiv-MT 6-speed manual transmission.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> There are minor exterior updates: revised grille and air intakes, and a reshaped rear valance panel. Skyactiv models receive a blue ring around the projectors in the headlamps.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> Car and Driver reported that such a Mazda3 can reach estimated EPA fuel-economy ratings of 30 mpg city and 39 to 40 highway.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
