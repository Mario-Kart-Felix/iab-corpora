            <h2>Vehicles <span>&middot;   Classifications
                &amp; Required Equipment</span></h2>
        </div>
        <p>The vehicle classification and required equipment for a
          vehicle depends on the type of vehicle and its intended use. Below
          is a list of the main vehicle classifications in Washington State along
          with a brief description and a link to additional requirements for
        each vehicle.</p>
        <p>If you are unable to get your questions answered by the information
          on our Web site,  please e-mail them to <a href="mailto:equipment@wsp.wa.gov">&#101;&#113;&#117;&#105;&#112;&#109;&#101;&#110;&#116;&#64;&#119;&#115;&#112;&#46;&#119;&#97;&#46;&#103;&#111;&#118;</a>.</p>
        <h4><a name="street" id="street"></a>Street Legal Vehicles</h4>
        <ul type="disc">
          <li><a href="docs/equipmt/passenger.pdf">Passenger Vehicle</a> â
            any vehicle that has four or more wheels in contact with the ground.
            These vehicles must meet the state and federal requirements for a
            motor vehicle.<br />
            <br />
          </li>
          <li><a href="docs/equipmt/motorcycle.pdf">Motorcycle</a> â 
          any vehicle that has two or three wheels in contact with the ground.
            These vehicles must meet the state and federal requirements for a
            motorcycle.<br /><br /></li>
            <li><a href="http://www.dol.wa.gov/vehicleregistration/offroadmotorcycles.html">Converted Off-Road Motorcycle</a> â 
          any vehicle designated by its manufacturer as off-road only that has two wheels and is designed to be straddled and steered with handlebars.  In order to convert an off-road motorcycle, owners must file a declaration form with Department of Licensing which includes certification by a licensed dealership or repair shop that the equipment meets the state and federal standards for a motorcycle.</li>
        </ul>
        <h4>Street legal vehicles with speed restrictions</h4>
        <ul type="disc">
          <li><a href="docs/equipmt/moped.pdf">Moped</a> â any vehicle that
            travels less than 30 mph AND has an engine size of less than 50cc
            AND has two or three wheels in contact with the ground. These vehicles
            must meet the state and federal requirements for a motor-driven cycle.<br />
            <br />
          </li>
          <li><a href="docs/equipmt/lowspeed_veh.pdf">Low Speed Electric Vehicles</a> â
            any electric vehicle that travels on four wheels at a speed less
            than 35 mph may be classified as a Medium Speed Vehicle (25-35mph)
            or a Neighborhood Electric Vehicle (up to 25mph). These vehicles
            must meet the state and federal requirements for low speed electric
            vehicles.</li>
        </ul>
        <h4><a name="not" id="not"></a>Motorized Devices not classified as vehicles</h4>
        <p>The following devices must operate under certain restrictions:</p>
        <ul type="disc">
          
          <li><a href="docs/equipmt/golf_cart.pdf">Golf Cart</a> â any vehicle originally designed and manufactured for operation on a golf course for sporting purposes and has a speed attainable in one mile of not more than 25 mph.  These may only be driven in a golf cart zone designated by city or county ordinance, and must be operated and have the required equipment as outlined in state law and local ordinances. <br /><br /></li>
          <li><a href="docs/equipmt/elect_bicycle.pdf">Electric Assisted Bicycle</a> â
            any bicycle with an electric motor that is not capable of propelling
            the device (with or without motive power) at speeds greater than
            20 mph. These vehicles must be ridden in accordance with all state
            and local restrictions and must have the required equipment as outlined
            in state law.<br />
            <br />
          </li>
          <li><a href="docs/equipmt/epmad.pdf">Electric Personal Assisted Mobility Device</a> (EPAMD) â 
          a self-balancing device with two wheels not in tandem, designed to transport only 
              one person by an electric propulsion system at speeds not greater than 20 mph.
              These vehicles must be ridden in accordance with all state and local restrictions 
            and must have the required equipment as outlined in state law.<br />
              <br />
          </li>
          <li><a href="docs/equipmt/motor_foot.pdf">Motorized Foot Scooter</a> â
            a device with no more than 2 ten-inch or smaller diameter wheels
            that has handlebars, is designed to be stood upon by the operator,
            and is powered by an internal combustion engine or electric motor
            that is capable of propelling the device with or without human propulsion at a 			speed no more than twenty miles per hour on level ground.
            These vehicles must be ridden in accordance with all state and local
            restrictions and must have the required equipment as outlined in
            state law.<br />
            <br />
          </li>
          <li><a href="docs/equipmt/offroad_atv.pdf">Off-Road Vehicle/Motorcycle</a> â
            any non-street licensed vehicle when used for recreational purposes
            on non-highway roads, trails, or a variety of other natural terrain.
            Such vehicles include, but are not limited to, all-terrain vehicles,
            motorcycles, four-wheel drive vehicles, and dune buggies. These vehicles
            must be ridden in accordance with all state and local restrictions. Except  that two-wheel off-road motorcycles may be licensable after January 1, 2012, if  they are converted to meet state and federal standards, see Converted Off-Road  Motorcycle for more information.</li>
        </ul>
        <h4><a name="trailers" id="trailers"></a>Trailers</h4>
        <p>Trailers must have all of the proper safety equipment installed
          on them in order to be legally operated on the public highway. Refer
          to the <a href="docs/cvd/170_129.pdf">Inspection and Trailer Requirements</a> 
          document for additional information outlining these requirements.</p>
        <p><a name="equip" id="equip"></a></p>
        <h3>Special Motor Vehicle Required Equipment</h3>
        <hr />
        <ul type="disc" class="indent">
          <li>Fenders - Shall cover the entire tread width, at least 15 degrees
            to the front, and 75 degrees to the rear. Shall extend down to at
            least the center of the axle. <a href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=46.37.500">RCW
            46.37.500</a> &amp; <a href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=204-10-022">WAC
            204-10-022</a></li>
          <li>Hood - With both primary and secondary latches. <a href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=46.37.517">RCW
              46.37.517</a> &amp; <a href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=204-10-022">WAC
            204-10-022</a><a href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=204-90-040"></a></li>
          <li>Doors - With door latches. <a href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=46.37.517">RCW
              46.37.517</a> &amp; <a href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=204-10-022">WAC
            204-10-022</a><a href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=204-90-040"></a></li>
          <li>Floor Pans - Under the entire passenger compartment, capable of
            supporting the weight of the number of passengers that the vehicle
            is designed to hold. <a href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=204-10-022">WAC
            204-10-022</a><a href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=204-90-040"></a></li>
          <li>Defroster/Defogger - All vehicles, after 1938, must be equipped
            with a working defroster/defogger.<a href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=204-10-022">WAC
            204-10-022</a><br />
              <br />
            * Optional on street rods (not less than 40 years old) and kit vehicles,
            operated on improved roads, and in &quot;good&quot; weather. <a href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/RCW/default.aspx?cite=46.37.518">RCW
            46.37.518</a> &amp; <a href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=204-10-022">WAC
            204-10-022</a><a href="http://apps.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=204-90-040"></a> </li>
        </ul>
        <h4>Aftermarket equipment or alterations</h4>
        <p>No alterations may be made to
          a vehicle which prohibits the required equipment on the vehicle from
          performing as intended. More information regarding restrictions
          and requirements for aftermarket equipment on vehicles may be found
          by clicking on the following <a href="passequip.htm">link</a>.</p>
