<p><strong>Luxury Cars at the 2013 Chicago Auto Show<br />
</strong></p>
<p><img width="350" height="250" align="right" alt="2014 Acura RLX" src="/assets/1/Page/2014_Acura_RLX_Vehicles.jpg" style="padding:5px;" />Though statistics prove that few people can afford a Cadillac or Mercedes-Benz, it&rsquo;s nice to dream. . . . From Acura to Volvo the floor of the Chicago Auto Show is littered with $50,000-plus luxury cars that bathe occupants in sumptuous leather and burled walnut and coddle with creature comforts like heated and cooled cup holders and massaging seats.</p>
<p>When you&rsquo;re spending enough money to buy a condo on the beach, you expect things and luxury cars deliver with the latest technology, safety features and indulgent comforts. But all luxury cars aren&rsquo;t created equal or to serve the same master. Some are flavored with a dash of sport, others pamper with luxurious appointments. Most blend the two and throw in a dash of advanced technology.</p>
<p>When choosing a luxury car you need to consider a couple of things. How much do you want to spend and how over the top do you want to go? For example, the BMW 5-Series is a fine midsize sedan that offers room for four adults and plenty of luxury appointments. But there are levels of luxury. For example, the base 528 lists for $47,800, but the line-topping 550 has a sticker $65,000. Add a couple of sport and technology packages to 550 and the price quickly rises to close to $70,000.</p>
<p>At the top of the class are flagship sedans like the Audi A8, BMW 7-Series, Jaguar XJ, Lexus LS 460, Mercedes-Benz S-Class, Porsche Panamera and the new Hyundai Equus. These cars are the pinnacle of automotive engineering and each a technological masterpiece of engineering and design.</p>
<p>So take a tour of the floor and remember if you have to ask, &quot;How much?&quot; You probably can&rsquo;t afford it! You can find the following luxury cars on display at the 2013 Chicago Auto Show or at the<a href="http://www.chicagoautoshow.com/vehicles_on_display/default.aspx"> Vehicles on Display</a> section of the auto show Web site.</p>
