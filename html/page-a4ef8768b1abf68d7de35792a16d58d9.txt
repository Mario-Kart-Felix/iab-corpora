I think it's a great compromise between a space-eating multilink rear suspension and a solid beam axle.  With a multilink rear suspension, there's usually a lot of camber change with wheel travel, and I hate that.  A twist beam axle will generally allow independent movement of each wheel, but also ties them together to prevent extreme differences in travel.<br />

I personally like the solid beam axle that we have now, but this is also looks like a good strong design.
