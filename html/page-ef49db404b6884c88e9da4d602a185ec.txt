        		    	    			    	    			    	    			    	    			    	    			    	    			    	    			    	    			    				<li><a href="/automotive/research/projects/tyre/index.html" title="Tyre dynamics">Tyre dynamics</a></li>

        	<div id='bodycopy'><p><img align="right" alt="The off-road facility" border="0" height="266" hspace="10" src="/sas/images/5364_lg_landrover in sand 2.jpg" title="" vspace="10" width="300" />The off-road-dynamic facility within the Centre for Automotive Technology (CAT) is the only centre of its type to study the relationship between machines and the soil environment in controlled, repeatable conditions.  £1.35 million has been invested in its design and development to meet the needs of those industries.</p><p>The aim of the facility is to enhance relationships with research bodies and commercial clients through the provision of research required for the continuing development of machine and environment evaluation techniques, soil dynamics, traction theory and off-road vehicle design.</p><p>Key customers include Land Rover and Klaas.</p><div><p><b>Research Capability</b></p><p>Off-Road-Dynamic involves the development of measurement method for the quantitative evaluation and modelling of tyre, off-road vehicle and implement performance.  Particular attention is paid to extreme off road surface conditions such as  loose, wind blown sands, slippery clay surfaces and wet grass, where vehicle performance can be severely limited and the environmental impact considerable.  The areas of work can be categorised broadly as soil vehicle and soil-implement interactions.</p><p>Soil vehicle studies include off-road tyre performance, measurement and characterisation, vehicle drag, thrust and slip quantification and traction surface creation and evaluation.</p><p>Soil-implement interaction studies include cable installation equipment, tillage/cultivation tool performance improvement, land anchor design and vehicle drag assessment.</p><p><b>Innovative Research Equipment</b></p><ul><li><b>A whole vehicle controlled moisture soil lane and bin</b><br />The soil lane consists of a large open top tank which is integral to the structure of the building.  This provides a 45m long, 5m wide trough with variable depth up to 1.5m into which soil can be placed in a highly controlled and repeatable manner.<br /><br /></li><li><b>A bespoke, mobile, sophisticated single wheel test apparatus<br /></b>This apparatus is capable of applying precise drive and vertical load control and is an essential piece of equipment for the advanced development of tyres – particularly in off-road applications.<br />This unique apparatus was designed, commissioned and is being further developed by the staff of the ORDF.<br /><br /></li><li><b>A whole vehicle Articulation Rig<br /></b>This rig consists of four vertical hydraulic cylinders each fitted with a contact pad containing transducers which accurately measure the vertical and horizontal force components independently.  Each pad has three axis of movement, one each in the longitudinal, lateral and vertical planes.</li></ul><p>Examples of applications include vehicle chassis/body shell stiffness evaluation, ground clearance measurement, suspension load and tyre contact patch measurement.</p><ul><li>A “Smart Winch” capable of applying precisely controlled loads up to 100kN and 50m on a vehicle</li></ul></div></div>
