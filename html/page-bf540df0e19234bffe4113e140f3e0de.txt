<p><b>Ford Performance Vehicles</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>-based, premium performance vehicle partner of automobile manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Ford_Australia" title="Ford Australia" class="mw-redirect">Ford Australia</a>. The company has produced a range of Ford based models under the <b>FPV</b> brand name since April 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-historyofFPV_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-historyofFPV-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The roots of FPV can be traced back to 1991, when the English automotive engineering company <a href="/wiki/Tickford" title="Tickford">Tickford</a> began a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Ford_Australia" title="Ford Australia" class="mw-redirect">Ford Australia</a> to produce high-performance variants of the <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> <a href="/wiki/Ford_Falcon_(Australia)" title="Ford Falcon (Australia)">Falcon</a> range. The partnership, Tickford Vehicle Engineering, saw the introduction of the <a href="/wiki/Ford_Falcon_XR6" title="Ford Falcon XR6" class="mw-redirect">Ford Falcon S-XR6</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ford_Falcon_XR8" title="Ford Falcon XR8" class="mw-redirect">Ford Falcon S-XR8</a> models. This was followed in October 1999 by the launch of the <a href="/wiki/Ford_Tickford_Experience" title="Ford Tickford Experience">Ford Tickford Experience</a> dealer network and the FTE T-Series range based on Ford’s <a href="/wiki/Ford_AU_Falcon" title="Ford AU Falcon">AU Falcon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ford_Fairlane_(Australia)" title="Ford Fairlane (Australia)">Fairlane</a> models. In 2002, following the purchase of Tickford by <a href="/wiki/Prodrive" title="Prodrive">Prodrive</a>, the Ford Performance Vehicles company was formed as a joint venture between Ford Australia and Prodrive. The FPV brand name was created to replace the FTE name and a restructured range was developed based on the <a href="/wiki/Ford_BA_Falcon" title="Ford BA Falcon">Ford BA Falcon</a> and headlined by the <a href="/wiki/Ford_Falcon_GT" title="Ford Falcon GT">FPV GT-P</a>.</p>
<p>In August 2012 it was announced that Ford Australia was to purchase the assets of Ford Performance Vehicles and continue the engineering, manufacturing and marketing of the FPV brand in Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>The 2003 BA range included the GT, GT-P, and the Pursuit. The GT was the entry-level vehicle that started with a <a href="/wiki/Suggested_retail_price" title="Suggested retail price">suggested retail price</a> of A$59,810. The GT-P was the upmarket version of the GT, with a price tag of A$69,850. The Pursuit was a ute (utility) version of the GT, featuring the same seats, basic dash/interior package and wheels. All three were powered by a unique version of Ford's 5.4-litre <a href="/wiki/Ford_Modular_engine" title="Ford Modular engine">Modular V8</a>, with DOHC 4-valve cylinder heads from the <a href="/wiki/Ford_Mustang" title="Ford Mustang">Mustang</a> Cobra R engine. FPV named this uniquely tuned engine as the <i>Boss 290</i> because of its power output. It produced 290&#160;kW (394&#160;PS; 389&#160;bhp) at 5500&#160;rpm and 520&#160;N·m (380&#160;lb·ft) of torque at 4500&#160;rpm.<br style="clear:both;" /></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004_BA_MkII_update">2004 BA MkII update</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2004 BA MkII update">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2004 BA MkII update" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The GT, GT-P and Pursuit received a new stripe package with bonnet decals, a six-speed Tremec T56 manual and the GT-P received 19" five-spoke alloy wheels. A new car and ute were added to the range, the <a href="/wiki/FPV_F6_Typhoon" title="FPV F6 Typhoon" class="mw-redirect">F6 Typhoon (sedan)</a> and F6 Tornado (ute). FPV also released the Super Pursuit, which was a Pursuit ute with GT-P extras.</p>
<p>They were FPV's version of the Ford Falcon <a href="/wiki/Ford_Barra_engine" title="Ford Barra engine">Barra engine</a>, featuring a 4.0 litre DOHC 24-valve turbocharged inline-six with variable cam timing, which produced 270&#160;kW (367&#160;PS; 362&#160;bhp) at 5250&#160;rpm and 550&#160;N·m (410&#160;lb·ft) at 2000 - 4250&#160;rpm.</p>

<p>No mechanical changes were introduced with this update; however, all models came standard with 19&#160;inch rims. Subtle styling changes were made, but the most significant news was the introduction of new Force 6 and Force 8 models. Built to rival HSV's <a href="/wiki/HSV_Senator_Signature" title="HSV Senator Signature" class="mw-redirect">Senator Signature</a>, they are mechanically identical to the auto-equipped GT model, but in a more luxury-focused package with more conservative visuals (no rear wing and more conservative colour range). The Force models are essentially an FPV version of the Fairmont Ghia (luxury model in the <a href="/wiki/Ford_Falcon_(Australia)" title="Ford Falcon (Australia)">Falcon</a> range). In the final months of the BF MkII Falcon, a trio of limited-editions were released - the GT 40th Anniversary,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> the F6 Typhoon R-Spec,<sup id="cite_ref-historyofFPV_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-historyofFPV-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> and the GT Cobra with a Cobra Ute - all three of which received stiffer "R-Spec" dampers, and in the case of the GT Cobra, a power hike. (The GT Cobra motor produced 405&#160;hp (302&#160;kW) and 398&#160;lb·ft (540&#160;N·m)).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>300 R Spec model Typhoons were produced in 2007, these had different Ardent Dark Silver colour accents on wheels and spoilers, leather upholstery, R spec floor mats, Brembo 4-pot front brakes, ten of these R-Spec Typhoons were exported by Ford Australia into Ford New Zealand, of that ten, one was fitted with a 6-speed manual transmission and the other nine were equipped with 6-speed automatic transmissions.</p>
<p>Only 200 BF GT 40th Anniversary models were produced, making it the rarest and most sought after of the limited edition models, as it was released to commemorate as well as celebrate the 40 year anniversary of the Falcon GT legend in Australia, which stretches back to 1967 with the release of the original XR GT Falcon.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_Utility_Vehicles_.28SUV.29">Sports Utility Vehicles (SUV)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sports Utility Vehicles (SUV)">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sports Utility Vehicles (SUV)" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="F6X">F6X</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: F6X">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: F6X" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In January 2008, Ford Performance Vehicles unveiled the FPV F6X, a high-performance, luxury version of the <a href="/wiki/Ford_Territory" title="Ford Territory">Ford Territory</a> <a href="/wiki/Crossover_(automobile)" title="Crossover (automobile)">crossover</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-F6X_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F6X-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The F6X is fitted with the <a href="/wiki/FPV_F6_Typhoon" title="FPV F6 Typhoon" class="mw-redirect">F6</a> 270 Turbo 4.0&#160;L DOHC in-line six-cylinder engine, a <a href="/wiki/ZF_Friedrichshafen" title="ZF Friedrichshafen">ZF</a> six-speed automatic transmission and a full-time all-wheel-drive system.<sup id="cite_ref-F6X_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F6X-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> It is the first non Falcon-based model to be produced by FPV and, with an engine producing 270&#160;kW (367&#160;PS; 362&#160;bhp) and 550&#160;N·m (406&#160;lb·ft), was claimed by the company to be the most powerful six-cylinder SUV available in Australia at that time.<sup id="cite_ref-F6X_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F6X-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The F6X was discontinued in February 2009, with approximately 226 examples having been built.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="FG_series">FG series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: FG series">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: FG series" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All models in the current FPV FG range are based on the <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> <a href="/wiki/Ford_FG_Falcon" title="Ford FG Falcon">Ford FG Falcon</a> and include the 6-<a href="/wiki/Cylinder_(engine)" title="Cylinder (engine)">cylinder</a> F6 <a href="/wiki/Sedan_(automobile)" title="Sedan (automobile)">sedan</a>, F6 <a href="/wiki/Coup%C3%A9_utility" title="Coupé utility">Ute</a> (the former Typhoon and Tornado names having been retired) and F6 E sedan, the V8 powered GS Sedan and Ute and the V8 engined GT, GT-P and GT E sedans. The 4.0&#160;L Turbocharged Inline Six produces 310&#160;kW (421&#160;PS; 416&#160;bhp) and 565&#160;N·m (417&#160;lb·ft), while the 5.4&#160;L V8 developed 315&#160;kW (428&#160;PS; 422&#160;bhp) and 551&#160;N·m (406&#160;lb·ft). The 5.4&#160;L V8 was replaced with the new 5.0&#160;L Supercharged Coyote (Miami) V8. The GT range now produces 335&#160;kW (449&#160;hp) and the GS 315&#160;kW (428&#160;PS; 422&#160;bhp). It is capable of a sub 5 second 0–100&#160;km/h (0-62&#160;mph) time, surpassing the HSV GTS (the FPV's main rival) in both power and torque. The decision was brought about because of pressure to convert to Euro IV emissions, which the 5.4&#160;L was unable to pass.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Miami_V8">Miami V8</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Miami V8">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Miami V8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2010 FPV retired the 5.4L Boss V8, in favour of a new supercharged 5.0L V8. The project, codenamed "Miami", is based on the <a href="/wiki/Ford_Modular_engine#5.0_L_Coyote" title="Ford Modular engine">Coyote 5.0 litre V8</a> and is a supercharged alloy quad cam engine.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> It has cost FPV $40 million and has taken 3 years to develop. The engine is offered in two versions; the less powerful variant produces 315&#160;kW (428&#160;PS; 422&#160;bhp) at 5750 rpm and 545&#160;N·m (402&#160;lb·ft) between 2000 - 5500 rpm. This version is offered in the GS sedan and ute. The more powerful version produces 335&#160;kW (455&#160;PS; 449&#160;bhp) between 5750 - 6000 rpm and 570&#160;N·m (420&#160;lb·ft) between 2200 - 5500 rpm. The latter is offered in the GT range, which includes GT, GT-P, and GT-E. Fuel economy was improved dropping 4.5% to 13.7&#160;L/100&#160;km for the auto and 13.6&#160;L/100&#160;km for the manual. No styling changes were made with this update, apart from different wheel designs and body striping. The model range has been changed slightly, the GS serves permanently as the entry-level variant for the sedan, and will be the only V8 ute, effectively taking the place of the Pursuit, which has been discontinued along with the Super Pursuit. The GS started at $56,990 for the sedan and the GT at $71,290.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sedans">Sedans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sedans">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sedans" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In August 2009 FPV launched new GS Sedan and GS Ute models,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> reviving a name that was first used for an option pack on the <a href="/wiki/Ford_XW_Falcon" title="Ford XW Falcon">XW Falcon</a> range of 1969. Unlike that earlier GS, the new model is a limited-edition model and it is intended to provide a lower entry-point into FPV's performance stable. At $54,950 (plus on-roads) for the Sedan, and $49,950 (plus) for the Ute, the GS variants sit between Ford's Falcon XR range and FPV's GT and F6. The pricing puts both the GS Sedan and Ute under the bar for the Luxury Car Tax.</p>
<p>Featuring GS graphics, FPV chassis enhancements and interior updates, and sitting on 19&#160;inch rims (Graphite for the Sedan, Alpine Silver for the Ute). Production includes 250 sedans and 75 utes. The GS features the new 5.0 litre V8, but produces slightly less power and torque than its more expensive V8 siblings. The engine makes 315&#160;kW (428&#160;PS; 422&#160;hp) and 545&#160;N·m (402&#160;lb·ft), and driving through the standard six-speed manual, runs a GT-spec exhaust system and sits on a GT-spec suspension set-up. The GS also offers a premium sound system, Bluetooth compatibility, iPod connectivity, GT instrumentation, starter button, and dual-zone climate control as standard features.<br style="clear:both;" /></p>

<p>The GT costs the same and has the same features as the F6, the major difference being the engine. It features a 5.0&#160;L supercharged V8, code named "Miami". The engine has 335&#160;kW (455&#160;PS; 449&#160;bhp) at 5750 rpm and 570&#160;N·m (420&#160;lb·ft) of torque between 2200 - 5500 rpm. Because of different shaped BOSS engines that are physically larger than the I6 engine in F6, the GT has a "power bulge" on the bonnet with GT decals. The GT has two transmission choices a 6-speed manual or a 6-speed automatic. Standard are 4-piston front brake calipers, 19-inch alloy wheels, dual zone climate control, and other items. The GT retails for $71,000.<br style="clear:both;" /></p>

It also includes larger brakes, adjustable pedals, performance seats, a 6-way power driver's seat, and extra GT-P decals. It also features 19-inch alloys; however, they have a different design to the regular GT.<br style="clear:both;" /></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="GT_E">GT E</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: GT E">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: GT E" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The GT E is the flagship of the new FPV range at a price of $82,000 (AUD). It is more focused on luxury rather than performance. It has the 5.0&#160;L supercharged V8 that is found in the GT models, which is mated to a <a href="/wiki/ZF_Friedrichshafen" title="ZF Friedrichshafen">ZF</a> 6-speed <a href="/wiki/Automatic_Transmission" title="Automatic Transmission" class="mw-redirect">automatic</a>. It has replaced the Force 6 and 8. The GT E comes with a boot lip spoiler, special alloy wheels and other features like a reversing camera, parking sensors, leather, and a wood grain interior. The GT-E gets the same sport luxury suspension found in the <a href="/wiki/Ford_FG_Falcon#G_Series" title="Ford FG Falcon">Ford G Series range</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="F6">F6</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: F6">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: F6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

2008–2010 FPV FG F6 sedan</div>

<p>The F6 is one of two turbocharged 6-cylinder vehicles in the FPV sedan range, with a suggested retail price of $66,590 (AUD). It comes with the same features as the F6 ute, except it also has a Control Blade independent rear suspension, dual-zone climate control, premium audio system, and reverse sensing system that are not standard on the ute. With 310&#160;kW (421&#160;PS; 416&#160;bhp) and 565&#160;N·m (417&#160;lb·ft), this can take it from 0–100&#160;km (0-62&#160;mph) in 4.9 seconds. The F6 is the quickest Ford vehicle ever sold in Australia.<br style="clear:both;" /></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="F6_E">F6 E</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: F6 E">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: F6 E" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The F6 E incorporates the features of the GT E with the turbocharged six cylinder engine from the F6.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Utes">Utes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Utes">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Utes" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="GS_Ute">GS Ute</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: GS Ute">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: GS Ute" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>The GS ute is now the only V8 powered ute in FPV's line-up. It is powered by the same 315&#160;kW (428&#160;PS; 422&#160;bhp) supercharged 5.0&#160;L V8 found in the GS sedan. Fuel economy for the GS ute is 14.0&#160;L/100&#160;km for the manual and 14.2&#160;L/100&#160;km for the automatic, both figures are small improvements over the 5.4&#160;L V8. The GS ute can be optioned with either a 6-speed manual or a ZF 6-speed automatic. The GS ute will start at $52,000.<br style="clear:both;" /></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pursuit_and_Super_Pursuit">Pursuit and Super Pursuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Pursuit and Super Pursuit">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Pursuit and Super Pursuit" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:FPV_FG_Super_Pursuit.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/FPV_FG_Super_Pursuit.jpg/220px-FPV_FG_Super_Pursuit.jpg" width="220" height="113" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/FPV_FG_Super_Pursuit.jpg/330px-FPV_FG_Super_Pursuit.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/FPV_FG_Super_Pursuit.jpg/440px-FPV_FG_Super_Pursuit.jpg 2x" /></a>

<p>FPV offered two V8 powered utes in the FG range, both using the 5.4&#160;L V8. The two models were discontinued when the company introduced the 5.0&#160;L V8 in October 2010. The Pursuit ute is effectively replaced by the new GS ute. In 2012 FPV released a limited edition Pursuit Ute alongside the GT R-Spec, The limited edition Pursuit Ute is powered by the FPV modified all-aluminium 315 kW 5.0-litre Harrop-supercharged V8 engine producing 545 Nm of torque between 2000 and 5500 rpm. It also shares styling cues with the GT R-Spec boasting Vixen coloured stripe packages and wheels.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><br style="clear:both;" /></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="F6_Ute">F6 Ute</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: F6 Ute">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: F6 Ute" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>The F6 Ute is the only 6-cylinder vehicle in the FPV Ute range; it starts off the FPV range at $57,990 (AUD). It is powered by a 4.0&#160;L turbo-charged DOHC 24 valve in-line six-cylinder engine, which produces a maximum power of 310&#160;kW (421&#160;PS; 416&#160;bhp) at 5500 rpm and maximum torque of 565&#160;N·m (417&#160;lb·ft) across the range from 1950 to 5200 rpm. The fuel consumption has dropped to 12.1&#160;L/100&#160;km (23.3&#160;mpg<sub><small>-imp</small></sub>; 19.4&#160;mpg<sub><small>-US</small></sub>) for the <a href="/wiki/Automatic_transmission" title="Automatic transmission">automatic</a>. The engine is mated to a new 6-speed manual transmission; however, a ZF 6-speed automatic is a no cost option. It is equipped with Dynamic Stability Control (DSC), 6 airbags, Electronic Brakeforce Distribution (EBD), 4-channel anti-lock braking system (ABS), Beltminder technology, a six CD player (MP3 compatible), FPV starter button, alloy pedal covers, and 19-inch (480&#160;mm) alloy wheels.<br style="clear:both;" /></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Motorsport">Motorsport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Motorsport">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Motorsport" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="V8_Supercars">V8 Supercars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: V8 Supercars">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: V8 Supercars" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Ford_Performance_Racing" title="Ford Performance Racing">Ford Performance Racing</a></div>
<p>FPR was created in 2003 to establish a link between FPV's road car range and the <a href="/wiki/V8_Supercars" title="V8 Supercars">V8 Supercars</a>. Given the team's massive budget, its early results were disappointing but a form reversal in 2006 saw the team finish 2nd in the teams' standings. Mark Winterbottom has also got 2nd place in the drivers championship in 2008. Current drivers are <a href="/wiki/Mark_Winterbottom" title="Mark Winterbottom">Mark Winterbottom</a> and Will Davison in the two FPR entries. Former drivers include <a href="/wiki/Craig_Lowndes" title="Craig Lowndes">Craig Lowndes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Seton" title="Glenn Seton">Glenn Seton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greg_Ritter" title="Greg Ritter">Greg Ritter</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Brabham" title="David Brabham">David Brabham</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Besnard" title="David Besnard">David Besnard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Bright" title="Jason Bright">Jason Bright</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steven_Richards" title="Steven Richards">Steven Richards</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Drifting">Drifting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Drifting">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Performance_Vehicles&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Drifting" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>FPV created a <a href="/wiki/Show_car" title="Show car">show car</a> dubbed the 'DRIF6' - an <a href="/wiki/FPV_F6_Typhoon" title="FPV F6 Typhoon" class="mw-redirect">F6 Typhoon</a> with modifications making the car suitable for competitive <a href="/wiki/Drifting_(motorsport)" title="Drifting (motorsport)">drifting</a>. The car was entered into the national-level Drift Australia Series in 2006, where it was driven by Adam 'Newtonmeter' Newton. One of the car's main objectives was to expose the FPV brand to a younger audience than its usual, traditional V8-driving crowd.</p>
<p>Apart from Adam Newton, only Gary Myers of Summernats Burnout fame has driven the car. Gary drove the F6 for Street Machine Magazine, and commented the vehicle had incredible power.</p>
<p>The F6 drift car managed to score a best qualifying position of 5th at Mallala Motorsport Park in Adelaide, South Australia. In the Queensland Round of racing, the car suffered damage when Warren Luff, a taxi driver took to the wheel for a few demo laps. Under Warren's control the car only made the end of the back straight of Queensland Raceway before it was turned passenger side first into the wall and Warren ended up in hospital with a fractured nose.</p>
<p>The damaged vehicle made an appearance in China to bolster one of its sponsors agendas. Ford Australia brought the vehicle back to Australia and it can be seen at the Ford Australia Discovery Centre in Geelong Victoria.</p>
