    <p>
<a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/blazing-buicks-335-hp-regal-gs-regal-sport-wagon-being-discussed/" target="_self">According to Bob Lutz</a>, GM planned all along to offer a Buick Regal wagon alongside the sedan in the United States. But GM&rsquo;s bankruptcy saw funds for the program diverted to more pressing priorities, and such a hauler was put on the back burner. It appears plans may be back on track, judging by these spy shots. 
</p>

<p>
The photos show an Opel Insignia <a href="/reviews/2009-vauxhall-insignia-wagon-driven-offers-glimpse-of-2011-buick-regal-first-drive-review" target="_self">Sports Tourer</a>&mdash;the Regal sedan is a rebadged Insignia four-door&mdash;that&rsquo;s undergone extensive Buick-fication, including the installation of a chrome multi-slat grille and Buick emblems and wheels. Not all things Opel are gone, however, as the rear hatch still wears an &ldquo;Insignia&rdquo; badge. More interesting, however, is the &ldquo;Turbo X&rdquo; stuck to its rump; it&rsquo;s a piece of jewelry we last saw&mdash;in a different font&mdash;on a limited run of 2008 Saab 9-3s, and its presence likely indicates that this particular example sports all-wheel drive (perhaps of the torque-vectoring variety, as on the Saabs) and a 2.0-liter turbo four. The 220-hp 2.0-liter is the only turbocharged engine offered in the U.S. Regal sedan, and testing any of the Europe-only engines over here wouldn&rsquo;t make much sense. The Regal&rsquo;s other engine is a 182-hp, naturally aspirated 2.4-liter four. 
</p>

<p>
The Regal wagon would compete with the <a href="/news/2011-acura-tsx-sport-wagon-official-photos-and-info" target="_self">Acura TSX Sport Wagon</a> and Audi A4 Avant. Like those cars&mdash;as well as GM&rsquo;s own <a href="/reviews/2010-cadillac-cts-sport-wagon-36-short-take-road-test" target="_self">Cadillac CTS Sport Wagon</a>&mdash;expect an Americanized Regal wagon to get a consumer-friendly name in an attempt to coddle U.S. consumers, most of whom are ninnies and won&rsquo;t buy anything labeled solely as a wagon. Regal Sport Wagon is a possibility, as is carrying over the Sports Tourer designation. 
</p>

<p>
Of course, what we <i>really</i> want is a high-performance variant. It could use the 255-hp, 2.0-liter turbo four found in the <a href="/news/buick-regal-gs-concept-auto-shows" target="_self">Regal GS sedan concept</a> or the 325-hp V-6 from the Opel Insignia OPC Sports Tourer, possibly paired with all-wheel drive. It seems unlikely, but the existence of the Cadillac CTS-V wagon shows that somebody somewhere in GM appreciates go-fast wagons. Make it so, General. 
</p>
