<h2>Repair costs on minivans and your auto insurance </h2>


<p>In crash tests, the Odyssey was the best performer in low  impact rear-end crashes, and performed as well as others in low impact tests on  front-end collisions. The worst performer in low impact crashes for both rear  and front tests was the Nissan Quest. The Toyota Sienna was the cheapest to  repair on the front end, but was the most expensive to repair on the back end.  On the rear bumper, the Honda Odyssey was cheapest to repair in the full rear  bumper test, and the Dodge Grand Caravan was the cheapest to repair in a rear  corner impact. The cost of repair depends on the price of the parts and  difficulty to repair. Automakers are trying to improve the bumper systems in  vehicles so they resist damage, but until they do, consumers should pay  attention to crash tests results. What's the importance of how minivans perform  in these tests? Simple, insurance companies use these statistics and ratings  when it comes to calculating your auto insurance premiums. Minivans that perform  better in tests and cost less to repair are going to cost less to insure versus  a hard to repair minivan.</p>
