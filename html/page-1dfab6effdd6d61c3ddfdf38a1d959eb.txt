                        <div id="greeting"></div><h1>
                        	Automotive Technician - Motorcycle</h1>
                        <h2>
                        	What does an automotive technician - motorcycle apprentice do?</h2>
                        <p>
                        	<b>An apprentice automotive technician - motorcycle apprentice&nbsp;is involved in:</b></p>
                        <ul>
                        	<li>
                        		servicing, repairing and overhauling the mechanical parts of motorcycles such as the engine, the transmission (clutch and
                        gearbox) and the suspension systems (springs, steering, brakes, wheels and tyres)</li>
                        	<li>
                        		assembling, maintaining, repairing and restoring motorcycles and other multi-wheeled all-terrain vehicles weighing less
                        than 450 kilograms.</li>
                        </ul>
                        <p>
                        	<b>As an automotive technician - motorcycle apprentice&nbsp;you must be able to:</b></p>
                        <ul>
                        	<li>
                        		discuss complaints with customers or the service manager</li>
                        	<li>
                        		diagnose problems and locate failures with the electrical system, engine, power train, suspension or frame by inspecting
                        the vehicle, listening to it operate and using testing equipment</li>
                        	<li>
                        		dismantle, adjust and repair or replace mechanical and electrical system parts and components</li>
                        	<li>
                        		perform routine maintenance such as cleaning and adjusting the carburetor, adjusting the clutch, brakes and drive chain</li>
                        	<li>
                        		replace and or rebuild worn parts</li>
                        	<li>
                        		operate machine tools, boring bar mills and lathes which are increasingly being used in industry.</li>
                        </ul>
                        <h2>
                        	What attributes will I need?</h2>
                        <ul>
                        	<li>
                        		manual dexterity</li>
                        	<li>
                        		mechanical aptitude and interest</li>
                        	<li>
                        		willingness to continuously upgrade your knowledge and skills</li>
                        	<li>
                        		keenness to use tools and equipment to perform tasks requiring precision</li>
                        	<li>
                        		enthusiasm to solve problems by experimenting</li>
                        	<li>
                        		ability to follow routine procedures for scheduled maintenance.</li>
                        </ul>
                        <h2>
                        	What working conditions could I expect?</h2>
                        <p>
                        	Automotive technician - motorcycle apprentice&#39;s&nbsp;that work for motorcycle dealerships also assemble new machines.
                        You&#39;ll usually work indoors in shops.</p>
                        <h2>
                        	How do I become an automotive technician - motorcycle apprentice?&nbsp;</h2>
                        <h3>
                        	If you are still at school</h3>
                        <p>
                        	You may be able to start an apprenticeship through Training in Schools. You can begin in Years 11 and 12 at the same time
                        as completing your Western Australian Certificate of Education. Speak to your school&rsquo;s Vocational Education and Training
                        coordinator to find out more.</p>
                        <h3>
                        	If you have left school</h3>
                        <p>
                        	You can be employed as an automotive technician - motorcycle apprentice and get paid while you work and train.</p>
                        <h2>
                        	How long will an apprenticeship take?&nbsp;</h2>
                        <p>
                        	A full-time automotive technician - motorcycle apprenticeship takes three-and-a-half years, however under the competency
                        based system, apprentices may be able to qualify sooner if they can show they have the skills to do the job. An automotive
                        technician - motorcycle apprenticeship can also be done part-time.</p>
