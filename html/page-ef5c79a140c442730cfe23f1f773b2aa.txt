Market" rel="bookmark">Cruze Hatchback Not Slated For N.A. Market</a></h2> 
<div class="post-info"><span class="date published time" title="2011-10-10T00:05:16+00:00">October 10, 2011</span>  by <span class="author vcard"><span class="fn"><a href="http://www.autotrends.org/author/admin/" title="Matt Keegan" rel="author">Matt Keegan</a></span></span>  </div><div class="entry-content"><p><em>Cruze down under adds a new body style.</em></p>
<p><center><strong><div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 435px"><img title="Holden Cruze hatchback" src="http://www.autotrends.org/images/holden-cruze-hatchback-1.jpg" alt="" width="425" height="317" /><p class="wp-caption-text">The Cruze hatchback joins the sedan in select global markets.</p></div></strong></center></p>
<p>The Chevrolet Cruze is one of the most popular compacts on the market, a five passenger sedan that competes against the <a href="http://www.autotrends.org/2013/06/07/now-introducing-the-2014-toyota-corolla/"  title="Toyota Corolla">Toyota Corolla</a>, Ford Focus, Hyundai Elantra, Honda Civic and other C-segment models. While cars such as the Focus and Mazda3 are also available as hatchbacks, GM has yet to commit to this body style for the Cruze, at least for drivers in the United States and Canada.</p>
<h3>Cruze Hatchback</h3>
<p>A Cruze hatchback is more than just a dream. It is a reality in several markets including in Australia where the car is known as the Holden Cruze. Holden is a GM subsidiary down under, a brand whose cars have shown up in the U.S. as the Pontiac GTO, Pontiac G8 and the current Chevrolet Caprice PPV.</p>
<p>This past week, Holden introduced the Cruze hatchback, stoking rumors that this particular body style will eventually be built stateside despite GM&#8217;s initial plans not to do so. [1] One reason why we won&#8217;t see the hatchback immediately has to do with build capacity – the Chevrolet plant in Lordstown, Ohio, is at full capacity, with no room to take on additional production.</p>
<h3>Michigan Plant</h3>
<p>However, GM&#8217;s Orion Township plant in Michigan does have both available capacity and the assembly line to handle the work. That plant currently builds the subcompact Chevy Sonic and has just now begun to produce the premium Buick Verano sedan. Room is available for a Cruze hatchback, but only if GM is willing to introduce this body style.</p>
<p>GM says that the Cruze has 14.6 cubic feet (413 liters) of storage without the rear seat folded down. With the rear seat folded down, storage capacity is more than doubled to 31.2 cubic feet (883 liters).[2] Couple the expanded storage capacity with the Cruze&#8217;s roomy front seats and two people have a compact vacation getaway vehicle that offers high fuel mileage and plenty of room to bring all of their stuff.</p>
<p><strong><center><div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 435px"><img title="Holden Cruze" src="http://www.autotrends.org/images/holden-cruze-hatchback-2.jpg" alt="" width="425" height="300" /><p class="wp-caption-text">Storage capacity is more than doubled with the rear seat folded down.</p></div></center></strong></p>
<h3>Two Reasons</h3>
<p>Certainly, Chevrolet can continue to sell the Cruze in North America with just one body style. However, it seems likely we&#8217;ll eventually see the hatchback here and for two reasons:</p>
<p>1. The market continues to shift to where hatchback sales rise.<br />
2. Sales of the Chevy Sonic hatchback take up more than the projected 40 percent market share, perhaps outselling the sedan. In this writer&#8217;s opinion, the Sonic hatchback is more practical than the Sonic sedan and nicer looking too.</p>
<p>The American consumer&#8217;s distaste for all thinks hatchback seem to be fading away. Memories of ugly Chevy, Ford, Dodge and AMC hatchbacks from the 1970s and 1980s are disappearing and are cars that are largely unknown to most drivers under the age of 40.</p>

<div class="post-info"><span class="date published time" title="2009-06-04T17:24:49+00:00">June 4, 2009</span>  by <span class="author vcard"><span class="fn"><a href="http://www.autotrends.org/author/mattk/" title="MattK" rel="author">MattK</a></span></span>  </div><div class="entry-content"><p><center><img alt="Holden Cruze" src="http://www.autotrends.org/images/holden-cruze-hatchback-1.jpg" title="Holden Cruze " class="aligncenter" width="425" height="317" /></center></p>
<p>General Motors has laid out billions of dollars to build the Cruze sedan, monies which include new engines and transmissions as well latest parts, components, you name it. Built from the ground up, Opel designers had a big hand in creating the Cruze, but soon Opel will be leaving the GM fold, likely to become a subsidiary of Magna International.</p>
<h3><span style="color: #333399;">A Holden Cruze Hatchback</span></h3>
<p></p>
<p>There is still one matter to be handled regarding the Cruze and that is to add a hatchback version to the line. With Opel out of the way, GM will need to turn to someone else to get that job done, a task that the company&#8217;s Australian subsidiary, <strong><a title="Holden Cruze hatchback" href="http://thearticlewriter.com/autowriter/concerning-the-cruze-holden-takes-over-where-opel-left-off/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview','/yoast-ga/outbound-article/http://thearticlewriter.com/autowriter/concerning-the-cruze-holden-takes-over-where-opel-left-off/']);">Holden</a></strong>, has agreed to handle.</p>
<p>Holden will invest $600 million to design and build the Cruze hatchback, a car that it fully intends to sell in other markets. Hatchbacks are not popular in North America, but the car could easily be sold across Europe, South America, Asia and Australasia where consumers enjoy this sort of body design. Right now, the Cruze is being sold in South Korea, China, Australia and will soon go on sale in Europe with North American production slated to begin in April 2010.</p>
<p><center><img alt="Holden Cruze" src="http://www.autotrends.org/images/holden-cruze-hatchback-2.jpg" title="Holden Cruze " class="aligncenter" width="425" height="" /></center></p>
<h3><span style="color: #333399;">Filling Available Plant Capacity</span></h3>
<p>Likely, the Holden Cruze hatchback would be built at the company&#8217;s Elizabeth plant in South Australia, the same plant that has sent American versions of the Holden Commodore (Pontiac G8) overseas for the past two years.  With the pending demise of Pontiac and with unused capacity waiting to be tapped, the Elizabeth facility is a natural for Holden.</p>
<p>GM Holden sent out a news release on Monday assuring its customers that the company was not being affected by General Motors&#8217; U.S. bankruptcy filing which took place that same day. GM&#8217;s Australia operations are fairly independent and funded separately though wholly-owned by GM. GM plans to keep Holden as part of its portfolio unlike Opel, Saab, Saturn, Hummer and Pontiac which are either being sold off or shut down.</p>
<p>Sales of the Holden Cruze for the Australian and New Zealand markets will begin this month. Holden is sourcing its Cruze models from GM Daewoo of South Korea.</p>
