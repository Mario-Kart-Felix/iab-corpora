<p><b>CODA Automotive Inc.</b> is a privately held American company headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California" class="mw-redirect">Los Angeles, California</a>, that designs, semi manufactures, and sells electric vehicles, and <a href="/wiki/Lifepo4" title="Lifepo4" class="mw-redirect">lithium-iron phosphate (LiFePO4)</a> battery systems built for automotive and power storage utility applications.<sup id="cite_ref-PRN-2011-11-10_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PRN-2011-11-10-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Miles_Electric_Vehicles" title="Miles Electric Vehicles">Miles Automotive</a> partnered with <a href="/wiki/Hafei" title="Hafei">Hafei</a> and Qingyuan Electric Vehicle to establish Coda Automotive as an affiliate company.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The word CODA is a <a href="/wiki/Coda_(music)" title="Coda (music)">musical term</a> that denotes the concluding passage of a piece of music, from the Italian word <i>coda</i> (tail). CODA Automotive has said that it chose the name because its electric vehicle technology represents an end for combustion engine vehicles, and the start of the electric vehicle era.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2009, CODA announced the creation and funding of LIO Energy Systems,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> a global joint-venture with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lishen_Power_Battery&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lishen Power Battery (page does not exist)">Lishen Power Battery</a>. LIO Energy Systems was formed with the purpose of designing, manufacturing, and selling battery systems for electric vehicles and utility applications. LIO Energy Systems will supply battery systems to CODA Automotive for use in the all-electric CODA, to other automotive OEMs globally, and to renewable energy producers, utilities and other power storage customers. LIO Energy Systems was later renamed CODA Energy.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>CODA’s sole vehicle offering is the <a href="/wiki/Coda_(electric_car)" title="Coda (electric car)">Coda all-electric sedan</a>, a four-door, five passenger <a href="/wiki/Electric_car" title="Electric car">electric car</a> powered by a battery pack that delivered a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">United States Environmental Protection Agency</a> <a href="/wiki/All-electric_range" title="All-electric range">rated range</a> of 88&#160;mi (142&#160;km), the longest among its class, although considerably less than the <a href="/wiki/Tesla_Roadster" title="Tesla Roadster">Tesla Roadster</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tesla_Model_S" title="Tesla Model S">Tesla Model S</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-EPArating_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EPArating-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The electric car was released in March 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-Launch0212_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Launch0212-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> and initially is available only in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Launch2011_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Launch2011-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Autoweek_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autoweek-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> After low inititial sales of the Coda Car, Coda Automotive terminated 15% of its workforce,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> and entered a period of financial difficulty.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> As of May 2013, Coda was seeking bankruptcy protection.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTimes_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_strategy_and_partners">Corporate strategy and partners</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coda_Automotive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Corporate strategy and partners">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coda_Automotive&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Corporate strategy and partners" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>CODA Automotive’s goal is to accelerate the adoption of <a href="/wiki/Electric_vehicle" title="Electric vehicle">electric vehicles</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy_technology" title="Renewable energy technology" class="mw-redirect">renewable energy technology</a> globally. To this end, CODA has focused on improving performance, safety, reducing cost, and commercializing production of battery systems built for automotive applications, which it views as the “chief enabling technology” for all-electric cars. To reduce the cost of building its vehicles, CODA controls all core design, and engineering work internally while partnering with established automotive manufacturers, and suppliers around the world. CODA’s supply chain partners include <a href="/wiki/BorgWarner" title="BorgWarner">BorgWarner</a>, UQM Technologies, <a href="/wiki/EnergyCS" title="EnergyCS">EnergyCS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Continental_Automotive_Systems" title="Continental Automotive Systems">Continental Automotive Systems</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Porche_Design_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Porche Design Group (page does not exist)">Porsche Design Studios</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delphi_Corporation" title="Delphi Corporation" class="mw-redirect">Delphi</a>, Celgard, Novolyte Technologies, OMITEC, <a href="/wiki/Lear_Corporation" title="Lear Corporation">Lear</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hella" title="Hella">Hella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hafei" title="Hafei">Hafei</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lishen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lishen (page does not exist)">Lishen</a>. In total, CODA has more than thirty suppliers, and partners on four continents.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> In March 2012, Chinese car maker <a href="/wiki/Great_Wall_Motors" title="Great Wall Motors">Great Wall Motors</a> formed a joint venture with Coda.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Models_and_products">Models and products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coda_Automotive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Models and products">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coda_Automotive&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Models and products" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>CODA made its consumer auto show debut at the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Auto_Show" title="Los Angeles Auto Show" class="mw-redirect">Los Angeles Auto Show</a>, November 19–28, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> As part of an ongoing commitment to educate consumers about sustainability, and green energy technology, CODA will host a series of educational seminars throughout the show, ultimately reaching more than 600 students. Like its business model, and car, CODA's booth was a break from tradition, and was designed to showcase the company's brand identity. "Zen meets Tech" is the overarching aesthetic theme. The booth was constructed largely out of eco-friendly materials. The walls are constructed from salvaged Picklewood that was collected from various sites in North America. The floor is made from recycled rubber, the graphics are printed on recycled papers with eco-friendly inks, and live plants have been incorporated. A central element of the booth is the "Commitment Wall", which visitors will populate with their thoughts on sustainability, the environment, and green technology. A large media wall showcased a variety of videos about CODA, its employees, and the benefits of electric vehicles.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coda_all-electric_car">Coda all-electric car</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coda_Automotive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Coda all-electric car">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coda_Automotive&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Coda all-electric car" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Coda_(electric_car)" title="Coda (electric car)">Coda (electric car)</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:CODA_sedan_WAS_2012_0838.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/CODA_sedan_WAS_2012_0838.JPG/220px-CODA_sedan_WAS_2012_0838.JPG" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/CODA_sedan_WAS_2012_0838.JPG/330px-CODA_sedan_WAS_2012_0838.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/CODA_sedan_WAS_2012_0838.JPG/440px-CODA_sedan_WAS_2012_0838.JPG 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:CODA_sedan_WAS_2012_0838.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf12/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>

<p>CODA’s first car is an <a href="/wiki/All-electric_vehicle" title="All-electric vehicle" class="mw-redirect">all-electric</a> four-door, five passenger <a href="/wiki/Battery_electric_vehicle" title="Battery electric vehicle">battery electric vehicle</a> (BEV), the CODA sedan. The car is powered by a 31 <a href="/wiki/KWh" title="KWh" class="mw-redirect">kWh</a> <a href="/wiki/Lifepo4" title="Lifepo4" class="mw-redirect">lithium ion iron phosphate (LiFePO4)</a> battery system that is substantially larger than that of other vehicles in its class.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> According to the <a href="/wiki/US_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="US Environmental Protection Agency" class="mw-redirect">US Environmental Protection Agency</a> (EPA), the Coda's 31 <a href="/wiki/KWh" title="KWh" class="mw-redirect">kWh</a> <a href="/wiki/Lifepo4" title="Lifepo4" class="mw-redirect">lithium ion iron phosphate (LiFePO4)</a> battery system delivers a <a href="/wiki/All-electric_range" title="All-electric range">range</a> of 88&#160;mi (142&#160;km), the longest among its class. EPA's rated the Coda's combined <a href="/wiki/Fuel_economy_in_automobiles" title="Fuel economy in automobiles">fuel economy</a> at 73 <a href="/wiki/Miles_per_gallon_gasoline_equivalent" title="Miles per gallon gasoline equivalent">miles per gallon gasoline equivalent</a>(3.2&#160;L/100&#160;km).<sup id="cite_ref-EPArating_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EPArating-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Range_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Range-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> The car is backed by a 3-year, 36,000 mile limited warranty<sup id="cite_ref-PRN001_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PRN001-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> and the battery system is backed by an 10-year, 100,000 mile limited warranty.<sup id="cite_ref-CodaLA2012_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CodaLA2012-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>CODA announced that net price will be <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$37,250</span> before any <a href="/wiki/Government_incentives_for_plug-in_electric_vehicles#United_States" title="Government incentives for plug-in electric vehicles">electric vehicle federal tax credit</a>, and other <a href="/wiki/Government_incentives_for_plug-in_electric_vehicles#Other_states" title="Government incentives for plug-in electric vehicles">state and local incentives</a> that are available in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-CodaLA2012_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CodaLA2012-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> Initial deliveries of the vehicle were planned for December 2010, but were pushed back to the second half of 2011 as CODA decided to have more time available to ensure the quality of the car,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> and later were again re-scheduled for late 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-Launch2011_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Launch2011-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> The latest market launch was slated for February 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> but the first deliveries to retail customers in the United States took place in March 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Launch0212_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Launch0212-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Initially the electric car is available only in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Launch2011_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Launch2011-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><br style="clear:both;" /></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Battery_production_and_distribution_joint-venture">Battery production and distribution joint-venture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coda_Automotive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Battery production and distribution joint-venture">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coda_Automotive&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Battery production and distribution joint-venture" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>CODA has a global joint-venture with Lishen Power Battery, a global battery cell supplier to <a href="/wiki/Samsung" title="Samsung">Samsung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Motorola_Mobility" title="Motorola Mobility">Motorola</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Apple,_Inc." title="Apple, Inc." class="mw-redirect">Apple</a>, for the design, manufacture, and sale of battery systems called LIO Energy Systems. The name was selected because “LIO” is the reverse spelling of “oil.” Together, CODA and Lishen developed a lithium iron phosphate battery cell for transportation, and utility applications, which includes, renewable energy (wind and solar power) storage.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> LIO Energy Systems currently operates a manufacturing facility in <a href="/wiki/Tianjin" title="Tianjin">Tianjin</a> and plans to build a U.S. facility in <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">Columbus, Ohio</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> Initially, the CODA will be the primary recipient of the battery systems produced by LIO. LIO's production capacity will total 1.4 billion amp hours in Tianjin, China at full scale. With the completion of the Ohio plant, total capacity will reach two billion Ah (6.3 million kWh) of energy storage.<sup id="cite_ref-codaautomotive.com_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-codaautomotive.com-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2010, <a href="/wiki/U.S._Secretary_of_Commerce" title="U.S. Secretary of Commerce" class="mw-redirect">U.S. Secretary of Commerce</a> <a href="/wiki/Gary_Locke" title="Gary Locke">Gary Locke</a> visited the LIO Energy Systems facility in <a href="/wiki/Tianjin" title="Tianjin">Tianjin</a> as part of the Obama administration's first cabinet-level trade mission to China. Commenting on the visit, Locke said, "International green technology partnerships can produce rapid job growth back home and deliver energy solutions abroad, and CODA's venture proves it."<sup id="cite_ref-codaautomotive.com_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-codaautomotive.com-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bankruptcy_and_legal_issues">Bankruptcy and legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coda_Automotive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Bankruptcy and legal issues">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coda_Automotive&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Bankruptcy and legal issues" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After selling approximately 100 Coda Cars in California, Coda filed for <a href="/wiki/Chapter_11,_Title_11,_United_States_Code" title="Chapter 11, Title 11, United States Code">Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection</a> on May 1, 2013. Coda Holdings stated that it expects to emerge out of the bankruptcy process to focus solely on its subsidiary <a href="/wiki/Coda_Energy" title="Coda Energy" class="mw-redirect">Coda Energy</a>, and thus abandon car manufacturing.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Car_Connection_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Car_Connection-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NYTimes_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Shortly after the bankruptcy announcement, former employee Tony Bulchak filed a class-action lawsuit against the automaker, alleging that Coda laid off 125 workers in December 14, 2012, without giving them a 60-day notice.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
