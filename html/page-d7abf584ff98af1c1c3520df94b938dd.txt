				<h3 id='section--WhatIsAnAutoAndDieselTechnologyDiploma'>What is an Auto and Diesel Technology Diploma?</h3>
<p>An auto and diesel technology diploma program will imbue you with the skills and experience you need to be a successful auto mechanic. Hands-on experience is essential as you learn about different auto technologies like hydraulics, electrical systems, welding, air intake, brake repair, and engine building. A typical diploma program for auto and diesel technology will include the following courses:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Engine Fundamentals
</li>
<li>Brakes
</li>
<li>Electrical Systems
</li>
<li>Heating and Air Conditioning
</li>
<li>Engine Rebuild
</li>
<li>Diesel Electrical Systems
</li>
<li>Computer Literacy
</li>
<li>Alignment/Steering/Suspension
</li>
</ul>
<h3 id='section--WhyGetADiplomaInAutoAndDieselTechnology'>Why Get a Diploma in Auto and Diesel Technology?</h3>
<p>A diploma program in auto and diesel technology will prepare you for a career as an automotive mechanic. This job necessitates extensive training and experienced due to the highly technical nature of the position. A degree program will ensure that you have the necessary skills and show employers that you are qualified for the position.
</p>
<h4 id='section--WhatIsTheJobOutlookForThoseWithADiplomaInAutoAndDieselTechnology'>What is the Job Outlook for Those With a Diploma in Auto and Diesel Technology?</h4>
<p>According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (<i>www.bls.gov</i>), the demand for automotive mechanics will increase about as fast as the national average until at least the year 2014. The outlook is especially good for those who have certified training in the field, as employees are expressing difficulty finding such people to fill positions. Basic electronics and computer courses also increase the desirability of an applicant to an employer.
</p>
<p>The hourly wage for an automotive service technician in 2004 was about $15.50</p>
