											<p id='intelliTxt' class="intro" data-module="article-intro">The Texas Statutes, which enumerate the official laws written by the Texas State Legislature, distinguish between motor vehicles operated on public property and private property. Generally, all motor vehicles are required to have headlights and tail lights. These are required to be on in poor visibility and from 30 minutes before sunset until 30 minutes after sunrise when driving on public roads or property.

																<div itemprop="step">
									<p>Sections 547.321 and 547.322 of the Texas Statutes require all motor vehicles to be equipped with at least two headlights and two tail lights. Texas law defines a motor vehicle as "a vehicle that is self-propelled"; however, Section 663.033 only requires all-terrain vehicles--defined as a motor vehicle designed for off-road use with three or four wheels--to be equipped with at least one headlight and one tail light.</p>								</div>
