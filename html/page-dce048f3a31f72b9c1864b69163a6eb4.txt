				<h1>Green Automotive Company’s Goingreen Announces Exclusive UK Distribution Of I’Moving Electric Commerical Vehicles</h1>
				<p></p>
<p style="font-weight:bold;">18th June 2013</p>
<p><a title="Visit the GACR Website" href="http://www.thegreenautomotivecompany.com"  target="_blank">Green Automotive Company</a>’s subsidiary, <a title="Visit the Goingreen Website" href="http://www.goingreen.co.uk" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.goingreen.co.uk']);" target="_blank">GoinGreen</a>, has signed an agreement with I’MOVING to exclusively distribute their range of <a title="Visit the GACR Website" href="http://www.goingreen.co.uk/commercial-evs/jolly-2000/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.goingreen.co.uk']);" target="_blank">pure electric commercial vehicles</a> in the UK. </p>
<p>They have also agreed to explore opportunities to collaborate in the introduction of these pure electric trucks and vans to the United States where vehicles have been submitted recently for homologation certification. Italy-based I’MOVING designs and manufactures environmentally friendly vehicles for both the public and private sector.</p>
<p style="margin:0px 0px; spacing:0;padding:0;">

<a href="http://www.thegreenautomotivecompany.com/wp-content/gallery/goingreen-imoving-agreement/imagefaamjolly2000brusselsairport.jpg" title="A FAAM Jolly 2000 at Brussels Airport" class="thickbox" rel="singlepic30" >
	<img class="ngg-singlepic" src="http://www.thegreenautomotivecompany.com/wp-content/gallery/cache/30__450x280_imagefaamjolly2000brusselsairport.jpg" alt="A FAAM Jolly 2000 at Brussels Airport" title="A FAAM Jolly 2000 at Brussels Airport" />
</a>
</p>
<p style="font-size:0.8em;margin:0px;spacing:0;padding:0;text-align:center;color:#45545F;font-weight:1200;">A FAAM Jolly 2000 at Brussels Airport, Belguim. (Click for larger image)</p>
</p>
<p>In its latest research, <a href="http://www.frost.com" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.frost.com']);" target="_blank" Title="Visit Frost and Sullivan's Website"> Frost &amp; Sullivan</a> has concluded that &#8211;
<p style="font-size:1.1em;font-weight:1200;color:#45545F;"><strong>&#8220;electric vehicles should rapidly grow to represent 4% to 10% of the total lightweight commercial vehicle market during the next two years. This space provides many opportunities for EV products due to the drivers often having fixed routes, predefined cargo weights, and relatively low mileage throughout the day&#8221;</strong>. </p>
<p>In addition, the vehicles regularly return “Back to Base” each night, offering a convenient charging location and predictable schedule.</p>
<p style="margin:0px 0px; spacing:0;padding:0;">

<a href="http://www.thegreenautomotivecompany.com/wp-content/gallery/goingreen-imoving-agreement/imagefaamecomilerome.jpg" title="A FAAM ECOMILE in Rome, Italy." class="thickbox" rel="singlepic29" >
	<img class="ngg-singlepic" src="http://www.thegreenautomotivecompany.com/wp-content/gallery/cache/29__450x280_imagefaamecomilerome.jpg" alt="A FAAM ECOMILE in Rome, Italy." title="A FAAM ECOMILE in Rome, Italy." />
</a>
</p>
<p style="font-size:0.8em;margin:0px;spacing:0;padding:0;text-align:center;color:#45545F;font-weight:1200;">A FAAM ECOMILE in Rome, Italy. (Click for larger image)</p>
</p>
<p>The I’MOVING pure electric range to be launched initially in the UK includes <a title="Visit the GACR Website" href="http://www.goingreen.co.uk/commercial-evs/jolly-2000/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.goingreen.co.uk']);" target="_blank">The Jolly 2000</a>, an N1, 3.5t GVW vehicle as well as its smaller version, <a title="Visit the GACR Website" href="http://www.goingreen.co.uk/commercial-evs/ecomile/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.goingreen.co.uk']);" target="_blank">The Ecomile</a>, that both qualify for the UK Government OLEV grant. </p>
<p>The vehicles are available with a huge range of body options from pick up to box vans. With a maximum 1.9t payload, the Jolly 2000 provides the ideal solution for the transportation of commercial goods. </p>
<p>Both vehicles use the latest lithium ion batteries and are particularly suited to Back to Base roles such as mail and parcel delivery, inner city requirements and local authority needs. </p>
<p>They have enjoyed growing sales success in cities throughout Europe and the world, including Rome, Milan, Frankfurt, Brussels, and Beijing.</p>
<hr style="margin-top:20px;margin-bottom: 20px;"/>
<h1 style="color: #8ba628;">Green Automotive director Ian Hobday on the company’s acquisition of Liberty Electric Cars and Newport Coachworks</h1>
<p><iframe src="http://player.vimeo.com/video/54625171?title=0&amp;byline=0&amp;portrait=0&amp;badge=0&amp;color=8ba628" frameborder="0" width="456" height="256"></iframe></p>
<p>Green Automotive Company in the USA recently acquired Liberty Electric Cars in the UK. Director Ian Hobday talks in this movie about the rationale behind the business move and why the future looks very bright for Electric Vehicles in general, and GAC, Newport Coachworks and Liberty Electric Cars in particular.</p>
<p>From business strategy to IP and Electric Vehicle technology advantages Hobday provides a useful insight into the rapidly changing world of personal and business transport, from the Company&#8217;s fully Electric Zero Emission Range Rovers capable of 100mph, 60mph in 7 seconds and an incredible 200 mile range on a single charge, through to Electric Buses that are being designed for production and zero emission roll out from GAC&#8217;s recently acquired Newport Coachworks Company.</p>
