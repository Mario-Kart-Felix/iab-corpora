							<a href="http://insideevs.com/category/chevrolet/">Chevrolet</a> March 2013 Plug-In Electric Vehicle Sales Report Card				</div><!--end #breadcrumbs-->    		<h1 class="entry-title">March 2013 Plug-In Electric Vehicle Sales Report Card</h1>
		
		<div class="entry-meta">
			<abbr title="April 2, 2013 at 10:45 am">147 days ago</abbr> by <a href="http://insideevs.com/author/statik/" title="Posts by Jay Cole" rel="author">Jay Cole</a> <span class="entry-comment"><a href="http://insideevs.com/march-2013-plug-in-electric-vehicle-sales-report-card/#comments" title="Comment on March 2013 Plug-In Electric Vehicle Sales Report Card">29</a></span>
		</div> <!-- end .entry-meta -->
		
	    <div class="clear"></div>
	    
	   	<div class="entry">
				   	
	    	<div id="attachment_5961" class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/dealership1.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-5961" alt="February Plug-In Sales Show Big Jump From 12 Months Ago" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/dealership1-300x181.jpg" width="300" height="181" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">March 2013 Marks The Highest All-Time Level Of Plug-In Sales For The United States</p></div>
<p>March of 2013 has set a new benchmark for sales of plug-in vehicles, as huge monthly gains at some automakers (and steady results at others) have pushed the overall number of plug-in sales to an all-time high in the US for March.</p>
<p>Most notably, large sales (<em>and production increases</em>) at Nissan with the LEAF, and at Tesla with the Model S, combined for over<strong> 4,000</strong> fully electric cars sold during the month, spurring the record result.</p>
<p><strong>NOTE: This scorecard is published as soon as the first auto maker reports, and is populated/updated throughout the day as soon as more data is available.</strong></p>
<p><em>(last update: Wednesday, April 3rd, 2013 @3:03pm)</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div id="attachment_1839" class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/may-plug-in-sales-volt-1680/voltmay12/" rel="attachment wp-att-1839"><img title="voltmay12" alt="" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/voltmay12-300x182.jpg" width="300" height="182" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">2013 Chevrolet Volt</p></div>
<p><strong>Chevrolet Volt: </strong>GM has a bit off an off month as only<strong> 1,478</strong> Volts were sold.  (<a href="http://insideevs.com/chevrolet-volt-sales-continue-to-grow-in-march-as-cadillac-elr-re-tooling-is-underway/" target="_blank"><em>full story here</em></a>)</p>
<p>Despite increasing production at the Hamtramck facility over the past two months to fill in dealer inventory, plug-in sales were a touch weak at the US automaker.  To remedy this, GM nationally discounted the car by <strong>$3,000</strong> to its dealers to round out the month.</p>
<p>In February,  Chevrolet sold <strong>1,626</strong> units, which was up <strong>60% </strong>up from a year ago&#8217;s result.  This month however GM found itself up against an incentive laden (and consumer hungry) March of 2012, when the company sold <strong>2,289</strong> units.</p>
<p>This is the first year-over-year miss for the Volt since it went on sale in December of 2010.  Sales were off 35%.  For the month, GM did continue to produce Volts at a break-neck speed, building 2,722 in Michigan.</p>
<p>Recently, Chevrolet has said that the 2014 edition of the Chevrolet Volt <a href="http://insideevs.com/2014-chevrolet-volt-orders-start-late-may-two-new-colors-announced/" target="_blank">will go on sale in &#8220;late May&#8221;</a> and has released the first couple new options on the car&#8230;new paint color choices.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div id="attachment_1840" class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/may-plug-in-sales-volt-1680/leafmay/" rel="attachment wp-att-1840"><img title="leafmay" alt="" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/leafmay-300x210.jpg" width="300" height="210" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">2012 Nissan LEAF</p></div>
<p><strong>Nissan LEAF: </strong>Nissan set the all-time monthly sales record for any pure electric vehicle sold in the US for March, with<strong> 2,236</strong> LEAFs sold.</p>
<p>As <a href="http://insideevs.com/new-2013-nissan-leaf-a-big-hit-over-1900-sold-in-march/" target="_blank">forcasted last week by Nissan execs at the NYC Auto Show</a>, March&#8217;s impressive result of  <em>&#8220;over 1,900&#8243;</em> LEAFs sold, announces Nissan&#8217;s return to competing for the crown of <em>top selling plug-in auto maker in the US*</em></p>
<p>Previously, in February Nissan sold only 653 LEAFs as the company almost completely ran out of 2012 inventory to sell while waiting on US production of 2013 LEAFs to begin.</p>
<p>We expect April&#8217;s result to be just as impressive, as Nissan enters the month with over 2,300 cars at the dealership level, with more on the way.  Nissan has said they expect inventory to be optimized by the end of next month.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div id="attachment_8722" class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/?attachment_id=8722"><img title="2013 tesla model s" alt="" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/2013-tesla-model-s-300x181.jpg" width="300" height="181" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">2013 Tesla Model S</p></div>
<p><strong>Tesla Model S: </strong>Tesla does not give out exact monthly sales&#8230;but they do give a lot of updates, and consumers fanatically track their progress, so we can make a <em>pretty accurate</em> guess.</p>
<p>After estimating Tesla sold about 1,200 Model S sedans in January (<em>thanks to a pull forward of sales in 2012</em>), and 1,400 in February (thanks to a week&#8217;s worth of down time in January, Tesla ramped <a href="http://insideevs.com/tesla-model-s-production-now-more-than-500-per-week-over-12-million-total-miles-driven-so-far/" target="_blank">weekly production to over 500 vehicles per week during the month</a>, and likely sold around<strong> 2,150</strong> cars in March of 2013.  We estimate around 4,700 sold in the quarter, besting earlier predictions by the company of 4,500.</p>
<p><strong>UPDATE:</strong>  At least 4 times a year we get quarterly updates on sales, and Tesla confirms not only was the company <a href="http://insideevs.com/tesla-model-s-quarter-1-sales-beat-target-by-exceeding-4750-units-automaker-reports-full-profitability/" target="_blank">profitable in Q1 of 2013</a>, but they sold more than<strong> 4,900 </strong>cars between January and March.  Just to make the numbers add up, we will bump our March sales estimate up by 150 units to<strong> 2,300</strong> Model S sedans sold in March.  <em>(hey, we were pretty close)</em></p>
<p>Tesla has also announced that the <a href="http://insideevs.com/entry-level-40kwh-tesla-model-s-cancelled-60-kwh-cars-all-get-supercharging-hardware/" target="_blank">40 kWh Model S would not be going into production</a>, and all 60 kWh cars would be built with the Supercharging hardware on board.  A base Tesla Model S (60 kWh) will now set you back $69,900, as opposed to the $57,400 in the last quarter of 2014.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div id="attachment_8702" class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/?attachment_id=8702"><img title="fisker karma aug" alt="" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/fisker-karma-aug-300x217.jpg" width="300" height="217" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Fisker Karma</p></div>
<p><strong>Fisker Karma: </strong>Like Tesla, Fisker does not report monthly sales.</p>
<p>But unlike Tesla, they really don&#8217;t make or sell any cars anymore.</p>
<p>It makes the March 2013 sales list as more of a novelty than anything else at this point, and because Fisker does still physically have a few dealership left selling cars.</p>
<p>That being said, the company has not built a single Karma  <a href="http://insideevs.com/fisker-to-negotiate-new-contract-with-a123s-wanxiang-on-batteries-for-karma-hasnt-built-a-car-since-july/" target="_blank">since July</a>, they have <a href="http://insideevs.com/henrik-fisker-turns-in-his-company-car-buys-new-karma-after-quitting-plus-opens-up-a-little/" target="_blank">no CEO</a>, a takeover bid by Geely Automotive <a href="http://insideevs.com/chinese-company-geely-backs-out-of-fisker-automotive-deal-doe-strings-get-in-the-way/" target="_blank">failed</a>, and Fisker has put <a href="http://insideevs.com/fisker-puts-their-employees-on-furlough-doe-loan-payment-incoming-in-april/" target="_blank">all its US workers on furlough</a>.</p>
<p>As for sales specifically, Fisker started the month with <strong>107</strong> 2012 Karmas inside the US still left to be sold, and they ended the month with <strong>88</strong>&#8230;using our patented <em>&#8220;Inside EVs Decoder Ring&#8221;</em> we will put Karma sales at <strong>19</strong> for March.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<dl id="attachment_17069">
<dt></dt>
</dl>
<div id="attachment_17069" class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/2013-fusion-energi.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-17069" alt="2013 Ford Fusion Energi" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/2013-fusion-energi-300x186.jpg" width="300" height="186" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">2013 Ford Fusion Energi</p></div>
<p><strong>Ford Fusion Energi:</strong></p>
<p>Ford now reports that sales of its Fusion Energi in March checked in at at decent <em>(especially considering the lack of inventory across the country</em>) at <strong>295</strong> units.</p>
<p>The Fusion plug in went on sale at the very end of last month, but still managed to sell <strong>119</strong> copies.  How short a window?  Well, when we talked to a representative in connection with Ford, just days before month&#8217;s end, he said that the company would not get any out during the month.</p>
<p>The Fusion Energi basically offers the same package as the C-Max Energi, but in a larger sedan package. The third plug-in to be offered from Ford has just been rated by the EPA at a combined 100 MPGe (92 MPGe highway, 108 MPGs city), and has <a href="http://insideevs.com/ford-fusion-energi-quietly-rated-at-21-miles-range-and-43-mpg/" target="_blank">an all-electric range of 21 miles</a>.</p>
<p>Unlike the C-Max Energi, the Fusion Energi has been priced in-line (and above) the other 5 Fusion trim levels in the model lineup, so as not to<em> &#8216;steal&#8217;</em> sales from itself. This has resulted in a starting MSRP of <a href="http://insideevs.com/ford-fusion-energi-priced-from-39495/" target="_blank">$39,495</a>, which we feel is a bit steep.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div id="attachment_8715" class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/?attachment_id=8715"><img title="toyota pip" alt="" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/toyota-pip-300x195.jpg" width="300" height="195" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">2013 Toyota Prius Plug-In</p></div>
<p><strong>Toyota Prius Plug-In:  </strong>Sales of the Toyota Prius Plug-In Hybrid remained relatively stable, but still a bit on the low side.  Toyota is reporting that it sold <strong>786</strong> units in <strong>March 2013</strong>.</p>
<p>After selling only <strong>693</strong> plug-ins Prius in February, and <strong>874</strong> in January, with no significant issues or inventory shortages, the other plug-in competition looks to be eroding the electric Toyota&#8217;s base.</p>
<p>For 2012, the Prius plug-in finished as the second best seller in the segment (behind the Chevrolet Volt), with<strong> 12,750</strong> units sold, but we now question whether Toyota can put up enough of a fight to hold that position against the likes of the new 2013 LEAF, the Tesla Model S, and a similar (but longer ranged) Ford C-Max Energi.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div id="attachment_13011" class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/october-2012-plug-in-electric-vehicle-sales-report-card/ford-c-max-energi-black/" rel="attachment wp-att-13011"><img title="Ford C-Max Energi Black" alt="" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/Ford-C-Max-Energi-Black-350x205.jpg" width="300" height="175" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">2013 Ford C-Max Energi</p></div>
<p><strong>Ford C-Max Energi:<br />
</strong></p>
<p>Sales of the C-Max Energi remain relatively stable, but improved slightly in March with Ford selling <strong>494</strong> units.</p>
<p>After a strong start in 2012, Ford&#8217;s much heralded Michigan assembly facility (MAP) just could not get the ball rolling in January and February, leaving dealers very short on C-Max plug-ins to sell.</p>
<p>In February <strong>334</strong> Energis were sold, after a dismal January result of <strong>338</strong>.  Ford entered 2013 with only 175 available units to sell, and ended February at around 600.   March&#8217;s result should be significantly improved, as Ford now has approximately 1,000 C-Max Energis in stock.   <em>Still not great, but getting there. </em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div id="attachment_11555" class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 274px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/september-2012-plug-in-electric-vehicle-sales-report-card/blaack-fit/" rel="attachment wp-att-11555"><img title="black fit" alt="" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/blaack-fit.jpg" width="264" height="174" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">2013 Honda Fit EV</p></div>
<p><strong>Honda Fit EV: </strong>In March, Honda sold 23 Fit EVs, and while that might not sound like much (<em>ok, it isn&#8217;t really</em>), it represents Honda&#8217;s 2nd best selling month of the EV, and equals the total amount of electric Fits the company sold in the first two months of the year.</p>
<p>In February, the Honda Fit EV took off,  improving on January&#8217;s result by near <strong>90% &#8230;from 8 units sold to <strong>15</strong>.  </strong><em>(Editor&#8217;s Note: w</em><em>e are working really hard to spin Honda&#8217;s results in a positive light this month)</em></p>
<p>Although the Fit EV is purely a compliance play for Honda to satisfy CARB, they have still built (and need to sell) 1,100 of the 82 mile EV. At the current selling rate of 14 per month, it is going to take Honda<strong> 6.5 years</strong> in total to move all their cars. We think it might be a <em>touch hard</em> selling 2013 model year cars in 2020.</p>
<p>We feel a price reduction (<a href="http://insideevs.com/heres-why-the-honda-fit-ev-is-not-nearly-as-expensive-as-you-might-think/" target="_blank">on the $389/lease offer</a>) is also in order.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div id="attachment_1862" class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/?attachment_id=1862"><img title="ffe" alt="" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/ffe-300x133.jpg" width="300" height="133" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">2013 Ford Focus Electric</p></div>
<p><strong>Ford Focus Electric:</strong><strong> </strong></p>
<p>In March, Ford enjoyed another decent performance out of its all electric car, as<strong> 180</strong> Focus EVs were sold..</p>
<p>Last month (February 2013) , Ford sold <strong>158</strong> Focus Electrics in <strong>February</strong>, or nearly double the amount the automaker sold in January.  However, Ford is not expecting 2013 sales of the model to <a href="http://insideevs.com/ford-doesnt-expect-focus-electric-sales-to-increase-by-much-in-2013/" target="_blank">improve by very much overall</a>, despite a tripling of the dealership base that is able to sell the plug-in car.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div id="attachment_11742" class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/september-2012-plug-in-electric-vehicle-sales-report-card/toyota-rav4-ev-in-shoreline-blue-pearl/" rel="attachment wp-att-11742"><img title="Toyota RAV4 EV in Shoreline Blue Pearl" alt="" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/Toyota-RAV4-EV-in-Shoreline-Blue-Pearl-300x213.jpg" width="300" height="213" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Continuing to Ruin our &#8220;All Black&#8221; Motif Of EVs, the All-new 2013 Toyota RAV4 EV</p></div>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Toyota RAV4 EV: </strong>March was the best result to date for Toyota&#8217;s electric SUV, with <strong>133</strong> plug-ins sold.</p>
<p>In February, only <strong>54</strong> plug-in SUVs were sold.</p>
<p>Toyota introduced a deep discount incentive of <a href="http://insideevs.com/toyota-discounts-the-rav4-ev-by-10000-with-0-financing-lease-from-499-zero-down/" target="_blank">$10,000 off RAV4 EV pricing</a> in February, with 0% financing. an offer that seemed to spur on new interest in the vehicle.</p>
<p>That discount, as well as federal and state incentives means that consumers in California were getting a total of $20,000 of the Toyota&#8217;s MSRP price of $49,800.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div id="attachment_1897" class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/may-plug-in-sales-volt-1680/mitsu-i/" rel="attachment wp-att-1897"><img class="size-medium wp-image-1897" title="mitsu i" alt="" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/mitsu-i-300x178.jpg" width="300" height="178" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">2012 Mitsubishi i</p></div>
<p><strong>Mitsubishi i-MiEV:   /thud</strong>&#8230;was the<strong> </strong>sound on Mitsubishi&#8217;s 2-month sales revival bottoming out in March, as the Japanese company ran low on old 2012 inventory to sell, and moved only <strong>31</strong> i-MiEVs during the month</p>
<p>Despite some massive sales improvements for the first months of 2013, make no mistake, that was a sell-off, not a resurgence.</p>
<p>For February, Mitsu sold more than 10 times March&#8217;s number, with <strong>337</strong> i-MiEVs after moving <strong>257</strong> off dealer lots in January.  To put that volume in context, Mitsubishi has had sold more i-MiEVs (625) already, than all of last year (588).    <a href="http://insideevs.com/lease-price-on-mitsubishi-i-drops-to-absurdly-low-69-per-month/" target="_blank">$69/month leases</a> can do that for you.</p>
<p>Sadly, the streak can&#8217;t last forever, as Mitsu is down to its last couple hundred 2012 model year i-MiEVs, and as one might notice, there is still no 2013 model year available in the US.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div id="attachment_17074" class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/2014-Accord-plug-in.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-17074" alt="2014 (yes, 2014) Honda Accord Plug-In (via automedia)" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/2014-Accord-plug-in-300x197.jpg" width="300" height="197" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">2014 (yes, 2014) Honda Accord Plug-In (via automedia)</p></div>
<p><strong>Honda Accord Plug-In: </strong>March was another month of &#8216;meh&#8217; results for the electric Accord as a paltry <strong>26</strong> were sold.</p>
<p>Even though Honda said their plug-in Accord went on sale on January 15th, there really hasn&#8217;t been any inventory at all to speak of.</p>
<p>In January, only <strong>two </strong>were sold, with <strong>17</strong> sales registered for February.</p>
<p>The inventory problem looks to persist as Honda ended March with less than <strong>two dozen</strong> plug-in Accords in stock.  Look for April&#8217;s result to also be low double-digits.</p>
<p>Officially classed as the new year&#8217;s first &#8217;2014&#8242; model, the Accord PHEV doesn&#8217;t come cheap, as Honda has <a href="http://insideevs.com/2014-honda-accord-plug-in-hybrid-priced-at-39780-13-miles-of-range-115-mpge/" target="_blank">recently priced the sedan at a very Fusion Energi-like $38,780</a>, markedly higher than consumer expectation for the car.</p>
<p>The Accord plug-in has a 13 mile all-electric range, and has been given a MPGe valuation of 115 MPGe, the highest rating of any plug-in extended range vehicle.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><strong>COMING In APRIL</strong></p>
<p><strong>Smart ForTwo Electric Drive: </strong>First set to arrive in Canada this month, and next month in the US, the Smart ForTwo Electric Drive sets a couple new benchmarks in the electric vehicle segment.</p>
<div id="attachment_25485" class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/smart-fortwo-ed-brabus.png"><img class="size-medium wp-image-25485" alt="Smart ForTwo ED (BRABUS edition)" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/smart-fortwo-ed-brabus-300x194.png" width="300" height="194" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Smart ForTwo ED (BRABUS edition)</p></div>
<p style="text-align: left;">A new &#8220;lowest price&#8221; EV price tag, starting at<strong> $25,000</strong> before federal credits (the Smart qualifies for the full $7,500 btw), but also is the first electric vehicle in the US to come as a Cabrio. Drop top fun begins at <strong>$28,000</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">The 2-seat Smart electric car gets <strong>68 miles</strong> of range from a 17.6 kWh battery.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">And before you say that might not be enough range for you; if you live in the city, you will easily destroy that range benchmark, as the Smart EV is a virtual brick in the wind tunnel skewing the range rating much lower than what can be expected driving in town. Conversely, that also makes the Smart EV a terrible choice if you drive on the highway a lot. City MPGe is rated at <strong>122</strong>, while highway fuel efficiency falls all the way to <strong>93</strong> MPGe.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Of note: Smart did record <strong>2</strong> Fortwo ED sales in January of 2013, but these were not the 3rd generational Smart EDs (2013 models) that are destined for public consumption in the spring of this year, but reportedly from an earlier 2nd generation offering.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><strong>Coming In July</strong></p>
<div id="attachment_25483" class="wp-caption alignleft" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/2014-fiat-500e-black.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-25483" alt="2014 Fiat 500e" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/2014-fiat-500e-black-300x180.jpg" width="300" height="180" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">2014 Fiat 500e</p></div>
<p><strong>Fiat 500e:</strong> <em> Fiatsler&#8217;s</em> answer to CARB compliance requirements has been slated to go on sale this summer, with a pricing announcement on the car coming April 13th.  InsideEVs expects the car to be priced just over $35,000.</p>
<p>The 500e&#8217;s 24 kWh lithium battery has been rated by the EPA to produce 87 miles of all electric range, and MPGe efficiency in the city is pegged at 122, and 108 on the highway, for a combined 116 MPGe figure.</p>
<p>Fiat has also announced this month that they will be <a href="http://insideevs.com/fiat-500e-purchasers-get-12-days-of-free-rentals-a-year-ev-to-be-priced-april-13th-on-sale-this-summer/" target="_blank">providing 12 free days* of car rentals per year</a> to purchasers of the EV, for when longer trips are required&#8230;at least for the first three years.</p>
