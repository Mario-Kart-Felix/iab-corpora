<h1>Hybrid car<br/></h1>
<!-- start: shared_blocks.33546700#below-h1 -->


<!-- end: shared_blocks.33546700#below-h1 -->
<div class="ImageBlock ImageBlockRight"><img src="http://www.auto-facts.org/images/Fisker-Karma-Blue.jpg" width="256" alt="Fisker Karma in Blue" title="Fisker Karma in Blue"></div>
<p>The <i>hybrid car</i> is becoming more popular as gas prices continue
 a record breaking roller coaster ride. The basic definition of this 
automotive technology is a vehicle that blends two different means of 
propulsion together. </p><p>The latest wave of these vehicles use different kinds of electric motors paired up with the standard internal combustion gas engine. This should not be confused with <a href="http://www.certifiedmastertech.com/wordpress/category/alternative-fuels/" target="_blank" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">alternative fuel vehicles</a> such as fuel cell vehicles that are hydrogen powered.Or the Fisker Karma pictured on the right that is a straight up electric car.<br/></p><p>Even a solar powered test car is not a hybrid unless it's dragging around a fossil fuel combustion engine. You could have an electric motor for propulsion and a gas engine just to charge the batteries. This would then fit in the category. I have been a student of <span style="color: green;">alternative fueled vehicles</span> for the last 2 decades. I was certified in CNG powered vehicles but I let it expire due to lack of public interest.
<br/></p>

<p>This type of alternative fueled vehicle just never caught on. Back 
ten years ago it looked like CNG (compressed natural gas) powered 
vehicles and propane vehicles where the future. </p><p>Although natural gas prices are cheaper then gasoline the prices of both of these fossil fuels are volatile and susceptible to large fluctuation. No disrespect to T-Boone 
Pickens, I do love his TV commercials and his book is inspirational to 
say the least. </p><p>I continue to wonder why if natural gas is so cheap and 
plentiful why was my gas heating bill record breaking last year despite a
 mild winter? Fox news also keeps telling me how much natural gas the 
U.S. is sitting on but they don't explain why infrastructure is not 
being developed to support CNG. Yes its true that every once in a
 while cng powered vehicles make another run at it. </p><p>Municipalities 
receiving subsidies to build fueling stations and incentives to perform conversions 
often make the local news because they are feel good stories for people supporting the green agenda. This can make the projects look more popular 
then they actually are in reality. Until hydraulic fracking is proven safe and all citizens believe in it cng powered passenger cars won't gain traction in my opinion. </p>
<div class="ImageBlock ImageBlockRight"><img src="http://www.auto-facts.org/images/electric-hybrid.jpg" width="160" alt="hybrid electric ca" title="hybrid electric ca"></div>
<p>In my opinion even companies that get natural gas for cheap do not like 
running their fleets on this power. I worked for a company that serviced
 a natural gas powered Fleet. This is why I became certified in CNG 
vehicles in the first place. </p><p>The systems where plagued with 
troubles mainly stemming from water created by pressure differentials. 
Although the tanks are placed in the center of the vehicle and are 
heavily shielded against impact these systems run at very high pressures
 and can be dangerous, again this is just my opinion.</p><p>I like the 
straight up electric car option better. Although the public does not 
seem impressed at the time of this writing. Chevrolet agrees with me but
 the Chevrolet Volt is not selling well in 2012 so far and the cost is 
high even with the tax credit. Also note the volt has a tiny gas engine 
but for charging the batteries not powering the vehicle. </p><p>I like 
the Nissan leaf as it does not carry around a heavy gas engine like the 
volt. I'm still trying to figure out why people love to drive golf carts
 and bumper cars but hate the idea of owning a vehicle that needs to be 
plugged in. I wrote a post on the auto repair information blog and 
invite your opinions on the <a href="http://www.certifiedmastertech.com/wordpress/2011/07/28/electric-vehicles-get-popular/" target="_blank" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">electric powered car</a> subject.</p><p>Sometimes I am disappointed in the popularity of the <span style="color: red;">hybrid car</span>.
 I have driven and worked on several different models of these vehicles 
and the technology is unimpressive. The vehicle also continues our 
dependency on foreign oil. When all is said and done yes you do get 
better fuel economy but it is not that drastic of an increase.
</p>

<p>The fleet company I work for now has a Toyota Prius that is 
struggling to get 50 mpg and we also have a Ford escape hybrid that 
pulls in around 28 mpg in city driving. </p><p>My girlfriends 2003 
Toyota Corolla (we call her Lola) gets about 35 mpg gallon without 
carrying around a golf cart motor. I mean no disrespect to the <i>hybrid car buyers</i> of the world. In fact there is no other out of the box really good affordable choice at this time. </p><p>It
 is just my educated opinion that this car is distracting automakers 
from finding a better and longer lasting solution. The car makers will 
build what is popular and sells well even if it is not in our best 
long-term interest. I will now get off my soapbox and tell you what I 
know about hybrid cars.
</p>
<h3>The hybrid car will save me money right?<br/></h3>
<div class="ImageBlock ImageBlockRight"><img src="http://www.auto-facts.org/images/cars-hybrid.jpg" width="300" alt="Nissan altima hybrid" title="Nissan altima hybrid"></div>
<p>The main financial advantage in some peoples mind is the tax credits. These <i>hybrid car tax credits</i> range between $ 2,000 and $7k dollars this depends on the model and in some cases where the vehicle is purchased. </p><p>The
 discount is not a tax deduction but a tax credit. Meaning it's 
subtracted directly from a tax bill. This requires extra work at tax time and should probably be done by a tax professional. To make this even more 
complicated if a taxpayer takes the credit and trades or sells the car before the useful life has expired a portion of the credit will 
be refunded.<br/></p>

<p>This is on a pro rated basis and declines with the age of the car. 
Over-all the higher ticket price of the vehicle mostly offsets any 
financial gains. Also note the tax credit might be coming to end if not 
extended by lawmakers.</p><p>The discount was originally set to 
expire in 2010 but still limps forward at the time of this writing. An 
interesting twist to the credit is the fact that it is based on a 
sliding scale tied to vehicle popularity. The more the unit sells the 
lower the tax credit. </p><p>The government designed this program to 
stimulate sales and drive the hybrid car forward favoring less popular 
models that need help. One fascinating thing to me about the cars 
themselves is the different methods used to manage the hybrid powertrain
 across manufactures.
</p>

<p>For example the Toyota uses a technique that stalls the gas engine 
when the vehicle is stopped. When you move again the electric motor gets
 you moving and the gas engine starts up and helps out all 
automatically. I mention this because itâs strange to feel the <span style="color: red;">gas engine</span> cut out at stoplights. </p><p>And
 if you plan on buying a hybrid car you should road test them around 
town to make sure your okay with the different quirks about each 
vehicle. I should also mention the Toyota Prius was recalled for 
computer system control issues recently (not all models). </p><p>Our 
fleet unit was included in this recall and the control system computer 
was replaced. Then it was recalled for a steering coupler inspection. 
Reports of broken steering couplers that attach the the steering wheel 
to the tires has been reported to break if the front wheels contact a 
curb with force. The manufacturers are still learning how to build these
 cars. My advice is to get an <a href="http://www.auto-facts.org/carwarranty.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">extended car warranty</a> that covers hybrid vehicle components.
