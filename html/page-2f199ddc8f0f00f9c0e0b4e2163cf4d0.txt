                        <p>Electric vehicles (EVs) are propelled by an electric motor (or motors) powered by rechargeable battery packs. EVs have several advantages over vehicles with internal combustion engines (ICEs):</p>
                        <ul>
                          <li><strong>Energy efficient.</strong> Electric vehicles convert about 59&#8211;62% of the electrical energy from the grid to  power at the wheels&#8212;conventional gasoline vehicles only convert about 17&#8211;21% of the energy stored in gasoline to power at the wheels.<sup><a href="#end-notes">*</a></sup></li>
                          <li><strong>Environmentally friendly.</strong> EVs emit no tailpipe pollutants, although the power plant producing the electricity may emit them. Electricity from nuclear-, hydro-, solar-, or wind-powered plants causes no air pollutants.</li>
                          
                          <li><strong>Performance benefits.</strong> Electric motors provide quiet, smooth operation and stronger acceleration and require less maintenance than ICEs.</li>
                          <li><strong>Reduce energy dependence</strong>. Electricity is a domestic energy source.</li>
                        </ul>
            
                        <p>EVs do, however, face significant battery-related challenges:</p>
                        <!-- <img src="/feg/images/ev/leaf-charging.jpg" alt="EV recharging" width="300" class="floatRight"> -->
						

                        
                        <ul>
                          <li><strong>Driving range. </strong>Most EVs can only go about 100&#8211;200 miles before recharging&#8212;gasoline vehicles can go over 300 miles before refueling. </li>
                          <li><strong>Recharge time.</strong> Fully recharging the battery pack can take 4 to 8 hours. Even a &quot;quick charge&quot; to 80% capacity can take 30 min.</li>
                          <li><strong>Battery cost:</strong> The large battery packs are expensive and may need to be replaced one or more times.</li>
                          <li><strong>Bulk &amp; weight:</strong> Battery packs are heavy and take up considerable vehicle space.</li>
                        </ul>
                        <p>However, researchers are working on improved battery technologies to increase driving range and decrease recharging time, weight, and cost. These factors will ultimately determine the future of EVs.</p>
