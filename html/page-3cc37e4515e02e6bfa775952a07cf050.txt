		<p><i><b>From &quot;B.M.&quot;: </b></i>&nbsp; Referring to the issue of 
		motorcycle specific oil vs. automotive oil used in motorcycles)</p>
		<p>&quot;...There is the comment (from an old Motorcycle Consumer News article 
		on motorcycle oil) &quot;The viscosity of petroleum-based oils, whether 
		designed for auto or motorcycle application, drop at approximately the same 
		rate when used in a motorcycle.&quot; and &quot;There is no evidence that 
		motorcycle-specific oils out-perform their automotive counterparts in viscosity 
		retention when used in a motorcycle.&quot;<br />
		<br />
		I do not doubt these statements but they ignore two pertinent issues. One, 
		viscosity retention is not the only measure of an oil's ability to protect 
		an engine.&nbsp; Many motorcycles have flat tappet cam &amp; rocker arm 
		interfaces, which create high shear stresses.&nbsp; </p>
		<p>Newer automotive oils have removed the additives most beneficial in resisting 
		these stresses (zinc? I'm not sure).&nbsp; This is a characteristic of the &quot;Energy 
		Conserving&quot; oils recommended for modern automotive engines.&nbsp; I 
		don't have any evidence of failures due to this in motorcycle engines, but 
		I have seen evidence of failures in older automotive engines.<br />
		<br />
		Two, clutch operation is NOT equal between moto &amp; auto oils.&nbsp; I 
		recently &quot;just tried&quot; an oil change with Valvoline 4-Stroke Motorcycle 
		oil.&nbsp; I had been using Havoline 10w-40 (non-energy conserving), and 
		difference was astounding.&nbsp; With the auto oil, I thought I was about 
		due for a new clutch.&nbsp; I had to work at getting a smooth engagement 
		without excessive slippage.&nbsp; With the moto oil, the clutch bite was 
		much better and still smooth.<br />
		<br />
		I know that many moto oils are quite expensive, but the Valvoline is only 
		slightly more expensive than auto oils, so cost is no longer an issue.&nbsp; 
		For me, it's a no-brainer. I'm sold on moto oils now.&quot;</p>
