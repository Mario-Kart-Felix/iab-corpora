<h3>The 2012 Ford Fiesta Hatchback Review</h3>
<p><em>Full Review by Andy: How to Romance a Car</em></p>
<p>Back when Americans were romancing gas-guzzling SUVs in the ‘90s, the rest of the world was wooing small gas-sippers like the Ford Fiesta. Recently, America packed up its bags and divorced the big lugs. Its ogling eyes now roam the streets for sexy small cars, and the 2012 Ford Fiesta hatchback is just the tease it needs.</p>
<p><em>Exterior</em><br />
The 2012 Fiesta hatchback takes a cue from its linguistic name and offers a fun, captivating exterior style that deserves to be called the life of the party. Styled under Ford’s Kinetic design language, the Fiesta brims with dazzling design elements, including dragons-eye headlamps, razor-slash side panels, and other monikers that bring to mind Halo video games.</p>
<p><em>Interior</em><br />
The packaging is just as important as the gift. This is not only applicable to Christmas and political speeches,<span id="more-10579"></span> but also to automotive design. The Ford Fiesta comes with a bow neatly tied on top, courtesy of its edgy interior design characterized by sharp angles, metallic trim, and two-tiered dash. While it may not classify as upscale, the Fiesta has an attractive, well-built interior that exceeds the expectations of its price tag. It is sure to lure in young drivers who desire a bold look in a peppy package.</p>
<p><em>Packin’ a Powertrain</em><br />
The Ford Fiesta is powered by a 1.6-liter inline four-cylinder engine which generates 120 horsepower, a high figure for the class. In base configuration, it is mated to a five-speed manual transmission. But with much of the world forgetting how to row their own, many may choose the optional six-speed dual-clutch automated manual transmission, which – in theory – provides faster shift times than a traditional automatic. The gearboxes return 38 or39 highway mpg, respectively; the optional SFE package bumps up fuel economy to the magic 40.</p>
<p><em>Driveline Performance</em><br />
The Fiesta has been the subject of a lot of hype. Much of the lionizing is deserved, but a few overly excited rumors have gone about touting the performance of the 2,600-lb hatchback. I hate to break it to you, but your Fiesta is not going to run circles around your neighbor’s Miata. With that admitted, the Fiesta is one of the best econoboxes to ever splash onto American shores. Although the Fiesta takes over nine seconds to slog to 60 mph, its high-revving straight-four provides immediate power delivery and quick acceleration off the line, while both transmissions quickly swap gears. It slaloms like Kjetil Andre Aamodt and jukes like Adrian Peterson. Okay, that may be hyperbolic, but you get the point.</p>
<p><em>Safety Information</em><br />
The Fiesta is outfitted with class-leading safety features, including a driver knee airbag, hill start assist, and four-wheel ABS. In NHTSA crash testing, the Fiesta walked away, so to speak, with an average of four stars, while the IIHS dubbed the Fiesta hatchback a 2012 Top Safety Pick.</p>
<p><em>Comfort &amp; Cargo</em><br />
Automotive journalists stuck in the yesteryears of 2000 may introduce the Ford Fiesta hatchback with the antiquated notion that the car-buying citizens of the U.S. of A. don’t like hatchbacks. That’s nonsense. True, there are still a few misguided fanatics that can’t stand the sight of an odd-numbered door, but most of America is gradually growing receptive to the lovable liftbacks. Here’s why: versatility. The Fiesta hatchback offers the performance of a sedan with the carrying capacity of an open boot, maxing out at 26 cubic feet with the rear seat folded. That’s twice as much as most small sedans, but pathetic compared to the <a title="2012 Kia Soul" href="http://zautos.com/vehicle/ebrochure/2012/Kia/Soul/Base-4dr-Wagon-6A/1.6L-I4-138hp-123ft.-lbs./6-Speed-Automatic/" target="_blank">Kia Soul</a> or <a title="2012 Hyundai Accent Hatchback" href="http://zautos.com/vehicle/ebrochure/2012/Hyundai/Accent/GS-4dr-Hatchback-6A/1.6L-I4-138hp-123ft.-lbs./6-Speed-Automatic/" target="_blank">Hyundai Accent hatchback</a>.</p>
<p><em>Feature Highlights</em><br />
The 2012 Ford Fiesta hatchback is available in two trims: <a title="2012 Ford Fiesta SE Hatchback" href="http://zautos.com/vehicle/ebrochure/2012/Ford/Fiesta/SE-4dr-Hatchback/1.6L-V4-120hp-112ft.-lbs./6-Speed-Automatic/?tab=fe" target="_blank">SE ($15,670)</a> and <a title="2012 Ford Fiesta SES Hatchback" href="http://zautos.com/vehicle/ebrochure/2012/Ford/Fiesta/SES-4dr-Hatchback/1.6L-V4-120hp-112ft.-lbs./6-Speed-Automatic/?tab=fe" target="_blank">SES ($17,500)</a>.</p>
<p>The SE is best described as suitable. It has necessities like full power accessories and air-conditioning, but few extra goodies to complements its base inventory. It does, notably, have a six-speaker stereo system with CD player and USB port. The SES is quite impressive for the price tag, offering an autodimming rearview mirror, upgraded sound system with satellite radio, LED rear parking lights, Ford SYNC, and more.</p>
<p>Tidbit Trio:<br />
1. Over 12 million Fiestas have been sold worldwide since the name’s original debut in 1976.<br />
2. Leather-upholstered heated front seats are optional.<br />
3. Ford SYNC comes with turn-by-turn navigation and emergency assist.</p>
<p>America has yet to move into the courtship stage – the Fiesta has more industry accolades than actual sales figures – but considering the hatchback&#8217;s efficient powertrain, fun driving dynamics, and exceptional interior, it may not be long before America drops to its knee and pops the question: “Can I please buy you, 2012 Ford Fiesta hatchback?”</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline">Details</span>:<br />
Engine: 1.6-liter inline four-cylinder<br />
Horsepower &amp; Torque: 120 hp @6,350 rpm, 112 lb-ft of torque @5,000 rpm<br />
Transmission: five-speed manual, six-speed dual-clutch automated manual<br />
Drivetrain: FWD<br />
Fuel Economy: 29/38/33 mpg (manual)<br />
Base MSRP: $15,670<br />
0 to 60 time: 9.5 seconds</p>
