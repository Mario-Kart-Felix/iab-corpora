In addition to announcing the upcoming debut, Chevy also teased us with a rather revealing image, for a teaser anyway.</p><p>This image depicts a minivan that boasts much bolder styling than your average people-hauling minivan. It features wrap-around headlights that come to a sharp point near the halfway point of the fenders. The windshield rakes rearward at a very shallow angle and its roofline actually has a slight curve to it. Really, this “minivan” looks closer to a crossover than an SUV.</p><p>Considering that the developing markets that Chevy has in its targets, Indonesia and Brazil, it is no wonder that it would bear the minivan classification instead of crossover. From personal experience, I can tell you that the term “crossover” is not a well-known one in Indonesia and only a handful of cars, including the <a
href="http://www.topspeed.com/cars/nissan-juke/ke3600.html" class='spip_out'>Nissan Juke</a> and <a
href="http://www.topspeed.com/cars/suzuki-sx4/ke943.html" class='spip_out'>Suzuki SX4</a>, bear that classification. We would assume much of the same is going on in Brazil.</p><p>The Spin will be available in both a five- and seven-seat capacity, but unlike American minivans, this one will boast hinged doors – again, bearing more resemblance to a crossover. To remain competitive in these developing markets and allow buyers to be able to fuel this new minivan, it will likely boast a sub-2.0-liter engine as its base level, with its smallest engine likely being the 141 PS (139 hp) 1.8-liter four-cylinder in the Chevy Orlando and <a
href="http://www.topspeed.com/cars/chevrolet-cruze/ke3230.html" class='spip_out'>Cruze</a>.</p><p>Given its size and the fact that its closest competitor will be the 2.4-liter-powered Honda Odyssey, which is far smaller than the American <a
href="http://www.topspeed.com/cars/honda-odyssey/ke1873.html" class='spip_out'>Honda Odyssey</a>, Chevy may offer it with an optional 171 PS (168 hp) 2.4-liter found in the <a
href="http://www.topspeed.com/cars/chevrolet-captiva/ke949.html" class='spip_out'>Captiva</a> SUV.</p><p>Chevy is set to fully unveil this upcoming minivan/crossover in the next few weeks and it’ll hit showroom floors early in 2013.
