<p>The <a href="/Vapid" title="Vapid">Vapid</a> <b>Minivan</b> is a four-door minivan in <a href="/Grand_Theft_Auto_IV" title="Grand Theft Auto IV">Grand Theft Auto IV</a>, and is to be featured in <a href="/Grand_Theft_Auto_V" title="Grand Theft Auto V">Grand Theft Auto V</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span></h3>
<p>The Minivan resembles a fourth generation (2001–2007) <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dodge_Caravan" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Dodge Caravan">Dodge Caravan</a> or (2001–2003) <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chrysler_Voyager" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Chrysler Voyager">Chrysler Voyager</a>. The taillights resemble those found on the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ford_Ranger_(North_America)#1998.E2.80.93present" class="extiw" title="wp:Ford Ranger (North America)">2001–2005 North American Ford Ranger</a>. The van may come with a roof-mounted luggage rack or a rear spoiler. Unlike other cars (like the <a href="/Vincent" title="Vincent">Vincent</a> and its spoiler variant), the badging does not change with its accessories, and it always bears a "Minivan Sport" badge. Unlike its real-life counterpart, the Minivan does not have rear sliding doors; instead, it has swinging rear doors, which are common on minivans sold in Europe and Japan.
</p><p>The Minivan is badged as both a <a href="/Vapid" title="Vapid">Vapid</a> vehicle and, in the case of the <a href="/Cabby" title="Cabby">Cabby</a> taxicab, a <a href="/Schyster" title="Schyster">Schyster</a>, which may be an allusion to the Dodge Caravan being sold under different names (Chrysler Town and Country and Plymouth Grand Voyager). it may also suggest a partnership between Vapid and Schyster, or it may suggest a larger mother company like <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Motors" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:General Motors">General Motors</a> which produces the same vehicles under different brands. Alternatively, the Cabby's "Limited Vapid Design" badging suggests that the Cabby could be manufactured under license by Schyster, or could be a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/badge_engineering" class="extiw" title="wp:badge engineering">rebadge</a> of the Vapid Minivan.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Performance">Performance</span></h3>
<p>The Minivan is powered by a 24V engine, presumably a V6 with 4 valves per cylinder, paired to a 4 speed gearbox in a RWD configuration. Acceleration is very poor, taking upwards of 10 seconds to reach 60mph (100 km/h). The top speed of the Minivan is also very poor (81 mph - 130 km/h), putting it on par with the <a href="/Willard" title="Willard">Willard</a> as the slowest passenger vehicle. The suspension is moderately firm, giving the Minivan ample support in the curves, albeit with drastic understeer. Braking is good, and ABS is standard across the line. Accident deformation is marginal and a head on crash with a wall can not only damage the outside the car, but the inside as well. However, it takes 3 to 4 direct frontal hits at a high speed to cause the car to stall or catch fire. Pedestrian safety is poor though as the shape of the car suggests that if involved in a crash with a pedestrian, there is a high risk of being pulled under the wheels and being crushed to death.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">Trivia</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/File:Minivan-MP3-front.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="/images/thumb/Minivan-MP3-front.jpg/200px-Minivan-MP3-front.jpg" width="200" height="126" class="thumbimage" srcset="/images/Minivan-MP3-front.jpg 1.5x, /images/Minivan-MP3-front.jpg 2x" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/File:Minivan-MP3-front.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>Distant view of a Minivan in Max Payne 3.</div></div></div>
<ul><li>The Minivan's generic name is probably a parody of the Dodge Caravan's.
</li><li>The Minivan can be sold for $2,000 at <a href="/Stevie" title="Stevie">Stevie's</a> <a href="/S%26M_Auto_Sales" title="S&amp;M Auto Sales">S&amp;M Auto Sales</a> in GTA IV after completion of <a href="/Stevie%27s_Car_Thefts" title="Stevie's Car Thefts">Stevie's Car Thefts</a>.
</li><li>The default radio station of the Minivan is <a href="/The_Vibe_98.8" title="The Vibe 98.8">The Vibe 98.8</a> in GTA IV.
</li><li>Despite the Minivan is, as implied, a van, the police dispatch in GTA IV calls it a hatchback.
</li><li>The Minivan is featured in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Payne_3" class="extiw" title="wp:Max Payne 3">Max Payne 3</a>, a <a href="/Rockstar_Vancouver" title="Rockstar Vancouver">Rockstar Vancouver</a> game, as a background vehicle in wide environment levels (including Chapter II: Nothing but the Second Best and Chapter IV: Anyone Can Buy Me a Drink).
</li></ul>
