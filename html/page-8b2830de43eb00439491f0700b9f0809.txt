<p align="left" style="margin-left: 20; margin-right: 20; margin-top: 1; margin-bottom: 1"><b><font color="#0000FF" face="Arial" size="5">Introduction</font></b></p>
<p align="left" style="margin-left: 20; margin-right: 20; margin-top: 1; margin-bottom: 1"><font face="Arial" size="3">Neighborhood&nbsp;
Electric Vehicles or NEV is a vehicle that is capable of traveling at a maximum
speed of 25mph.&nbsp; They come&nbsp; with safety features like headlights, turn
signals and seat belts.&nbsp; In most States and in some Canadian provinces they can be operated
on the roads where the posted speed limit is 35mph or less and can cross streets
posted at 45mph or less.&nbsp;&nbsp; They may
also be referred to as Low Speed Vehicles or LSVs.&nbsp; Some states, such as
Washington and Montana have also passed Medium Speed Vehicle legislation
allowing some NEVs to be modified for speeds of 35mph and allow them to run on
roads with a posted speed limit of 45mph or less - check with your State's DMV
to see if your state allows LSVs of MSVs in your state.</font></p>

<p align="left" style="margin-left: 20; margin-right: 20; margin-top: 1; margin-bottom: 1"><font face="Arial" size="3"><a href="http://www.itiselectric.com/">Dynasty
Motorcars</a> , based in British Columbia, Canada, launched their IT NEV amid much
Fanfare.&nbsp; They built a small number of these vehicles and started to put

shutdown operations.&nbsp; They were subsequently bought out

<p align="left" style="margin-left: 20; margin-right: 20; margin-top: 1; margin-bottom: 1"><font face="Arial" size="3">I
had a chance to talk to Dynasty Motorcars at EVS 20 and the good news is that
they are selling vehicles as fast as they can build them which looks good for
the long term health of the company.</font></p>
<p align="left" style="margin-left: 20; margin-right: 20; margin-top: 1; margin-bottom: 1">&nbsp;</p>
<p align="left" style="margin-left: 20; margin-right: 20; margin-top: 1; margin-bottom: 1"><font face="Arial" size="3">The
IT is a four seat sedan that reminds me a little of the new beetle.&nbsp; Unlike
most NEVs it does not look like a golf cart.&nbsp; It is powered by an advanced
DC motor and features regenerative braking.&nbsp; six twelve volt Trojan flooded
cell batteries give the IT a quoted range of 30 miles (expect about 20 in real
world driving) at the top speed of 25 mph.&nbsp; It also features a turf mode
which limits the top speed to twelve mph for&nbsp; use on the golf course.&nbsp;
An additional twelve volt battery is used to power the auxiliary electrical
system.&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></p>
<p align="left" style="margin-left: 20; margin-right: 20; margin-top: 1; margin-bottom: 1">&nbsp;</p>
<p align="left" style="margin-left: 20; margin-right: 20; margin-top: 1; margin-bottom: 1"><font face="Arial" size="3">There
are several other body styles that are also available including a van and a convertible.&nbsp;
The Sport is a four seat vehicle that uses the same drive train as the IT but
has a look that is more like a golf cart.&nbsp; They also have the Tropic which is
basically the same as the Sport but has a more open body design, the Utility
which is designed as a pickup truck, and the Van.</font></p>
