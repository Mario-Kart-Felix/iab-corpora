        <h1>
            Online Motorcycle Mechanic School        </h1>
                <div class="program_facts">
<div class="program_facts_header">Related Industry Info</div>
There were about 15,000 US motorcycle mechanic jobs in 2002<br /><br />About 24% of motorcycle repair technicians are self employed<br /><br /></div>
<div id="top_blurb">
<p>While getting your motorcycle mechanics training, you will get extensive hands on experience working on:                        <div id="header_sub_blurb_text_toggle" style="display: none" ></p>
<ul>
<li>motorcycle engines</li>
<li>electrical systems</li>
<li>fuel systems</li>
<li>Exhaust systems</li>
<li>brake systems</li>
<li>transmissions</li>
<li>all other aspects of a motorcycle</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p>In motorcycle mechanic school you will learn to diagnose engine, mechanical, fuel, and electrical problems, and how to make repairs in a minimal amount of time.</p>
<p>Skilled mechanics that have been trained in motorcycle mechanic schools are in high demand. For some, the work is limited to one manufacturer, such as Harley, Honda, Kawasaki, Suzuki, Yamaha, or some other type of motorcycle. A job as a motorcycle mechanic will may include work on:</p>
<ul>
<li>motorcycles</li>
<li>motor scooters</li>
<li>mopeds</li>
<li>dirt bikes</li>
<li>ATVs</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p>The popularity of motorcycles continues to rise, and completing training at a motorcycle mechanics institute will give you the opportunity to do something you love - make the machine purr like a kitten, and earn a good living at the same time.</p>

						this.value = '';
						$(this).css('color', 'black');
					}
				});
				element.blur(function(){
					if(this.value == '')
					{
						this.value = defaultText;
						$(this).css('color', 'grey');
					}
				});
