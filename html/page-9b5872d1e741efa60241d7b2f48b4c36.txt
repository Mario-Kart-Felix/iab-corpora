							One more thing to note:  the 240 wagon's original tire size in the US was 185R14.  (For Canada, it was 185/75R14.)<p>185R14 (equivalent to 185/80R14) is no longer available; the closest alternate tire size is 195/75R14.  Many owners mistakenly put on 185/70R14 tires, which is the sedan's tire size, even though these tires have a lower load rating.<p>A temporary spare tire size 175R14 was originally included with the wagon, but a full size 185R14 or 195/75R14 tire does fit in the spare tire holder of the wagon.<p>See the tire specs in the owner's manual:<br><A HREF="http://new.volvocars.com/ownersdocs/1986/1986_240/86240_04.htm#pg88" TARGET="_blank">http://new.volvocars.com/owner...#pg88</A> (scroll down to "pg 93. Specifications" and the section that says "Tire Pressure Label")<p>Batteries are another thing to be aware of.  Older ones used battery group 46, which is no longer available (the last vendor offering them, Sears Die Hard International, stopped offering them a few years ago).  Group 24 is the same size and terminal position (and higher specified capacity), but the rails to clamp it down are on the long side instead of the short side like on group 46.  The easiest way to clamp down a group 24 where a group 46 was is to use big heavy duty zip ties.<p>It is common to find Volvo 240 cars with the reverse lens missing.  Fortunately, just the reverse lens can be bought from vendors like <A HREF="http://www.fcpgroton.com" TARGET="_blank">http://www.fcpgroton.com</A> (apparently, there was a production run of just those lenses in the mid-1990s due to them falling out frequently).<BR><BR><br />
