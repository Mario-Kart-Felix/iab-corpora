    <p>
When <a href="/reviews/2001-lexus-is300-first-drive-review" target="_self">the first generation of the Lexus IS</a> was launched in 1998, it represented a remarkable departure from the brand's well-established, conservative design language. The car&rsquo;s wedge-like shape and pronounced character lines defined its profile, and clear taillights started a phenomenon in tuner circles. (The car remains wildly popular among tuners today.) <a href="/news/lexus-is-auto-shows" target="_self">The second generation</a>, launched in 2005, was a bit more sedated, but its Quaalude-inspired design was forgiven thanks to the fantastic <a href="/reviews/2008-lexus-is-f-long-term-road-test" target="_self">IS F</a> with its 416-hp, 5.0-liter V-8. And now, Lexus is launching the third generation of its IS compact performance sedan&mdash;<a href="/news/2014-lexus-is-sedan-prototype-drive-review" target="_self">a prototype of which we&rsquo;ve already driven</a>&mdash;and the brand is staying true to its original formula.
</p>

<p>
The aggressively styled third generation launches with the proven, naturally aspirated 2.5-liter V-6 in the IS 250, and the 3.5-liter V-6 in the IS 350; power ratings will remain at 204- and 306 horsepower, respectively. Both models are available with rear- and all-wheel drive.
</p>

<p>
Sadly, the manual transmission is history. At least this decision is sweetened significantly by the fact that the rear-drive IS 350 now comes with the eight-speed automatic straight from the IS F. This transmission is an ultra-quick box that blips the throttle on downshifts when in manual mode. The all-wheel-drive IS 350 and both IS 250s, on the other hand, are fitted with the current model's six-speed slushbox. All models come with a Drive Mode Select system that manipulates the throttle response, transmission, and chassis in several modes.
</p>

<p>
Europeans&mdash;and inhabitants of &ldquo;select international markets&rdquo;&mdash;will be offered the IS 300h hybrid, featuring a 2.5-liter Atkinson-cycle engine and an electric motor that is mated to a CVT box. Speaking of Europeans, the diesel-powered IS that had been made available to them&mdash;in two strengths, no less&mdash;has been canned, thanks in large part to its slow sales performance.
</p>

<p>
At first glance, the IS is&mdash;yet again&mdash;obviously not your typical Lexus. While the new IS retains the typical rear-wheel-drive proportions of its predecessors, its details are overtly aggressive. The car&rsquo;s spindle grille is featured prominently, there are jagged headlight clusters with L-shaped daytime running lights underscoring said clusters, and aggressively stretched taillights.
</p>

<p>
The interior is rather sporty as well, with a center console and a steering wheel inspired by the <a href="/reviews/2012-lexus-lfa-review" target="_self">LFA</a>. A central clock creates a visual link to the more-upscale Lexus sedans, and the air-conditioning system is operated by touch-sensitive buttons.
</p>

<p>
There is, of course, another F Sport model, which is even-more extreme in appearance, and sportier in its content. Besides visual changes, it offers altered suspension- and power-steering tuning, bespoke 18-inch aluminum wheels, and a unique instrument cluster with a centrally located tachometer. However, the IS F&mdash;and the IS convertible&mdash;will carry on in its present form until 2014.
</p>

<p>
There is no premium car launched today without a plethora of assistance systems. The Lexus IS includes a lane-departure system, a blind-spot monitor, and a rear cross-traffic alert that monitors cross traffic while the car is in reverse. Automatic high beams are standard, as well. And this compact sedan packs in 10 air bags.
</p>

<p>
The 2014 Lexus IS makes its in-the-metal debut at the 2013 Detroit auto show before going on sale in mid-2013. Pricing information should be available closer to the car&rsquo;s on-sale date, but don&rsquo;t expect any major changes. 
</p>
