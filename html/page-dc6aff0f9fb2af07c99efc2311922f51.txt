						<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en"><span dir="auto">Luxury vehicle</span></h1>
			<div id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Luxury vehicle</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Marketing" title="Marketing">marketing</a> term for a vehicle that provides luxury — pleasant or desirable features beyond strict necessity — at increased expense (see <a href="/wiki/Luxury_goods" title="Luxury goods">luxury goods</a>).</p>
<p>The term suggests a vehicle with higher quality equipment, better performance, more precise construction, comfort, higher design, technologically innovative modern, or features that convey an image, brand, <a href="/wiki/Social_status" title="Social status">status</a>, or prestige, or any other 'discretionary' feature or combination of them. The term is also broad, highly variable and relative. It is a perceptual, conditional and subjective attribute that may be understood differently by different people; "what may be luxury for one may be premium for another."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In contemporary usage, the term may be applied to any vehicle type— including <a href="/wiki/Sedan_(car)" title="Sedan (car)" class="mw-redirect">sedan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coup%C3%A9" title="Coupé">coupe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hatchback" title="Hatchback">hatchback</a>, <a href="/wiki/Station_wagon" title="Station wagon">station wagon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Convertible_(car)" title="Convertible (car)" class="mw-redirect">convertible</a> body styles, as well as to <a href="/wiki/Minivan" title="Minivan">minivans</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Crossover_(automobile)" title="Crossover (automobile)">crossovers</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Sport_utility_vehicle" title="Sport utility vehicle">sport utility vehicles</a> and to any size vehicle, from small to large—in any price range.<sup id="cite_ref-consumer_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-consumer-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Moreover, there is a convergence in the markets and a resulting confusion of luxury with high price: where there may have been a clear difference in price between luxury and others, there is no longer an absolute separation between premium and luxury, with what may be premium brands now more expensive than the equivalent so-called luxury ones.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition">Definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Definition">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Definition" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Duesenberg.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Duesenberg.jpg/220px-Duesenberg.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Duesenberg.jpg/330px-Duesenberg.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Duesenberg.jpg/440px-Duesenberg.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Duesenberg.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf13/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
A <a href="/wiki/Duesenberg" title="Duesenberg">Duesenberg</a>, "one of the greatest luxury cars"<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> with custom body by Willoughby</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Automobile manufacturers market specific makes and models that are targeted at particular socio-economic classes, and thus "social status came to be associated more with a particular vehicle than ownership of a car per se."<sup id="cite_ref-berger_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-berger-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Therefore, automakers differentiate among their product lines in "collusion" with the car-buying public.<sup id="cite_ref-berger_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-berger-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> While a high price is the most frequent factor, it is "styling, engineering, and even public opinion which cars had the highest and lowest status associated with them."<sup id="cite_ref-berger_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-berger-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Every era in automobile history has had "a group of car marques and models that have been expensive to purchase, due to their alleged superiority of their design and engineering".<sup id="cite_ref-berger_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-berger-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Aimed at wealthy buyers, such automobiles might be generically be termed <i>luxury cars</i>."<sup id="cite_ref-berger_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-berger-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> This term is also used for unique vehicles produced during "an era when luxury was individualistic consideration, and coachwork could be tailored to an owner like a bespoke suit."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> Although there is considerable literature about specific marques, there is a lack of systematic and scholarly work that "analyzes the luxury car phenomenon itself."<sup id="cite_ref-berger_7-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-berger-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Luxury vehicle makers may either be stand-alone companies in their own right, such as BMW and Mercedes, or a division/subsidiary of a mass market automaker (e.g., Lexus is part of Toyota). <a href="/wiki/Badge_engineering" title="Badge engineering">Badge engineering</a> is often used for cost savings, for example, the Lincoln vehicles that are based on Ford <a href="/wiki/Automobile_platforms" title="Automobile platforms" class="mw-redirect">platforms</a> or Acura models that are modified mass-market Honda cars.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Though widely used, the term luxury is broad and highly variable. It is a perceptual, conditional and subjective attribute and may be understood differently by different people: "What is a luxury car to some... may be 'ordinary' to others."<sup id="cite_ref-consumer_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-consumer-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_references">Global references</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Global references">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Global references" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Isotta-Fraschini_8A-Roadster_Front-view.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Isotta-Fraschini_8A-Roadster_Front-view.JPG/220px-Isotta-Fraschini_8A-Roadster_Front-view.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Isotta-Fraschini_8A-Roadster_Front-view.JPG/330px-Isotta-Fraschini_8A-Roadster_Front-view.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Isotta-Fraschini_8A-Roadster_Front-view.JPG/440px-Isotta-Fraschini_8A-Roadster_Front-view.JPG 2x" /></a>

<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Isotta-Fraschini_8A-Roadster_Front-view.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf13/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Italian <a href="/wiki/Isotta_Fraschini" title="Isotta Fraschini">Isotta Fraschini</a> Tipo 8A S LeBaron Boattail Roadster was a 1930s luxury car<sup id="cite_ref-conceptcarz.com_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-conceptcarz.com-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a>, the "luxury vehicle" segment is classified as F-segment. However, the boundaries between the traditional segments are increasingly becoming blurred and diluted as features once exclusive to luxury vehicles become standard equipment on even small cars.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/ACRISS_Car_Classification_Code" title="ACRISS Car Classification Code">ACRISS</a> is a code system used by many <a href="/wiki/Car_rental" title="Car rental">car rental</a> companies in the US for classifying vehicles (but not brands or marques). The system includes <b>Luxury</b> and <b>Premium</b> categories.</li>
<li><b>Australia:</b> In Australia, for taxation purposes a luxury car is defined as a passenger car whose value exceeds a certain threshold<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> (see: <a href="/wiki/Luxury_Car_Tax" title="Luxury Car Tax">Luxury Car Tax</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li><b>France:</b> In France the term "voiture de luxe" is used.<sup id="cite_ref-grandes_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandes-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li><b>Germany:</b> In Germany the term "Upper class" (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Oberklasse</i></span>) is used.<sup id="cite_ref-obere_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obere-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li><b>Russia:</b> Russian markets use the term "representative class vehicle" (<span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru"><i>автомобиль представительского класса</i></span>, also translated as "luxury vehicle").<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li><b>United States</b> Consumer Guide's categorization which sorts luxury vehicles by size acknowledges that there can be considerable price variations within a class; for instance the <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Town_Car" title="Lincoln Town Car">Lincoln Town Car</a> has a relatively low MSRP (Manufacturer's Suggested Retail Price) compared to the <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_XJ" title="Jaguar XJ">Jaguar XJ</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW_7_Series" title="BMW 7 Series">BMW 7 Series</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lexus_LS" title="Lexus LS">Lexus LS</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_S-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz S-Class">Mercedes-Benz S-Class</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-howstuffworks1_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-howstuffworks1-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Luxury_market_segments">Luxury market segments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Luxury market segments">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Luxury market segments" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Premium_compact_segment">Premium compact segment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Premium compact segment">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Premium compact segment" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Compact_executive_car" title="Compact executive car">compact executive car</a> segment or premium compact class<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> is relatively new, having been initiated by several European brands in the mid-2000s, and have displaced their compact executive cars to constitute the least expensive offerings in their lineups. The classification varies, for instance Consumer Guide Automotive in the U.S. considers the <a href="/wiki/Audi_A3" title="Audi A3">Audi A3</a> and <a href="/wiki/Audi_A4" title="Audi A4">Audi A4</a> as part of the premium compact segment due to similar size and MSRP, though these are known in Europe as a small family car and a compact executive car, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-consumerguideauto.howstuffworks.com_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-consumerguideauto.howstuffworks.com-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> Adding to some confusion, <a href="/wiki/General_Motors" title="General Motors">General Motors</a> has positioned Buick as a "premium" nameplate to compete with Lincoln and front-wheel drive Acura and Lexus models, while <a href="/wiki/Cadillac" title="Cadillac">Cadillac</a> is aimed at the "luxury" segment which generally encompasses the performance-oriented <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a> marques.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ExpertReview_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ExpertReview-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The premium compact segment is targeted at a niche market of consumers who found the existing entry-level luxury offerings (mostly consisting of compact executive cars - see below) to be too expensive.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> By offering a smaller, lighter, more fuel-efficient, and less expensive vehicle, premium compacts introduces younger buyers to the luxury marque, in hopes of retaining the coveted customer loyalty. This includes the <a href="/wiki/Audi_A3" title="Audi A3">Audi A3</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW_1_Series" title="BMW 1 Series">BMW 1 Series</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lexus_CT" title="Lexus CT">Lexus CT</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_CLA-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz CLA-Class">Mercedes-Benz CLA-Class</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_B-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz B-Class">Mercedes-Benz B-Class</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aston_Martin_Cygnet" title="Aston Martin Cygnet" class="mw-redirect">Aston Martin Cygnet</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Volvo_V40" title="Volvo V40">Volvo V40</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-theglobeandmail.com_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theglobeandmail.com-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Premium compacts may share components with mass market cars from the marque's parent company (the Audi A3 for example, shares much of its engineering with the cheaper <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Golf" title="Volkswagen Golf">Volkswagen Golf</a>), and/or have less sophisticated platforms compared to upmarket vehicles in the lineup (such as the Mercedes-Benz B-Class which is front-wheel drive compared to the other marque's more expensive cars that are rear-wheel drive). The body style tends to be a <a href="/wiki/Hatchback" title="Hatchback">hatchback</a> or compact wagon, previously associated with economy cars but regaining popularity in the United States for its afforded utility, although as of 2013 there are sedans such as the <a href="/wiki/Audi_A3#8V" title="Audi A3">Audi A3</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_CLA-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz CLA-Class">Mercedes-Benz CLA-Class</a>. The luxury branding and style, high-quality interior materials, wide range of convenience features, and performance powertrains are key to distinguishing them from mass market equivalents (one mistake made by the <a href="/wiki/Cadillac_Cimarron" title="Cadillac Cimarron">Cadillac Cimarron</a>) and making these appeal to consumers.<sup id="cite_ref-theglobeandmail.com_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theglobeandmail.com-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entry-level_luxury.2Fcompact_executive_cars">Entry-level luxury/compact executive cars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Entry-level luxury/compact executive cars">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Entry-level luxury/compact executive cars" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This category is known as the <a href="/wiki/Compact_executive_car" title="Compact executive car">compact executive car</a> in Britain where it specifies both the price range and vehicle dimensions. In the U.S., there is a broader category called entry-level luxury, which includes the bottom vehicles in the line-up of luxury brands as well as the top-of-the-line models of some non-luxury brands.<sup id="cite_ref-consumerguideauto.howstuffworks.com_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-consumerguideauto.howstuffworks.com-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Dimensionally, compact executive cars are smaller than <a href="/wiki/Mid-size_car" title="Mid-size car">Mid-size cars</a> and sometimes even smaller than <a href="/wiki/Compact_cars" title="Compact cars" class="mw-redirect">Compact cars</a><a href="/wiki/Small_family_cars" title="Small family cars" class="mw-redirect">Small family cars</a>. Mass market compact cars typically use the economical <a href="/wiki/Front-engine,_front-wheel_drive_layout" title="Front-engine, front-wheel drive layout" class="mw-redirect">front wheel drive</a> <a href="/wiki/Transverse_engine" title="Transverse engine">transverse engine</a> <a href="/wiki/Automobile_layout" title="Automobile layout">layout</a>, well suited to the inline-4 engine, which also maximizes interior room.<sup id="cite_ref-rearwheeldrive.org_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rearwheeldrive.org-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> Several compact executive cars are <a href="/wiki/Front-engine,_rear-wheel_drive_layout" title="Front-engine, rear-wheel drive layout" class="mw-redirect">rear-wheel drive</a> with <a href="/wiki/Longitudinal_engine" title="Longitudinal engine">longitudinal engines</a>, for improved stability and handling,<sup id="cite_ref-rearwheeldrive.org_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rearwheeldrive.org-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> and in order to accommodate the larger size of higher-performance engines (straight-6, V6, rarely V8), with <a href="/wiki/Four-wheel_drive" title="Four-wheel drive">four-wheel drive</a> often being available.<sup id="cite_ref-howstuffworks1_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-howstuffworks1-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> Compact executive cars also tend have more complicated <a href="/wiki/Independent_suspension" title="Independent suspension">independent suspensions</a>, sportier transmissions, and <a href="/wiki/Redline" title="Redline">high revolution engines</a> that may require premium gasoline. The more complex powertrain and mechanical layouts of compact executive cars comes at increased cost and reduced interior passenger and trunk space.<sup id="cite_ref-howstuffworks1_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-howstuffworks1-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Compact executive cars include the <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_159" title="Alfa Romeo 159">Alfa Romeo 159</a>, <a href="/wiki/Volvo_S60" title="Volvo S60">Volvo S60</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lexus_IS" title="Lexus IS">Lexus IS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_G" title="Infiniti G">Infiniti G</a>/<a href="/wiki/Infiniti_Q50" title="Infiniti Q50">Q50</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW_3_Series" title="BMW 3 Series">BMW 3 Series</a>, <a href="/wiki/Audi_A4" title="Audi A4">Audi A4</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cadillac_ATS" title="Cadillac ATS">Cadillac ATS</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_C-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz C-Class">Mercedes-Benz C-Class</a> which particularly emphasize <a href="/wiki/Sports_sedan" title="Sports sedan">sporty handling</a>. In the United States and Canada these vehicles are known as entry-level luxury cars.<sup id="cite_ref-Edmunds.com_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Edmunds.com-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CanadianDriver_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CanadianDriver-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The American and Canadian classification of entry-level luxury includes is a broad category which includes not only compact executive cars, but also midsize cars such as the <a href="/wiki/Lexus_ES" title="Lexus ES">Lexus ES</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acura_TL" title="Acura TL">Acura TL</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buick_LaCrosse" title="Buick LaCrosse">Buick LaCrosse</a>, being similar in price to the BMW 3 Series and Mercedes-Benz C-Class.<sup id="cite_ref-caranddriver1_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-caranddriver1-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> However, the ES, TL, and LaCrosse do not fall under the European luxury classification of compact executive car nor are they sold in Europe. The ES and TL actually began as <a href="/wiki/Badge_engineered" title="Badge engineered" class="mw-redirect">badge engineered</a> versions of the mid-size <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Camry" title="Toyota Camry">Toyota Camry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Honda_Accord" title="Honda Accord">Honda Accord</a>, respectively, retaining the <a href="/wiki/Front-engine,_front-wheel_drive_layout" title="Front-engine, front-wheel drive layout" class="mw-redirect">front wheel drive</a> <a href="/wiki/Transverse_engine" title="Transverse engine">transverse engine</a> <a href="/wiki/Automobile_layout" title="Automobile layout">layout</a>, whereas Toyota's current <a href="/wiki/Lexus_IS" title="Lexus IS">Lexus IS</a> does not share a <a href="/wiki/Automobile_platform" title="Automobile platform">platform</a> with mass market Toyota cars. The ES and TL are not considered <a href="/wiki/Executive_car" title="Executive car">executive cars</a> either (that slot is filled by the <a href="/wiki/Lexus_GS" title="Lexus GS">Lexus GS</a> and <a href="/wiki/Acura_RL" title="Acura RL">Acura RL</a>, respectively), despite similar dimensions, since executive cars usually emphasize higher performance, often being <a href="/wiki/Front-engine,_rear-wheel_drive_layout" title="Front-engine, rear-wheel drive layout" class="mw-redirect">rear-wheel drive</a> with <a href="/wiki/Longitudinal_engine" title="Longitudinal engine">longitudinal engines</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Edmunds.com_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Edmunds.com-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CanadianDriver_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CanadianDriver-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup> The present iterations of the ES and LaCrosse emphasize "comfortable, reasonably priced luxury",<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup> while the TL has recently been targeted at the <a href="/wiki/Sports_sedan" title="Sports sedan">entry-level luxury sport market</a> due to its SH-AWD.<sup id="cite_ref-caranddriver1_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-caranddriver1-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Recently, the entry-level luxury has been very competitive, and there has been price-overlapping with well-equipped non-luxury cars.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ottawacitizen1_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ottawacitizen1-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> The bestselling vehicles of the marque are frequently compact executive cars, such as the BMW 3 Series that makes up 40% of the vehicles that BMW sells worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cars.com_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cars.com-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mid-luxury.2Fexecutive_cars">Mid-luxury/executive cars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mid-luxury/executive cars">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mid-luxury/executive cars" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The mid-luxury segment is commonly referred to as <a href="/wiki/Executive_car" title="Executive car">executive cars</a> in Britain, <i>Obere Mittelklasse</i> in German, and <i>Grandes Routières</i> in French.<sup id="cite_ref-grandes_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandes-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-obere_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obere-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup> Examples of models in this category include <a href="/wiki/Acura_RL" title="Acura RL">Acura RL</a>, <a href="/wiki/Audi_A6" title="Audi A6">Audi A6</a>, <a href="/wiki/Volvo_S80" title="Volvo S80">Volvo S80</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW_5_Series" title="BMW 5 Series">BMW 5 Series</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_E-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz E-Class">Mercedes-Benz E-Class</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_XF" title="Jaguar XF">Jaguar XF</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cadillac_CTS" title="Cadillac CTS">Cadillac CTS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lexus_GS" title="Lexus GS">Lexus GS</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_M" title="Infiniti M">Infiniti M</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-high_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-high-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Although having similar dimensions to <a href="/wiki/Mid-size_car" title="Mid-size car">mid-size cars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Large_family_car" title="Large family car" class="mw-redirect">large family cars</a>, executive cars are engineered and positioned as premium vehicles with better performance and technology amenities. There are also higher development and production costs, as many well-known mid-luxury cars use the longitudinal (usually front engine rear wheel drive layout) engine mounting which affords space for powerful engines such as a V8, instead of the more economical <a href="/wiki/Automobile_layout#Characteristics" title="Automobile layout">transverse engine layout</a> of mass market cars which are restricted to inline-4 or V6. Executive cars usually have lower sales and production volumes compared to <a href="/wiki/Mid-size_car" title="Mid-size car">mid-size cars</a>, as base trims with less equipment and smaller engines are not sold in U.S. and Canadian markets.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup> Also due to the problem of steep depreciation, especially concerning cars from less prestigious brands, most executive cars are produced by marques that specialize in larger/more expensive vehicles. Particularly in the United States, the mid-luxury segment is dominated by <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a> and <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a>, as executive cars from lesser-known manufacturers such as <a href="/wiki/Citro%C3%ABn" title="Citroën">Citroën</a> and <a href="/wiki/Infiniti" title="Infiniti">Infiniti</a> have not had much success.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Vehicles in this segment include the mid-range models of several luxury car manufacturers.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> There are also some <a href="/wiki/Full-size_cars" title="Full-size cars" class="mw-redirect">full-size cars</a> in this segment, such as the <a href="/wiki/Kia_Cadenza" title="Kia Cadenza">Kia Cadenza</a> and <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_M" title="Infiniti M">Infiniti M</a>. Executive cars such as the BMW 5 Series are crucial to a luxury automaker's bottom line, and although not the highest-selling model, they generate a significant amount of profits due to the lucrative technology options.<sup id="cite_ref-cars.com_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cars.com-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="High-end_luxury.2Ffull-size_luxury_cars">High-end luxury/full-size luxury cars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: High-end luxury/full-size luxury cars">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: High-end luxury/full-size luxury cars" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Also known as <a href="/wiki/Car_classification#Full-size_luxury_car.2FGrand_saloon" title="Car classification">full-size luxury cars, grand saloons</a>, or premium large cars, while "Oberklasse" is used in Germany. Many of these are the marque's showcases for the newest automotive technology.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> Several nameplates also offer long-wheelbase versions that offer additional rear legroom and amenities.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> Full-size luxury cars sold in Canada and the U.S. typically have mid-displacement V8 engines, though recently some marques have offered six cylinder powerplants but without much success.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nytimes1_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes1-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nationalpost1_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nationalpost1-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Vehicles in this category include some of the models from the flagship lines of luxury car brands. Examples include the <a href="/wiki/Porsche_Panamera" title="Porsche Panamera">Porsche Panamera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Audi_A8" title="Audi A8">Audi A8</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW_7_Series" title="BMW 7 Series">BMW 7 Series</a>, <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_Q45" title="Infiniti Q45">Infiniti Q45</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_XJ" title="Jaguar XJ">Jaguar XJ</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lexus_LS" title="Lexus LS">Lexus LS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_S-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz S-Class">Mercedes-Benz S-Class</a>, <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Phaeton" title="Volkswagen Phaeton">Volkswagen Phaeton</a>, and in the US, the <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Town_Car" title="Lincoln Town Car">Lincoln Town Car</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_300C" title="Chrysler 300C">Chrysler 300C</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cadillac_XTS" title="Cadillac XTS">Cadillac XTS</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-howstuffworks1_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-howstuffworks1-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-high_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-high-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> in 2004, the bestselling full-size luxury nameplates were the <a href="/wiki/Lexus_LS" title="Lexus LS">Lexus LS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_S-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz S-Class">Mercedes-Benz S-Class</a>, and <a href="/wiki/BMW_7_Series" title="BMW 7 Series">BMW 7 Series</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ultra-luxury_cars">Ultra-luxury cars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ultra-luxury cars">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ultra-luxury cars" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>R.L. Polk and Company, a global automotive information and marketing firm that provides services<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup> to automotive and related industries, has defined the term "super luxury" segment for luxury cars costing more than <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$100,000</span>.</p>
<p>This bracket includes the entire lineup of <a href="/wiki/Rolls-Royce_(car)" title="Rolls-Royce (car)" class="mw-redirect">Rolls Royce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bentley" title="Bentley">Bentley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maybach" title="Maybach">Maybach</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Maserati" title="Maserati">Maserati</a>, as these marques do not offer lower-priced offerings in the entry-level luxury or mid-luxury segments like more mainstream luxury brands like <a href="/wiki/Audi" title="Audi">Audi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Porsche" title="Porsche">Porsche</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-editorial.autos.msn.com_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-editorial.autos.msn.com-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup> As of late 2011, super luxury automakers have been "pledging higher sales and offering ever sexier models" despite the economic uncertainty.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Rolls Royce and Bentley have attempted to move downmarket somewhat with offerings priced considerably lower than their traditional offerings (<a href="/wiki/Rolls-Royce_Phantom_(disambiguation)" title="Rolls-Royce Phantom (disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect">Rolls Royce Phantom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bentley_Mulsanne_2011" title="Bentley Mulsanne 2011" class="mw-redirect">Bentley Mulsanne</a>) such as the <a href="/wiki/Rolls_Royce_Ghost" title="Rolls Royce Ghost" class="mw-redirect">Rolls Royce Ghost</a><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Bentley_Continental_GT" title="Bentley Continental GT">Bentley Continental GT</a>, creating what <i><a href="/wiki/Car_and_Driver" title="Car and Driver">Car and Driver</a></i> describes as the "entry-opulent segment".<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57"><span>[</span>57<span>]</span></a></sup> Bentley, Maybach, and Rolls-Royce vehicles share platforms and engines with other luxury brands from their parent auto company, however <a href="/wiki/Rolls-Royce_Motor_Cars" title="Rolls-Royce Motor Cars">Rolls-Royce</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bentley" title="Bentley">Bentley</a> are assembled in England (separate from the rest of <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Group" title="Volkswagen Group">Volkswagen Group</a>'s production plants) and this "exclusivity" has made these British marques a sales success. By comparison, Maybachs are built alongside the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_S-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz S-Class">Mercedes-Benz S-Class</a> flagship sedan which has explained why it has not fared well in the market, furthermore the Maybach's pedigree was virtually unknown outside of Germany unlike its British rivals which have long enjoyed renown worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58"><span>[</span>58<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59"><span>[</span>59<span>]</span></a></sup> A Bentley executive was quoted as saying "that the brand’s exclusivity, history and obsessive luxury help to convince customers that a Bentley is worth the price" which is at least twice that of a flagship luxury car from BMW or Mercedes-Benz.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60"><span>[</span>60<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The top-performing variants of flagship cars from <a href="/wiki/Audi" title="Audi">Audi</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a> are often included here as well. They often have 12-cylinder (usually <a href="/wiki/V12_engine" title="V12 engine">V12</a>) engines for great refinement and smoothness,<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup> even though contemporary V8 engines can make similar output while being less expensive.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62"><span>[</span>62<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup> While BMW sells far fewer V12-engined 7-Series vehicles than V8 versions, the V12 retains popularity in the US, China, and Russia, as well as maintaining the marque's prestige in the luxury vehicle market segment.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64"><span>[</span>64<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65"><span>[</span>65<span>]</span></a></sup> These trims also feature standard luxury and technology features that would be considered optional on their V8 siblings.<sup id="cite_ref-nationalpost1_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nationalpost1-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-editorial.autos.msn.com_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-editorial.autos.msn.com-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66"><span>[</span>66<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>There are ultra-high performance cars from "exotic brands" that also exceed <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$100,000</span>, but would not necessarily be categorized as luxury automobiles, such as the sports cars from <a href="/wiki/Ferrari" title="Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lamborghini" title="Lamborghini">Lamborghini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Porsche" title="Porsche">Porsche</a>, although the <a href="/wiki/Porsche_Panamera" title="Porsche Panamera">Porsche Panamera</a> does compete in the high-end luxury/full-size luxury category.<sup id="cite_ref-editorial.autos.msn.com_54-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-editorial.autos.msn.com-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67"><span>[</span>67<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Luxury_SUV.2FCrossover">Luxury SUV/Crossover</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Luxury SUV/Crossover">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Luxury SUV/Crossover" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This category was created with <a href="/wiki/Kaiser_Jeep" title="Kaiser Jeep">Kaiser Jeep</a>'s 1966 <a href="/wiki/Jeep_Wagoneer" title="Jeep Wagoneer">Super Wagoneer</a> as the first true luxury <a href="/wiki/Four-wheel_drive" title="Four-wheel drive">four-wheel drive</a> (4x4) vehicle.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68"><span>[</span>68<span>]</span></a></sup> It was the first SUV to offer a <a href="/wiki/V8_engine" title="V8 engine">V8 engine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Automatic_transmission" title="Automatic transmission">automatic transmission</a>, and luxury car trim and equipment in a serious off-road model.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69"><span>[</span>69<span>]</span></a></sup> The Super Wagoneer was "a pioneer that blazed a trail for today's luxury SUVs".<sup id="cite_ref-stuff66_70-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stuff66-70"><span>[</span>70<span>]</span></a></sup> It came with long list of standard equipment that included <a href="/wiki/Bucket_seating" title="Bucket seating" class="mw-redirect">bucket seating</a> with center console, <a href="/wiki/Air_conditioning" title="Air conditioning">air conditioning</a>, seven-position tilt steering wheel, a <a href="/wiki/Vinyl_roof" title="Vinyl roof">vinyl roof</a>, as well as "Antique Gold" trim panels on the body sides and tailgate.<sup id="cite_ref-stuff66_70-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stuff66-70"><span>[</span>70<span>]</span></a></sup> After <a href="/wiki/American_Motors" title="American Motors">American Motors Corporation</a> (AMC) purchased Jeep, the vehicles were upgraded and refined, including features such as an optional electric sliding steel <a href="/wiki/Sun_roof" title="Sun roof" class="mw-redirect">sun roof</a>, "possibly the first offered on an SUV".<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71"><span>[</span>71<span>]</span></a></sup> The late-1970s Jeep Wagoneer Limited "set the sport-utility market on its ear ... was the most luxurious four-wheeler anyone had ever seen."<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72"><span>[</span>72<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The venerable Grand Wagoneer was still the leader in the luxury SUV market in 1986, and continued to be produced virtually unchanged through 1991, long after Chrysler bought out American Motors Corporation (AMC) in 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73"><span>[</span>73<span>]</span></a></sup> The SUV market soon expanded with new entrants. By the mid-1990s, the U.S. Big Three automakers, "especially with Ford's Explorer and Jeep's Grand Cherokee, dominate[d] the market for the red-hot sport/utilities."<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74"><span>[</span>74<span>]</span></a></sup> The fastest growing sector of this market was for the so-called luxury SUVs, which included the Jeep Grand Cherokee ... the Grand Cherokee's allure: "This vehicle is proof you can have a true off-road vehicle without giving up luxuries and amenities" with the Jeep providing a crucial new intangible factor for buyers — image.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75"><span>[</span>75<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The SUV models generated higher-profit-margins than ordinary automobiles, and automakers introduced new luxury models during the late 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-Frankel_2006_35_76-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Frankel_2006_35-76"><span>[</span>76<span>]</span></a></sup> For some manufacturers such as Porsche and BMW, luxury SUVs were the first SUV models they produced. Luxury SUVs catered particularly to the U.S. market where station wagons were unpopular, often being produced in North America (such as <a href="/wiki/BMW_Spartanburg" title="BMW Spartanburg" class="mw-redirect">BMW Spartanburg</a>) instead of the luxury marque's home country. Some of these models were not traditional SUVs based on light trucks, rather they are classified as <a href="/wiki/Crossover_(automobile)" title="Crossover (automobile)">crossovers</a> using unibody constructions. SUVs from non-luxury brands had experienced a surge in popularity through early 2000s, causing the traditional luxury marques to follow.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77"><span>[</span>77<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (December 2011)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>SUVs from the luxury marques grew at almost 40 percent to more than 430,000 vehicles, excluding SUV-only brands like <a href="/wiki/Hummer" title="Hummer">Hummer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Land_Rover" title="Land Rover">Land Rover</a>, while luxury car sales in the U.S. during 2003 suffered a 1% decline, and non-luxury SUV sales were flat. By 2004, 30 percent of major luxury brands' U.S. sales are now SUVs. Luxury brands in particular led the development of <a href="/wiki/Crossover_(automobile)" title="Crossover (automobile)">crossover SUVs</a> (as opposed to <a href="/wiki/Body-on-frame" title="Body-on-frame">body-on-frame</a> SUVs), making it one of the fastest growing segments in the market, as the forecast for 2002 was approximately 240,000 vehicles and that could double by 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-findarticles1_78-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-findarticles1-78"><span>[</span>78<span>]</span></a></sup> Research data showed luxury SUV buyers are compared those vehicles to SUVs of mass market brands, and not shopping around luxury cars, thus the SUV is becoming the key to bringing new customers to the luxury dealerships.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79"><span>[</span>79<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Certain luxury SUVs use <a href="/wiki/Body-on-frame" title="Body-on-frame">body-on-frame</a> underpinnings, often being <a href="/wiki/Badge_engineering" title="Badge engineering">badge-engineered versions</a> of their non-luxury counterparts, and retaining the rugged off-road and towing capabilities. Examples include the <a href="/wiki/Lexus_LX" title="Lexus LX">Lexus LX</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Navigator" title="Lincoln Navigator">Lincoln Navigator</a>, which are the premium versions of the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Land_Cruiser" title="Toyota Land Cruiser">Toyota Land Cruiser</a> (itself a luxurious SUV in the United States) and <a href="/wiki/Ford_Expedition" title="Ford Expedition">Ford Expedition</a>, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80"><span>[</span>80<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Other luxury SUVs are <a href="/wiki/Crossover_(automobile)" title="Crossover (automobile)">crossovers</a> using <a href="/wiki/Unibody" title="Unibody" class="mw-redirect">unibody</a> construction, often sharing the platform with compact executive and executive cars. For example, the <a href="/wiki/Infiniti_FX" title="Infiniti FX">Infiniti FX</a> is based upon the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_FM_platform" title="Nissan FM platform">Nissan FM platform</a> that also underpins other Infiniti cars. <a href="/wiki/Audi" title="Audi">Audi</a> and <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a> developed crossovers to compete in the SUV segment as they did not have an existing <a href="/wiki/Body-on-frame" title="Body-on-frame">body-on-frame</a> vehicle in their lineup.<sup id="cite_ref-findarticles1_78-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-findarticles1-78"><span>[</span>78<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81"><span>[</span>81<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Lexus_RX" title="Lexus RX">Lexus RX</a> was the earliest luxury crossover on the market, and it has since been the best-selling luxury vehicle in the US, so it has inspired similar competitors from rival marques.<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82"><span>[</span>82<span>]</span></a></sup> While early luxury crossovers released in the late 1990s have resembled traditional boxy SUVs, recent offerings have prioritized <a href="/wiki/Sport_sedan" title="Sport sedan" class="mw-redirect">sportiness</a> over utility — such as the Infiniti FX and <a href="/wiki/BMW_X6" title="BMW X6">BMW X6</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-edmunds_2010_FX35_83-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-edmunds_2010_FX35-83"><span>[</span>83<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cars.com_2009_FX35_84-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cars.com_2009_FX35-84"><span>[</span>84<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85"><span>[</span>85<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>Luxury cars tend to offer a higher degree of comfort than their mainstream counterparts, common amenities include genuine leather upholstery and polished "woodgrain-look" dashboards.<sup id="cite_ref-high_42-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-high-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> Compared to mainstream vehicles, luxury cars have traditionally emphasized comfort and safety.<sup id="cite_ref-Lienert.2C_D._.2811_May_2007.29._Safest_Cars_2007._.27.27Forbes.27.27._86-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lienert.2C_D._.2811_May_2007.29._Safest_Cars_2007._.27.27Forbes.27.27.-86"><span>[</span>86<span>]</span></a></sup> Luxury vehicles are also a status symbol for <a href="/wiki/Conspicuous_consumption" title="Conspicuous consumption">conspicuous consumption</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-forbes1_87-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes1-87"><span>[</span>87<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Contemporary luxury cars also offer higher performance and better handling, which is often known as <a href="/wiki/Sports_sedan" title="Sports sedan">sport luxury</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88"><span>[</span>88<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89"><span>[</span>89<span>]</span></a></sup> However in Europe, where large-displacement engines are often heavily taxed and many luxury buyers shy away from conspicuous consumption, brands offer buyers the option of removing exterior engine-identifying badges.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes1_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes1-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Forbes noted that the reputation of luxury marques enables them to continually introduce many new safety technologies and comfort amenities, such as <a href="/wiki/Anti-lock_braking_system" title="Anti-lock braking system">anti-lock brakes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electronic_stability_control" title="Electronic stability control">electronic stability control</a> and <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> entertainment systems, before they trickle down to mass market cars. Numerous "<a href="/wiki/Intelligent_car" title="Intelligent car" class="mw-redirect">smart car</a>" features are largely only found on luxury cars as of 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-forbes1_87-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes1-87"><span>[</span>87<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Front-engine,_rear-wheel_drive_layout" title="Front-engine, rear-wheel drive layout" class="mw-redirect">rear-wheel drive</a> with <a href="/wiki/Longitudinal_engine" title="Longitudinal engine">longitudinal engines</a> (FR) is a common layout of luxury cars. European marques like <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Cars" title="Jaguar Cars">Jaguar</a> have almost never adopted <a href="/wiki/Automobile_layout#Front-wheel_drive_layouts" title="Automobile layout">front-wheel drive</a> and retained a lineup mostly or entirely made up of FR cars. Japanese brands such as <a href="/wiki/Lexus" title="Lexus">Lexus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Infiniti" title="Infiniti">Infiniti</a> also have predominantly FR lineups. The FR layout, while more expensive than the FF, has been retained by these luxury manufacturers as it allows for higher performance engines (particularly the <a href="/wiki/Straight-six_engine" title="Straight-six engine">straight-6</a>, <a href="/wiki/V8_engine" title="V8 engine">V8</a>, and other engine configurations with more cylinders), better handling, and a smoother ride. <sup id="cite_ref-nytimes1_50-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes1-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90"><span>[</span>90<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91"><span>[</span>91<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92"><span>[</span>92<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>American manufacturers also largely followed the FR for their luxury brands (as well as their mass-market cars of the time). However, due to the <a href="/wiki/Arab_Oil_Embargo" title="Arab Oil Embargo" class="mw-redirect">Arab Oil Embargo</a> of 1973 and the 1979 fuel crises, began eliminating their FR platforms in favor of the more economical <a href="/wiki/Front-engine,_front-wheel_drive_layout" title="Front-engine, front-wheel drive layout" class="mw-redirect">front wheel drive</a> <a href="/wiki/Transverse_engine" title="Transverse engine">transverse engine</a> <a href="/wiki/Automobile_layout" title="Automobile layout">layout</a> (FF). Chrysler went 100% FF by 1990 and <a href="/wiki/General_Motors" title="General Motors">GM</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Cadillac" title="Cadillac">Cadillac</a> and <a href="/wiki/Buick" title="Buick">Buick</a> brands for the US were entirely FF by 1997. One of the few notable holdouts was Ford's <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Town_Car" title="Lincoln Town Car">Lincoln Town Car</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_LS" title="Lincoln LS">Lincoln LS</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93"><span>[</span>93<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the 21st century, as part of the revamp of its design and image,<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes.com_94-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes.com-94"><span>[</span>94<span>]</span></a></sup> Cadillac returned most of its lineup (sedans, roadsters, crossovers and SUVs) to rear- or all-wheel-drive, the only exceptions being the front-wheel drive <a href="/wiki/Cadillac_BLS" title="Cadillac BLS">Cadillac BLS</a> (which is not sold in North America)<sup id="cite_ref-Car_And_Driver_Article_On_Cadillac_BLS_Debut_95-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Car_And_Driver_Article_On_Cadillac_BLS_Debut-95"><span>[</span>95<span>]</span></a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Cadillac_DTS" title="Cadillac DTS">Cadillac DTS</a>. <a href="/wiki/Chrysler" title="Chrysler">Chrysler</a> returned its <a href="/wiki/Full-size" title="Full-size" class="mw-redirect">full-size</a> cars to this layout with the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_300" title="Chrysler 300">Chrysler 300</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-slate1_96-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-slate1-96"><span>[</span>96<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-leftlanenews1_97-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-leftlanenews1-97"><span>[</span>97<span>]</span></a></sup> Ford's Lincoln retained the longtime FR platform for the Town Car, intended for use as a <a href="/wiki/Limousine" title="Limousine">limousine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chauffeur" title="Chauffeur">chauffeured</a> car.<sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98"><span>[</span>98<span>]</span></a></sup> However, newer offerings such as the <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_MKZ" title="Lincoln MKZ">MKZ</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_MKS" title="Lincoln MKS">MKS</a> use newer FF platforms shared with mainstream Ford vehicles, with all-wheel drive as an option.<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99"><span>[</span>99<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_sales">History and sales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: History and sales">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxury_vehicle&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: History and sales" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:1933_Rolls-Royce_Phantom_II_Continental.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/1933_Rolls-Royce_Phantom_II_Continental.jpg/220px-1933_Rolls-Royce_Phantom_II_Continental.jpg" width="220" height="120" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/1933_Rolls-Royce_Phantom_II_Continental.jpg/330px-1933_Rolls-Royce_Phantom_II_Continental.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/1933_Rolls-Royce_Phantom_II_Continental.jpg/440px-1933_Rolls-Royce_Phantom_II_Continental.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:1933_Rolls-Royce_Phantom_II_Continental.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.22wmf13/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Rolls-Royce Phantom II Continental was a British luxury car</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the United States luxury market, Cadillac and Lincoln had long been the best-selling and second best-selling luxury brands until 1998, when they were overtaken by Japanese and German brands.<sup id="cite_ref-100" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-100"><span>[</span>100<span>]</span></a></sup> Since the 2000s, with the <a href="/wiki/Cadillac_CTS" title="Cadillac CTS">Cadillac CTS</a>, the marque has seen a resurgence in sales and brand value.<sup id="cite_ref-101" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-101"><span>[</span>101<span>]</span></a></sup> Ford's Lincoln, which had seen sales fall as a result of an aging lineup, has attempted to return that luxury marque to competitiveness, by releasing new models such as the <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_MKS" title="Lincoln MKS">Lincoln MKS</a>, as well as divesting itself of its other <a href="/wiki/Premier_Automotive_Group" title="Premier Automotive Group">Premier Automotive Group</a> brands.<sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102"><span>[</span>102<span>]</span></a></sup> Mercedes-Benz and BMW were the top-selling luxury import until 1991, when they were overtaken by Lexus.<sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103"><span>[</span>103<span>]</span></a></sup> Since 2000, Lexus has been the number-one-selling luxury car marque in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-2008luxe_104-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2008luxe-104"><span>[</span>104<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sales_105-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sales-105"><span>[</span>105<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Since the 1980s, a host of new manufacturers have entered the luxury market to challenge the traditional players.<sup id="cite_ref-eisenstein_106-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eisenstein-106"><span>[</span>106<span>]</span></a></sup> The three major Japanese auto manufacturers, <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toyota" title="Toyota">Toyota</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nissan" title="Nissan" class="mw-redirect">Nissan</a>, created their respective luxury brands particularly for the US. As a result of <a href="/wiki/Voluntary_Export_Restraints" title="Voluntary Export Restraints" class="mw-redirect">voluntary export restraints</a> imposed in 1981, these manufacturers were limited to a number of vehicles they could export. While these companies sidestepped this by establishing US production facilities for mass market vehicles, their home factories soon begun producing higher-priced cars as they carried a greater profit margin per car. <a href="/wiki/Acura" title="Acura">Acura</a> was launched in 1986, while <a href="/wiki/Lexus" title="Lexus">Lexus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Infiniti" title="Infiniti">Infiniti</a> were unveiled in 1989. By 1992, these three divisions had sales of over <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$3.5 billion</span>, using lower prices and innovation to take market share from both domestic (Cadillac, Lincoln) and the European (Mercedes, Volvo, BMW, Audi and Jaguar) luxury car makers.<sup id="cite_ref-107" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-107"><span>[</span>107<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hyundai" title="Hyundai">Hyundai</a> had recently released the <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Genesis" title="Hyundai Genesis">Genesis</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Equus" title="Hyundai Equus">Equus</a>, hoping to repeat the same strategy of undercutting their established competitors.<sup id="cite_ref-eisenstein_106-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eisenstein-106"><span>[</span>106<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Late-2000s_recession" title="Late-2000s recession" class="mw-redirect">Late-2000s recession</a> was the first time since the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a> of the 1930s that the luxury car market suffered considerably, something not seen in previous economic downturns. Many such customers saw their net worth decline following the collapse in <a href="/wiki/Financial_crisis_of_2007%E2%80%932010" title="Financial crisis of 2007–2010" class="mw-redirect">financial markets and real-estate values</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-forbes1_87-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes1-87"><span>[</span>87<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-108"><span>[</span>108<span>]</span></a></sup> For instance, some of the steepest dropoffs came at the high end, including the <a href="/wiki/BMW_7_Series" title="BMW 7 Series">BMW 7 Series</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rolls-Royce_Phantom_(2003)" title="Rolls-Royce Phantom (2003)">Rolls Royce Phantom</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a> unexpectedly dropped the starting price of its all-new <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_W212" title="Mercedes-Benz W212">2010 E-Class</a>. The unusually sharp decline in luxury car sales have led observers to believe that there is a fundamental shift and reshaping of the luxury automotive market, with one industry official suggesting that the marques no longer command the premiums that they used to, and another saying that conspicuous consumption was no longer attractive in poor economic conditions. <sup id="cite_ref-eisenstein_106-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eisenstein-106"><span>[</span>106<span>]</span></a></sup> Additionally, mainstream brands have been able to offer amenities and devices such as leather, wood, and anti-lock brakes, previously found only on luxury cars, as the costs decline.<sup id="cite_ref-eisenstein_106-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eisenstein-106"><span>[</span>106<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>However, luxury vehicle sales have not collapsed as much as their non-luxury counterparts.<sup id="cite_ref-109" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-109"><span>[</span>109<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-110" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-110"><span>[</span>110<span>]</span></a></sup> Luxury vehicle marques generally benefited from financially healthier dealerships, better leasing and certified pre-owned programs and loyal customers, so sales are expected to rebound more quickly than mass market cars. <sup id="cite_ref-forbes1_87-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes1-87"><span>[</span>87<span>]</span></a></sup> Others note that there is growing interest in luxury vehicles from emerging markets such as China and Russia.<sup id="cite_ref-eisenstein_106-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eisenstein-106"><span>[</span>106<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The entry-level luxury segment has been very competitive, and there has been price-overlapping with well-equipped non-luxury cars.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_111-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-111"><span>[</span>111<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-112" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-112"><span>[</span>112<span>]</span></a></sup> For instance, in Canada, several luxury manufacturers set sales records in August 2009, due mostly to aggressive incentives on entry-level luxury vehicles.<sup id="cite_ref-ottawacitizen1_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ottawacitizen1-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-113" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-113"><span>[</span>113<span>]</span></a></sup> In September 2009, BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Lexus and Audi all saw their Canadian sales increase by more than 10 per cent compared to a year earlier, despite overall Canadian auto sales being down 3.5 per cent compared to September 2008. The head of Mercedes-Benz Canada suggested that the brand "has been able to attract 'middle-class' consumers even during the recession because of the sense that owning a Mercedes comes with 'membership in a club'." BMW Canada's chief said luxury cars continued to be attractive, "I think due to new product offensives and due to new design and due to the fact that we are the benchmark in all areas when it comes to fuel efficiency... that together stimulates a lot of the market".<sup id="cite_ref-114" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-114"><span>[</span>114<span>]</span></a></sup> BMW has managed to remain profitable in 2009 while other competitors were posting losses, by scaling down production quickly to avoid cash burn through bloated inventories.<sup id="cite_ref-online.wsj.com_115-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-online.wsj.com-115"><span>[</span>115<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a> was the best-selling luxury vehicle manufacturer by sales, ahead of rivals <a href="/wiki/Audi_AG" title="Audi AG" class="mw-redirect">Audi AG</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daimler_AG" title="Daimler AG">Daimler AG</a>'s Mercedes-Benz marque. <sup id="cite_ref-online.wsj.com_115-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-online.wsj.com-115"><span>[</span>115<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-116" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-116"><span>[</span>116<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
