<p>You could consider the 2013 Honda Civic a knee-jerk reaction of sorts. From its CEO on down, Honda was clearly stung by the criticism it received when its all-new 2012 Honda Civic lineup debuted, s<a title="What the 2013 Honda Civic needs" href="http://wot.motortrend.com/what-the-2013-honda-civic-needs-to-regain-a-compact-segment-lead-279711.html" target="_blank">o much so that it&#8217;s introducing a suite of changes to the 2013 Civic</a>, which will make its debut at the 2012 L.A. auto show later this month. Until then, Honda is teasing us with the first official photos of the 2013 Civic sedan.</p>
<p><a href="http://wot.motortrend.com/?attachment_id=290057" rel="attachment wp-att-290057"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-290057" title="2013 Honda Civic EX L Navi Sedan front clip 300x215 photo" src="http://stwot.motortrend.com/files/2012/11/2013-Honda-Civic-EX-L-Navi-Sedan-front-clip-300x215.jpg" alt="2013 Honda Civic EX L Navi Sedan front clip 300x215 image" width="300" height="215" /></a>The 2012 Civic took a lot of flak for its exterior design, and from the looks of the photos, the updated Civic is adopting a number of <a title="styling cues from the 2013 Honda Accord" href="http://wot.motortrend.com/refreshing-or-revolting-2013-honda-accord-245369.html/0/" target="_blank">styling cues similar to that of the all-new Honda Accord</a>. The 2013 Honda Civic arguably looks sportier than before, thanks in part to a black honeycomb mesh grille and a redesigned lower bumper with chrome trim. Headlights have been updated, and higher-end trims get integrated fog lights. The Civic&#8217;s hood has also been retouched for a more aggressive look, and out back, jewel-like taillights flank an elongated chrome trim piece on the trunk lid. The rear bumper has also been treated with a new lower diffuser panel with a honeycomb mesh vent.</p>
<p>Although photos and details about the interior of the 2013 Honda Civic aren&#8217;t available as of yet, we expect more<a target="_blank" title=" Accord" href=" http://www.motortrend.com/new_cars/04/honda/accord/"> Accord</a>-like flourishes to trickle down to its smaller stablemate. Don’t be surprised to see more standard features such as Bluetooth and USB ports, along with a more driver-centric center stack.</p>
<p>Despite the backlash centered around 2012 Civic, Honda&#8217;s bread-and-butter compact continues to finish at or near the top of the segment in sales month-after-month. <a title="Through October, 254,716 examples have been sold" href="http://wot.motortrend.com/october-compact-sales-toyota-corolla-back-on-top-chevrolet-cruze-slips-to-third-285605.html" target="_blank">Through October, 254,716 examples have been sold</a>, making it the best-selling compact so far this year in the U.S.</p>
