						<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en"><span dir="auto">Antique car</span></h1>

<p>An <b>antique car</b>, in the general sense of the term, is an <a href="/wiki/Automobile" title="Automobile">automobile</a> that is an <a href="/wiki/Antique" title="Antique">antique</a>. Narrower definitions vary based on how old a car must be to qualify. In the United States, an antique car is generally defined as a car over 45 years of age, this being the definition used by the Antique Automobile Club of America. However, the legal definitions for the purpose of <a href="/wiki/Antique_vehicle_registration" title="Antique vehicle registration">antique vehicle registration</a> vary widely. The antique car era includes the <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_automobile#Veteran_era" title="History of the automobile">Veteran era</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Brass_Era_car" title="Brass Era car">Brass era</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Vintage_car" title="Vintage car">Vintage era</a>, which range from the beginning of the automobile up to the 1930s. Later cars are often described as <a href="/wiki/Classic_car" title="Classic car">classic cars</a>. In original or originally restored condition antiques are very valuable and are usually either protected and stored or exhibited in car shows but are very rarely driven.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_automobile" title="History of the automobile">History of the automobile</a></div>
<p>The <b>Veteran car</b> era, <b>Brass car</b> era, and the <b>Vintage car</b> era, are part of the <b>Antique car</b> classification as all automobiles produced prior to <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> are considered to be antiques.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Europe">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Europe" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> Eve in 1801, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Trevithick" title="Richard Trevithick">Richard Trevithick</a> of England built and ran a steam-powered carriage, called the <a href="/wiki/Puffing_Devil" title="Puffing Devil" class="mw-redirect">Puffing Devil</a>, that is considered the first horseless carriage but <a href="/wiki/Nicolas-Joseph_Cugnot" title="Nicolas-Joseph Cugnot">Nicolas-Joseph Cugnot</a> has the claim of the first steam powered vehicle with the <i><b>fardier à vapeur</b></i> in 1770. The first production of automobiles was by <a href="/wiki/Karl_Benz" title="Karl Benz">Karl Benz</a> in 1888 in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> and, under licence from Benz, in France by Emile Roger.</p>
<p>The time line is not exact but Thomas Davenport as well as Robert Anderson (of Scotland) built a battery electric car between 1832 and 1839.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: United States">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: United States" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The era of <a href="/wiki/Automobiles" title="Automobiles" class="mw-redirect">automobiles</a> began in the U.S. when George Selden of Rochester, NY filed a patent on May 8, 1879, but the patent was not approved until November 5, 1895. This was the first U.S. patent for an automobile. By the time a patent was approved many automobiles were in production. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Duryea" title="Charles Duryea">Charles Duryea</a> built a three-wheeled, gasoline powered vehicle in 1893, and his company built 13 cars of the same design in 1896. Gasoline automobiles were produced by <a href="/wiki/Elwood_Haynes" title="Elwood Haynes">Elwood Haynes</a> in 1894, by <a href="/wiki/Ransom_Olds" title="Ransom Olds" class="mw-redirect">Ransom Olds</a> in 1895, <a href="/wiki/Charles_King" title="Charles King">Charles King</a> and <a href="/wiki/Henry_Ford" title="Henry Ford">Henry Ford</a> in 1896. Automobile races stirred the public interest and bicycle and buggy manufacturers began to convert to making automobiles.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Racing">Racing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Racing">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Racing" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Racing began shortly after the production of the automobile. The first official auto race in the world was a 732 mile, round trip race, in the <a href="/wiki/Paris%E2%80%93Bordeaux%E2%80%93Paris_race" title="Paris–Bordeaux–Paris race" class="mw-redirect">Paris–Bordeaux–Paris race</a>. The first race in the U.S. was the six car, 54.36 mile race, from <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>’s Jackson Park to <a href="/wiki/Evanston,_Illinois" title="Evanston, Illinois">Evanston, Illinois</a> and back. Racing necessitated the need for advancement and helped spur inventions that assisted in improvements to the automotive industry.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Automobile_production_eras">Automobile production eras</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Automobile production eras">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Automobile production eras" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Veteran_Era">Veteran Era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Veteran Era">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Veteran Era" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Veteran era began with the invention of the automobile and continued up to 1890.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brass_Era">Brass Era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Brass Era">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Brass Era" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Brass Era is considered from 1890 to 1918.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Periods of the Brass Era are referred to as the Horseless carriage era.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> The Horseless Carriage Club of America (HCCA) defines this era as "any pioneer gas, steam and electric motor vehicle built or manufactured prior to January 1, 1916".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Antique_era_.28before_1920.29">Antique era (before 1920)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Antique era (before 1920)">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Antique era (before 1920)" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The era of antique cars actually covers a specific time period from the beginning up to 1920 or prior to <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The time period for antique cars includes the Veteran Era as well as the Brass era.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vintage_Era">Vintage Era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Vintage Era">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Vintage Era" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The vintage era followed WWI from 1920 to 1930. There were over 500 automotive companies in 1910 but by the time of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a> of 1929 only 60 had survived, and twelve years later there were fewer than 20.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antique_car&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: United Kingdom" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> the Brass era is split into two eras; the Veteran era and the <a href="/wiki/Edwardian_era" title="Edwardian era">Edwardian era</a>. The veteran era includes cars produced before 1905. The Edwardian era includes cars produced between 1 January 1905 and 31 December 1918. This is considered pre World War 1 cars in the U.S. The <b>Vintage</b> era is considered to cover cars made between the 1918 ( the end of the First World War) and 1930. <i><b>Post vintage</b></i> cars are those made from 1 January 1931 to the start of World War 2 (September 1939). Antique cars are all cars made up to September 1939 which was the start of World War 2. Modern cars were made after 31 December 1959.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>Some consider such collectibles be a form of <a href="/wiki/Investment" title="Investment">investment</a>. Buying a particular antique car is then done primarily in view of profit in a future sale and not of enjoying a drive or taking pleasure in <a href="/wiki/Automotive_restoration" title="Automotive restoration">restoration</a> work. The market for antique cars fluctuates wildly over the years. There have been periods, like the 1980s, which have seen strong and continued increase in price, but other periods (e.g. the early 1990s, and 2008–2009) which saw precipitous declines.</p>
