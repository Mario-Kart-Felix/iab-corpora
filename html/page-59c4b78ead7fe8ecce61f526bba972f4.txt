		<h1><a href="http://www.vintageautomotive.net/?p=156" rel="bookmark">The Sincere Definition of Numbers Matching</a></h1>
		
		<div class="date">
			January 2, 2011 &middot; Posted in <a href="http://www.vintageautomotive.net/?cat=8" title="View all posts in News" rel="category">News</a> &middot; <span>Comments Off</span>&nbsp;

		<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-family: Arial;"><strong>Numbers Matching: From a Legal Standpoint</strong><strong></strong></span></h2>
<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-family: Arial;"><strong>By:  Bryan W. Shook, Esquire</strong><strong></strong></span></h2>
<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-family: Arial;"><strong>Attorney-at-Law</strong><strong></strong></span></h2>
<h2 style="text-align: center;"><strong><a href="mailto:BShook@dplglaw.com"><span style="font-family: Arial;">BShook@dplglaw.com</span></a></strong></h2>
<p><span style="font-family: Arial;">A seller, an auction house, a broker, etc., all have express duties to not mislead buyers.  It is the presentation that entices and induces a prospective buyer to make an offer or bid on a car.  Often included within this presentation is the phrase “numbers matching”.  Rarely in any field does a single term mean so much; in the field of car collecting, the term “numbers matching” means everything; or does it?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: Arial;">The phrase “numbers matching” was coined an untold number of years ago to describe a vehicle which retained its original driveline (i.e. the driveline that was installed into the vehicle during its initial assembly at its manufacturer’s plant).  Specifically and most important in this definition is the engine; as this is the single most important aspect of a vehicle’s originality.  There are some in the hobby, however that would have you believe that the phrase “numbers matching” has parted ways with its original and understood definition.  These individuals would have you believe that the phrase &#8220;numbers matching&#8221; means that the driveline, has numbers appearing on its components, that look as though they could have been placed on there during the initial assembly process on the manufacturer’s line.  This is where the issue with restamped engines and transmissions becomes ever apparent.</span></p>
<p><strong><span style="font-family: Arial;">This disingenuous play on words is polluting our hobby.</span></strong></p>
<p><span style="font-family: Arial;">Numbers Matching means ORIGINAL; the phrase and the meaning of “numbers matching” have never parted company.  Numbers matching still means, as it always has, that the engine, transmission and rear axle are original to that particular vehicle.  For the phrase to have any other meaning would render it flawed and unnecessary.  The use of the phrase “numbers matching” in a disingenuous fashion opens the seller to a high level of legal exposure.  The buyer who learns after he purchases the vehicle that the vehicle, is not “as advertised”, has the right to revoke his acceptance of the vehicle and enjoys many protections that come along with legal revocation.  For example, these protections could include a statutorily created security interest in the vehicle up to the amount paid for the vehicle, plus certain expenses and other damages.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: Arial;">Any misleading use of the phrase &#8220;numbers matching&#8221; blackens the eye of the hobby.  The only reason one would use such a deceitful definition of &#8220;numbers matching&#8221; would be in a cacluated attempt to mask the true nature of the vehicle for self-serving purposes.  This ultimately has a negative impact on the hobby.  </span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: Arial;">For more information on what your rights are in such a transaction, please call or email me, I would be more than happy to discuss this or any other matter concerning car collecting with you.</span></p>

		<h1><a href="http://www.vintageautomotive.net/?p=103" rel="bookmark">United States Congress sets National Collector Car Appreciation Day as July 9, 2010</a></h1>
		
		<div class="date">
			May 8, 2010 &middot; Posted in <a href="http://www.vintageautomotive.net/?cat=8" title="View all posts in News" rel="category">News</a> &middot; <span>Comments Off</span>&nbsp; 
		</div>
		
		<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="color: #000000;"><strong><span style="font-size: 16pt; mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">NATIONAL COLLECTOR CAR APPRECIATION DAY</span></strong></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="color: #000000; font-size: small;">By: Bryan W. Shook, Esquire</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="color: #000000;">Email: </span><a href="mailto:bshook@dcdlaw.net"><span style="color: #000000;">bshook@dplglaw.com</span></a></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: left; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-family: &amp;amp; COLOR; font-size: 12pt;">May 4, 2010 – At the persuasion of the SEMA Action network with assistance from automotive restoration manufacturers association (ARMO), the United States Senate approved Senate Resolution 213 on May 4, 2010.   The Resolution sets National Collector Car Appreciation Day as July 9, 2010.  Local, regional and national events to celebrate the newly enacted appreciation day are being developed across the country.  This marks a large step forward in collector car hobby. </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-family: &amp;amp; COLOR; font-size: 12pt;">The resolution, which can be viewed online at <a title="Library of Congress - Thomas.Gov" href="http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/query/z?c111:S.RES.513:" target="_blank">http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/query/z?c111:S.RES.513:</a> states that:</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-family: &amp;amp; COLOR; font-size: 12pt;"> </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-family: &amp;amp; COLOR; font-size: 12pt;">Designating July 9, 2010, as `Collector Car Appreciation Day&#8217; and recognizing that the collection and restoration of historic and classic cars is an important part of preserving the technological achievements and cultural heritage of the United States.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-family: &amp;amp; COLOR; font-size: 12pt;">Whereas many people in the United States maintain classic automobiles as a pastime and do so with great passion and as a means of individual expression;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-family: &amp;amp; COLOR; font-size: 12pt;">Whereas the Senate recognizes the effect that the more than 100-year history of the automobile has had on the economic progress of the Nation and supports wholeheartedly all activities involved in the restoration and exhibition of classic automobiles;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-family: &amp;amp; COLOR; font-size: 12pt;">Whereas collection, restoration, and preservation of automobiles is an activity shared across generations and across all segments of society;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-family: &amp;amp; COLOR; font-size: 12pt;">Whereas thousands of local car clubs and related businesses have been instrumental in preserving a historic part of the heritage of this Nation by encouraging the restoration and exhibition of such vintage works of art;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-family: &amp;amp; COLOR; font-size: 12pt;">Whereas automotive restoration provides well-paying, high-skilled jobs for people in all 50 States; and</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-family: &amp;amp; COLOR; font-size: 12pt;">Whereas automobiles have provided the inspiration for music, photography, cinema, fashion, and other artistic pursuits that have become part of the popular culture of the United States: Now therefore, be it</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt;"><span style="font-family: &amp;amp; COLOR; font-size: 12pt;">Resolved, That the Senate&#8211;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt; padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-family: &amp;amp; COLOR; font-size: 12pt;">(1) designates July 9, 2010, as `Collector Car Appreciation Day&#8217;;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt; padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-family: &amp;amp; COLOR; font-size: 12pt;">(2) recognizes that the collection and restoration of historic and classic cars is an important part of preserving the technological achievements and cultural heritage of the United States;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt; padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-family: &amp;amp; COLOR; font-size: 12pt;">(3) encourages the Department of Education, the Department of Transportation, and other Federal agencies to support events and commemorations of `Collector Car Appreciation Day&#8217;, including exhibitions and educational and cultural activities for young people; and</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 0pt; padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-family: &amp;amp; COLOR; font-size: 12pt;">(4) encourages the people of the United States to engage in events and commemorations of `Collector Car Appreciation Day&#8217; that create opportunities for collector car owners to educate young people on the importance of preserving the cultural heritage of the United States, including through the collection and restoration of collector cars.</span></p>
