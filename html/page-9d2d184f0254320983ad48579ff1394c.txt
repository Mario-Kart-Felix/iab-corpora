<div id="article_story_body" class="article story"><div class="articlePage"><ul class="socialByline">By <li>SANTANU CHOUDHURY</li>
            </ul><p>NEW DELHI—<a href="/public/quotes/main.html?type=djn&amp;symbol=DAI.XE" class="companyRollover link11unvisited">Daimler</a> AG's  
<span data-widget="dj.ticker" data-ticker-name="DAI.XE"></span>
 luxury-car division, Mercedes-Benz, Thursday introduced the A-Class premium hatchback in India as competition intensifies from <a href="/public/quotes/main.html?type=djn&amp;symbol=BMW.XE" class="companyRollover link11unvisited">BMW</a> AG, 
<span data-widget="dj.ticker" data-ticker-name="BMW.XE"></span>
  <a href="/public/quotes/main.html?type=djn&amp;symbol=NSU.XE" class="companyRollover link11unvisited">Audi</a> AG  
<span data-widget="dj.ticker" data-ticker-name="NSU.XE"></span>
 and Jaguar Land Rover Automotive PLC in this potential growth market for luxury cars. </p><div class="insetContent insetCol3wide embedType-image imageFormat-D"><div class="insetTree">
                <div id="articleThumbnail_1" class="insettipUnit insetZoomTarget"><div class="insetZoomTargetBox"><div class="insettipBox"><div class="insettip"><p><a>Enlarge Image</a></p></div></div><a ><img src="http://si.wsj.net/public/resources/images/OB-XQ747_imerc0_D_20130530054746.jpg" vspace="0" hspace="0" border="0"  height="174" width="262"  alt="image"/></a><div id="articleImage_1" class="insetFullBracket"><div class="insetFullBox"><div class="insetButton"><a class="insetClose">Close</a></div><img src="http://si.wsj.net/public/resources/images/OB-XQ747_imerc0_G_20130530054746.jpg" vspace="0" hspace="0" border="0"  height="369" width="553"  alt="image"/></div></div></div>
                
                
                
                
                
                <cite>Handout/Mercedes-Benz</cite>
                <p class="targetCaption">Mercedes-Benz India Managing Director and Chief Executive Eberhard Kern at the launch of the A-Class in Mumbai on Thursday.</p>
            </div></div></div><p>The A-Class is the smallest and lowest-priced Mercedes-Benz model in India, and will add to the B-Class hatchback introduced last September. </p>
<p>The introduction of the A-Class is part of the company's plans to offer new products in India this year, Mercedes-Benz India said. It didn't give any sales projection for the car. </p>
<p>Hatchbacks accounted for the bulk of nearly 1.9 million cars sold in India in the financial year ended March 31, but most of those were comparatively lower-priced cars from companies such as <a href="/public/quotes/main.html?type=djn&amp;symbol=532500.BY" class="companyRollover link11unvisited">Maruti Suzuki India</a> Ltd., 
<span data-widget="dj.ticker" data-ticker-name="532500.BY"></span>
  <a href="/public/quotes/main.html?type=djn&amp;symbol=005380.SE" class="companyRollover link11unvisited">Hyundai Motor</a> Co., 
<span data-widget="dj.ticker" data-ticker-name="005380.SE"></span>
  <a href="/public/quotes/main.html?type=djn&amp;symbol=500570.BY" class="companyRollover link11unvisited">Tata Motors</a> Ltd., 
<span data-widget="dj.ticker" data-ticker-name="500570.BY"></span>
  <a href="/public/quotes/main.html?type=djn&amp;symbol=7201.TO" class="companyRollover link11unvisited">Nissan Motor</a> Co.  
<span data-widget="dj.ticker" data-ticker-name="7201.TO"></span>
 and <a href="/public/quotes/main.html?type=djn&amp;symbol=F" class="companyRollover link11unvisited">Ford Motor</a> Co.  
<span data-widget="dj.ticker" data-ticker-name="F"></span>
 </p><p>Customers of the A-Class would have the option to choose between a 2.2-liter, four-cylinder diesel engine and a 1.6-liter four-cylinder gasoline engine. Both engines will come with seven-speed automatic transmissions. </p>
<p>The diesel model has been priced at about 2.19 million rupees ($39,107) at showrooms in Mumbai, while the gasoline model will cost 2.27 million rupees. </p>
<p>The A-Class will face competition from BMW's 1-Series hatchback, which is scheduled for introduction in a few months, and the Mini range in India. BMW already assembles a few variants of the Mini premium small car at its factory near the southern port city of Chennai. </p>
<p>Mercedes-Benz assembles the S-Class, E-Class and C-Class sedans, the Actros range of heavy trucks, and buses at a factory in the western Indian city of Pune. </p>
<p>It also imports and sells vehicles such as the CLS-Class and SLK-Class. The A-Class will also be imported as a completely built unit. </p>
