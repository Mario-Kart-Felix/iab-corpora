<h1><a href="/2013/02/chicago-auto-show-2014-ford-transit-connect-wagon/" title="Permalink for Chicago Auto Show: 2014 Ford Transit Connect Wagon">Chicago Auto Show: 2014 Ford Transit Connect Wagon</a></h1>
</div>
<div class="postbyline">
By <a href="/author/alex-dykes/" title="Posts by Alex L. Dykes" rel="author">Alex L. Dykes</a> on February 8, 2013 </div><!-- end postbyline -->
<div class="postbody">
<!-- google_ad_section_start --><p><a href="2014-ford-transit-connect-wagon-4-2/" rel="attachment wp-att-476912"><img class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-476912" title="2014 Ford Transit Connect Wagon-4" pagespeed_lazy_src="http://images.thetruthaboutcars.com/2013/02/2014-Ford-Transit-Connect-Wagon-4-450x300.jpg" alt="" width="450" height="300" src="/mod_pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisible(this);"/></a></p>
<p>If you were an early adopter of Ford&#8217;s compact cargo hauler, news of the 2014 Transit Connect may upset you. Rather than letting the old baby-Transit languish in America like Ford did once upon a time with thee Focus, the new Euro van is coming to America later this year. With new engines, new transmissions and a corporate grille, should you put your purchase on hold?</p>
<p><span id="more-476907"></span></p>
<p>In a word: yes. The new Transit Connect isn&#8217;t just more stylish than its predecessor, it comes with a host of improvements for people and cargo hauling alike. Payload remains somewhat low at 1,600lbs but cargo room is up thanks to an extended wheelbase model. Ford is even tossing in their 6.5 inch version of MyFord Touch.</p>
<p><a href="2014-ford-transit-connect-wagon-11/" rel="attachment wp-att-476908"><img class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-476908" title="2014 Ford Transit Connect Wagon" pagespeed_lazy_src="http://images.thetruthaboutcars.com/2013/02/2014-Ford-Transit-Connect-Wagon-450x300.jpg" alt="" width="450" height="300" src="/mod_pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisible(this);"/></a></p>
<p>If people carrying is your goal, that extended model will now haul 7 passengers and Ford improved interior parts quality. While the last Transit connect was slathered in hard Euro plastics, the 2014 model borrows from Ford&#8217;s Focus, Escape and Fiesta parts bins. The result is a mini-van that looks like one of the family and finally doesn&#8217;t have any painted metal showing on the inside.</p>
<p>Under the hood Ford is using a naturally aspirated 2.5L four cylinder or a force-fed 1.6L four borrowed from the Escape and Fusion. Assuming the 1.6L doesn&#8217;t get recalled, the boosted four&#8217;s broad torque range coupled with Ford&#8217;s 6-speed automatic should improve the Connect&#8217;s performance on the road. Ford is claiming the combo will also be good for 30MPG on the highway which is impressive for a commercial vehicle, as long as they can meet their claim.</p>
