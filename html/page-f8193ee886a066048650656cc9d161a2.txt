<p>One of the questions we field most often for this &ldquo;old-car insurance&rdquo; category is, &ldquo;How do you define an antique auto?&rdquo; An antique auto is generally 25 or more years old and is maintained primarily for use in parades, exhibitions, club activities, and other functions of public interest.</p>
<h2 class="h2-content-title">Save on Antique Car Insurance</h2>
<p>Antique autos are eligible for many of the same discounts as &quot;everyday&quot; vehicles. For example, you may be eligible for discounts if you have an anti-theft device in your antique car, if you take an approved Defensive Driver Course, or if bundle your antique car insurance with homeowners insurance.</p>
<p>Availability of discounts and credits may vary by state or policy plan.</p>
<h2 class="h2-content-title">Antique Auto Insurance Coverage</h2>
<p>In most states, the amount of coverage provided for an antique auto is determined using &quot;stated amount&quot; - an amount that you and The Hartford agree on as the maximum value for the vehicle. In the event of a loss, a claim adjuster will determine the current value of the antique auto, which cannot be more than the &quot;stated amount&quot; listed on the policy.</p>
<p>In some states, The Hartford is introducing antique auto coverage based on &quot;agreed value.&quot; This is the amount of coverage that you and The Hartford agree on as the value for the car, based on a recent, certified appraisal. The following coverages are available for antique autos (varies by state):</p>
<ul>
    <li>Bodily Injury Liability</li>
    <li>Property Damage Liability</li>
    <li>Physical Damage to your auto (mandatory for antique autos)</li>
</ul>
<p>For an additional premium, you may be able to add the following optional coverages (varies by state):</p>
<ul>
    <li>Towing and Labor</li>
    <li>Extended Transportation</li>
    <li>Full Glass</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="h3-content-title">No Drop Promise</h3>
