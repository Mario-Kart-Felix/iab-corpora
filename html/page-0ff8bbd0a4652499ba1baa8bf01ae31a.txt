		<p>While the 2013 Cadillac CTS offers a powerful V6 engine and a composed ride, test drivers note that it&rsquo;s larger than many competing sport sedans in the class. As a result, some say that the CTS doesn&rsquo;t feel as agile as rivals like the <a href="/cars-trucks/Infiniti_G37/2013/">Infiniti G37</a> or <a href="/cars-trucks/BMW_3-Series/2013/">BMW 3-Series</a>. CTS-V models are full-fledged performance cars, with suspension and engine upgrades that make them worthy competitors to cars like the BMW M3 and <a href="/cars-trucks/Lexus_IS/">Lexus IS-F</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li class="quote quote-neg">"V6-powered CTS models don't quite reach the level of handling polish of its European and Japanese competitors." -- <a href="http://consumerguideauto.howstuffworks.com/2013-cadillac-cts.htm" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Consumer Guide</a>&nbsp;</li>
<li class="quote quote-neg">"The CTS also drives quite well, with respectable power from the larger 3.6-liter V6 engine and confident handling. However, when you drive it back to back with its import competitors, even these admirable qualities tend to fall short of the now-current standard." -- <a href="http://www.edmunds.com/cadillac/cts/2013/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Edmunds</a></li>
<li class="quote quote-pos">"Through twisty two-lanes, fast freeways, urban congestion and mixed-speed suburban sprawl, we greatly appreciate the 2013 Cadillac CTS' responsive steering, world-class roadholding and powerful brakes." -- <a href="http://www.kbb.com/cadillac/cts/2013-cadillac-cts/?r=489889311129737800" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Kelley Blue Book</a>&nbsp;</li>
<li class="quote quote-pos">"The sedan moves out well, and the V-6's mechanical growl sounds good in the process." -- <a href="http://www.cars.com/cadillac/cts/2012/expert-reviews/?revid=58496" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Cars.com</a> (2012)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Acceleration and Power</h3>
<p>Three engines are available in the 2013 Cadillac CTS. The base engine in the sedan is a 3.0-liter V6 that makes 270 horsepower, while a 318-horsepower, 3.6-liter V6 is optional. The CTS coupe gets the 3.6-liter V6 as standard equipment. A six-speed automatic transmission and rear-wheel drive are standard, though all-wheel drive is available on both models.</p>
<p>CTS-V sedans and coupes get a supercharged 6.2-liter V8 engine that generates 556 horsepower at 6,100 rpm and 551pound-feet of torque at 3,800 rpm. A six-speed manual or automatic is available. The <a href="http://www.fueleconomy.gov/feg/Find.do?action=sbs&amp;id=32596&amp;id=32539&amp;id=32594&amp;id=32402" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">EPA</a> reports that the V6-powered CTS sedan gets 18/27 mpg city/highway, while CTS-V models use significantly more fuel.</p>
<p>Reviewers generally recommend the larger V6 engine, noting that it makes the CTS a better performer, and has a negligible effect on fuel economy. In general, most auto writers also approve of the CTS&rsquo; automatic transmission, noting its smooth operation. While high-performance CTS-V models are quicker than most cars on the street, one reviewer says that they&rsquo;re still comfortable and composed on daily commutes.</p>
<ul>
<li class="quote quote-pos">"The transmission is generally seamless." -- <a href="http://consumerguideauto.howstuffworks.com/2013-cadillac-cts.htm" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Consumer Guide</a>&nbsp;</li>
<li class="quote">"Power delivery from the base 3.0-liter V6 is sluggish compared to the broad-shouldered 3.6-liter engine. Considering that both engines achieve virtually identical fuel economy, we suggest springing for the bigger V6 if your budget allows." -- <a href="http://www.edmunds.com/cadillac/cts/2013/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Edmunds</a>&nbsp;</li>
<li class="quote quote-pos">"For a serious performance car - the CTS-V Sedan can deliver blistering acceleration from zero to 60 mph of just 3.9 seconds - the driving experience is surprisingly calm and quiet." -- <a href="http://www.kbb.com/cadillac/cts/2013-cadillac-cts/?r=489889311129737800" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Kelley Blue Book</a></li>
<li class="quote quote-pos">"The CTS comes standard with a 3.0-liter V-6 engine, but our test car's optional 3.6-liter V-6 and six-speed automatic transmission are a special pair among drivetrains." -- <a href="http://www.cars.com/cadillac/cts/2012/expert-reviews/?revid=58496" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Cars.com</a> (2012)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Handling and Braking</h3>
<p>The 2013 CTS impresses reviewers with precise steering and a composed ride, however, one test driver says that the CTS&rsquo; large size means that it lacks the athletic handling you&rsquo;ll get with some smaller cars in the segment. CTS-V models come with <a href="/cars-trucks/browse/cadillac/">Cadillac&rsquo;s</a> Magnetic Ride Control suspension system, which features selectable Tour and Sport modes that adjust the CTS&rsquo; handling characteristics. One reviewer notes that while the optional sport suspension improves handling, it also has a negative effect on ride comfort.</p>
<ul>
<li class="quote quote-pos">"Steering feel is precise, and lean in corners is well checked." -- <a href="http://consumerguideauto.howstuffworks.com/2013-cadillac-cts.htm" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Consumer Guide</a>&nbsp;</li>
<li class="quote quote-neg">"This Cadillac's size, while beneficial for passengers, also makes it feel less agile and maneuverable than other so-called sport sedans." -- <a href="http://www.edmunds.com/cadillac/cts/2013/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Edmunds</a>&nbsp;</li>
<li class="quote quote-pos">"Given its performance-focused engineering, one might expect a correspondingly firm ride, but we find the suspension surprisingly supple even on badly deteriorated pavement." -- <a href="http://www.kbb.com/cadillac/cts/2013-cadillac-cts/?r=489889311129737800" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Kelley Blue Book</a>&nbsp;</li>
<li class="quote quote-neg">"Our test CTS had the optional performance suspension, and the car felt as firm as one of the high-performance V-Series versions that Cadillac sells, with harsh, jarring responses over bumps." -- <a href="http://www.cars.com/cadillac/cts/2012/expert-reviews/?revid=58496" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Cars.com</a> (2012)</li>
</ul>
