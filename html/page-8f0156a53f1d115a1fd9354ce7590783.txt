        <h4>
          Place of Business Inspection 
</h4>
        <p>
          After you have arranged an appointment with an Occupational Licensing Inspector he or she will inspect the place of business where the dealer conducts business. 
        </p>
        <ul>
          <li>
            The office of the principal place of business and each location of the dealership must be established to the extent that its construction is not temporary, transitory, or mobile in nature, except that a trailer coach office is acceptable providing it is not part of the dealer's vehicle inventory being offered for or subject to sale while being used as an office of the dealership and otherwise meets the requirements of the Vehicle Code. The place of business is a place actually occupied either continuously or at regular periods by the dealer. <a href="/pubs/vctop/d01/vc320.htm">Section 320, <acronym title="California Vehicle Code">CVC</acronym></a>. 
            <br>
          </li>
          <li>
            Inspect all books and records pertinent to the business. <a href="/pubs/vctop/d01/vc320.htm"><acronym title="California Vehicle Code">CVC</acronym> Section 320 (a)</a>, <a href="/pubs/vctop/d02/vc1670.htm"><acronym title="California Vehicle Code">CVC</acronym> Section 1670</a>, and <a href="/pubs/vctop/d02/vc1671.htm"><acronym title="California Vehicle Code">CVC</acronym> Section 1671 </a>and <a href="http://ccr.oal.ca.gov/"><acronym title="California Code of Regulations">CCR</acronym></a> Title 13, Section 270.00, 270.02, 270.04 and 272.00 
          </li>
          <li>
            Inspection of Display Area (not required if wholesale only). After you have arranged an appointment with an Occupational Licensing Inspector he or she will inspect the display area to ensure: 
            <ul>
              <li>
                The display area of the principal place of business shall be of sufficient size to physically accommodate vehicles of a type for which the dealership is licensed to sell. 
              </li>
              <li>
                The display area must be clearly for the exclusive use of the dealer for display purposes only. 
              </li>
              <li>
                Additional display areas are permitted within a radius of 1,000 feet from the principal place of business and any licensed branch location without being subject to separate licensing. However, such display areas must also meet the signing requirement as identified in <a href="/pubs/vctop/d05/vc11709.htm">Vehicle Code Section 11709 (a)</a>.Title 13, Section 270.08, 270.08 (a) and 270.08 (b) 
              </li>
            </ul>
          </li>
          <li>
            Inspection of Signs (not required if Wholesale Only Dealer). After you have arranged an appointment with an Occupational Licensing Inspector he or she will inspect the sign(s) to ensure: 
          </li>
        </ul>
        <p class="blockindent4">
          Signs must be of a permanent nature, erected on the exterior of the office or on the display area, and be constructed or painted and maintained so as to withstand reasonable climatic effects and be readable as provided for in <a href="/pubs/vctop/d05/vc11709.htm"><acronym title="California Vehicle Code">CVC</acronym> Section 11709</a>. <a href="http://ccr.oal.ca.gov/"><acronym title="California Code of Regulations">CCR</acronym></a>, Title 13, Section 270.06 
        </p>
        <p class="blockindent4">
          A temporary sign or device may suffice when a permanent sign is on order. Evidence of such order shall be submitted to the Department prior to issuance of a temporary permit or license. <a href="/pubs/vctop/d05/vc11709.htm"><acronym title="California Vehicle Code">CVC</acronym> Section 11709(a)</a> 
        </p>

        <h4>
          Other Permits and Licenses 
        </h4>
<p style="font-weight:bold;">
          Copies of the following permits and/or licenses from other agencies must be submitted with your application: 
        </p>
        <h4>
          Board of Equalization Resale Permit (Revenue and Taxation Code, Section 6066 and 6067) 
        </h4>
        <p>
          All licensees selling vehicles or vessels, retail or wholesale, are required to file an application for a Seller's Permit. The purpose of the permit is to enable the licensee to collect taxes on sales. There is no fee for the permit, but a deposit may be required. Applications can be made through your local Board of Equalization office. A copy of this permit is required as part of the application for a dealer and lessor-retail license. Pursuant to California Vehicle Code Sections 11617(a)(6) and 11721(f), the Department may automatically cancel your dealer or lessor-retail license for failure to maintain a valid seller's permit. 
        </p>
        <p>
          Pursuant to Revenue and Taxation Code Section 6014, &quot;Seller&quot; includes every person engaged in the business of selling tangible personal property of a kind the gross receipts from the retail sale of which are required to be included in the measure of the sales tax. 
        </p>
        <p>
          Questions should be referred to BOE at (800) 400-7115 or refer to information published at <a href="http://boe.ca.gov/pdf/pub73.pdf">Board of Equalization Publication 73(<acronym title="Portable Document Format">PDF</acronym>)</a>. 
        </p>
<h4>
          City and/or County Business License 
        </h4>
<p>
          Licensees are required to obtain a city or county business license by the city or county licensing section in the area where the licensee is doing business. Contact your local city or county to obtain the business license. A copy of this permit is required as part of the application for a dealers license.  If the City/County does not issue business licenses; a tax certificate or letter on non-issuance is required as part of the application for a dealers license. 
        </p>

        <h4>
          Dealer License Exemption 
        </h4>
        <p>
          A dealer who does not have an established place of business in this state but who is currently authorized to do business as, and who has an established place of business as a vehicle dealer in another state is not subject to licensure under this article if the business transacted in California is limited to the importation of vehicles for sale to, or the export of vehicles purchased from, persons licensed in California under <a href="/pubs/vctop/d05/vc11700.htm"><acronym title="California Vehicle Code">CVC</acronym> Section 11700</a> et seq., and <a href="/pubs/vctop/d05/vc11700_1.htm"><acronym title="California Vehicle Code">CVC</acronym> Section 11700.1</a>. 
        </p>
        <hr>
        <h4>
          Dealer License Exclusions 
        </h4>
        <h4>
          The term &quot;dealer&quot; does <em>not</em> include any of the following: 
        </h4>
        <ul>
          <li>
            Insurance companies, banks, finance companies, public officials, or any other person coming into possession of vehicles in the regular course of business, who sells vehicles under a contractual right or obligation, in performance of any official duty, or in authority of any court of law, if the sale is for the purpose of saving the seller from loss or pursuant to the authority of a court. 
          </li>
          <li>
            Persons who sell or distribute vehicles of a type subject to registration for a manufacturer to vehicle dealers under this code, or who are employed by manufacturers or distributors to promote the sale of vehicles dealt in by those manufacturers or distributors.&nbsp; However, any of those persons who also sell vehicles at retail are vehicle dealers and subject to this code. 
          </li>
          <li>
            Persons regularly employed as salespersons by vehicle dealers licensed under this code while acting in the scope of that employment. 
          </li>
          <li>Persons  engaged exclusively in the bona fide business of exporting vehicles or of  soliciting orders for the sale and delivery of vehicles outside the territorial  limits of the United States,  if no federal excise tax is legally payable or refundable on any of the  transactions.&nbsp; Persons not engaged exclusively in the bona fide business  of exporting vehicles, but who are engaged in the  business of soliciting orders for the sale and delivery of vehicles, outside  the territorial limits of the United States are exempt from licensure as  dealers only if their sales of vehicles produce less than 10 percent of their  total gross revenue from all business transacted. </li>
          <li>
            Persons not engaged in the purchase or sale of vehicles as a business, who dispose of any vehicle acquired and used in good faith, for their own personal use, or for use in their business, and not for the purpose of avoiding the provisions of this code. 
          </li>
          <li>
            Persons who are engaged in the purchase, sale or exchange of vehicles, other than motorcycles subject to identification under this code, which are not intended for use on the highways. 
          </li>
          <li>
            Persons temporarily retained as auctioneers solely for the purpose of disposing of vehicle stock inventories by means of public auction on behalf of the owners at the owner's place of business, or as otherwise approved by the department, if intermediate physical possession or control of, or an ownership interest in, the inventory is not conveyed to the persons as retained. 
          </li>
          <li>
            Persons who are engaged exclusively in the business of purchasing, selling, servicing, or exchange racing vehicles, parts for racing vehicles and trailers designed and intended by the manufacturer to be used exclusively for carrying racing vehicles.&nbsp; A &quot;racing vehicle&quot; is defined as a motor vehicle of a type used exclusively in a contest of speed or in a competitive trial of speed which is not intended for use on the highways. 
          </li>
          <li>
            Any person who is a lessor. 
          </li>
          <li>
            Any person who is a renter. 
          </li>
          <li>
            Any salvage pool. 
          </li>
          <li>
            Any yacht broker who is subject to the Yacht and Ship Brokers Act <a href="http://www.leginfo.ca.gov/cgi-bin/displaycode?section=hnc&amp;group=00001-01000&amp;file=700-740">(Article 2 (commencing with Section 700) of Chapter 5 of Division 3</a> of the Harbors and Navigations Code) and who sells boat trailers in conjunction with the sale of a vessel. 
          </li>
          <li>
            Any licensed automobile dismantler who sells vehicles that have been reported for dismantling as provided in <a href="/pubs/vctop/d05/vc11520.htm">Vehicle Code Section 11520</a>. 
          </li>
          <li>
            The Director of Corrections when selling vehicles pursuant to <a href="/pubs/vctop/appndxa/penalco/penco2813_5.htm">Section 2813.5 of the Penal Code</a>. 
          </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
          Any public or private nonprofit charitable, religious, or educational institution or organization that sells vehicles if all of the following conditions are met: 
</p>
        <ul>
          <li>
            The proceeds of the sale of the vehicles are retained by that institution or organization for its charitable, religious, or educational purposes. 
          </li>
          <li>
            The vehicles sold were donated to the institution or organization. 
          </li>
          <li>
            They meet all of the applicable equipment requirements of <a href="/pubs/vctop/vc/vctoc.htm">Vehicle Code Division 12 </a>(commencing with Section 24000) and have been issued a certificate pursuant to Section <a href="/pubs/vctop/appndxa/hlthsaf/hs44015.htm">44015 of the Health and Safety Code</a>. 
          </li>
          <li>
            The institution or organization has qualified for state tax-exempt status under <a href="http://www.leginfo.ca.gov/cgi-bin/displaycode?section=rtc&amp;group=23001-24000&amp;file=23701-23712">Section 23701d of the Revenue and Taxation code</a>, and federal tax-exempt status under Section <a href="http://www.fourmilab.ch/ustax/www/t26-A-1-F-I-501.html">501</a> (c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. 
          </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
          Any motor club, as defined in <a href="http://www.leginfo.ca.gov/cgi-bin/displaycode?section=ins&amp;group=12001-13000&amp;file=12140-12159">Section 12142 of the Insurance Code</a>, that does not arrange or negotiate individual motor vehicle purchase transactions on behalf of its members but refers members to a new motor vehicle dealer for the purchase of a new motor vehicle and does not receive a fee from the dealer contingent upon the sale of the vehicle. 
        </p>
