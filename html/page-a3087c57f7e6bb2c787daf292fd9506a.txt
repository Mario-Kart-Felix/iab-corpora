    $('ul.tabs').each(function () {

        // For each set of tabs, we want to keep track of
        // which tab is active and it's associated content
        var $active, $content, $links = $(this).find('a');

        // If the location.hash matches one of the links, use that as the active tab.
        // If no match is found, use the first link as the initial active tab.
        $active = $($links.filter('[href="' + location.hash + '"]')[0] || $links[0]);
        $active.addClass('active');
        $content = $($active.attr('href'));

        // Hide the remaining content
        $links.not($active).each(function () {
            $($(this).attr('href')).hide();
        });

        // Bind the click event handler
        $(this).on('click', 'a', function (e) {
            // Make the old tab inactive.
            $active.parent().removeClass('active');
            $content.hide();

            // Update the variables with the new link and content
            $active = $(this);
            $content = $($(this).attr('href'));

            // Make the tab active.
            $active.parent().addClass('active');
            $content.show();

            // Prevent the anchor's default click action
            e.preventDefault();
        });
    });</script>

    <h6 class="font18"><a href="/Blog/post/2013-Cadillac-XTS-Quick-Spin-Test-Drive-Review.aspx" data-role="ajax-nav">2013 Cadillac XTS Quick-Spin Test-Drive Review</a></h6>

    <span class="author"><a href="/Blog/author/Gary-Witzenburg.aspx">Gary Witzenburg</a></span> on <span class="pubDate" itemprop="datePublished">07.02.2012 07:42</span>
    <div class="text">
        <p>Missing from Cadillac's arsenal has been a state-of-the-art large sedan to replace both the aging STS and the ancient DTS, both of which ceased production in 2011. But now, say hello to this <a title="2013 Cadillac XTS L.A. Auto Show Debut" href="http://www.automedia.com/Blog/post/2013-Cadillac-XTS-New-Formula-for-Luxury-2011-LA-Auto-Show-%28Video%29.aspx" target="_blank">2013 Cadillac XTS</a>&mdash;easily the best Cadillac sedan ever. Starting at about $44,000, it's priced to compete with "mid-price luxury" imports like the Audi A6, BMW 5 Series and Mercedes-Benz E-Class, yet it's big, roomy, dynamically competent and richly-equipped enough to give the much more expensive Audi A8, BMW 7 Series and Mercedes-Benz S-Class large luxury sedans some serious trouble.</p>
<p>The 2013 XTS (the "TS" means "Touring Sedan") wears a Cadillac "Art and Science" tuxedo, softer and more formal than the <a title="Cadillac CTS Coupe" href="http://www.automedia.com/2011_Cadillac_CTS_Coupe/rts20101001cc/1" target="_blank">harder-edged CTS</a> suit, yet it's no question a size-larger sibling. The interior cleverly blends traditional simplicity, elegant luxury and high technology with premium materials, and an unprecedented (for Cadillac) level of connectivity. In roominess, Cadillac XTS is comparable to full-size sedans and well better than midsize rivals, especially the rear seat's 40 inches of legroom&mdash;and its 18-cu.-ft. trunk trumps even full-size competitors.</p>
<p>Standard on all models is Cadillac-exclusive <a href="http://www.cadillac.com/cadillac-cue.html" target="_blank"><strong>CUE</strong></a> (Cadillac User Experience), a customizable user interface using an eight-inch touchscreen in the center stack, a faceplate below it, and steering wheel controls. It's first in the auto industry to use capacitive-touch control&mdash;the technology that lets you select, slide and expand icons and images on tablets and "smart" phones&mdash;with proximity sensing, haptic feedback, natural voice recognition, and gesture recognition that anticipates what you want on-screen even as your finger approaches.  <a href="/Blog/post/2013-Cadillac-XTS-Quick-Spin-Test-Drive-Review.aspx" >more</a>

Smart and sensible, four-door Accords have been offered here since 1976, and they&rsquo;ve been built in the U.S. since 1982. Our tester&rsquo;s V6 proved smooth and responsive (the four-cylinder is also very smooth and quite fuel-frugal), with 271 horsepower and a five-speed automatic. The steering is light and accurate, while the ride and handling are definitely aimed more toward comfort than ultimate handling.         <p class="MsoNormal">Chrysler&rsquo;s <a title="SRT Performance" href="http://www.automedia.com/SRT_Performance/dsm20110801rt/1">SRT</a> (Street and Racing Technology) group develops performance versions of Chrysler products that focus on five specific attributes: &ldquo;awe-inspiring&rdquo; powertrains, outstanding ride and handling, &ldquo;benchmark&rdquo; braking, &ldquo;aggressive&rdquo; exteriors and &ldquo;race-inspired&rdquo; interiors. The 2012 Chrysler 300 SRT8 wears its own front fascia with a new grille and new Chrysler wing badge. It&rsquo;s lowered a half inch and sports SRT-exclusive body-color side sill cladding. The smooth five-speed automatic (not yet a six-speed or the regular 2012 300&rsquo;s new, more fuel-efficient eight-speed) offers a choice of &ldquo;Normal&rdquo; or &ldquo;Sport&rdquo; modes and new steering wheel paddles for quick and easy manual shifting.  <a href="/Blog/post/Quick-Spin-Test-Drive-2012-Chrysler-300-SRT8.aspx" >more</a>

    <span class="font18 bold"><a href="/Blog/post/Gas-Mileage-How-Does-Mazdas-SKYACTIV-Technology-Compare.aspx" data-role="ajax-nav">Gas Mileage: How Does Mazda’s SKYACTIV Technology Compare?</a></span><br/>

    <div class="text">
        <p class="MsoNormal">No question about it, fuel-economy estimates have been rising. Not for all cars and trucks, by any means. But a surprising number of vehicles are earning estimates from the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) that forge well ahead of the numbers issued for that model a couple of years back.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">No radical technology can account for the difference. For the most part, these improvements have occurred because of comparatively modest tweaks to existing engines. With one exception, that is: Direct Injection. Getting the air/fuel mixture into the engine&rsquo;s cylinders as efficiently as possible makes a substantial difference in overall gas mileage.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">Unlike most companies with rising fuel-economy figures, Mazda has assigned a name to its latest fuel-efficient powertrain technology: SKYACTIV. Introduced as an option for the redesigned <a title="Mazda3 Skyactiv Review" href="http://www.automedia.com/2012_Mazda_Mazda3_SKYACTIV/rts20111101m3/1">2012 Mazda3</a> sport compact, the system includes a SKYACTIV-G direct-injection 2.0-liter engine and a SKYACTIV-Drive six-speed automatic transmission. Rounding out the triple threat, Mazda dubs its six-speed manual transmission SKYACTIV-MT.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">With the automatic transmission, a Mazda3 sedan with SKYACTIV earns an EPA estimate of 28 mpg in city driving and 40 mpg on the highway. That&rsquo;s considerably better than the conventional 2.0-liter engine, available since the 2010 redesign, which gets a 24/33 mpg estimate.  <a href="/Blog/post/Gas-Mileage-How-Does-Mazdas-SKYACTIV-Technology-Compare.aspx" >more</a>

    <span class="font18 bold"><a href="/Blog/post/Quick-Spin-Test-Drive-2012-Volkswagen-Passat-TDI.aspx" data-role="ajax-nav">Quick-Spin Test Drive: 2012 Volkswagen Passat TDI</a></span><br/>

    <span class="author"><a href="/Blog/author/Ben-Lewis.aspx">Ben Lewis</a></span> on <span class="pubDate" itemprop="datePublished">01.17.2012 11:02</span>
    <div class="text">
        <p class="MsoNormal">If you haven&rsquo;t been to your friendly VW dealer lately, you might be surprised. The automaker is releasing a deluge of new products aimed squarely at the U.S., with plans to be one of the big dogs in the marketplace. The award-winning, redesigned <a title="Volkswagen Passat" href="http://www.automedia.com/2012_Volkswagen_Passat/rts20111101vp/1">2012 Volkswagen Passat</a> sedan might just help them hit that goal.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">While the all-new <a title="Volkswagen Beetle" href="http://www.automedia.com/2012_Volkswagen_Beetle/rts20111001vb/1">2012 VW Beetle</a> is sure to capture a lot of attention, it&rsquo;s really the recently-released Jetta and all-new Passat that are the bread-and-butter vehicles Volkswagen needs to sell to become a big number player.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">The new <a title="Volkswagen Jetta" href="http://www.automedia.com/2012_Volkswagen_Jetta_GLI/rts20110901vj/1">2012 Volkswagen Jetta</a> has been a wonderful first start, with strong sales. But with the 2012 Passat, VW is going into Toyota Camry/Honda Accord/Nissan Altima/Hyundai Sonata territory&mdash;home of the most popular-selling cars in the country. Can it compete?</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">It certainly has the looks to do it. This is one handsome car. With crisp, clean lines, the <a title="Volkswagen Passat" href="http://www.automedia.com/Blog/post/Quick-Spin-Test-Drive-2012-Volkswagen-Passat.aspx">2012 Volkswagen Passat</a> says &ldquo;Audi&rdquo; much more than it does &ldquo;VW.&rdquo; The fit-and-finish of our tester was perfect, and in its rich Tungsten Silver looked like a million bucks. </p>
<p class="MsoNormal">The drive is just as impressive. Our Passat tester was the TDI turbo diesel teamed to the six-speed DSG automatic gearbox. It&rsquo;s a lovely combination, with loads of low-end power, lightning-fast, butter smooth shifts, and a quiet, luxurious demeanor. And with up to 43 mpg and a range of (gulp!) 795 miles, you&rsquo;ll need a pit stop long before your Passat does.  <a href="/Blog/post/Quick-Spin-Test-Drive-2012-Volkswagen-Passat-TDI.aspx" >more</a>

    <span class="font18 bold"><a href="/Blog/post/Quick-Spin-Test-Drive-2012-Buick-Verano.aspx" data-role="ajax-nav">Quick-Spin Test Drive: 2012 Buick Verano</a></span><br/>

    <span class="author"><a href="/Blog/author/Gary-Witzenburg.aspx">Gary Witzenburg</a></span> on <span class="pubDate" itemprop="datePublished">01.16.2012 02:23</span>
    <div class="text">
        <p class="MsoNormal">The <a title="2012 Buick Verano" href="http://www.automedia.com/Blog/post/Detroit-Auto-Show-Preview-2012-Buick-Verano.aspx">2012 Buick Verano</a> is essentially a whole new class of car: a surprisingly affordable luxury compact. Roughly the same size as the current Chevy Cruze (with which it shares GM&rsquo;s latest compact-car architecture), it competes with Lexus&rsquo; IS 250, Audi&rsquo;s A4, Acura&rsquo;s TSX and other luxury compacts for thousands of dollars less.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">Like its larger siblings, Enclave, LaCrosse and Regal, the 2012 Buick Verano exterior wears the marque&rsquo;s signature waterfall grille, sweeping side sculpting, an arching roofline connecting its steeply raked windshield and rear pillars, fine (calligraphy-like) exterior detailing and iconic chrome &ldquo;portholes,&rdquo; one per engine cylinder. Its headlamps are serious-looking blue translucent projector beams, and its standard wheels are substantial 18-inch alloys.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">Verano also boasts what has recently become another Buick signature feature, an ultra-quiet, elegantly-designed interior with exceptional seat comfort and support, premium soft-touch materials, rich leathers, warm woods, metallic accents, fabric-wrapped pillars and soft, ice-blue ambient lighting. Buick&rsquo;s extensive &ldquo;Quiet Tuning&rdquo; uses acoustic laminated windshield and side glass and substantial sound-absorbing measures to make 2012 Buick Verano probably the quietest vehicle in its segment, including those much pricier German and Japanese luxury compacts. </p>
<p class="MsoNormal">Among its many standard features are 10 airbags, GM StabiliTrak stability control, four-wheel-disc anti-lock brakes, steering wheel radio controls, automatic climate control, remote starting&mdash;linked with the car&rsquo;s climate system, rear defogger and (on the leather model) heated outside mirrors and seats&mdash;front and rear reading lamps and a locking, sliding console armrest.  <a href="/Blog/post/Quick-Spin-Test-Drive-2012-Buick-Verano.aspx" >more</a>
