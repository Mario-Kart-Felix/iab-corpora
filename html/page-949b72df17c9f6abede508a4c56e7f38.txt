			<div class="entrytext">
				<p>Diesel-powered engines are standard in trucks, cars, trains, farm equipment and buses and are becoming more mainstream. Diesel mechanics are specially skilled automotive mechanics that can diagnosis,repair and maintain diesel engines. It is common for a diesel mechanic to handle all types of repairs &#8211; working on an electrical system one day and then undergoing some major engine repair the next.</p>
<p>Diesel maintenance has become more and more complex over the year as more components are being used. Nowadays microprocessors regulate fuel and engine timing and emission control systems are standard. Diesel mechanics use computers these days to diagnosis and adjust the various functions of diesel engines.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://automechanicsalary.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/diesel-mechanic-salary.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-26" title="diesel-mechanic-salary" src="http://automechanicsalary.org/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/diesel-mechanic-salary-300x225.jpg" alt="diesel mechanic salary" width="300" height="225" /></a></p>
<p>The Bureau of Labor Statistics reported in May of 2008 that the median hourly wages of diesel mechanics was just under $19.00 an hour. The lowest 10% earned $12.50 an hour while the highest 10% earned over $28.00 an hour. Of course, considering that these figures are three years old, those figures are undoubtedly a bit higher today. A person who is hired as a beginning diesel mechanic can expect to earn around the $19 an hour mark while a certified, highly experienced diesel mechanic will earn at least $28 per hour. Also, seasoned diesel mechanics working for large companies overseeing other mechanics can make around $60,000 annually.</p>
<p>As of May 2008, diesel mechanics working in Napa, California, Fairbanks, Alaska, Oakland, California, Kokoma, Indiana and Hanford, California made the most money as these professionals earned an average of $58,000 per year.</p>
<p>Many diesel mechanics are employed by fleet owners and operators as well as by independent repair shops.  These mechanics most usually receive a commission which consists of receiving a portion of the labor costs charged to the customers. The amount of this commission varies greatly from one company to another and also is dependent upon geographical location.</p>
<p>Although national certification is not a requirement for employment as a diesel mechanic, many mechanics do seek out certification because it helps them advance and land new jobs which pay well. As with many occupations that offer certification, putting forth the effort to become a certified diesel mechanic will pay off financially. The National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence or ASE certification is the recognized industry standard for diesel mechanics. There are several areas one can earn certification in and a person must pass at least one of the exams and prove that he/she has two years of work experience in order to become certified.</p>
