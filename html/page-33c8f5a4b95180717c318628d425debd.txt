<p>Public opinion is an odd thing. Sometimes it’s absolutely spot on, while other times, well, the public is just plain wrong. But once faith has gone, it takes a painfully long time to rebuild. Once scorned, it’ll be a while before many of us go near that guilty party again, if ever. That is, if there’s even a choice. </p>
<p>And so, to Proton. Public confidence in the national carmaker has been tailing off for years, and not without good and/or (most likely and, for most of us) frequent reason. There was the <a href="http://paultan.org/2008/08/10/proton-waja-cps-test-drive-review/" target="_blank">Waja</a>, <a href="http://paultan.org/2008/08/13/proton-gen2-cps-highline-manual-test-drive/" target="_blank">Gen 2</a>, <a href="http://paultan.org/2010/03/18/proton-persona-elegance-facelift-officially-launched/" target="_blank">Persona</a>, <a href="http://paultan.org/2011/12/22/driven-proton-exora-bold-turbo-our-first-impressions/" target="_blank">Exora</a>, and last year, the much-awaited and hyped <a href="http://paultan.org/2012/05/15/driven-proton-preve-turbo-cvt-and-iafm-manual-tested/" target="_blank">Preve</a>. <p>The real turnaround will come only a few years down the line, when buyers get confident and feel they can put their hard-earned cash behind the vehicles from the first national car company once more. 					<h2 class="xlarge"><a href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/27/peugeot-208-hybrid-fe/" title="Permalink to Peugeot 208 HYbrid FE updated for Frankfurt 2013" rel="bookmark">Peugeot 208 HYbrid FE updated for Frankfurt 2013</a></h2>

						</p>
						
<!-- old share button location -->
<div style="margin-top: 5px; background-color: #f9f9f9; padding: 5px 0 5px 8px; border-top: 1px solid #dddddd; border-bottom: 1px solid #dddddd">
<div class="fb-like" data-href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/27/peugeot-208-hybrid-fe/" data-send="true" data-layout="button_count" data-width="150" data-show-faces="false" style="vertical-align:top;zoom:1;*display:inline"></div>
<div class="g-plusone" data-size="medium" data-href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/27/peugeot-208-hybrid-fe/"></div>
</div>

			        
					<div class="post-content">
						<p><a href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/27/peugeot-208-hybrid-fe/peugeot-208-hybrid-fe-03/" rel="attachment wp-att-194915"><img src="http://s4.paultan.org/image/2013/08/Peugeot-208-Hybrid-Fe-03-630x323.jpg" alt="Peugeot-208-Hybrid-Fe-03" width="630" height="323" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-194915" /></a></p>
<p>We first got wind of the Peugeot 208 HYbrid FE at the Geneva show earlier this year, but it&#8217;s presence at the show was only a teaser, with no actual car or real technical details revealed. The 208 HYbrid FE makes a proper debut at the Frankfurt show happening next month, and here are the initial details.</p>
<p>The hybrid 208 is a collaborated between Peugeot and Total. It&#8217;s a full petrol hybrid without plug-in functions. Acceleration is relatively brisk &#8211; it is said to hit 100km/h in 8 seconds despite having just a three-cylinder engine &#8211; this is a big improvement over the 14 seconds that a regular Peugoet 208 with a 1.0 litre three-cylinder engine takes.</p>
<p>The 208 Hybrid FE&#8217;s body is 25% more aerodynamic than a regular production 208. Modified body parts include a more aerodynamic body and roof design, a smaller front grille, bespoke low-resistance 19 inch Michelin tyres that are tall and thin, and rear cameras in place of door mirrors. Rear track is narrower by 40mm.</p>
<p><a href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/27/peugeot-208-hybrid-fe/peugeot-208-hybrid-fe-04/" rel="attachment wp-att-194914"><img src="http://s4.paultan.org/image/2013/08/Peugeot-208-Hybrid-Fe-04-630x416.jpg" alt="Peugeot-208-Hybrid-Fe-04" width="630" height="416" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-194914" /></a></p>
<p>There are big changes to the 208&#8242;s 1.0 litre three-cylinder motor in the base car. The engine is modified to use the Miller combustion cycle, and compression ratio is increased from 11:1 to a high 16:1. Such a high compression ratio increases chances of knocking, so Peugeot took some steps to avoid this. </p>
<p>The rate of unburned elements in the combustion chamber is reduced by valves of increased diameter, exhaust pipes are redesigned, as is the manifold, and an adapted valve lift pattern is introduced. Furthermore, the heat exchanges are optimised in the cylinder block by means of coolant passages arranged between the cylinders. The coolant now passes through the cylinder head along its entire length to recover the heat around each source: combustion chambers, between injectors and spark plugs.</p>
<p>Fuel injection switches from regular port injection to direct injection, which allows more precise measurement of fuel. The nitrated steel crankshaft, the titanium con rods and the aluminium-copper alloy pistons are cut from solid so they are lighter. Finally, the engine displacement has been increased to 1,233cc by increasing both the bore and stroke to 75mm by 93mm.</p>
<p><a href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/27/peugeot-208-hybrid-fe/peugeot-208-hybrid-fe-01/" rel="attachment wp-att-194916"><img src="http://s4.paultan.org/image/2013/08/Peugeot-208-Hybrid-Fe-01-630x318.jpg" alt="Peugeot-208-Hybrid-Fe-01" width="630" height="318" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-194916" /></a></p>
<p>The weight of the cylinder head, also cut from a solid block, is halved due to the reduced thicknesses permitted by its design and the characteristics of the aluminium used. The coolant circuit and the inlet line are made by rapid prototyping of resins charged with glass fibres or carbon. To do this, the laser beam of a 3D printer fuses successive layers of aluminium powder. This produces a functional component quickly and more cheaply in the context of individual specialised manufacture. </p>
<p>Peugeot claims friction losses which typically consume 20% of the engine power have been reduced by approximately 40%. A special 0W12 grade engine oil of extremely low viscosity has been developed by Total Lubricants using ultra-fluid based oils and by the addition of friction reducing agents containing molybdenum. Together with the other optimisations, the engine maintains the 68 horsepower rating of the unmodified engine while being more fuel efficient.</p>
<p>This engine is paired to an electric motor which is said to be built with the expertise gained from racing the Peugeot 908 HYbrid4 race car. The motor is only 7kg, and develops 30kW as a motor and 100kW as a brake generator. Maximum torque is 30Nm, and it can spin up to 40,000rpm. It is able to power the car alone in zero emissions mode. It&#8217;s powered by a lithium ion battery consisting of 90V cells, weighing 25kg. It&#8217;s installed with the 20 litre petrol tank on a subframe which is bolted under the rear bench seat.</p>
<p><a href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/27/peugeot-208-hybrid-fe/peugeot-208-hybrid-fe-02/" rel="attachment wp-att-194917"><img src="http://s5.paultan.org/image/2013/08/Peugeot-208-Hybrid-Fe-02-630x196.jpg" alt="Peugeot-208-Hybrid-Fe-02" width="630" height="196" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-194917" /></a></p>
<p>The 208 HYbrid FE&#8217;s gearbox also has weight reductions. The casing is cut from a solid block. To reduce friction losses, the teeth of the gears are given a tungsten carbide chrome coating. Greasing is redesigned to reduce the volume of lubricant necessary and prevent splashing over the gears, which also consumes energy. The prototype gearbox oil is developed by Total Lubricants based on bio-sourced oils, of 75W grade with a very high viscosity index (higher than 250).  This produces a 3% reduction in consumption. </p>
<p>With all the improvements above, you&#8217;ll see why 14 seconds in a normal 1.0 litre 208 drops to 8 seconds with this concept 208 hybrid All in all, Peugeot says the weight reduction, aerodynamics and tyres contribute about 4 seconds to the improvement in acceleration, while the electric motor reduces another 2 seconds. The air conditioning is gone too, BTW.</p>
<p>We&#8217;ll put up a proper gallery of the 208 HYbrid FE when Peugeot releases it.</p>

						<p><a href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/27/peugeot-3008-facelift/peugeot-3008-facelift-7/" rel="attachment wp-att-194729"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-194729" alt="peugeot-3008-facelift-7" src="http://s5.paultan.org/image/2013/08/peugeot-3008-facelift-7-e1377576141854-630x324.jpg" width="630" height="324" /></a></p>
<p>Peugeot has finally given the <a href="http://paultan.org/2013/03/15/spied-peugeot-3008-hybrid4-undergoing-testing-in-malaysia-is-the-diesel-hybrid-launching-soon/" target="_blank">3008</a> a rather-needed facelift &#8211; the <a href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/15/video-2014-peugeot-308-interior-design-explained/" target="_blank">308</a>-based crossover eschews its gaping mouth for the neater corporate face, joining the others in the family. This one&#8217;s also <a href="http://paultan.org/topics/special/2013-frankfurt-motor-show/" target="_blank">Frankfurt</a>-bound.</p>
<p>The facelifted Peugeot 3008 for global markets gets reworked head and tail lamps, plus extra chrome trim throughout and new-design alloys, which are available in 16- to 18-inch sizes. Changes are limited inside, but you&#8217;ll find new upholstery choices, a short and stubby automatic gear lever with a new surround and a flat-bottomed steering wheel with chromed inserts.</p>
<p>This is different from the <a href="http://paultan.org/2012/08/17/peugeot-3008-with-new-face-surfaces-in-china/" target="_blank">Chinese-market Peugeot 3008 facelift</a>, which has actually been on sale there for half a year already &#8211; that one has a different grille, headlamps, fog lamp housings and front apron, not to mention quite a bit of chrome and the addition of roof rails. The <a href="http://paultan.org/2012/09/19/peugeot-3008-napapijri-another-exclusive-edition/" target="_blank">pre-facelift model</a> was imported into China; the facelift is built by the <a href="http://paultan.org/2013/07/04/dongfeng-buying-into-psa-peugeot-citroen/" target="_blank">Dongfeng-PSA</a> joint venture, and isn&#8217;t sold anywhere else.</p>

<p>Back to the global-market facelift model &#8211; powerplants include a 115 hp 1.6 litre in e-HDi FAP and HDi FAP versions, a 2.0 litre HDi FAP in 150 hp and 163 hp states of tune, a 120 hp 1.6 litre VTi and a 1.6 litre THP in 155 hp and 163 hp states of tune.</p>
<p>The <a href="http://paultan.org/2013/03/15/spied-peugeot-3008-hybrid4-undergoing-testing-in-malaysia-is-the-diesel-hybrid-launching-soon/" target="_blank">HYbrid4</a> variant sees a 2.0 litre HDi FAP (driving the front wheels) working together with a 37 hp electric motor (driving the rear wheels) to provide a combined output of 200 hp. This one&#8217;s capable of returning an impressive 3.4 litres per 100 km. The <a href="http://paultan.org/2013/03/15/spied-peugeot-3008-hybrid4-undergoing-testing-in-malaysia-is-the-diesel-hybrid-launching-soon/" target="_blank">pre-facelift 3008 HYbrid4</a> was spotted at JPJ back in March.</p>

					<h2 class="xlarge"><a href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/27/peugeot-308-r/" title="Permalink to Peugeot 308 R concept to premiere in Frankfurt" rel="bookmark">Peugeot 308 R concept to premiere in Frankfurt</a></h2>

						<p class="postmetadata">
							<span class="category">In <a href="http://paultan.org/topics/special/2013-frankfurt-motor-show/" title="View all posts in 2013 Frankfurt Motor Show" rel="category tag">2013 Frankfurt Motor Show</a>, <a href="http://paultan.org/topics/cars/" title="View all posts in Cars" rel="category tag">Cars</a>, <a href="http://paultan.org/topics/cars/concepts/" title="View all posts in Concept Cars" rel="category tag">Concept Cars</a>, <a href="http://paultan.org/topics/intl-news/" title="View all posts in International News" rel="category tag">International News</a>, <a href="http://paultan.org/topics/cars/peugeot/" title="View all posts in Peugeot" rel="category tag">Peugeot</a></span>							 / <span class="author">By <a href="http://paultan.org/author/jonathanjamestan/" title="Posts by Jonathan James Tan" rel="author">Jonathan James Tan</a></span>							 / <time datetime="2013-08-27" pubdate>27 August 2013 9:35 am</time>							 / <a href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/27/peugeot-308-r/#comments" title="Comment on Peugeot 308 R concept to premiere in Frankfurt">27 comments</a>					
						</p>
						
<!-- old share button location -->

						<p><a href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/27/peugeot-308-r/peugeot-308-r-concept-frankfurt-1/" rel="attachment wp-att-194691"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-194691" alt="peugeot-308-r-concept-frankfurt-1" src="http://s4.paultan.org/image/2013/08/peugeot-308-r-concept-frankfurt-1-e1377511464144-630x363.jpg" width="630" height="363" /></a></p>
<p>It looks like the <a href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/15/video-2014-peugeot-308-interior-design-explained/" target="_blank">second-gen Peugeot 308</a> won&#8217;t be debuting in <a href="http://paultan.org/topics/special/2013-frankfurt-motor-show/" target="_blank">Frankfurt</a> alone &#8211; the first images and details of a hot Peugeot 308 R concept have emerged ahead of its debut in Frankfurt.</p>
<p>Resplendent in a head-turning two-tone red and black dress, the Peugeot 308 R concept sits 26 mm lower than the base 308, and its track is 30 mm wider as well.</p>
<p>The car sports a bodykit comprising side skirts and bigger bumpers that boast massive and densely-slatted air intakes. The front grille gets a unique treatment that sort of makes it look like a chequered flag, the bonnet gets twin louvres and the side mirrors are sharper-ended.</p>
<p>Special bodywork components help distinguish the 308 R Concept from the normal 308 &#8211; made of carbon-fibre, the doors, front wings, the body sides and sills, bonnet and bumpers are secured to the metal structure, which retains only the roof and tailgate of the original.</p>
<p><a href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/27/peugeot-308-r/peugeot-308-r-concept-frankfurt-3/" rel="attachment wp-att-194693"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-194693" alt="peugeot-308-r-concept-frankfurt-3" src="http://s5.paultan.org/image/2013/08/peugeot-308-r-concept-frankfurt-3-e1377596149370-630x374.jpg" width="630" height="374" /></a></p>
<p>Huge 19-inch five twin-spoke alloys &#8211; wrapped with 235/35 tyres &#8211; are crammed into the wheel arches, and braking system components include four-piston calipers and Alcon discs (380 mm front, 330 mm rear).</p>
<p>Look round the back and you&#8217;ll find slim twin tailpipes that sit flush with the bumper. You&#8217;ll also see a roof spoiler that&#8217;s recessed in the middle. Blink and you&#8217;ll miss a tiny &#8216;R&#8217; badge just ahead of the front doors.</p>
<p>The 308 R features the same 1.6 litre EP6CDTR THP engine found in the <a href="http://paultan.org/2013/07/17/peugeot-rcz-r-goodwood-video/" target="_blank">Peugeot RCZ R</a>, with output numbers being 270 hp at 6,000 rpm and 330 Nm of torque between 1,900 and 5,500 rpm. The power is deployed to the front wheels through a six-speed manual gearbox, with a Torsen differential in attendance.</p>
<p>A sign of things to come from the French carmaker&#8217;s newest C-segmenter? <em>Mais oui!</em></p>

<p>We were right &#8211; the <a href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/24/mystery-concept-car-goes-viral/">mystery concept car</a> that we showed you over the weekend is in fact a Volvo. The shape of the grille and the shape of the hood was unmistakable! This teaser video was released today on the Volvo Cars News YouTube channel, which features the same concept car.</p>
<p>The video shows a design sketching the same &#8216;letter T sideways&#8217; design that we saw in the concept car&#8217;s headlamps, and somewhere at the 11 second mark we see a split-second flash of a concept car. The flash is really too brief to see, but since we can pause the video we managed to screencap it for you.</p>
<p>The <a href="http://paultan.org/2013/08/26/volvo-mystery-concept-car-identity-revealed/volvo-concept-2/">badging on the rear</a> says Concept C Coupe, so we&#8217;re guessing that it could preview a future C-segment coupe? It looks like the Volvo C70 2-door coupe is making a comeback! The teaser video&#8217;s description mentions Frankfurt, so we&#8217;ll probably see this in the metal then. Until then, have a look at the 5 screen captures from the video in the small gallery below.</p>
