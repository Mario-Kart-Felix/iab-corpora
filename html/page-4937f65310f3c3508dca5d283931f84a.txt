<p><b>Breakdown cover</b> is a service that provides assistance to motorists, or bicyclists,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> whose vehicles have suffered a mechanical failure that is significant enough to leave them stranded at their present location.</p>
<p>In most areas, the service is provided by competing commercial service providers, with a set yearly fee to purchase the service. In some areas, there is a government-sponsored or -sanctioned monopoly, and the service may be in the form of an insurance policy with premiums, instead of a member subscription fee.</p>
<p>In Europe, it is popularly available via each country's national automobile membership association, but may also be made available as part of the service of a <a href="/wiki/Vehicle_insurance" title="Vehicle insurance">vehicle insurance</a> company, or other companies whose primary business is to offer such assistance. Many automobile manufacturers offer roadside assistance for their customers, sometimes for free for some period after the purchase of a new vehicle.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roadside_assistance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roadside_assistance&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The term <i>breakdown cover</i> is most common in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> elsewhere, it may also be referred to as <b>emergency roadside repair</b> or <b>roadside assistance</b>. Such services originated with early national <a href="/wiki/Motor_club" title="Motor club">motoring organisations</a>, such as the member clubs of the United States' <a href="/wiki/American_Automobile_Association" title="American Automobile Association" class="mw-redirect">American Automobile Association</a> (AAA), the United Kingdom's Automobile Association (AA), Germany's <a href="/wiki/ADAC" title="ADAC">Allgemeiner Deutscher Automobil-Club</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Automobile_Association" title="Canadian Automobile Association">Canadian Automobile Association</a> (CAA). Many of these associations were founded as membership-based clubs for early enthusiast motorists; services to assist members were introduced sometime later, with the creation of a fleet of assistance vehicles. In the case of the UK AA, these were traditionally <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle" title="Motorcycle">motorcycle</a>-mounted prior to the introduction of <a href="/wiki/Van" title="Van">vans</a>.</p>
<p>When communication technology and availability made it practical, a network of <a href="/wiki/Emergency_telephone" title="Emergency telephone">emergency phone boxes</a>, placed at intervals by the roadside, was introduced in some countries. In recent years, the widespread ownership of <a href="/wiki/Mobile_phones" title="Mobile phones" class="mw-redirect">mobile phones</a> has, to a large degree, supplanted the need for an emergency phone network.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roadside_assistance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Services">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roadside_assistance&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Services" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Breakdown cover may include <a href="/wiki/Jump_start_(vehicle)" title="Jump start (vehicle)" class="mw-redirect">jump starting</a> an <a href="/wiki/Automobile" title="Automobile">automobile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tow_truck" title="Tow truck">towing</a> a <a href="/wiki/Vehicle" title="Vehicle">vehicle</a>, helping to change a <a href="/wiki/Flat_tire" title="Flat tire">flat tire</a>, providing a small amount of <a href="/wiki/Fuel" title="Fuel">fuel</a> when a vehicle <a href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/runs_out" class="extiw" title="wikt:runs out">runs out</a> of it, pulling out a vehicle that is stuck in <a href="/wiki/Snow" title="Snow">snow</a> or helping people who are <a href="/wiki/Key_(lock)" title="Key (lock)">locked</a> out of their cars.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>There are also many private roadside assistance companies in the United States. Most can offer these services for a fair price without being a member to a large motor club group.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Volunteerism">Volunteerism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roadside_assistance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Volunteerism">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roadside_assistance&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Volunteerism" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most roadside assistance is done via contracted services, as described above. Many large Jewish communities on the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast of the United States</a> offer a free, volunteer service called <a href="/wiki/Chaverim_(volunteers)" title="Chaverim (volunteers)">Chaverim</a> ("friends"), that performs similar services, supported by donations. Most Chaverim organizations usually do not offer towing, and have limited geographic service areas.</p>
