<h1>2011 APEAL Study Results</h1>
</div>
<div class="yui3-u-1">
<span class="gray">7/27/2011</span>
</div>
</div>
<div class="content">
<div>In spite of high consumer demand for tech-related features, increasing government fuel economy and safety standards, and ever-changing market conditions, auto manufacturers are managing to design and build vehicles that owners find appealing. Indeed, owner satisfaction with the performance and design of their new vehicles is at its highest levels since 2006, according to a new study from J.D. Power and Associates.<br><br>The <em>J.D. Power and Associates 2011 Automotive Performance, Execution and Layout (APEAL) Study<sup>SM</sup></em> examines how gratifying a new vehicle is to own and drive. The study reveals that the industry average APEAL score in 2011 increases by 3 index points from last year-to 781 (on a 1,000-point scale) from 778 in 2010. The <em>2011 APEAL Study</em> is based on owner evaluations of more than 90 vehicle attributes, and complements the recently released <a href="/content/detail.htm?jdpaArticleId=1481" target="_blank"><em>J.D. Power and Associates 2011 US Initial Quality Study<sup>SM</sup></em> (IQS)</a>, which focuses on problems experienced by owners during the first 90 days of ownership. <br><br><b>The APEAL Study measures owner satisfaction in 10 categories:</b><a href="/consumer-ratings/awardees.htm" target="_blank" title="2011 APEAL Study Award Recipients Photo Gallery"><img alt="2011 APEAL Study Award Recipients" src="http://images.dealer.com/jdpa/editorial_topics/2011-apeal/apeal11-award-recipients-gallery-1.jpg" align="right" border="0" height="254" vspace="10" width="310"></a></div><ul><li>Fuel Economy</li><li>Audio/Entertainment/Navigation</li><li>Vehicle Interior</li><li>Seats</li><li>Engine</li><li>HVAC</li><li>Storage</li><li>Visibility</li><li>Driving Experience</li><li>Vehicle Exterior</li></ul><br>Of the 10 APEAL measures, Vehicle Exterior, Interior, and Seats have improved the most, each by 6 index points, compared to 2010. Fuel Economy is the only measure that has decreased from last year-by 17 index points-likely due to increasing gas prices, which are about 30% higher compared to the same time period last year.<br> <div><br><br><br><br><table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" height="120" width="540">      <tbody><tr>        <td height="25" valign="middle" width="20">b&gt;</td>        <td width="255"><a href="/consumer-ratings/awardees.htm" target="_blank" title="2011 APEAL Study Award Recipients Photo Gallery"><img alt="Gallery: 2011 APEAL Award Recipients" src="http://images.dealer.com/jdpa/editorial_topics/2011-apeal/apeal11-button-awardrecipients-2011.jpg" align="left" border="0" height="50" width="255"></a></td>        <td width="10">&nbsp;</td>        <td width="255"><a href="/content/detail.htm?page=3&amp;jdpaArticleId=1482" title="2011 APEAL Study: Top 3 Models by Category"><img alt="2011 APEAL: Top 3 Models by Category" src="http://images.dealer.com/jdpa/editorial_topics/2011-apeal/apeal11-button-top3-2011.jpg" align="left" border="0" height="50" width="255"></a></td>      </tr>      <tr>        <td height="25" valign="middle" width="20">b&gt;</td>        <td width="255"><a href="/consumer-ratings/ratings.htm?study=909201135" title="2011 APEAL Study: Ratings by Category"><img alt="2011 APEAL: Ratings by Category" src="http://images.dealer.com/jdpa/editorial_topics/2011-apeal/apeal11-button-ratingscat-2011.jpg" align="left" border="0" height="50" width="255"></a></td>        <td width="10">&nbsp;</td>        <td width="255"><a href="/consumer-ratings/ratings.htm?study=909201136" target="_blank" title="2011 APEAL Study: Ratings by Brand"><img alt="2011 APEAL: Ratings by Brand" src="http://images.dealer.com/jdpa/editorial_topics/2011-apeal/apeal11-button-ratingsbrand-2011.jpg" align="left" border="0" height="50" width="255"></a></td>      </tr>    </tbody></table></div>
