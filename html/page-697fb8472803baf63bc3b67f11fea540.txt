			<h1 class="articleTitle">Motorcycle Mechanic Degree Program Overviews and Career Info</h1>

			<p class="date">
				
			</p>
			<p class="wikiDescription">Motorcycle mechanic degree programs are offered by community colleges and trade schools at the associate and bachelor's degree level. Students learn to repair and maintain motorcycles and other types of vehicles with small engines. Some schools also partner with motorcycle manufacturers to offer specialized training for a specific brand.

			
			

			</p>

			<div class="wikiContent">
				
						<h2 id="section---AssociateOfAppliedScienceInMotorcycleServiceTechnology">Associate of Applied Science in Motorcycle Service Technology</h2>
<p>Associate degree programs in motorcycle service technology train students to perform many aspects of motorcycle repair. In addition to learning to work on foreign and American bikes, many programs also train students to work on other types of recreational equipment, such as snowmobiles, scooters, watercraft and all-terrain vehicles. Students learn to diagnose problems, make repairs and install computerized components. They also learn the basics of repair shop operation, including interacting with customers, providing estimates and ordering parts.
</p>
<h3 id="section---EducationalPrerequisites">Educational Prerequisites</h3>
<p>Admission to associate degree programs generally requires proof of high school graduation or a GED. High school courses in math, science and automotive maintenance can provide a solid background for <a class="external" href="http://education-portal.com/articles/BMW_Motorcycle_Mechanic_Job_Description_and_Requirements_for_Becoming_a_BMW_Motorcycle_Mechanic.html">motorcycle mechanic</a> associate degree programs.
</p>
<h3 id="section---ProgramCoursework">Program Coursework</h3>
<p>Coursework in motorcycle mechanic degree programs includes classroom and lab instruction covering engines, fuel and electrical systems, brakes, transmissions and body work. Many programs also include business, computer and accounting fundamentals. Course topics often address:
</p>
<ul><li>Theory of small engines
</li><li>Motorcycle drive systems
</li><li>Applied mathematics
</li><li>Introduction to electronics
</li><li>Troubleshooting and diagnostics
</li><li>Business law
</li><li>Communication in the workplace
</li><li>Motorcycle frame welding
</li><li>Introduction to hydraulics
</li></ul><h3 id="section---SalaryInformationAndEmploymentOutlook">Salary Information and Employment Outlook</h3>
<p>The Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) predicted average job growth of nearly nine percent for <a class="external" href="http://education-portal.com/harley_davidson_motorcycle_mechanics_diploma_programs_in_wy.html">motorcycle mechanics</a> during the 2008-2018 period, increasing the number of jobs from 18,800 to 20,500. Dealers employ the largest number of motorcycle mechanics; however, many also work for independent repair shops or are self-employed. In 2006, the annual median wage for mechanical mechanics was $14.45 per hour.
</p>
<h3 id="section---ContinuingEducationInformation">Continuing Education Information</h3>
<p>Many states have license requirements for motorcycle mechanics and permit requirements for trainees. Applicants must often pass a written exam to obtain a license and may need to complete additional training for license renewal. Employers may also require motorcycle mechanics to take additional certification courses or to obtain training to work on bikes made by specific manufacturers, such as Yamaha, Honda or Suzuki. Some schools also offer degree programs affiliated with a particular manufacturer.
</p>
<h2 id="section---AssociateSDegreeInHarleyDavidsonMotorcycleRepairAmpMaintenance">Associate's Degree in Harley-Davidson Motorcycle Repair &amp; Maintenance</h2>
<p>This is a manufacturer-specific degree program preparing students for careers working with a particular brand of motorcycle, the Harley-Davidson. Students may earn Associate of Applied Science degrees while preparing for careers with Harley-Davidson in fields ranging from service to sales. Programs usually take two years to complete and include classroom instruction, hands-on training and an internship.
</p>
<h3 id="section---EducationalPrerequisites-2">Educational Prerequisites</h3>
<p>There are no educational requirements specific to Harley-Davidson associate degree programs; however, students must meet the admission requirements of the institution offering the program such as a high school diploma or GED, placement test and ACT scores may also be required.
</p>
<h3 id="section---ProgramCoursework-2">Program Coursework</h3>
<p>Students learn the basics of Harley-Davidson diagnostics, repair and maintenance.  Coursework also covers sales and marketing, technical writing and information about the Harley-Davidson company, such as history and manufacturing. Programs also require the completion of a 320-hour internship at a Harley-Davidson dealership or factory. Coursework topics include:
</p>
<ul><li>Harley-Davidson electricity basics
</li><li>Harley-Davidson suspension and frames
</li><li>Harley-Davidson drive system
</li><li>Harley-Davidson engines
</li><li>Harley-Davidson management
</li></ul><h3 id="section---PopularCareerOptions">Popular Career Options</h3>
<p>Students who graduate from Harley-Davidson degree programs can pursue a variety of careers, ranging from manufacturing to service to sales. Some students may also pursue careers as Harley-Davidson instructors. Job titles include:
</p>
<ul><li>Technician
</li><li>Parts manager
</li><li>Service consultant
</li><li>Sales associate
</li><li>Technical advisor
</li></ul><h3 id="section---ContinuingEducationInformation-2">Continuing Education Information</h3>
<p>Some schools offer bachelor's degrees in motorcycle technology, towards which credits earned in associate's degree programs may be applied.  At select schools, graduates of Harley-Davidson associate's degree programs may pursue bachelor of applied science degrees with a Harley-Davidson motorcycle service technology emphasis.
</p>
<h2 id="section---BachelorOfAppliedScienceDegreeInTechnology">Bachelor of Applied Science Degree in Technology</h2>
<p>Some schools offer Bachelor of Applied Science degree programs in technology, with an emphasis in motorcycle service technology. These programs have been designed specifically for graduates of technical associate degree programs. Having completed most of the technical courses at the associate degree level, bachelor's degree programs in this field emphasize skills that may be used in management careers. Students in these programs learn about engineering concepts and automotive industry management principles, while also strengthening communication and leadership skills.
</p>
<h3 id="section---EducationalPrerequisites-3">Educational Prerequisites</h3>
<p>Students must often complete an associate degree program in motorcycle technology, or a related field, from an accredited institution before enrolling in a bachelor's degree program in motorcycle technology. Technical associate program credits may usually be applied toward the completion of a Bachelor of Applied Science degree.
</p>
<h3 id="section---ProgramCoursework-3">Program Coursework</h3>
<p>Bachelor of Applied Science degree programs with an emphasis in motorcycle technology are often designed to complete education begun at the associate degree level in the same field. Coursework at the bachelor's degree level often emphasizes technical theory, business management and general studies. Upper-division course topics may include:
</p>
<ul><li>Professional development
</li><li>Industrial supervision
</li><li>Part design
</li><li>Sales and marketing
</li><li>Automotive service management
</li></ul><h3 id="section---PopularCareerOptions-2">Popular Career Options</h3>
<p>Graduates of Bachelor of Applied Science programs in motorcycle technology have strong technical and management skills, preparing them for careers such as:
</p>
<ul><li>Motorcycle dealership management
</li><li>Service manager
</li><li>Sales associate</li></ul>

        function bindCloseToBody() {
            $('body').one('mousedown', function() {
                $('.adInfoTooltip, .adInfoTooltip img').removeClass('active');
            });

        function evalOffsets() {
            if ($('.adInfoTooltip img.active').length > 0) {
                computeTooltipOffsetX();
                computeTooltipOffsetY();
            }
        }

        function computeTooltipOffsetX() {
            $('.adInfoTooltip img.active').not('.schoolsCap .adInfoTooltip img').each(function () {
                var containerWidth = $('#container').width();
                var windowWidth = $(window).width();
                var newOffset = -1 * ((windowWidth - containerWidth) / 2);

                if (newOffset < -191) {
                    newOffset = -191; // this val matches the CSS at pageload ie default;
                }
                else if (newOffset > 0) {
                    newOffset = 0; // if the window is smaller than the container;
                }
                $(this).css('right', newOffset + 'px');
            });
        }

        function computeTooltipOffsetY() {
            //for bottom tool tip when on search page and there is no text above search results
            var tt = $('.schoolsCap .adInfoTooltip img.active');

            tt.each(function () {
                var ofs = $(this).offset();


                console.log(ofs);
                if (ofs.top <= 74) {
                    $(this).css({top: -69 + 'px'});
                } else if(ofs.top < 74) {
                    $(this).css({top: -106 + 'px'});
                }
            });
        }
