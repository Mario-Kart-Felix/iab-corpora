		<h1 class="entry-title">Motorcycle Mechanic Schools in NH</h1>

	    <div class="entry-content">
		    <p>People who are interested in engine repair and mechanics may find a career as a motorcycle technician to be an interesting and rewarding path. For most people, the beginning of a journey into a career in motorcycle repair will begin with choosing a motorcycle repair school. Motorcycle schools in New Hampshire generally require students to complete a core curriculum that covers the basics of how a motorcycle works and troubleshooting mechanical issues. <a href="http://findautomotiveschools.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/Motorcycle-Mechanic-Schools-in-New-Hampshire.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-1568" alt="Motorcycle-Mechanic-Schools-in-New-Hampshire" src="http://findautomotiveschools.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/Motorcycle-Mechanic-Schools-in-New-Hampshire-300x180.jpg" width="300" height="180" /></a>After completing the core classes, students are typically allowed the opportunity to specialize. The Wyotech curriculum offers examples of common program concentrations from which students may choose.</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Applied Service Management</strong></span> - Students who wish to manage a department within a motorcycle repair shop or even own a motorcycle shop will likely find a concentration in Applied Service Management to be helpful. Through this program, students learn the theory behind running a repair shop, securing financing and investors, creating a business plan, and managing repair shop records. Completion of the Applied Service Management concentration prepares students for a career as a fleet manager, shop manager/operator, service writer, service adviser, or owner.</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Harley-Davidson Concentration</strong></span> - The Harley-Davidson Concentration prepares students disassemble, diagnose, service, and inspect Big Twin and V-Rod engines. Students also learn to perform necessary adjustments and measurements. Completion of the Harley-Davidson concentration equips students to perform duties in an entry level position at a dealership that services Harley-Davidson motorcycles or to work at a performance oriented shop or specialty business.</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>European Concentration</strong></span> - Wyotech&#8217;s European concentration focuses on Triumph and Ducati motorcycles. Students learn to test and repair the engines, ancillary components, electrical systems, transmissions, and fuel systems of bikes that are made by these two manufacturers.</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Asian Concentration</strong></span> - The Asian concentration equips students with a skill set to diagnose, repair, and service modern motorcycles that are produced by Asian manufacturers. The manufacturers that are covered in the courses include Honda, Kawasaki, Yamaha, and Suzuki.</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Off-Road Power Concentration</strong></span> - The Off-Road Power Concentration teaches students repair and diagnostic skills for personal watercraft, ATVs, and snowmobiles. Students learn to use test equipment and manufacturer-approved diagnostic and repair procedures.</p>
<h2>Motorcycle Mechanic Specialization</h2>
<p>The Universal Technical Institute&#8217;s Motorcycle Mechanic Institute allows students to specialize in working on specific brands after completing their core curriculum. After completing a 36 week program that covers core skills followed by a 12 week elective, students may choose a manufacturer specialty elective that may last from 12 to 24 weeks. Featured manufactutrers include Honda/Hontech, Suzuki/FAST, Yamaha/YAMPRO, Harley-Davidson, Kawasaki/K-Tech Specialist, and BMW/Motorrad.</p>
<h2>Certifications</h2>
<p>Students who wish to become motorcycle technicians have several opportunities to enhance their education and job prospects by obtaining a certification or participating in a recognized program. Automotive Youth Educational Systems in a program in which high school and technical students may participate that connects students to proper training and potential future employers in the mechanical field.</p>
<p>The National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence offers ASE certification, which is available to technicians who successfully complete the organization&#8217;s exam. Certification ensures future employers and customers that a technician has demonstrated competence and possessed the minimum level of expertise and skill that is required by the organization. Becoming ASE certified can enhance job prospects and is an attractive credential that repair shops often display to their customers.</p>
<p>The National Automotive Technicians Education Foundation provides accreditation to schools which offer programs that meet their educational standards. Students may also enhance their employment prospects by attending a school that is NATEF accredited as employers may rest assured that the potential employee has received the training that is required to performed his or her job duties.</p>
<h2>Motorcycle Mechanic Salary in NH</h2>
<p>The average yearly motorcycle mechanic salary in New Hampshire is slightly more than $37,500. Motorboat mechanics and mechanics who service recreational vehicles earn roughly the same.</p>
<h2>Mechanic Jobs in New Hampshire</h2>
<p>Potential employers for motorcycle repair technicians include dealerships. Dealerships that are located in New Hampshire include Honda, Kawasaki, Suzuki, BMW, Triumph, and Harley-Davidson. Technicians may also be employed by shops motorcycle enthusiasts may frequent to enhance tuning and performance of their vehicles or general shops that service motorcycles of all varieties. Students who study repair and diagnostics of recreational vehicles may find additional opportunities at shops that service vehicles like snowmobiles, ATVs, and watercraft. New Hampshire is also home to the Rockon motorcycle manufacturer, which manufactures two wheeled off-road motorcycles.</p>
<p>For people who are mechanically inclined, a career as a motorcycle technician may provide mental stimulation and the excitement of working with vehicles that are enjoyed as a hobby by people from many different walks of life.</p>
