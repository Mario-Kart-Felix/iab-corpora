				<blockquote class="postcontent restore">
					I just came back from a 315 kilometer trip with the Odyssey. What a wicked van. I am impressed with the handling and power of that minivan. <br />
<br />
It's extremely comfortable, it's easy to find the right driving position, accelerations are fun, quiet and smooth engine. At traffic lights, you really have to check the tach to see if the engine is running.<br />
<br />
I didnt like the center stack, where the radio and temperature controls are. The radio is placed a bit too high. The shifter is ugly and badly placed. Still prefer the column shifter from the DCX minivans. The gauges are really nice, someking of a LED lighting, very nice.<br />
<br />
The interior is well finished, the door panels are thick and close like a tank. The materials they used to finish the interior as a rich texture with excellent fit and finish. Cabin is quiet, even at 120 km/h. Suspension is solid, no wierd noise, front or rear. I find the independent rear suspension does help alot with the handling. Liked it.<br />
<br />
I got some numbers with gas mileage. I did the trip back and forth, cruise set at 110 km/h, drove down down Ottawa and back, giving me an impressive 30mpg (canadian, approx 26mpg US). Giving the engine size, it seems like very good gas mileage.<br />
<br />
They are priced approximately the same as the DCX minivans. Bought new, the Odyssey is a good purchase (local dealer advertise '06 Odyssey with $1500 off and financing from 2,9% for 60 months). On the used car market, nothing will beat the DCX minivans.
				</blockquote>

</div> How many remember when &quot;Town &amp; Country&quot; was a luxury minivan that was fully loaded? <img src="http://forum.chryslerminivan.org/images/smilies/confused.png" border="0" alt="" title="Confused" class="inlineimg" /> <br />
 <br />
My sister's 1986 Caravan SE had a very nice, comfortable interior with carpet and soft cloth completely covering the doors. Fast forward to January 2000 when the Plymouth name was dropped and the former 2000 Plymouth Voyager and Grand Voyager became the 2000 Chrysler Voyager and Chrysler Grand Voyager on the same vehicle that had NO carpet and NO soft cloth on the doors.<br />
 <br />
I realized in 2002 that my Chrysler T&amp;C LX with L option (called T&amp;C eL) was really a Plymouth Grand Voyager SE. It had NO carpet and NO soft cloth on the door panels. Up through 2000, a Chrysler T&amp;C LX was a well equipped, nice minivan but sadly, in 2001, the T&amp;C LX was nothing but a Plymouth Grand Voyager SE.<br />
 <br />
People are voting with their checkbooks and the Odyssey EX-L, Touring, Sienna XLE and Limited have become the luxury minivans. Chrysler has fooled no one by placing the Chrysler Town &amp; Country name on what is really a Plymouth.<img src="images/avatars/jpshakehead.gif" border="0" alt="" title="Jpshakehead" class="inlineimg" /> <br />
 <br />
Placing a little fake wood on the center portion of the dash and doors and calling it a Chrysler Town &amp; Country Limited does NOT magically change a plain, cheap plastic trimmed Plymouth into a luxury minivan. <img src="http://forum.chryslerminivan.org/images/uploadedsmilies/Wow1.gif" border="0" alt="" title="Wow1" class="inlineimg" /> <img src="http://forum.chryslerminivan.org/images/uploadedsmilies/nut.gif" border="0" alt="" title="Nut" class="inlineimg" />
