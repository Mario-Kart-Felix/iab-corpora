June 24th, 2013.<br />
Let me first start off by saying that my name is Billy and I am a prospective student of this school. I have rated things at middle rates, because I have not attended NADC, now currently known as LCT. I will be posting what I think about it today, after financial aid, six months, one year, and after graduation. The reason I am doing so is because I see so many lies and "good" comments about this school and it is difficult to believe, because they don't have any factual information to prove they are not some lowlife or another school rep or this schools rep. So here is what I thought of today.<p>Today I went for a tour of the school with my wife Anne, who I absolutely adore. After the tour I was suppose to meet up with my rep, Frank D. First thoughts as I was driving from Murfreesboro, TN to find this place, was "I need to buy some type of gun, oh wait now I don't, never mind I need one." The reasoning of why I thought that was because on the trip there, there were nice cars such as Mercedes and Chrysler's,and nice buildings. Then I drive a little and see run down ghetto looking places with graffiti and junker, creepy cars and this continued until I got to the college.<p>The college at first sight seemed run down and have been around for a while and I thought could use some sprucing up, but then I thought, it was built in 1919 and some expansions were probably added, but those could have been in 1920's or 1930's, I don't know, I would have to check the building records. So for such a old school, it isn't in that bad of shape. The location, I personally would not want my wife walking alone or even with me. I would agree with some comments that, if you are staying at the dorms, please walk with a huge crowd, or pack some heat, because it does look like an uneasy place to be at night. As I went on my tour I noticed the inside looked and felt like a mechanic shop, it was a great feeling especially if you love cars and love the feeling of being in a shop. It had that distinct smell, tons of engines, transmissions, parts, and cars. Now what I mean, by a mechanic shop was, no A/C just the wind and air from outside and the noise of people working on vehicles, but at the same time, it did look run downed, but come on it's a 1919 building. <p>       This is my thought of the building and site and location.<br /> My rep, Frank D. was a good guy. He talked like a car salesman, but at the same time was honest about it. He told me he was "the devils advocate", when I told him I am going home to talk about it more with my wife. He was a former Marine and had "USMC" tattooed on his right, front fore-arm. He was real sweet though and understanding about how I have a child on the way. Yes, people that want money will say anything and do anything to get you to join, but honestly, in my opinion he didn't worry to much about it. He told us not to worry about the money and look as an investment, which trust me at 26,520.00 dollars, that not in reg fee or the tools if you want them. <p>A). Registration fee is 100.00 dollars, can pay 25 dollars on reg fee and they will put 75 in the financial aid department.<p>B). Tools are 998.00 dollars, which can be added to your tuition cost if you do not want to pay up front. ,<p>I was thinking about the money, but the more I thought about it, I am not taking English, Math, Science, etc etc., I am taking just what I need to get in and get out to start my career. Which, to me 30,000 thousand is not a lot of money when you get to skip all those "non-important" classes, you get help with job placement, if you need a "LOA" (Leave of absence) you talk to the campus management and have an exit interview with the officials of the Education Department and they will determine what's in the best interest of the student, meaning you can have up to 180 calender days off from school and pick up right from where you left off. I know, "they will determine what's in the best interest of the student" could mean they won't grant it, but if it is a death or birth of a family member, meaning Mother, Father, Son, Daughter, Wife, Father-in-law, etc etc., they, by state and federal law CAN NOT punish you or tell you no about taking "LOA" if it is for direct family.<p>     So, my overall thought about NADC/LCT so far, is it is in a rough looking neighborhood, but as long as you stay in a big group you will be fine. The building is old and needs to be updated, but renovations cost money, and that at the moment is not on their to do list, so be happy you have a place to learn that is so close to home. Finally, the rep's do seem to actually care, because Frank, would stop talking and listen to mine and my wife's questions and answer them, and personally they seem 50/50 on the money issue. They do want some money, but who doesn't? Also they don't care about the money, it's a tough experience to explain, so I guess to see what I mean, go and check out the college.<p><i>Billy</i><br /> Hoping to be a student and graduate from LCT in 2014.Have questions?
