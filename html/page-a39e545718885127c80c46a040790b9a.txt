<p>
   Japanese artist Chicara Nagata is showing &#8220;art pieces, most commonly known as motorcycles&#8221; at <a onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.mbandf.com/mad-gallery/current/index.php?');"  href="http://www.mbandf.com/mad-gallery/current/index.php?" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">MB&amp;F&#8217;s M.A.D. Gallery</a> in Geneva until February, 2013. For the past 20 years, Chicara Nagata has been dedicating his entire energy &#8220;to the creation of art pieces, most commonly known as motorcycles.&#8221; says M.A.D. Gallery. Chicara draws, crafts and assembles close to 500 components that are then added to a vintage engine, manufactured between 1939 and 1966. The number of pieces and the time spent on each machine gives a completely different dimension to the motorcycle. &#8220;His obsession for detail is not surprising when you know that he is a graphic designer who spent nearly 3 years into the completion of his first piece of art CHICARA ONE, he finished in 2004 after 7500 hours of work.&#8221; The unique pieces, named Chicara Art one to four, have won six consecutive awards at shows in Belgium, France, and Germany &#8220;before garnering global recognition at the AMDChampionship (the most recognized custom bike award worldwide).&#8221; Take a look!
