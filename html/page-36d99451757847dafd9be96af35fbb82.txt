<p>Infiniti says it will reveal an all-new sports sedan at the Detroit<a target="_blank" title=" auto show" href=" http://www.automobilemag.com/auto_shows/"> auto show</a> in January 2013 &#8212; but the company won&#8217;t say what the car will be. While there&#8217;s a strong chance the Detroit debut will be the next Infiniti G sedan, it could also be a front-wheel-drive sedan that uses a Mercedes-Benz platform.</p>
<p>We know that Infiniti is hard at work revamping its G sedan, and we&#8217;ve even <a target="_blank" href="http://rumors.automobilemag.com/caught-camouflaged-infiniti-g-sedan-in-germany-175203.html">spied a camouflaged test car</a> running in Germany. The current car (pictured) hasn&#8217;t had a major revamp since its introduction in 2007, and it desperately needs upgrades to stay competitive with European rivals like the BMW 3-Series. Rumors suggest the<a title=" new car" href=" http://www.automobilemag.com/new_cars/"> new car</a> will use an updated version of its current 3.7-liter V-6 engine, with the potential for a Mercedes-sourced engine as an option. The top-spec G37 IPL could become a much more aggressive performance machine, possibly housing a <a href="http://rumors.automobilemag.com/report-infiniti-planning-530-hp-twin-turbo-v-6-for-next-gen-g37-ipl-126573.html">twin-turbo V-6 engine with as much as 530 hp</a>. After the sedan debuts, Infiniti will also launch the familiar G coupe and G convertible.</p>
<p>Yet most reports suggest the Infiniti G will make its public debut <a href="http://rumors.automobilemag.com/report-next-infiniti-g-sports-car-to-debut-at-geneva-show-in-2013-166513.html">at the Geneva Motor Show</a> in the spring, not at Detroit. That could mean that the January debut will instead be Infiniti&#8217;s front-wheel-drive luxury car.</p>
<p>Infiniti previously confirmed that it will <a href="http://rumors.automobilemag.com/official-infiniti’s-new-compact-model-will-ride-on-mercedes-benz-platform-75633.html">build a compact car</a>, loosely based on the Etherea concept (pictured below), <a href="http://rumors.automobilemag.com/detroit-2012-nissan-executive-vice-president-andy-palmer-on-future-products-technology-100685.html">on a Mercedes platform</a>. That will likely be the MFA front-wheel-drive architecture used for cars like the new A- and B-Class. Designed to rival the BMW 1-Series and Audi A3, the compact Infiniti is hoped to be a big hit in Europe &#8212; Infiniti reportedly hopes the car would account for 40 percent of its annual sales there. A selection of gasoline and diesel four-cylinder Mercedes engines will power the car.</p>
<p>If Infiniti follows the trends of most other automakers, we can expect to see teaser photos and/or videos emerge over the next few weeks before the Detroit auto show. That should give us a better idea of exactly which sports sedan the luxury brand will introduce in January.</p>
<p>Source: Infiniti</p>
