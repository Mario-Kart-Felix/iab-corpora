<p>Sports wagon fans in the United States are eager to get their hands on the 2013 BMW 3 Series Sports Wagon. BMW announced that the new long roof 3, is part of the BMW 328i, 330d, and 320d Touring produce line and will be available to American drivers as early as spring of 2013. Currently the 328i is the only one of these models available in the United States.</p>
<p>The 2013 BMW 3 Series Sports Wagon is larger than the current model with 10% more cargo space. It will be available in a sport, luxury or modern version. A special edition M Sport Line will have an interior and exterior trim that will set it apart from the rest. Whatever version you decide on, the backseats have a 40, 20, 40 split to help access the cargo space. They also come standard with a hidden storage area, partition net, and if the cargo cover isn’t in use, it can be stored under the cargo floor.</p>

<p>An interesting feature in the 2013 BMW 3 Series Sports Wagon is the rear window and tailgate. The window opens separately to make it quick and easy to load and unload smaller items and opens up rather than out for added convenience. The tailgate is power operated. This is the first time that a power tailgate will be offered on a Sports Wagon. The standard option offers access via your remote key, or you can add Comfort Access to your options in order to have hands free opening. For the hands free opening, you stick your foot under the rear bumper and the tailgate will automatically open.</p>
<p>All the other favorite bells and whistles will also be included as standard or options for the 2013 BMW 3 Series Sports Wagon. The famous BMW Apps and color HUD will be available along with Lane Departure Warning, Blind Spot Detection, Rear-view Camera with Top View and BMW Parking Assistant. The BMW Assist telematics service will also be available.</p>
