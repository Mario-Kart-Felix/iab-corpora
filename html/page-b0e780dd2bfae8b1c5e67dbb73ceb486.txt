<p>DETROIT, MI- Electrified vehicles aren&#8217;t as prominent at the <a href="http://www.mlive.com/naias">2013 North American International Auto Show</a> as they have been in the past, but there are still dozens on display.</p><p>Here&#8217;s a look at the top 10 must-see electrified vehicles, as well as some other notables at the show:</p><blockquote style="margin: 0 0 0 40px; border: none; padding: 0px;"><p><b>10.</b> <a href="http://topics.mlive.com/tag/Chevrolet%20Volt/index.html">2013 Chevrolet Volt: </a>The extended-range vehicle from General Motors Co. has been in the market for more than two years, but it remains an important (and controversial) vehicle for the Detroit-based automaker. It continues to compete in the EV market place, as others like it continue to enter the market.</p><p><b>9.</b> <a href="http://photos.mlive.com/detroit/2013/01/2014_honda_accord_plug-in.html">2014 Honda Accord plug-in hybrid</a>: This plug-in hybrid electric vehicle is priced at just over $40,000, and can achieve 115 miles per gallon equivalent. It also features the sedan&#8217;s new, more aggressive styling.</p><p><b>8. </b><a href="http://www.mlive.com/auto/index.ssf/2012/11/ford_triples_c-max_energi-cert.html">2013 Ford C-Max Energi</a>: The C-Max Energi, a plug-in hybrid, is part of Ford Motor Co.&#8217;s first electrified-exclusive lineup of vehicles. Placed to go head-to-head with Toyota Motor Corp.&#8217;s Prius lineup, the C-Max Energi boasts a 550-mile total range and 95 MPGe in electric mode.  </p><p><b style="font-size: 1em;">7.&nbsp;</b><span style="font-size: 1em;"><a href="http://photos.mlive.com/detroit/2013/01/mercedes-benz_b-class_electric.html">Mercedes-Benz B-Class Electric Drive concept</a>: The vehicle is almost blinding when you go near it, thanks to the vehicle&#8217;s mirror-like exterior. Mercedes-Benz reportedly hinted that the concept, which has a 124-mile drive range, is almost a production vehicle, and that a market launch is already planned for 2014.</span></p><p><b>6.</b> <a href="http://www.mlive.com/auto/index.ssf/2013/01/ford_2013_fusion_plug-in_tops.html">2013 Ford Fusion Energi</a>: If you haven&#8217;t seen the new design of the Ford Fusion, you must heave been under a rock for the past year. The new design, which is featured on the Energi plug-in as well as the hybrid and gasoline models, is beautiful. The Fusion Energi also features a 100 MPGe combined and a 21-mile all-electric driving range.</p><p><b>5.</b> <a href="http://photos.mlive.com/detroit/2013/01/honda_ev-ster.html">Honda EV-Ster concept</a>: Straight from &#8220;Minority Report,&#8221; this two-seater will most likely never come to fruition, but it is fun to look at. According to Honda, the all-electric coupe has a top speed of 99 miles per hour and can achieve zero-to-37 miles per hour in 5 seconds (yes, 37 mph, not 60 mph). The design is what makes it great though, not the performance.</p><script type="text/javascript">
