                n class="dateline">LOS ANGELES -- </span> The <a href="http://www.laautoshow.com/" target="_blank">Los Angeles Auto Show</a> announced today that its media-only Ride & Drive will expand this year to present not only <a href="http://laautoshow.com/GreenAndAdvancedTechnologyRideAndDrive" target="_blank">fuel-efficient vehicles</a>, but also vehicles featuring the most advanced in-car technologies. Approximately 25 vehicles from 12 manufacturers will be featured in the new <a href="http://laautoshow.com/GreenAndAdvancedTechnologyRideAndDrive" target="_blank">Green and Advanced Technology Ride & Drive</a> representing the leading edge of automotive innovation today. The event represents a one-of-a-kind opportunity for media to experience back-to-back drives from 9:30 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. on Thursday, Nov. 29 on both city streets and in closed-course demonstrations.
<P> The participating vehicles represent the latest evolutions of electric, hybrid, clean diesel and fuel cell, while the latest <a href="http://laautoshow.com/GreenAndAdvancedTechnologyRideAndDrive" target="_blank">driver assist platforms</a> to be featured include systems such as front crash mitigation, adaptive cruise control, radar-based object detection systems, blind spot monitoring and more. Some of the vehicles on display are so new or in such limited production that the Ride & Drive provides a truly exclusive opportunity for a hands-on experience.		</p>

		
		<P> 
			Participating vehicles at the time of press include:</p>
		<ul type="disc">
<li><b>Acura ILX Hybrid</b>, featuring a 1.5 liter four-cylinder hybrid gasoline engine complemented by an electric motor. </li>
<li><b>Chevrolet Volt</b>, the top-selling plug-in electric vehicle in the U.S., which also features a range-extending internal combustion engine. </li>
<li><b>Fiat 500e</b>, the 100 percent electric version of the recently re-introduced micro-car classic equipped with a 100 hp equivalent electric motor. </li>
<li><b>Fisker Karma</b>, the first luxury EV, featuring an electric motor delivering 959 ft. lbs. of torque paired with a range-extending four-cylinder engine. </li>
<li><b>Honda</b> will show multiple vehicles including the all-new, more upscale and premium <b>2013 Civic sedan</b>; <b>Fit EV</b>, the all-electric battery version of the Fit subcompact which achieves 118 MPGe; the all-new, completely redesigned <b>Accord</b> with LaneWatch lane assist technology, Forward Collision Warning (FCW) and Lane Departure Warning (LDW); and the <b>Accord Plug-in Hybrid</b>, a PHEV version of the popular Accord model that targets 110 MPGe. </li>
<li><b>Mercedes-Benz GL 350 BlueTec</b>, a clean diesel SUV also equipped with Collision Prevention Assist, radar-based active controls for avoiding accidents. </li>
<li><b>Mitsubishi i-MiEV</b>, an all-electric subcompact hatchback that has earned 112 MPGe rating. </li>
<li><b>Nissan Leaf</b>, a 100 percent electric vehicle that also features the Carwings smartphone app to activate controls including remote activation of battery charging and climate controls. </li>
<li><b>Smart EV</b> all-electric micro-car, which has received a 50 percent power boost with improved Li-ion battery technology. </li>
<li><b>Subaru Outback </b>with Eyesight, which uses a dash-mounted camera to deliver driver assist features such as adaptive cruise control and lane departure warning. </li>
<li><b>Toyota</b> will feature four vehicles including its all-electric <b>RAV4</b>, a groundbreaking electric vehicle at 76 MPGe that is the result of a collaboration with Tesla Motors; the new 40 MPG <b>Avalon hybrid</b> featuring a 2.5 liter four-cylinder internal combustion engine paired with two electric motors; the <b>Prius</b> <b>Plug-in</b>, a next-generation hybrid that allows all-electric driving at speeds up to 60 mph and advanced safety technology including a Pre-Collision System; and the FCHV-adv, a <b>fuel cell hybrid</b> concept vehicle demonstrating breakthrough hydrogen cell technology that can take a vehicle over 500 miles on a single fill-up. </li>
<li>Volkswagen's latest, including the new 2013 <b>Jetta Hybrid</b> featuring a turbocharged 1.4 liter four-cylinder engine paired with a 27 hp electric motor and an "electric-only" mode; the torquey clean diesel 2013 <b>Beetle TDI</b> equipped with a 2.0&#xA0; liter turbo-diesel engine, and demonstrating one of the latest biofuel advancements, <b>Volkswagen Passat TDI powered by Solazyme renewable diesel</b>.</li></ul></p><P> The event is sponsored by the <a href="http://laautoshow.com/GreenAndAdvancedTechnologyRideAndDrive" target="_blank">Alliance of Automobile Manufacturers</a> and Nielsen Automotive. On the morning of the Ride & Drive, Nielsen Automotive will announce the winner of the second annual <a href="http://laautoshow.com/PressConferenceSchedule" target="_blank">Nielsen Automotive Green Marketer of the Year Award</a>. The award measures the most effective green marketer and will focus on understanding which auto brand raised awareness and changed perceptions around environmental issues most effectively.</p><P>
