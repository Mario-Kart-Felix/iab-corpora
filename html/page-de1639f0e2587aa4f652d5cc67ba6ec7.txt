		<div class="entry-meta">
			<abbr title="June 25, 2013 at 8:04 am">64 days ago</abbr> by <a href="http://insideevs.com/author/eric-loveday/" title="Posts by Eric Loveday" rel="author">Eric Loveday</a> <span class="entry-comment"><a href="http://insideevs.com/jonway-auto-to-launch-3-electric-vehicles-in-2013-including-two-versions-of-electric-minivan/#comments" title="Comment on Jonway Auto to Launch 3 Electric Vehicles in 2013, Including Two Versions of Electric Minivan">1</a></span>
		</div> <!-- end .entry-meta -->
		
	    <div class="clear"></div>
	    
	   	<div class="entry">
				   	
	    	<div id="attachment_34329" class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 510px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/zap-jonway.jpg"><img class="size-full wp-image-34329" alt="Yes, ZAP is Tied to Jonway" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/zap-jonway.jpg" width="500" height="150" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Yes, ZAP is Tied to Jonway</p></div>
<p>Jonway Auto (Yes, Zap is involved here) says it&#8217;ll launch 4 electric vehicles in China before the end of 2013.</p>
<div id="attachment_35290" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/zap-jonway-turkey-e380-ev-5.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-35290" alt="Zap Jonway Shows Off The E380 In Turkey This Past December" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/zap-jonway-turkey-e380-ev-5-300x240.jpg" width="300" height="240" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Zap Jonway Shows Off The E380 In Turkey This Past December</p></div>
<p>On that list are the 5-Star MiniVan EV-L,  5-Star MiniVan EV-LA, 5-Door E-Falcon SUV and the 3-Door E380 SUV.</p>
<p>That&#8217;s quite the lineup of electric vehicles, if you ask us.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">According to Jonway, the 5-Star MiniVan will be first up.  The 5-Star Minivan EV-L (Lithium) has a range of 88 miles and a peak hp rating of 148.  A battery swap version of the Minivan-L is in the works.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">The 5-Star Minivan-LA (Lead Acid) is the lead acid version. Its price is estimated at <span class="xn-money">$12,000 US</span>.  Range for this version is listed at 50 miles and its peak hp rating is, well, way less than the lithium-ion version.</p>
<p>The E-380SUV, as well as the E-Falcon SUV, are offered in two versions, too (lithium-ion and lead-acid).  Why this lead-acid option?  The answer is cost.  Zap says the lead-acid versions sacrifice performance and range, but barely put a dent in the pocketbook.</p>
<div id="attachment_35291" class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/5-door-suv-taxi.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-35291" alt="5 Door SUV Taxi (based on the E380)" src="http://insideevs.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/5-door-suv-taxi-300x186.jpg" width="300" height="186" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">5 Door SUV Taxi (based on the E380)</p></div>
<p>And in China, where prices of electric vehicles seem to be astronomically high, these lead-acid models are expected to sell well simply because they&#8217;re nearly as cheap as the gas-fueled competition.</p>
