<div class="Section">
<h2><span>Lithium-Ion Batteries on the Rise</span></h2>

<p class="TextStyle1">Measures are being adopted in a variety of different fields to help prevent global warming by reducing CO<sub>2</sub> emissions. As a result, the automotive industry has become a focal point  when considering impact on the environment.<br />
More importantly, advances in environmental technology, such as the emergence of hybrid electric vehicles in the 1990's, have brought innovation to an era where vehicles have been traditionally powered by gasoline. The key feature of hybrid technology is surrounded by the augmentation of the engine with an electric motor, ultimately achieving improved fuel economy and significantly reducing the amount of CO<sub>2</sub> produced by the burning of fuel.</p>

<div class="ImgW180SetStyle ClearFix">
<p class="Img"><img src="/environment/showcase/solution/mobility/images/img_lithiumion/01.jpg" width="180" height="130" alt="[Image] Leading contender for clean motoring!" /></p>
<div class="ImgSet">
<p class="TextStyle1">The Lithium-Ion Battery has attracted attention due to its potential for use in hybrid electric vehicles. Hitachi is a leader in its development. What's more, the market for Lithium-Ion Batteries is rapidly expanding as an alternative to the nickel-metal hydride batteries, which have been utilized in the hybrid market thus far. In addition to its smaller size and lighter weight, Lithium-Ion Batteries deliver performance that helps to protect the environment with features such as improved charge efficiency without memory effect(*1).<br />
In an environment where motor vehicle requirements including lower exhaust emissions and better fuel economy are prevalent, it is anticipated that the practical use of hybrid, electric, and fuel cell vehicles will continue to increase.</p>
</div>
</div>

<dl class="AdditionalNotesStyle2 ClearFix">
<dt>*1</dt>
<dd>Memory effect: The effect observed by repeatedly recharging a battery while it still contains a certain level of charge, causing the discharge voltage to lessen and the capacity of the battery to diminish as a result.</dd>
</dl>

<p class="ImgOnlyStyle CenterAdjust"><img src="/environment/showcase/solution/mobility/images/img_lithiumion/02.jpg" width="550" height="353" alt="[Image] Advances in Lithium-Ion Battery Technology" /></p>
</div><!--/Section-->


<div class="Section">
<h2><span>Lithium-Ion Batteries Basics</span></h2>

<p class="TextStyle1">Currently, Lithium-Ion Batteries are widely used in mobile phones and notebook PCs because they have the durability to be recharged and reused repeatedly. The compactness, lightweight and durability, coupled with its powerful characteristics in these batteries, have created a demand for use as small batteries in mobile devices and the larger batteries for hybrid electric vehicles.</p>

<div class="ColumnSet">
<div class="Column1andHalf FirstItem">
<p class="ImgOnlyStyle CenterAdjust"><img src="/environment/showcase/solution/mobility/images/img_lithiumion/04.jpg" width="300" height="195" alt="[Image] Lithium-Ion Battery and Lithium-Ion Battery module" /></p>
</div><!--Column1andHalf-->
<div class="Column1andHalf LastItem ">
<p class="ImgOnlyStyle CenterAdjust"><img src="/environment/showcase/solution/mobility/images/img_lithiumion/03.jpg" width="180" height="130" alt="[Image] Small and Durable" /></p>
</div><!--/Column1andHalf-->
</div><!--/ColumnSet-->


<p class="TextStyle1">Our third-generation Lithium-Ion Battery being mass produced since 2010 has the power density of about three times in comparison with a nickel-metal hydride battery in present market, and our fourth-generation currently under development has the power density of about four times. The batteries also produce higher output (boosting vehicle power), higher efficiency (avoiding wasteful use of electricity), and provides excellent durability, compared with the life of the battery being roughly equivalent to the life of the vehicle. They are regarded as the leading role of next-generation battery.<br />
The Lithium-Ion Batteries supplied by Hitachi are flourishing in a wide range of different applications including cars, buses, commercial vehicles and trains. </p>


<p class="ImgOnlyStyle CenterAdjust"><img src="/environment/showcase/solution/mobility/images/img_lithiumion/05.jpg" width="570" height="235" alt="[Image] Left : Nissan Tino Hybrid released in 2000 Previously sold on limited basis in Japan. Right : 2007 Mitsubishi Fuso Aero Star Eco-Hybrid Available in Japan" /></p>
</div><!--/Section-->

<div class="Section">
<h2><span>Provision of System Products for Hybrid Electric Vehicles</span></h2>

<p class="TextStyle1">Systems for hybrid electric vehicles are comprised of several critically important components. In addition to the Lithium-Ion Batteries, the system capability also includes electric motor to augment the engine, and a power inverter to drive the motor. Hitachi has created a high level of confidence, trust and global recognition for its in-house production including these system components. In fact,  Hitachi has been at the forefront, establishing full-scale production of Lithium-Ion Batteries for hybrid electric vehicles and supplying the product to customers in Japan, North America and other countries around the world.</p>

<p class="ImgOnlyStyle CenterAdjust"><img src="/environment/showcase/solution/mobility/images/img_lithiumion/06.jpg" width="550" height="335" alt="[Image] An example of system diagram for Hybrid electric vehicles" /></p>

<div class="ImgW180SetStyle ClearFix">
<p class="Img"><img src="/environment/showcase/solution/mobility/images/img_lithiumion/07.jpg" width="180" height="130" alt="[Image] Response to the demand for more than 100,000 units of HEVs" /></p>
<div class="ImgSet">
<p class="TextStyle1">Hitachi Group has brought together not only electronic control technologies and automotive battery technologies, but also the material and component technologies. It has an extensive track record of supplying products ranging from battery cells to battery system packs, mainly to automobile manufacturers and railway companies.<br />
We completed mass-production line in October 2009 that monthly produces 300,000 cells. As a result, our monthly productive capacity became 340,000 cells by adding 40,000 cells produced by existing line, and we can meet the demand of over 100,000 hybrid electric vehicles (HEVs) a year. This productive capacity for automotive Lithium-Ion Battery is the world top level.<br />
Hitachi will continue to contribute to the future of the global environment with products that emphasize long life, high output, high energy density, high reliability and low cost.</p>
</div>
</div>


</div><!--/Section-->


<div class="Section">
<p class="TextStyle1">The Hitachi Group views the Lithium-Ion Batteries for Hybrid Electric Vehicles as one of the ways we can achieve our Environmental Vision 2025, with a goal of helping to reduce annual CO<sub>2</sub> emissions by 100 million tons by 2025 through our products and services.</p>
