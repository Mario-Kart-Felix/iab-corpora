<h3 class="glossary-title">Vehicle Stability Control (VSC)</h3>
<div class="glossary-description"><p>System that ensures control in cornering situations the way ABS does under braking and TRC does under acceleration. The VSC system utilises electronic sensors in conjunction with the ABS and TRC hardware to help control any potential understeer or oversteer situations. Understeer is when the vehicle loses front-wheel traction and wants to push forward nose first. Oversteer is when the rear wheels lose traction and the tail begins to slide.</p></div>
</div></div>

<div class="glossary-description"><p>System designed to maximise safety when accelerating on slippery road surfaces or when the available grip differs from one side of the vehicle to the other (such as when two wheels are on a gravel or grass verge). Operated by controlling the throttle and/or applying the brakes to any wheel that is slipping. Image shown for illustration purposes only.</p></div>

<div class="glossary-description"><p>Active Braking with intelligence (AB-i) is a system designed to help stop quickly and safely in an emergency. <div class="glossary-description"><p>Electronic Brake-force Distribution (EBD) is a system that maximises braking efficiency. <h3 class="glossary-title">Rack & Pinion Item</h3>
<div class="glossary-description"><p>A rack and pinion is a type of linear actuator that comprises a pair of gears which convert rotational motion into linear motion. A circular gear called "the pinion" engages teeth on a linear "gear" bar called "the rack"; rotational motion applied to the pinion causes the rack to move, thereby translating the rotational motion of the pinion into the linear motion of the rack.</p></div>

<h3 class="glossary-title">Traction Control (TRC)</h3>
<div class="glossary-image"><img src="/static/images/16dmf-camry-traction-control-940x529.jpg" alt="Camry Traction control" width="300" height="169" /></div>

<h3 class="glossary-title">Vehicle Stability Control (VSC)</h3>
<div class="glossary-description"><p>System that ensures control in cornering situations the way ABS does under braking and TRC does under acceleration. Understeer is when the vehicle loses front-wheel traction and wants to push forward nose first. Oversteer is when the rear wheels lose traction and the tail begins to slide.</p></div>
