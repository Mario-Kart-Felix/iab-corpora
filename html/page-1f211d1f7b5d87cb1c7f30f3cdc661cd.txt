											<h1 class="entry-title">Museum Directory</h1>
					
					<div class="entry-content">
						<div>
<div style="text-align: left;"><img style="width: 445px; height: 109px;" alt="" src="/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/newMuseumDirectory-35.jpg" /></div>
<div>
<p><span style="color: #ff0000; font-size: large;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Alabama</strong></span></span></p>
<p><strong>BARBER VINTAGE MOTORSPORTS MUSEUM: </strong>Home to the world’s best motorcycle collection, the museum now has over 1200 vintage and modern motorcycles and racecars and the largest collection of Lotus cars as well as other significant makes. The collection is the largest of its kind in the world. There are approximately 750 of the 1300 motorcycles on display at any given time. These bikes range from 1902 to current-year production. The common street bikes represented, as well as rare one-off Gran Prix race machinery. Open daily except Mondays. Admission fee. 1899 Mahoning Avenue, Warren, Ohio 44483. (330) 394-1899. <a href="http://www.packardmuseum.org" target="_blank">www.packardmuseum.org</a></p>
<p><strong>SNOOK&#8217;S DREAM CARS:</strong> Housed in a replica of a 1940’s-era Texaco gas station, the collection includes everything from hood ornaments to window cranks along with its showroom of dream cars from the 1930s to the 1960s. Open daily 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. Admission fee. 13920 County Home Road, Bowling Green, Ohio 43402. (419) 353-8338. w<a href="http://ww.snooksdreamcars.com" target="_blank">ww.snooksdreamcars.com</a></p>
<p><span style="color: #ff0000; font-size: large;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Oklahoma</strong></span></span></p>
<p><strong>MUSCLE CAR RANCH:</strong> This outdoor museum displays muscle cars on a former 70-acre dairy farm. There are also Mack trucks and vintage motorcycles among several restored classic neon signs. Open daily. Donations accepted. 3609 S. 16th Street, Chickasha, Okla. 73018. (405) 222-4910. <a href="http://www.musclecarranch.com" target="_blank">www.musclecarranch.com</a><br />
<strong><br />
TOWNSEND&#8217;S CLASSIC AND ANTIQUE AUTO MUSEUM: </strong>About 100 automobiles from 1905 to the 1950s. Open Monday, Friday, and Saturday 11 a.m. to 6 p.m. Admission fee. 8901 N. Harrison, Shawnee, Okla. 74804. (405) 275-9780.<br />
<span style="color: #ff0000; font-size: large;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong><br />
Pennsylvania</strong></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><strong><span style="font-size: medium;"><span style="font-family: Times New Roman;">AMERICA ON WHEELS TRANSPORTATION MUSEUM:</span></span></strong></span><span style="font-size: medium;"><span style="font-family: Times New Roman;"> American On Wheels offers a glimpse of the past, present and future of our nation&#8217;s ofer-the-road transportation system and preserves our transportation history by looking back in time, collecting valuable artifacts and vehicles illustrating the tremendous impact transportation has had on our daily lives. Open daily from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.  5 North Front Street, Allentown, PA 18102. (610) 432-4200. </span></span><span style="font-size: medium;"><span style="font-family: Arial;"><a href="http://www.americaonwheels.org" target="_blank"><span style="font-family: Times New Roman;">www.americaonwheels.org</span></a></span><span style="font-family: Times New Roman;">.</span></span></p>
<p><strong>ANTIQUE AUTOMOBILE CLUB OF AMERICA MUSEUM:</strong> A collection of 130 automobiles spanning eight decades, from Model Ts to muscle cars. Vehicles from the museum are often lent to film production companies; one bus was in “Forrest Gump” and a blue 1937 Packard was used in the filming of 2006’s “The Good Shepherd.” The museum’s bus collection is one of the nation’s largest. Open daily 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Admission fee. 161 Museum Drive, Hershey, Pa. 17033. (717) 566-7100. <a href="http://www.aaca.org/museum" target="_blank">www.aaca.org/museum</a><br />
<strong><br />
EASTERN MUSEUM OF MOTOR RACING:</strong> A collection of vintage racecars and artifacts, as well as a research library of rare books, documents and photos about auto racing — mostly open wheel and dirt track racing popular in the Northeast. Open 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Saturdays and Sundays, April through October. Free. 100 Baltimore Road, York Spring, Pa. 17372. (717) 528-8279. <a href="http://www.emmr.org" target="_blank">www.emmr.org</a></p>
<p><strong>FRICK&#8217;S CAR AND CARRIAGE MUSEUM:</strong> Carriages, horseless carriages and vintage vehicles illustrate Pittsburgh’s place in automotive history. A film depicts the beginning of automotive history. Other highlights include coal and steel baron Henry Clay Frick’s 1914 Rolls-Royce Silver Ghost touring car and Howard Heinz’s 1898 Panhard, said to have been the first car in Pittsburgh. Open 10 a.m. to 5 p.m., closed Monday. Free. 7727 Reynolds St., Pittsburgh, 15208. (412) 371-0600. <a href="http://www.frickart.org" target="_blank">www.frickart.org</a><br />
<strong><br />
GRICE CLEARFIELD COMMUNITY MUSEUM: </strong>Classic cars surrounded by mounted wild game and fish. Cars include a McLaughlin jumpseat carriage, a 1925 Ford Model T one-ton truck, a 1929 Model A street rod, a 1934 Ford sedan, a 1959 Cadillac convertible and a 1979 Dodge Li’l Red Express. Open daily. Admission fee. 119 North Fourth St., Clearfield, Pa. 16830. (814) 768-7332.</p>
<p><strong>HARLEY-DAVIDSON FACTORY TOUR AND EXHIBIT:</strong> Displays at the Vaughn L. Beals Tour Center detail the plant’s history and guide visitors through the motorcycle manufacturing process at the site of Harley-Davidson’s largest plant. The tour starts with a movie and continues for a behind-the-scenes look at operations in York. Open Monday through Friday, 8 a.m. to 4 p.m. Free. 1425 Eden Road, York, Pa. (414) 343-7850. <a href="http://www.harley-davidson.com" target="_blank">www.harley-davidson.com</a></p>
<p><strong>THE MACK TRUCKS HISTORICAL MUSEUM: </strong>Visitors can see the original sightseeing buses built by Jack and Augustus Mack at the turn of the 20th century, a 1911 Mack delivery truck and Mack-made trucks used in World War I, in addition to 80,000 photos dating back to 1905. Open 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Mondays, Wednesdays and Fridays. Free. 997 Postal Rd., Allentown, Pa. 18103. (610) 266-6767. <a href="http://www.macktrucks.com" target="_blank">www.macktrucks.com</a></p>
<p><strong>ROLLS-ROYCE FOUNDATION LIBRARY AND MUSEUM: </strong>Displays of original and restored Rolls-Royce and Bentley automobiles and engines. Open 10 a.m. to 2 p.m. Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday. 189 Hempt Road, Mechanicsburg, Pa. 17050. (717) 795-9400. <a href="http://www.rollsroycefoundation.com" target="_blank">www.rollsroycefoundation.com</a><br />
<strong><br />
SIMEONE FOUNDATION AUTOMOTIVE MUSEUM</strong>: One of the greatest collections of racing sports cars in the world. Assembled by renowned neurosurgeon Dr. Frederick Simeone over a span of 50 years, the Museum contains over 60 of the rarest and most significant racing sports cars ever built. The Museum also holds regularly scheduled “demonstration driving days” that feature extremely rare racing sports cars from the Simeone collection being driven on a three-acre lot in back of the Museum. Open every day except Monday. 6825-31 Norwitch Drive, Philadelphia, Pa., 19153; Ph: 215-365-7233; <a href="http://www.SimeoneMuseum.org" target="_blank">www.SimeoneMuseum.org</a><br />
<strong><br />
STATE MUSEUM OF PENNSYLVANIA: </strong>An industrial history collection including transportation artifacts like horse-drawn wagons, carriages, sleighs and automobiles. Highlights are a Conestoga wagon and two early Pennsylvania-made cars. Open daily except Monday. Free. 300 North St., Harrisburg, Pa. 17120 (717) 787-4980. <a href="http://www.statemuseumpa.org" target="_blank">www.statemuseumpa.org</a></p>
<p><strong>SWIGART ANTIQUE AUTO MUSEUM:</strong> This collection contains a diverse selection of one- and two-cylinder cars. Other vehicles displayed are an 1899 Locomobile, a 1904 Cadillac rear entrance tonneau, a 1905 Rambler and a 1904 Franklin. There are also vehicles from mostly forgotten makers like Crest, Jewel, Mora and even Sears, which once sold cars through its stores and catalogs. Open daily Memorial Day through October, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Admission fee. Route 22, Huntingdon, Pa. 16652. (814) 643-0885. <a href="http://www.swigartmuseum.com" target="_blank">www.swigartmuseum.com</a></p>
<p><span style="color: #ff0000; font-size: large;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Rhode Island</strong></span></span></p>
<p><strong>PRONYNE MOTOR SPORTS MUSEUM: </strong>A collection of antique racecars housed in a former baked goods factory. Call for hours. 8 Cleveland St., Pawtucket, R.I. 02862. (401) 447-4202. <a href="http://www.pronynemotorsports.com" target="_blank">www.pronynemotorsports.com</a><br />
<span style="color: #ff0000; font-size: large;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong><br />
South Carolina</strong></span></span></p>
<p><strong>BMW ZENTRUM:</strong> A museum about the past, present and future of BMW at its only American plant. Exhibits include displays of BMW’s work with hydrogen-powered cars as part of its CleanEnergy program and a tribute to the company’s motorcycle heritage and a selection of successful BMW racecars. Plant tours are currently suspended. Open Monday through Friday 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. Free. Exit 60, Interstate 85, Spartanburg, S.C. 29304. (864) 989-6000. <a href="http://www.bmwzentrum.com" target="_blank">www.bmwzentrum.com</a></p>
<p><strong>THE DARLINGTON RACEWAY STOCK CAR MUSEUM/ N.M.P.A. HALL OF FAME: </strong>On the grounds of the Darlington Raceway, old stock cars, trophies and racing engines and the National Motorsports Press Association’s Hall of Fame. Open daily 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Admission fee. 1301 Harry Byrd Highway, Darlington, S.C. 29532. (843) 395-8821. <a href="http://www.darlingtonraceway.com" target="_blank">www.darlingtonraceway.com<br />
</a><br />
<span style="color: #ff0000; font-size: large;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>South Dakota</strong></span></span></p>
<p><strong>PIONEER AUTO SHOW:</strong> About 250 cars, motorcycles and tractors spanning the last century. Open daily. Admission fee. Interstate 90 and US 83, Murdo, S.D. 57559. (605) 669-2691. <a href="http://www.pioneerautoshow.com" target="_blank">www.pioneerautoshow.com</a></p>
<p><strong>TELSTAR MUSTANG-SHELBY-COBRA MUSEUM: </strong>Many restored Mustang, Shelby and Cobra cars. By appointment. Admission fee. 1300 South Kimball Street, Mitchell, S.D. 57031. (605) 996-6550. <a href="http://www.telstarmotors.com" target="_blank">www.telstarmotors.com</a><br />
<span style="color: #ff0000; font-size: large;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong><br />
Tennessee</strong></span></span></p>
<p><strong>FLOYD GARRETT&#8217;S MUSCLE CAR MUSEUM: </strong>More than 90 muscle cars belonging to businessman Floyd Garrett. Open daily. Admission fee. 320 Winfield Dunn Parkway, Sevierville, Tenn. 37876. (865) 908-0882. <a href="http://www.musclecarmuseum.com" target="_blank">www.musclecarmuseum.com</a></p>
<p><strong>LANE MOTOR MUSEUM: </strong>About 150 rare cars and motorcycles from all over the world are on display. Highlights include microcars, motorcycles, prototypes, competition cars and amphibious and military vehicles. Open daily except Tuesday and Wednesday. Admission fee. 702 Murfreesboro Pike, Nashville. 37210. (615) 742-7445. <a href="http://www.lanemotormuseum.org" target="_blank">www.lanemotormuseum.org</a></p>
<p><strong>SMOKY MOUNTAIN CAR MUSEUM:</strong> A collection of cars owned by the famous and infamous, including Elvis Presley, Al Capone and Hank Williams. Open daily May through October, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Admission fee. 2970 Parkway, Pigeon Forge, Tenn. 37861.</p>
<p><strong>SMOKY MOUNTAIN MOTORCYCLE MUSEUM:</strong>  Features motorcycles from around the world. Wednesday-Saturday hours: 10 a.m.-5 p.m.; Sunday: noon-5 p.m. Closed Monday and Tuesday. Admission: $10. 205 W. Main St Dandridge TN 37725.  . <a href="http://www.smokymountainmotorcyclemuseum.com" target="_blank">www.smokymountainmotorcyclemuseum.com</a>.</p>
<p><span style="color: #ff0000; font-size: large;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Texas</strong></span></span></p>
<p><strong>CENTRAL TEXAS MUSEUM OF AUTOMOTIVE HISTORY: </strong>A collection of several hundred cars showing the evolution of the automobile, including a rare British-built 1911 Napier and a 1933 Duesenberg roadster. From April 1 to Sept. 30, open Wednesday through Sunday, and Friday through Sunday the rest of the year. Admission fee. 2502 Highway 304, Smithville, Tex. 78957. (512) 237-2635. <a href="http://www.ctmah.org" target="_blank">www.ctmah.org</a></p>
<p><strong>NATIONAL MUSEUM OF FUNERAL HISTORY:</strong> Houses the country&#8217;s only collection of funeral service artifacts and features renowned exhibits on one of man&#8217;s oldest cultural customs. Drawing from the Museum&#8217;s rare collection of funeral service vehicles, the Historical Hearses exhibit sheds light on the evolution of funerary customs. Highlights include a 1916 Packard funeral bus and a 1921 motorized hearse. Open daily. Admission fee. 415 Barren Springs Drive, Houston, TX 77090-5918. PH: 281-876-3063. <a href="http://www.nmfh.org" target="_blank">www.nmfh.org</a>.</p>
<p><strong>PANHANDLE-PLAINS HISTORICAL MUSEUM: </strong>Texas’s largest history museum includes a transportation exhibit that illustrates the many methods used to travel the Texas panhandle, from buggies, sleighs and wagons to automobiles from 1900 to the 1950s. A 1903 Model A Ford on display bears serial number 28, which the museum says makes it the oldest known automobile made on an assembly line. Open daily. Admission fee. 2503 Fourth Avenue, Canyon, Tex. 79016. (806) 651-2244. <a href="http://www.panhandleplains.org" target="_blank">www.panhandleplains.org</a></p>
<p><strong>PATE MUSEUM OF TRANSPORTATION:</strong> The collection of A. M. Pate Jr., former owner of the Texas Refinery Company in Fort Worth, who died in 1988. On display are more than 30 vintage cars, including Rolls-Royces from the 1920s and 1930s and a 1938 Pierce-Arrow. Open daily except Mondays. Donations accepted. 18501 Highway 377 South, Fort Worth, Tex. 76101. (817) 396-4305.</p>
<p><strong>PETROLEUM MUSEUM CHAPARRAL GALLERY: </strong>The exhibit pays tribute to Jim Hall and his Chaparral racing cars, seven of which are on display. Hall is known as for introducing the movable wing in 1965. Open daily. Admission fee. 1500 Interstate 20 West, Midland, Tex. 79701. (432) 683-4403. <a href="http://www.petroleummuseum.org" target="_blank">www.petroleummuseum.org</a></p>
<p><span style="color: #ff0000; font-size: large;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Utah</strong></span></span></p>
<p><strong>KAISER BILL&#8217;S:</strong> A car collection dedicated to the preservation of older American-built automobiles, their history and legacy. The collection includes cars made by companies like the old Kaiser-Frazer Corporation of Willow Run, Mich. Auto memorabilia is also displayed. Tours are arranged by phone. Free. 18530 West Yellowstone Road, Altonah, Utah 84002. (435) 454-3098. <a href="http://www.kaiserbill.com" target="_blank">www.kaiserbill.com<br />
</a><br />
<strong>KIMBALL-BROWNING CAR MUSEUM: </strong>In the old Union Station railroad depot, 55 vehicles representing the innovation and elegance of the golden age of automobiles are on display, including Stearns, Pierce-Arrows, Packards, Lincolns and Cadillacs of the 1930s. Open daily, except Sundays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Admission fee. 2501 Wall Avenue, Ogden, Utah 84401. (801) 393-9886. <a href="http://www.theunionstation.org" target="_blank">www.theunionstation.org</a></p>
<p><span style="color: #ff0000; font-size: large;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Virginia</strong></span></span></p>
<p><strong>CAR &amp; CARRIAGE CARAVAN MUSEUM AT LURAY CAVERNS: </strong>Among 140 transportation exhibits are authentically restored vehicles like a Conestoga Wagon, an 1892 Benz, a 1908 Baker Electric, a 1913 Stanley Steamer and Rudolph Valentino’s 1925 Rolls-Royce. Open daily. Admission fee. 970 U.S. Highway 211 West, Luray, Va. 22835. (540) 743-6551. <a href="http://www.luraycaverns.com" target="_blank">www.luraycaverns.com.</a></p>
<p><strong>ROARING TWENTIES ANTIQUE CAR MUSEUM:</strong> A collection of cars mostly from the 1920s and 1930s. Call for appointment and directions, May through October. Admission fee. Route 1 Box 576 Hood, Va. 22723. (540) 948-6290. <a href="http://www.roaring-twenties.com" target="_blank">www.roaring-twenties.com</a></p>
<p><strong>VIRGINIA MUSEUM OF TRANSPORTATION: </strong>Though the museum’s focus is on railroads, the Auto Gallery opened in 2007 to showcase a century of automotive history. Open daily. Admission fee. 303 Norfolk Avenue, Roanoke, Va. 24016. (540) 342-5670. <a href="http://www.vmt.org" target="_blank">www.vmt.org.</a></p>
<p><span style="color: #ff0000; font-size: large;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Washington</strong></span></span><br />
<strong><br />
CARR&#8217;S ONE OF A KIND IN THE WORLD MUSEUM: </strong>Carr&#8217;s museum holds an eclectic selection of items, with a focus on celebrity automobiles, including JFK&#8217;s personal 1962 Lincoln Continental, Jackie Gleason&#8217;s 1968 limo, Elvis Presley&#8217;s 1973 Lincoln Mark IV and a Silver Streak 1979 Lincoln Versaille. Mr. Carr mans the admission booth and serves as ad hoc tour guide. You&#8217;re encouraged to touch, and even sit in, the seats once warmed by famous backsides. The rest of the museum&#8217;s collection is an odd mixture of items, with the unifying characteristic of having stimulated to Carr&#8217;s acquisitive nature. The skin of an electrocuted boa constrictor hangs across the room from Chinese vases, and stuffed frog-shaped bean bags, plastic toys and other oddities occupy otherwise-empty spaces in between. Open Sat-Sun 1-4pm. Admission fee. 5225 N. Freya, Spokane, WA; 509-489-8859<br />
<strong><br />
HAROLD LEMAY MUSEUM:</strong> Harold E. LeMay, who made his fortune in the garbage and recycling industry, amassed a huge collection of automobiles, trucks, motorcycles and related memorabilia. The LeMay collection of more than 3,000 vehicles was named the world&#8217;s largest by Guinness World Records. Today, a sizable portion of his collection is housed in the museum, which is expected to move from the LeMay homestead into new quarters off Interstate 5 near the Tacoma Dome in 2010. Open daily Tuesdays through Saturdays and Sundays June through August. Admission fee. 325 152nd Street East, Tacoma, Wash. 98445. (253) 536-2885. <a href="http://www.lemaymuseum.org" target="_blank">www.lemaymuseum.org</a><br />
<strong><br />
LYNDEN PIONEER MUSEUM: </strong>Exhibits focus on Whatcom County, Wash., including its early fire trucks and other vehicles. Open daily. Admission fee. 217 Front Street, Lynden, Wash. 98264. (360) 354-3675. <a href="http://www.lyndenpioneermuseum.com" target="_blank">www.lyndenpioneermuseum.com</a><br />
<span style="color: #ff0000; font-size: large;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong><br />
Washington DC</strong></span></span></p>
<p><strong>SMITHSONIAN INSTITUTIONS: </strong>The National Museum of American History’s America On the Move Exhibit uses the museum’s collection of early automobiles to demonstrate how the nation moved ahead on wheels. Closed for renovation until Summer 2008. Free. 14th St. and Constitution Ave. N.W., Washington. (202) 633-1000. <a href="http://americanhistory.si.edu/onthemove" target="_blank">http://americanhistory.si.edu/onthemove</a></p>
<p><span style="color: #ff0000; font-size: large;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Western Canada</strong></span></span></p>
<p><strong>REYNOLDS-ALBERTA MUSEUM: </strong>More than 350 vehicles, including a rare 1929 Duesenburg Phaeton Royale Model J and a 1913 Classic Six, the oldest known Chevrolet production model. Open daily except Mondays from Sept. 4 to May 20; open daily the rest of the year. Admission fee. Highway 13, Wetaskiwin, Alberta. (780) 361-1351. <a href="http://www.machinemuseum.net" target="_blank">www.machinemuseum.net</a></p>
<p><strong>MANITOBA ANTIQUE AUTOMOBILE MUSEUM:</strong> An extensive antique automobile collection with common models like Ford and less common ones like Maxwell and Russell-Knight. Open 9 a.m. to 9 p.m., May 1 through Sept. 30. Admission fee. Highway 1, Elkhorn, Manitoba. (204) 845-2604. <a href="http://www.mbautomuseum.com" target="_blank">www.mbautomuseum.com</a></p>
<p><strong>ASSINIBOIA AND DISTRICT MUSEUM:</strong> A collection including about 20 classic cars from 1916 to 1950 and their role in the development of the area. Open daily in July and August, Monday through Friday the rest of the year. Admission fee. 506 3rd Avenue West, Assiniboia, Saskatchewan. (306) 642-5353. <a href="http://www.assiniboia.net/html/visiting/museum.html" target="_blank">www.assiniboia.net/html/visiting/museum.html</a></p>
<p><strong>WESTERN DEVELOPMENT MUSEUM: </strong>This branch of the museum has about 50 vehicles dating from about 1900 to 1981, including a 1914 McLaughlin. Open daily 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Admission fee. 50 Diefenbaker Drive, Moose Jaw, Saskatchewan. (306) 693-5989. <a href="http://www.wdm.ca" target="_blank">www.wdm.ca</a></p>
<p><span style="color: #ff0000; font-size: large;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Wisconsin</strong></span></span></p>
<p><strong>WISCONSIN AUTOMOTIVE MUSEUM:</strong> Cars built in Wisconsin, like the Kissel and the Nash, plus 90 other rarities like REOs and Pierce-Arrows. Open daily from May 1 to Sept. 30. Closed Mondays and Tuesdays from Oct 1 to April 30. Admission fee. 147 North Rural Street, Hartford, Wis. 53027. (262) 673-7999. <a href="http://www.wisconsinautomuseum.com" target="_blank">www.wisconsinautomuseum.com</a></p>
<p><span style="color: #ff0000; font-size: large;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Wyoming</strong></span></span></p>
<p><strong>YELLOWSTONE NATIONAL PARK:</strong> In the park&#8217;s heritage and research center in Gardiner, Mont., a collection of 30 horse-drawn and motorized vehicles, ranging from stagecoaches operated by the park to the first car — a Ford Model T — allowed in the park. Call for hours. Admission fee. Yellowstone National Park, Wyo. 82190. (307) 344-7381. <a href="http://www.nps.gov/yell" target="_blank">www.nps.gov/yell</a></p>
