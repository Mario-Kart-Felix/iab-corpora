                                    <h2><span>Best Answer</span> - Chosen by Asker</h2>
                              <div class="content">Driving an automatic is not being lazy, as a driver of mainly automatics for the last 30 years I look upon you as somebody who is joining the ranks of the sensible. <br>
Today there is enough hassle when driving without having the bother of a manual gearbox.<br>
As to what you should buy its down to you all modern auto cars are pretty good it depends on the type, style or make you want, if its new then price will be a limiting factor, if its second hand then its also down to whats available, many auto cars on the second hand market come via Motability most of these are low use fully maintained cars.<br>
With regard to the fuel consumsion of auto cars, with modern ones there is very little differnce, you have to remember that unlike manual cars an auto car is always in the correct gear witrh the engine running at its most efficient level and any small increase over a manual (if any) is off set by the reduction of work load on the driver.<br>
Over the years I&#039;ve driven Fiesta, Focus, Mondeo&#039;s, Granada, Corsa&#039;s, Astra&#039;s, Golf, Suzuki, Hyundia&#039;s, Micra plus some I can&#039;t remember then there&#039;s the older ones like the Triumph 2000 and the comercial automatics I drove as a professional driver each had there good and bad points, its all down to personal choice.</div>
