                                     <h2 class="post_title_nothumb" id="post-127">Who Should Buy a Minivan?</h2>
                                   
                 <div class="entry_single">
                    <p>Minivans are popular family vehicles for a number of reasons. They are primarily people-moving vehicles, allowing larger numbers of people to be comfortably seated than would be possible in a sedan and still have some cargo space left over. For many families the choice comes down to minivan vs. SUV in order to have the space required.</p>
<p>Although a minivan will get worse gas mileage than most cars it is a more fuel efficient choice than an SUV. If you need to move a lot of people on a regular basis, a minivan is both cheaper to purchase and cheaper to fuel than an SUV. Since sedans are an unrealistic option for large groups of people the choice usually comes down to minivan vs. SUV.</p>
<p>Minivans were created with the aim of providing a vehicle that could hold a lot of people like a conventional van but drive more like a car. Unlike full-size vans, their miniature cousins can fit into traditional parking spaces and go almost anywhere a car can go. Because they are built to drive more like a car, minivans will usually ride more nicely than SUVs of comparable quality.</p>
<p>Safety is a major concern for parents when choosing a vehicle. Although SUVs have improved their safety rankings in recent years with the advent of stability assist equipment and other technology, they are still more prone to rollover than some other vehicles. While neither minivans nor SUVs are inherently unsafe, there is often more attention paid to getting high safety ratings by the manufacturers of the former.</p>
<p><img src="http://autoplanamerica.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/minivans.jpg" alt="Minivans" title="Minivans" class="alignright size-full wp-image-157" /></p>
<p>A minivan is an expensive new vehicle but it is still cheaper than an SUV. There is also the option to buy used in order to pay less as well. Used minivans can have a lot of life left in them but give you a considerable break in price. If you only need the extra space rarely you might want to look into a mini van rental.</p>
<p>People with special needs may also benefit from choosing a minivan. Specially-equipped wheelchair mini vans are popular vehicles with the parents and caregivers of disabled individuals. The rear seats of most minivans can be removed to provide space for even a large, motorized wheelchair.</p>
<p>A minivan is a great choice for carting around a larger family and still having space for the groceries or sports gear. If the new prices on these vehicles put them out of the running, consider approaching local car dealerships about their used minivans. Safe, spacious and practical, a minivan is a solid investment for a big family, a carpooling parent or any other large group that needs transportation.</p>
