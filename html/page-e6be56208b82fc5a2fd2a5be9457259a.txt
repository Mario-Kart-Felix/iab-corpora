				  <h1 class="sub-article-head">Nebula Auto strengthens off-road vehicle portfolio for Indian market</h1>

			
	  						<p class="submitted">
																<span class="author-name-title"> 
							By <a href="/reporter/Michael-Gonsalves">Michael Gonsalves</a>						</span>
										
					<span id="submitted-on-date">
						Dec 09 2012					</span> 
					<span id="submitted-on-place">, Pune</span>
				</p>
			 
  						<div class="terms">
					<span>Tags: </span><a href="/news">News</a>		
				</div>
						
			
  <div class="content clearfix">
			  	
	
		<div class="cont-inner">
			
				   
						<div class="article-content">
				Nebula Automotive, an off-road vehicle maker, has rolled out four new high-end models of all terrain vehicles (ATVs) in the 300cc to 800cc range for sports and recreation purpose in the Indian market under the Nebula brand.<br/><br/>
“The demand for ATVs has been growing in the Indian market and to fuel our business further we have introduced four new models,” Sukhdev Asnani, managing director, Nebula Automotive, told Financial Chronicle. The Pune-based company has been assembling and selling for testing and brand building a range of over 15 ATVs in the 110cc to 800cc range for the last three years.<br/><br/>
“With the launch of HS800, AX700, Mammoth800 and three-wheel Trike300, Nebula Automotive offers a full spectrum of vehicles ranging from 110cc junior and uniage ATVs to 800cc twin-seaters ideal for serious off-road enthusiasts,” Asnani said.<br/><br/>
These new models carry a sticker price of Rs 7.77 lakh to Rs 16.50 lakh. “All these vehicles imported through completely built unit route from China attract 112 per cent duty,” Asnani said. The base models carry a price tag of Rs 90,000. These off-road vehicles do not require licence to drive them, he said.<br/><br/>
Nebula Automotive competes with American Polaris and Indian Adventure Wheels based in New Delhi, Power Sports in Bangalore and Harison Motors in Pune in the Indian market.<br/><br/>
Nebula Automotive, which has an assembly plant in Pune has signed a memorandum of understanding with three Chinese automobile companies. “We have tied up with Loncin, Kazuma and GY6 auto companies in China from whom we import components and assemble ATVs at our facility in Pune,” Asnani said. He said the company imports 80 per cent of the components from China, South Korea and Taiwan and the balance from the Indian suppliers. Nebula Automotive has invested a total of Rs 15 crore in the ATVs business.<br/><br/>
The Nebula HS800 is a twin-seater ATV and has an 800cc water-cooled V-Twin 8-valve engine with fuel injection speeding up to 70 km per hour. “This model can switch between two-wheel drive and four-wheel drive modes,” Asnani said.<br/><br/>
The Nebula AX700 is a single-seater with 686cc liquid-cooled EFI-engine with CVT transmission, front and rear disc brakes, a 3-way locking differential. Priced at Rs 12 lakh, it has two-wheel drive and four-wheel drive modes, Asnani said.<br/><br/>
The company also launched electric folding bicycle used on golf campuses. It costs Rs 1.20 lakh. Last year, the company sold 700 vehicles and in the first nine months this year 450 units.<br/><br/>
Asnani said ATVs are fun and utility sports vehicles. “The vehicle can be used for sheer fun, off road utility, transportation and adventurous sports,” he said. These vehicles in India are increasingly used in locations such as beaches, resorts, hill stations, hilly tracks, sand dunes and five star clubs, he said.<br/><br/>
The company is also targeting rich farmers as potentially buyers for whom the vehicles will offer thrill, luxury and status symbol, Asnani said. It has one company owned showroom each in Pune and Navi Mumbai.<br/><br/>
“We plan to open 15 franchise dealer networks by the end of March 31, 2013 and 15 more next in financial year,” Asnani said.<br/><br/>
