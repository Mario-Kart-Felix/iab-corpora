<p>One of the many reasons that some people choose to purchase a sports utility vehicle is to take it <b>off road</b>. <b>SUV</b> off-roading capabilities are impressive in many cases. Whether you look for 4x4 SUVs or other all wheel drive SUVs, many of these vehicles are designed specifically to be taken off road. Beware, however, that some SUVs you may buy are not intended to be taken off road. These vehicles, like compact or mid-size SUVs with only rear or front wheel drive, are better inclined to be operated on the paved road. Even when considering the SUVs that are primarily designed to be used off road, it's important to also consider the other vehicles designed to be used in 4x4 settings with rough terrain. Read on for a brief comparison of the two.</p>
<p><b>Benefits and Risks of SUVs</b></p>
<p>SUVs provide a solid all-around vehicle choice for a variety of purposes. Whether you decide to use your SUV for rugged off-road driving or simply for standard practice, you'll find the engine and the handling of the car will respond well to any number of driving challenges. SUVs are generally capable of reaching high top speeds and can accelerate very rapidly in comparison with other vehicles as well, meaning you'll have no trouble with a 4-wheel drive SUV getting stuck on hills or in any difficult patch of road.</p>
<p>On the other hand, SUVs tend to be big and heavy vehicles that oftentimes do not get the best gas mileage; even compared with other 4x4 cars. These vehicles are sturdy in the event of a crash, but they are very dangerous when it comes to the likelihood they'll roll over or flip when driving at a high speed. Additionally, SUVs tend to cost quite a bit more than many other types of vehicles. When you're considering off-road capable SUVs, this is particularly a problem. Most lower level and less expensive trim options for SUVs contain either front or rear wheel drive only, and the more expensive options are the only ones that tend to also have all-wheel drive capabilities.</p>
<p><b>Benefits and Risks of Other 4x4s</b></p>
<p>Many other vehicles are capable of handling off-road driving. These include the Hummer H1, the Jeep Wrangler and more. Many of these vehicles are specifically designed for off-road driving, and can be much more efficient at this task than their SUV brethren. This is a distinct advantage that some of these vehicles have over SUVs. Additionally, some of them, like the Wrangler, are on average lighter than most SUVs, meaning they'll be able to travel over a wider array of terrains.</p>
<p>On the other hand, 4x4 vehicles are oftentimes too specifically designed to be able to function well on standard roads. They may get poor mileage or have a difficult time handling.</p>
<p>To choose the vehicle that is right for you, ask a dealer in your area for more advice.<b><br /></b></p>
