		<p class="style6" style="width: 777px">
		<span lang="EN-US" style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US" class="style3">This 
		is a new design for a universal gear indicator that can be fitted to any 
		motorcycle as an aftermarket accessory. Its main advantage is that its operation 
		depends entirely on the gear shift lever movement, instead of connecting 
		to speedometer and tachometer sensors (found in expensive commercial devices), 
		which are rarely available in older motorcycles. It consists of a main circuit 
		including a 7&#8209;segment LED indicator, two Hall sensors that are attached 
		to the motorcycle frame, and a small magnet placed on the gear shift lever.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
		<p>&nbsp;</p>
		<p>&nbsp;</p>
		<p><img alt="" src="Image_1.jpg" width="680" height="317"><br>

		<p class="style6" style="width: 772px">
		<span lang="EN-US" style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US" class="style3"><br>
		The main circuit is based on an AVR ATTINY25/45/85 microcontroller, which 
		reads the signals of the two Hall sensors and the neutral switch and outputs 
		the current gear number to a 7&#8209;segment LED indicator, through a 4026 counter/decoder.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
		<p class="style6" style="width: 772px">
						<o:p></o:p></p>

		<a href="Schematic.pdf">Download schematic in .pdf format</a><o:p></o:p></span></p>

		<span lang="EN-US" style="background-position: 0% 0%; font-family: &quot;Tahoma&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;; mso-highlight: yellow; mso-ansi-language: EN-US; background-image: none; background-repeat: repeat; background-attachment: scroll;">
		<a href="Gear_Indicator_EAGLE_files.zip">Download EAGLE files in .zip format</a><o:p></o:p></span></p>
		<p>&nbsp;</p>
		<p class="style9" style="width: 759px">
		<span lang="EN-US" style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US" class="style2">The source 
		code is written in AVR-GCC (WinAVR) and can be programmed with the default 
		fuses using an AVR programmer (default : ATTINY25 microcontroller and USBTiny 
		programmer). </span><span class="style2">
		<span lang="EN-US" style="mso-fareast-font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US; mso-fareast-language: EL; mso-bidi-language: AR-SA" class="style3">
		Moreover, the constant <b>TOP_GEAR 5</b> should be changed to <b>6</b> 
		for six-gear motorbike</span></span><span style="mso-fareast-font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; color: black; mso-ansi-language: EL; mso-fareast-language: EL; mso-bidi-language: AR-SA" class="style2">s.</span></p>

		<span lang="EN-US" style="font-family: &quot;Tahoma&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><o:p>

		<p class="style8">
		<span lang="EN-US" style="font-family: &quot;Tahoma&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><o:p>
						&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>
		<p class="style10" style="width: 762px">
		<span lang="EN-US" style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US">The suggested implementation 
		for the main circuit is a small size, double-sided PCB, with SMD packages 
		for the microcontroller and the decoder ICs. The 7-segment LED is placed 
		in a secondary PCB, connected vertically to the main one in a modular fashion 
		(see pictures). Two PCBs for different Kingbright LED footprints (red and 
		blue) are also provided.</span></p>

		open (there is a gear on), there appears to be a very small current (&lt; 
		0.5 mA) sinking through R3, due to the voltage difference between the 
		neutral switch connection (TO_POWER-4) and the microcontroller. If the

		<p class="style10" style="width: 769px">
		<span lang="EN-US" style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US" class="style2">A successful 
		circuit build will do a self-test when connected solely to 12V power (pins 
		TO_POWER_1 and TO_POWER_2), by cycling through all digits on the 7-segment 
		display (see video below). After the self-test, the current gear will be 
		shown and can be changed by the shift lever movement. Note that a gear is 
		changed when the magnet&#39;s <b>south</b> pole is <b>drawn away</b> from the 
		sensor (north pole will not work). Moreover, if a neutral gear is detected 
		(from the neutral switch connected to TO_POWER_4), the display resets to 
		zero (also acting as a self-calibrating feature if anything goes wrong). 
		Finally, when the power is turned off, the last shown gear is stored in 
		the MCU&#39;s flash EEPROM and restored when the device is turned on again.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
