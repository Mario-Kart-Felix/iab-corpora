		<p>Roundly aerodynamic with flaring nostrils and racy silver rally stripe, this customized 2003 Dodge Caravan looks like an Easter egg on steroids. Built by American Products Company to showcase the company's products, this Caravan transforms a minivan into a megavan and pops the preconceptions of docility and dormancy that have radically reduced the minivan's coolness factor over the last couple of decades.<br clear="all"> </p>
	

                            
                            
                            
                            
                            
                            
                            
                        
                            
                            
                            
                            
<div class="gallery-link Img-right" style="position: relative">
    
    
    <div id="ctl00_ContentPlaceHolderLeft_ctl02_ctl00_rpPages_ctl00_rpPageElements_ctl02_cImage_divImg">
            <a class="gallery-link-pop" href="/brandpages/dodge/0608tr_2003_custom_dodge_caravan/photo_02.html" title="2003 Custom Dodge Caravan Rear Wheel" >
                <img src="http://image.truckinweb.com/f/brandpages/dodge/8324946+w195/0608tr_10_z+2003_custom_dodge_caravan+rear_wheel.jpg" itemprop="image" alt="2003 Custom Dodge Caravan Rear Wheel" onerror="javascript:this.src='/_SiteConfigs/_global/images/no_photo_100px.gif'" /></a>
        </div>
    
</div>

                            
                            
                            
                            
                        
                            

		<p>The vehicle's factory ostrich-egg stance has been sexed up and slammed thanks to an airbag suspension dampened by Bilstein shocks. Tucked underneath is a set of 19-inch Ro_Ja wheels wrapped in Yokohama racing rubber. Take note of the wide lip on those rear wheels. The minivan was configured by the factory for front-wheel drive, but APC converted it to rear-wheel drive; hence the offset on the rear tires. APC left those wheels on there when they converted the minivan back to front-wheel drive, keeping the meaty, aggressive RWD look. One-off upper and lower grilles aren't the only things ventilating the engine compartment. Check out the louvers on the upper left and right side of the factory hood, near the hood's center, that lend a classic hot-rod look. Of course, that big ram air scoop certainly helps to soup up the Caravan's aesthetic. </p>

		<p>But that scoop isn't just for looky-lews. It's the mouth that feeds air into a Vortec supercharger that really wakes up that factory 3.8L V-6. Precision Tuning headers pass gas to the 3-inch Precision Tuning exhaust, which opens flush through a port in the body in front of the left rear wheel. Everything else you see on the exterior is stock, except for the one-off taillights and Viper Red PPG paint split by silver Viper stripes, thanks to a Corona paintmaster named Prox. Musclecar black dominates the interior, particularly on the suede dash and carpet. The black headliner is woven by a carbon fiber pattern. </p>
