						<h1><a href="http://automotivetribune.com/?p=804" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Have You Seen These? &amp; Special Roadways">Have You Seen These? &#038; Special Roadways</a></h1>
						<div class="meta">
							<span class="time">March 27, 2009</span>
							<span class="categories"><a href="http://automotivetribune.com/?cat=90" title="View all posts in Cars &amp; Road Tests" rel="category">Cars &amp; Road Tests</a></span>
							<span class="discuss"><a href="http://automotivetribune.com/?p=804#respond" title="Comment on Have You Seen These? &amp; Special Roadways">No Comments</a></span>
							
													</div>
						
												
						<p><a href="http://automotivetribune.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/dsc_0080-large.jpg" ><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-826" title="dsc_0080-large" src="http://automotivetribune.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/dsc_0080-large-300x199.jpg" alt="dsc_0080-large" width="300" height="199" /></a>At the recent <a href="?p=712">Ormond Beach Birthplace Of Speed</a> event reported in the Tribune we had the opportunity to spot a unique <strong>Ural Patrol 2-Wheel Drive Motorcycle</strong>.  Impressive is the only way to put our response.</p>
<p><a href="http://automotivetribune.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/rear.jpg" ><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-825" title="rear" src="http://automotivetribune.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/rear-300x235.jpg" alt="rear" width="300" height="235" /></a>Looking at the photos shows beautifully crafted bodywork and functional components.  Universal joint assemblies, drum brake actuation, coil / shock assembly, finish nuts, and so much more.  At the absolute minimum this is a stunning side car motorcycle!</p>
<p><a href="http://automotivetribune.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/photo-1.jpg" ><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-824" title="photo-1" src="http://automotivetribune.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/photo-1-300x163.jpg" alt="photo-1" width="300" height="163" /></a>Several years ago I spotted this unique custom built <strong>Personal WaterCraft Carrier</strong> when driving across <a href="http://sofia.usgs.gov/virtual_tour/alalley/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://sofia.usgs.gov']);">Alligator Alley</a>.  This car was very professionally done in exterior appearance.  This driver certainly deserves recognition for their engineering and craftsmanship.</p>
<p><a href="http://automotivetribune.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/photo_3.jpg" ><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-823" title="photo_3" src="http://automotivetribune.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/photo_3-300x166.jpg" alt="photo_3" width="300" height="166" /></a>For drivers, Alligator Alley, I75,  represents one of the last bastions of go-fast asphalt because east-west at the south end of Florida it is about 100 miles coast to coast with about 70 miles on Alligator Alley in the unpopulated area, excluding<em> gators.  </em>Essentially when you get on you go a long way to get off which eliminates local traffic. <a href="http://automotivetribune.com/?p=804#more-804"  class="more-link">&#8230; Continue Reading</a></p>
