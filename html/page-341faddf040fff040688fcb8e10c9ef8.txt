							<p>(DETROIT FREE PRESS) - The minivan of the future - or at least Chrysler's future - is on display at the 2012 North American International Auto Show. </p>
<p>Dubbed the 700 C for now, the silver minivan with chrome accents appears to be lower to the ground and more aerodynamic than the current Dodge Caravan or the Chrysler Town &amp; Country. </p>
<p><a href="http://www.freep.com/" target="_blank"><strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Click here for more photos from the Detroit Free Press</span></strong></a></p>
<p>Fiat and Chrysler CEO Sergio Marchionne, said today the company is trying to decide whether or not it wants to make two minivans or just one. Either way, his goal is to make all of the design decisions by the end of the second quarter and to start making preparations for production by the end of this year. </p>
<p>"I expect to have something tangible ....that we can take back into clinics probably in the first part of the second quarter," Marchionne said, "and start industrializing it in second half of this year." </p>
<p>The concept is so new, Marchionne said he wasn't even informed of its name. </p>
<p>"It was nameless yesterday," Marchionne told reporters on Monday. </p>
<p>For years, Auburn Hills automaker has produced two top-selling minivans -- the Chrysler Town &amp; Country and the Dodge Caravan. </p>
<p>But the minivan segment is evolving quickly, and Marchionne said Chrysler needs to reclaim leadership in minivan design. </p>
<p>"The next evolution of the minivan needs to put us right back into the box when we launched the minivan," Marchionne said. "The 700 C is a prime example of what the design office can do, and is one way we can peel away the skin of the onion." </p>
<p>Sales of the Town &amp; Country dropped 16% in 2011 to 94,320 while sales of the Caravan increased 7.3% to 110,862. The Toyota Sienna was the top-selling minivan in 2011 with 111,429 sold, a 13.3% increase. </p>
<p>By having two minivans, Chrysler has been able to cover a wide price range for consumers. The Caravan sells for about $20,000 to $30,000 while the more upscale Caravan sells for between $30,000 and $40,000, Reid Bigland, head of U.S. sales and CEO of the Dodge brand said today. </p>
<p>With the Caravan and the Town &amp; Country combined, Chrysler was able to capture 50% of U.S. minivan sales in 2011 and 75% of Canadian minivan sales, Bigland said. </p>
<p>"We invented the minivan and we have continued to demonstrate leadership in the segment continuing to innovate and adapt the minivan to meet the consumer's changing needs," Bigland said. </p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
							<p>
							Detroit Free Press
