Since Ford lacked its own front-drive architecture, a prototype was built with a Honda Civic engine and transaxle &mdash; an ironic prediction of the Honda Odyssey. Henry Ford II squelched the idea, but Lee Iacocca and his Ford expats revived the idea with the front-wheel-drive K-car upon arriving at Chrysler.</p><p>The van Chrysler introduced in 1983 offered kid-friendly sliding doors, flexible cargo space, carlike handling, easy entry and fuel efficiency &mdash; all minivan staples today. In going small, Chrysler hit big. By 1988, it was selling more than 450,000 annually. Competitors countered.</p><p>"It doesn't happen very often that a manufacturer has the opportunity to create a new segment and shape the industry," said Bruce Velisek, head of large car and MPV marketing for the Chrysler brand. "We have over 13 million customers who bought our van."</p><p><strong>The drive for relevance</strong></p><p>To envision the future, consider the present. Chrysler and its rivals are all trying to stay relevant in a sea of crossovers. Key players include the Dodge Grand Caravan, Chrysler Town & Country, Honda Odyssey, Toyota Sienna, Nissan Quest and Mazda5. Each has popular features that form the next generation's stew.</p><p>Chrysler Group splits its efforts between the family-friendly Grand Caravan and luxury-oriented Town & Country. Trademark Stow n' Go seats that flip into floor wells, Blu-ray disc players and large sliding consoles are popular with buyers.</p><p>Honda's Odyssey focuses on cutting-edge style, safety, class-leading fuel efficiency of 28 mpg and wide-screen video monitors. The "Z" windowline adds flair, while a sophisticated rear suspension delivers a smooth ride. Its durability is legendary.</p><p>"It is not your typical box on wheels," said James Jenkins, senior product planner for Odyssey. "It is a phenomenal driving vehicle. Fuel economy is top of class. Over 80 percent of Odysseys have power tailgates. Power sliding doors, blind spot monitors and dual wide-screen monitors with HDMI (high-definition multimedia interface) input are important."</p><p>The Toyota Sienna's calling cards are quality, widescreen video and available Barcaloungers in back. Toyota's Entune system lets owners access mobile apps like Bing, MovieTickets.com, OpenTable, and Pandora Internet radio.</p><p>Nissan's Quest luxuriates with cavernous, leather-lined interiors that rival the company's Infiniti brand for upscale buyers who choose minivans for their ease of use and comfort as much as for kids.
