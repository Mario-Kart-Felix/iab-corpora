        <h4>What defines a luxury car?</h4>
        <p>Car makers call for the government to rethink what is considered to be a luxury vehicle.</p>
	</div><!-- class:fdVideoWof -->
</div><!-- id:video-player-content -->

            
        <p>Is a<strong>&#160;<a target="_blank" href="http://news.drive.com.au/drive/car-of-the-year/dcoty-2012-best-people-mover-finalists-20120924-26h25.html">Toyota Tarago</a> </strong>a luxury car? According to the federal government, it is.</p>

        <p>Mercedes-Benz is leading a renewed push by carmakers to change the government's definition of luxury.</p>

                    
        <p>The Federal Chamber of Automotive Industries, as well as BMW, Audi and Toyota, are also calling for clearer guidelines about what cars attract the government's controversial luxury car tax, which at the moment covers about 15 per cent of Australia's new car market.</p>

                                    <!-- cT-imageLandscape -->
        <div class="cT-imageLandscape">

            <img src="http://images-2.drive.com.au/2013/07/25/4600702/07TaragoV6-04hr_729-620x349.jpg" alt="Toyota Tarago." />
                <p>The Toyota Tarago is classified as a luxury car. </p>
        </div>



        <p>While Mercedes is pushing for greater benefits for fuel-efficient cars, Toyota wants the threshold of about $60,000 lifted, while FCAI chief executive Tony Weber has called for the &#8220;distortionary tax&#8221; to be removed altogether.&#160;</p>

            <div id="adspot-300x250-pos-3" class="hidden"> <small>Advertisement</small> <script type="text/javascript"> var erA = document.createElement('iframe'); erA.setAttribute("id", "dcAd-1-4"); erA.setAttribute("src", "http://ad-apac.doubleclick.net/N6411/adi/onl.drive/newsandreviews/motornews;cat=newsandreviews;cat1=motornews;ctype=article;pos=3;" + document.dcAdsCParams +"sz=300x250;tile=4;ord=" + dcOrd + "?"); erA.setAttribute("width", "300"); erA.setAttribute("height", "250"); erA.setAttribute("scrolling", "no"); erA.setAttribute("marginheight", "0"); erA.setAttribute("marginwidth", "0"); erA.setAttribute("allowtransparency", "true"); erA.setAttribute("frameborder", "0"); erA.frameBorder = 0; document.dcdAdsE.push(erA); document.dcdAdsEH.push("adspot-300x250-pos-3"); document.dcdAdsEC.push("ad adCentred"); document.dcdAdsR.push("dcAd-1-4"); </script> <noscript> <iframe id="dcAd-1-4" src="http://ad-apac.doubleclick.net/N6411/adi/onl.drive/newsandreviews/motornews;cat=newsandreviews;cat1=motornews;ctype=article;pos=3;sz=300x250;tile=4;ord=9392121.0?" width='300' height='250' scrolling="no" marginheight="0" marginwidth="0" allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0"> </iframe> </noscript> </div>
        <p>&#8220;The basic rate kicks in at $60,000, and luxury at that price is debatable, especially for families which have four children. And the rate of tax [33 per cent] is just simply too high," Mr Weber said.</p>

        <p>Toyota sells the most vehicles affected by the tax, which applies to cars that cost at least $60,316.&#160;One of its flagship people-movers, the V6 Ultima Tarago, priced at $71,490 plus on-road costs,&#160;is considered a luxury car by the government.</p>

        <p>"Our customers already pay a range of taxes when buying a new car &#8211; GST, stamp duty and registration fees &#8211; and shouldn&#8217;t be penalised with an additional tax,&#8221; <strong><a target="_blank" href="http://drive.com.au/toyota ">Toyota</a></strong>&#160;media manager Beck Angel said.</p>

        <p>&#8220;At the very least, the luxury car tax should be reformed to feature a more realistic threshold and to exclude low emission vehicles and parts and accessories."&#160;</p>

        <p>A higher threshold already exists for fuel-efficient cars; if a car uses no more than seven litres of petrol over 100 kilometres, the tax doesn't come into play until the price tag hits $75,375. But luxury car makers have proposed a second threshold for ultra fuel-efficient vehicles.</p>

        <p>Mercedes-Benz Australia Pacific spokesman David McCarthy says the tax as it stands does not provide adequate incentives for buyers who want fuel-efficient technologies. He suggests a second $100,000 fuel efficiency threshold which would apply to cars that consumed five litres of petrol or less over 100 kilometres.</p>

        <p>&#160;&#8220;The reality is that the government isn&#8217;t going to abolish the luxury car tax &#8211; they earn $500 million a year from it," he said.</p>

        <p>&#8220;There are a lot of cars coming that can return fuel figures of five litres per 100 kilometres or less, and [a second threshold] fits with the opposition&#8217;s direct action policy and it fits with the government&#8217;s policy.</p>

        <p>&#8220;We&#8217;ve seen a real change in buyer behaviour with the seven-litres-over-100-kilometres limit; it has become a bit of a badge of honour for people to say they pay no luxury car tax, or they pay less.</p>

        <p>&#8220;We could send a really strong message that if you&#8217;re buying a car that returns five litres over 100 kilometres or less and it&#8217;s an expensive car, there should be an incentive.&#8221;</p>

        <p><strong><a href="http://www.drive.com.au/bmw" target="_blank">BMW</a></strong>&#160;and&#160;<strong><a href="http://www.drive.com.au/audi" target="_blank">Audi</a></strong>&#160;have endorsed Mercedes&#8217; proposal.</p>

        <p>&#8220;It&#8217;s something that is of great interest to us. From our point of view, we&#8217;re certainly striving to reduce emissions and a second threshold would be something of great interest to us,&#8221; BMW Australia spokesman Lenore Fletcher said.</p>

        <p>Audi&#160;Australia&#160;spokeswoman, Anna Burgdorf, adds: &#8220;Do we think that customers should be encouraged with a lower luxury car tax? Absolutely. It&#8217;s very much part of the platform we campaigned when the [tax] was a topic for discussion. Anything we can do to encourage people to buy more efficient vehicles is positive.&#8221;</p>

        <p>A spokesman for Treasurer Chris Bowen said there were no plans to alter the tax guidelines.</p>

        <p>&#8220;The government currently has no plans to introduce an additional threshold or scrap the luxury car tax,&#8221; he said.</p>

        <p>Mr McCarthy admitted there was some self-interest in his proposal, with Mercedes this week launching its first diesel-hybrid vehicle in&#160;Australia,&#160;<a href="http://smh.drive.com.au/new-car-reviews/mercedesbenz-e300-first-drive-review-20130723-2qhbf.html" target="_blank"><strong>the E300 Bluetec</strong>.</a>&#160;But he reiterated that changes to the tax were desperately required.</p>

        <p>&#8220;The large car isn&#8217;t dead, and if you provide a compelling reason &#8211; in this case the safety and technology and the innovation in the drivetrain and the fact that it is a full-size five-passenger car &#8211; then people will buy it,&#8221; he says.</p>

        <p>&#8220;If they made a five-litres-over-100-kilometres limit at $100,000, it would take about $8000 off the price of the E300 ($108,900). If that were the case,&#160;we&#8217;d work really hard to make sure there was not a premium on the hybrid version over the regular model it is based on.&#8221;</p>
