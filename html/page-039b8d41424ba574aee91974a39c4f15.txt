			<h1 class="entry-title"><a href="http://www.earthtechling.com/2013/01/infiniti-q50-hybrid-showcased-at-2013-detroit-auto-show/">Infiniti Q50 Hybrid Showcased At 2013 Detroit Auto Show</a></h1>

			<div class="row2">

			

			<p class="byline-sm">by <a href="http://www.earthtechling.com/author/nino/" title="Posts by Nino Marchetti" rel="author">Nino Marchetti</a> <img alt='' src='http://1.gravatar.com/avatar/751e151b0b1bf03b7cd27b70db7e1af9?s=25&amp;d=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.earthtechling.com%2Fwp-content%2Fthemes%2Fearthtechling-update%2Fimages%2Fet-100.jpg%3Fs%3D25&amp;r=G' class='avatar avatar-25 photo' height='25' width='25' /></p>

			</div><!-- .row2 -->

			<div class="entry wpsr_floatbts_anchor">
				
			<span class="wpsr_floatbts_anchor"></span><p><a href="http://www.earthtechling.com/tag/infiniti/">Infiniti</a> brought along with it to the <a href="http://www.earthtechling.com/tag/detroit-auto-show/">2013 Detroit Auto Show</a> a new sports sedan for 2014 that includes a hybrid powertrain option. The Q50 will debut this summer in the United States, followed by other global markets later in the year. No pricing was mentioned.</p>
<p>The Q50 hybrid offering <a href="http://infinitinews.com/en-US/infiniti/usa/channels/us-united-states-infiniti-models-infiniti-q50/releases/2014-infiniti-q50-sports-sedan-unleashes-inspired-performance-and-hospitality">will make use of Infiniti&#8217;s of</a> the auto brand&#8217;s &#8220;Direct Response Hybrid&#8221; system,Â combining a 3.5-liter 24-valve DOHC aluminum-alloy V6 and compact laminated lithium-ion battery design with a one-motor/two-clutch motor control, according to Infiniti. The system&#8217;s V6 is rated at 296 horsepower (est.) and 255 lb-ft of torque (est.), while the 50 kW electric motor is rated at 67 horsepower (est.) and 199 lb-ft of torque (est.). The hybrid system net power is 354 horsepower (est.).</p>
<div id="attachment_114256" class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 610px"><img class="size-full wp-image-114256" alt="Infiniti Q50 Hybrid (image via Infiniti)" src="http://www.earthtechling.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/nissan-q50hybrid.jpg" width="600" height="354" /><p class="wp-caption-text">Infiniti Q50 Hybrid (image via Infiniti)</p></div>
<p>This system will be mated with aÂ 7-speed electronically controlled automatic transmission with manual shift mode and available steering column-mounted magnesium paddle shifters. The Q50 is available in rear-wheel drive as well as all-wheel offerings.</p>
<p>Infiniti has included what it terms a number of &#8220;world&#8217;s first technologies&#8221; that are designed to make the driving experience better for those behind the wheel. The first is calledÂ Direct Adaptive Steering, which allows for &#8220;independent control of the Q50&#8242;s tire angle and steering inputs, transmitting the driver&#8217;s intentions to the wheels faster than a mechanical system.&#8221; This is said to increase driving performance, allowing for four different steering settings that provide &#8220;customization by driver preference or road conditions.&#8221;</p>
<p>The other &#8220;first&#8221; is something called Active Lane Control, making use of what&#8217;s described as &#8220;a camera-based straight-line stability system to further enhance on-center driving capability and improve vehicle stability.&#8221; It is part of a larger offering in the car&#8217;s technology that&#8217;s not only for &#8220;adjusting for unintended lane drift, but also making finer adjustments for minor road surface changes or crosswinds (as detected by the camera-based, lane marker detection system).&#8221;</p>
<p>There&#8217;s also a whole array of internal bells and whistles on the Q50, including dual touch-screens and an array of advanced functions, features and custom apps.</p>
