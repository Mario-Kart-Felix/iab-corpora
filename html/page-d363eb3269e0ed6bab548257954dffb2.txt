								<h1 class="single-title post-title" itemprop="headline">Top 5 Hybrid Cars In 2012</h1>
                                
								<p class="meta"> <time datetime="2012-03-27">03/27/2012</time>, <a href="http://www.automotivetips.org/author/victor/" title="Posts by Victor" rel="author">Victor</a>, <a href="http://www.automotivetips.org/top-5-hybrid-cars-in-2012/#comments" title="Comment on Top 5 Hybrid Cars In 2012">7 Comments</a></p>
													
							</header> <!-- end article header -->
					
							<section class="post_content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
	                        									<p style="text-align: justify;">Hybrid cars are appearing more and more in the mainstream. Auto manufacturers now offer consumers a much wider choice of hybrid vehicles, ranging from trucks with electric hybrid assist to boost gas mileage to nearly full-electric cars with an onboard engine to charges the batteries, such as the Chevrolet Volt. In basic terms, a hybrid vehicle uses a combination of gas or diesel engine and an electric motor to propel the car. The use of an electric motor boosts the total power of the car and reduces the amount of fuel used to travel a specific distance. The new breed of hybrid cars allows the consumer to choose a fuel-efficient vehicle in whatever class of car she is looking for: sexy, sporty, luxury or just highly efficient transportation. Here are five new or improved hybrid vehicles to watch for in 2012:</p>
<p><strong>Toyota Prius</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Toyota is working hard to make sure the Prius stays at the front of the hybrid car pack. New models and features will be introduced for<a href="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/toyota-prius1.jpg"><img class="alignright  wp-image-305" title="toyota-prius" src="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/toyota-prius1-300x164.jpg" alt="" width="240" height="131" /></a> 2012. The Prius C is the latest version of the hybrid Prius. The C includes nine airbags, Toyota&#8217;s Entune wireless communication technology and is rated for 53 miles per gallon. A plug-in version of the Prius will be offered in 15 states in 2012, more states in 2013. Plug in technology allows a car owner use home electric power to charge the batteries, saving even more on gas. Finally, the Prius V, a station wagon like model comes out this year. The V has more cargo space than the Ford Escape SUV hybrid and is rated up to 44 miles per gallon.</p>
<p><strong><br />
<a href="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/2013_chevy_malibu_hybrid.jpg"><img class="alignleft  wp-image-306" title="2013_chevy_malibu_hybrid" src="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/2013_chevy_malibu_hybrid-300x198.jpg" alt="" width="240" height="158" /></a>2013 Chevrolet Malibu eAssist</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Chevy is launching an all new Malibu in 2012, listed as a 2013 model. The new Malibu is both sportier and more efficient than the previous version of the car. The Chevy eAssist system is a mild hybrid setup using an electric motor/generator, lithium-ion battery back and regenerative braking to increase the Malibu&#8217;s fuel economy. The new 2013 Malibu is rated for 37 miles per gallon on the highway.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">
<p style="text-align: justify;"><strong><a href="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/01-ford-c-max-energi630opt.jpg"><img class="alignright  wp-image-307" title="01-ford-c-max-energi630opt" src="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/01-ford-c-max-energi630opt-300x191.jpg" alt="" width="240" height="153" /></a>Ford C-Max Hybrid<br />
</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">In the fall of 2012, Ford will bring the 2013 C-Max Hybrid to the U.S. The C-Max Hybrid is based on the C-Max minivan/ multi-purpose vehicle Ford sells in Europe. The hybrid C-Max will be manufactured and sold in the U.S. The C-Max resembles the Prius V in form. The C-Max hybrid uses a Atkinson-cycle four-cylinder, light weight lithium-ion battery pack and a continuously variable transmission. The C-Max will be rated at up to 44 miles per gallon. A plugin version of the hybrid C-Max, called the Energi, is scheduled for release in early 2013.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">
<p><strong><a href="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/600-jetta-span.jpg"><img class="alignleft  wp-image-308" title="600-jetta-span" src="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/600-jetta-span-300x172.jpg" alt="" width="240" height="138" /></a>Volkswagen Jetta Hybrid</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Americans may not be aware, but Volkswagen is on a path to become the world&#8217;s largest car manufacturer within the next five years. To reach that goal the company needs to offer hybrid models and the first will be a hybrid version of the Jetta to be launched on the fall of 2012 as a 2013 model. The new Jetta will bring sporty affordability to the choices of hybrid cars. The hybrid Jetta will be fitted with a turbo-charged 1.4 liter gasoline engine producing 150 horsepower. The combined electric motor boosts total horsepower to 177. The transmission is a 7-speed, dual clutch unit. A lightweight lithium-ion battery pack, which weights just 80 pounds, will be mounted behind the rear seat, balancing the car&#8217;s weight distribution for better handling. With all of this sportiness, the VW Jetta hybrid will still get up to 42 miles per gallon.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/BMW-3-Series-Hybrid.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-309" title="BMW-3-Series-Hybrid" src="http://www.automotivetips.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/BMW-3-Series-Hybrid-300x200.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="200" /></a>BMW 3-Series and 5-Series ActiveHybrid</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">BMW is taking the same ActiveHybrid technology and applying it to the company&#8217;s popular 3 and 5-Series sports sedans. The 5-Series ActiveHybrid will be available in Spring 2012 and the 3-Series in the Fall. The hybrid technology is mounted along with serious gasoline power. The 5-Series has a twin-turbo, V-6 engine and the hybrid powertrain puts out 335 horsepower, 10 percent more than the non-hybrid 5-Series. The luxury hybrid sports sedan is rated at 35 miles per gallon on the highway. The smaller 3-Series hybrid will also have a 6-cylinder motor and has produced over 37 miles per gallon on an European test circuit. The BMW ActiveHybrid system allows these cars to go up to 37 miles per hour on electric power alone. The battery pack recharges from regenerative power when the car is coasting or braking.</p>
<p>2012 will be an exciting year for fans of hybrid cars. The choices range from extremely fuel-efficient cars getting over 50 miles per gallon &#8211; higher if the plug-in mode is used &#8211; and high power autobahn blasting sports sedans. which still get close to 40 miles per gallon. A hybrid car allows a car owner to have the cake and eat it too when it comes to driving fun and fuel efficiency.</p>
