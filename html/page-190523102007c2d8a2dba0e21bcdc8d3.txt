The best picture quality you can find....period! <br><br>I tend to study reviews and comparison shop before I buy anything. After reading reviews from dozens of websites I went to Tweeter, Circuit City, Best Buy, Car Audio, and several other stores to see the TV's myself. Two things became apparent Circuit Cityhad the lowest prices and the highly reviewed 8.5' Jensen was the cream of the crop offering the best picture and quality in my $500 price range. I went back to Circuit City to buy it and found out it wouldn't fit in my 2005 Suburban. The large rear ceiling mount A/C and heating controls had to be removed to install a TV and the small profile of the TV would not cover the hole, leaving a 1/4' gap in the headliner. My only options were to accept the headliner gap or pick a different TV with a larger profile. The headliner gap was unacceptable so I decided to go with a larger TV.<br><br>I hadn’t planned on spending more so I went back to the drawing board and compared the higher price range TV’s. Just as before, the Jensen (12.1” screen) blew away every other TVfor sharpness, clarity, and color and it really wasn’t even a contest. I can’t emphasize enough that you need to go look at TV monitors mounted in stores so you can see the picture quality. I did not see another TV anywhere at any price that had better picture quality than either of the Jensens period. Even though it was more than double what we had planned on spending, we winced and went with the 12.1' and had Circuit City install it. <br><br>PRO’s<br>* Wireless transmission - the kids can wear headphones so I can still listen to the radio or CD player. <br>* Large picture size - this makes anything under 12' obsolete. <br>* Unmatched picture quality - if you have not seen it in person YOU HAVE TO, it's amazing. <br>* Has a wireless game controller with 30 games - don't expect any games you know, these are knock-offs of primitive early 80's style games but still a fun little touch. <br>* Sleek low profile - looks like a factory installed TV. <br>* Remote control - full function remote allows anyone to completely control the TV. <br>* Auto memory - when you stop the DVD the next time you turn it on it goes back to the point you stopped it. A small detail but I have seen ones where they start from the beginning every time you turn it on, a major pain. <br><br>CON’s<br>* Remote control function mostly - only play, stop and eject have buttons on the TV console. All other functions (fast forward, slow, etc.) must be done with remote control. <br>* Blocks a huge portion ofthe rear-view mirror - any TV will do this, but you can forget using the rear view mirror when this is down. <br>* DVD slot on right side - actually I think this should be a plus, I think they meant to force the passenger to change the DVD, not the driver. I must sheepishly admit I have no problem ejecting and replacing the DVD while driving though. <br>* You will get lonely - my wife now sits in the backseat to watch TV with the kids….or is that a pro? <br>* When traveling with multiple vehicles all the kids will be in your car because of this TV - not really a con but something you should be ready for<br><br>All told, we wound up spending over double what we planned on spending and we don't regret it. The product is well worth the price and with the durability I expectfrom Jensen it should be a great deal over the years. The 12.1' screen is huge, the picture quality is unbelievable, and Jensen has a solid reputation of building quality products. There are dozens of TV's you can buy in the $1100 range that are no wherenear the quality of this unit. You can find many cheaper TV’s, but you will get a cheap TV. Go with Jensen and you will be happy. <br><br><br><br><br>I had the unit installed yesterday andam very happy with it so far. When the unit was first installed, it had a “smudge” on the screen that could not be wiped off with a cloth. Without me even having to ask, the manager of the installation department told the installer to take the unit outand put in a new one. It only took a few minutes to swap the units and I was very happy that they did it without me having to ask.<br><br>The unit fits very nicely in my 2002 Honda Odyssey. The unit is fairly slim and since the van has no center overhead console or light, there was plenty of room for the unit. Also, I was very pleasantly surprised to see that the color of the unit matched my grey interior. The unit that is pictured online is black (and that is actually how the unit comes), but what the website does not tell you is that the box includes two plastic bezels/covers for the unit. One in grey and the other is tan. However, the picture on the website is NOT the 12.1” unit. The unit that was installed in my van has the DVD slot on the right side. The picture online shows it in front of the unit (just like the 8.5” unit). This is not a huge deal, but it is a little bit difficult to put a DVD in from the driver’s seat since the DVD slot is on the opposite side.<br><br>Another thing that the website does not mention is the format compatibility. After I bought the unit I was a little bit apprehensive because I did not know whether it would play my DVD-Rs or MP3 discs. But after the installation I was happy to find out that the unit plays both DVD-R andMP3 discs. This was a concern of mine since I was also not able to test out the unit prior to buying because the store had not yet installed the 12.1” model in the showroom. Another plus is that there is also an RCA input on the back of the unit so that you can connect video from another source such as a camcorder, etc. When I put a DVD-RW of a tv program that I recorded on my DVR, the disc did not play very well. So, the DVD-RW compatibility doesn’t seem to be very good.<br><br>A very unexpected bonus that the website does not mention was the integrated game system that is built into the unit. It comes with 30 games (reminiscent of the old Nintendo games from the late 80s). It also comes with a wireless game controller which is separate from the DVD remote control. I am not very much into video games, but the installers thought it was very cool when they “tested” the game feature of the unit.<br><br>The only things that I would improve upon the unit would be (1) having the DVD slot on the front of the unit rather than the passenger side, (2) having the screen be able to swivel from side to side, and (3) a media card reader slot, while not imperative, would have been nice.<br><br>Regarding the 1-5 ratings, I did not install the unit myself, so I have no idea what the 'ease of installation' is. Also, I've only had the unit a couple days, so I don't know about the durability. But I did buy the extended warranty based upon the recommendation of buyers/reviewers of the 8.5' Jensen system so I am not worried so muchabout the durability now since I know that Circuit City will replace the unit if it has any problems. The system seems easy enough to use, although my wife couldn't figure out right away how to change the screen settings from wide screen to normal. <br><br>Just purchased this past weekend and wow, what a picture. Installed in a 2004 Nissan Quest. Drive otu price after tax was $1435.00. Included 4 year warranty, antenna extensions (2) and the hard wired modulator. <br><br>We got the 12 inch version because ofthe 3rd row. Didnt want to have to squint to view it. Well, there is no squinting at all. In fact, I was following behind in another car and I could see what movie was playing and knew which part it was at. <br><br>Ease of use, No brainer. My eight year old can run it.<br><br>Ease of installation. No brainer there too. I let the guys at Circuit City do it. Lets face it, its an expensive toy that needs to be installed correctly. They did a great job. I also had it hard wired into the sound system rather than going with the Wireless Modulator. Well worth the extra money.<br><br>Durablitiy. No problem there. Should it break, I bought the warranty. If the thing hiccups or has any problems, they take it out and put a new one in. I got the 4 year plan. Again, its an expensive toy that kids will be using.
