    <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded"><h1>Diesel Technology</h1><h2>Learn more about Lincoln's Diesel Mechanic schools<br /> and the Diesel Technology program</h2><p>From 18-wheelers, to buses and construction equipment, diesel engine-driven machines serve as the behind-the-scenes network that keeps our society moving. And Lincoln's Diesel mechanic schools prepares you for a career working with the colossal powered machines that impact our lives in big ways.</p><p>Diesel and truck technicians don’t just service trucks – they maintain buses and other diesel powered industrial machines. At Lincoln, we maximize Diesel training by bringing diesel engines, tractors, trailers and other off-road construction equipment inside our giant diesel shops for you to train on.</p><h3>Diesel Training Classes And More</h3><p>As an aspiring Diesel Technician, hands-on training plays a critical role in your education.</p><ul><li style="margin-left: 0.25in;">Perform a complete disassembly and assembly of a diesel engine, to include the cylinder head, block and timing gears, by following class instruction and the engine manufacturer’s service information.</li><li style="margin-left: 0.25in;">Get the opportunity to possibly train and apply for positions directly with companies and manufacturers who specialize in diesel-powered machines, off-road construction equipment and trucks in your area.*</li><li style="margin-left: 0.25in;">Complete tune-ups, as well as perform wheel alignments to tractors and trailers using computerized equipment.</li><li style="margin-left: 0.25in;">Service, repair and replace the components of truck suspension systems and frame as part of your diesel training.</li><li style="margin-left: 0.25in;">Gain knowledge in how to identify, locate and diagnose the components of truck brake systems, as they apply to hydraulic, air over hydraulic or air brake systems.</li><li style="margin-left: 0.25in;">Classes are taught by <a title="Learn more about Lincoln's partnership with ASE - click here. " href="http://www.lincolnedu.com/about/partnerships" target="_blank">ASE (Automotive Service Excellence) certified instructors</a>.</li><li style="margin-left: 0.25in;">Learn how to service and repair diesel fuel systems such as the Common Rail System, Detroit Diesel Electronic Controls (DDEC), Caterpillar EUI and other systems.</li><li style="margin-left: 0.25in;">Train inside our A/C and Electrical Accessories classrooms where you’ll find training board simulators such as starting/charging systems, power windows, locks and other electrical systems found in today’s modern trucks.</li></ul><h3>Careers As A Diesel Mechanic</h3><p>From fleet maintenance departments, private repair enterprises, and franchised truck repair organizations, to truck leasing and rental operations, Lincoln's Diesel mechanic training can lead to job opportunities nationwide. Our <a title="Put Career Services to work for you. Click here to learn how we help our graduates find their first job after graduation." href="http://www.lincolnedu.com/career-services" target="_blank">Career Services department</a> knows this firsthand, and will work hard to help you connect with these opportunities.</p><p>Want to find out more about Lincoln’s Diesel mechanic schools? <a title="Request more information by clicking here." href="http://www.lincolnedu.com/contact">Request more information by clicking here</a>.</p><p><em>*These programs are sponsored by individual manufactures and qualifications and acceptance into these programs is governed by their criteria.</em></p></div></div></div>  </div>
