<br/>
If you're in the market for a new electric vehicle, now is definitely the time to buy or lease. Following price drops on just about every other EV on the market, <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/toyota/">Toyota</a> is looking to push a few more all-electric <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/toyota/rav4/">RAV4</a> crossovers out the door over the Labor Day weekend. <em>The Detroit News</em> is reporting that, through September 3, anyone looking to get into a <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/tag/rav4+ev/">RAV4 EV</a> in Los Angeles or San Francisco, can get a deal: a $299 a month lease (no word on down payment, though) or zero-percent financing for 60 months for buyers.<br/>
<br/>
The $49,800 RAV4 EV normally leases for twice as much - <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2012/09/14/toyota-rav4-ev-epa-rating-78-mpge-103-mile-range-lease/">$599 a month</a> &ndash; so this move could be an attempt to sell more of the <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2012/05/14/evs-why-toyota-set-sales-numbers-for-rav4-ev-at-just-2-600/">2,600 units Toyota has planned to produce</a>. Or maybe, if you thought $599 was a fair price, you can now get two RAV4 Evs. Although temporary, the price drop comes at a time when many other EVs on the market have had their prices reduced, including the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2013/01/14/nissan-leaf-becomes-least-expensive-5-seat-ev-with-massive-price/">Nissan Leaf</a>, <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2013/08/06/chevy-lops-4-000-off-price-of-2014-volt/">Chevy Volt</a>, <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2013/08/08/smart-fortwo-electric-drive-lease-now-139-mo/">Smart Fortwo ED</a> and <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2013/05/30/honda-fit-ev-lease-drops-to-259-adds-unlimited-miles/">Honda Fit EV</a>, not to mention already low prices for the <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2013/07/29/fiat-500e-199-lease-deal-sounds-great-is-nearly-impossible-to/">Fiat 500e</a> and <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2012/11/27/chevy-spark-ev-unveiled-priced-under-25k-with-tax-incentives/">Chevy Spark EV</a>.<br/>

<br/>
<a href="http://www.autoblog.com/mazda/">Mazda</a> is set to expand production of its <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/tag/mazda+skyactiv/">Skyactiv</a> engines after critical and commercial acclaim for the fuel-sipping powerplants. The Japanese manufacturer has a number of plans in the works to bump up production, with the first being a 25-percent increase in output from its Hiroshima, Japan engine facility.<br/>
<br/>
Besides adding a new line, Mazda will modify the line that built MZR engines, a family of mills that includes the 2.3-liter, turbocharged four-cylinder found in the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/mazda/mazdaspeed3/">Mazdaspeed3</a> and the 2.0-liter found in the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/mazda/mx-5+miata/">MX-5 Miata</a>. The bump in production is just part of Mazda's goal of selling 1.7 million vehicles globally by 2016, with 80 percent of those vehicles expected to wear a Skyactiv badge.<br/>
<br/>
Mazda also builds Skyactiv engines at a joint-venture facility with <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/ford/">Ford</a>, in Changan, China, while a Mexican facility will go online by March of 2014. Skyactiv engines currently power the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/mazda/mazda3">Mazda3</a>, <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/mazda/mazda6">Mazda6</a> and <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/mazda/cx-5/">CX-5</a>.

One of the treasures of ZZ Top's excellent 1979 album <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YB6-44vJwPk"><em>Deg&uuml;ello</em></a> is the rather funky track <em>I'm Bad, I'm Nationwide</em>. We can imagine that song blasting from the six-speaker sound system in <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/nissan/leaf">Nissan Leaf</a> all over the US now that <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/nissan">Nissan</a> is getting ready to get serious nationwide. The world's most popular EV, initially marketed and popular mostly on the West Coast, has gained substantial momentum within the flyover states. This helped the <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2013/08/01/chevy-volt-sales-drop-to-1-788-nissan-leaf-up-to-1-864-in-july/">sales rate through the first seven months of the year</a> more than tripled from a year earlier. Indeed, a recent jump in Leaf demand in the Atlanta, GA area has pushed that market to No. 3 among US cities, with locales such as Nashville, TN and St. Louis, MO sneaking into the US Top 10.<br/>
<br/>
Granted, the Coast is still the king when it comes to Leaf sales. It's even Nissan's best-selling model in San Francisco, CA, Portland, OR and Seattle, WA. Big state incentives and HOV-lane allowance for solo Leaf drivers keep San Francisco and Los Angeles, CA in the top two slots for US Leaf sales, with Seattle and Portland coming in at No. 4 and No. 5, respectively. Rounding out the Top 10 are Honolulu, HI, San Diego, CA, Sacramento, CA and, of course, the aforementioned Nashville and St. Louis. Chicago, IL and Denver, CO are in a dead heat for 11th place, according to the Japanese automaker. Somewhat surprisingly, while New York, NY comes in 15th in Leaf sales, it is <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2013/08/17/over-half-of-all-evs-in-america-are-in-these-five-cities/">in the top five overall for EVs, according to R.L. Polk</a>. Check out Nissan's press release <a href="/2013/08/27/nissan-leaf-sales-ready-to-expand-beyond-early-adopter-markets/#continued">below</a>.

<a href="http://www.autoblog.com/photos/nissan-leaf-autonomous-drive/" target="_blank"><img alt="Nissan Leaf Autonomous Drive demonstrator" data-mep="4196" src='http://o.aolcdn.com/js/x.gif' data-src="http://o.aolcdn.com/dims-shared/dims3/GLOB/crop/4589x3084+221+0/resize/628x417!/format/jpg/quality/85/http://hss-prod.hss.aol.com/hss/storage/adam/145fd28588a966381bbd63339bed0d17/D21F1068.jpg"/></a><br/>
<br/>
<a href="http://www.autoblog.com/nissan/">Nissan</a> will bring the autonomous car to consumers by the end of this decade. The announcement was made by CEO <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/tag/carlos+ghosn/">Carlos Ghosn</a> at the company's US headquarters in Irvine, CA. Nissan has already begun construction of a dedicated proving ground for the self-driving cars in Japan, with completion targeted for the end of 2014.<br/>
<br/>
Teaming with MIT, Stanford, Oxford and others, Nissan has already outfitted <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/nissan/leaf/">Leaf</a> EVs with the Autonomous Drive (Nissan's brand name for the tech), a suite of new technologies developed from the brand's existing Safety Shield technology. The current iteration of Autonomous Drive uses the Around-View Monitoring system and laser scanners to analyze the environment, while artificial intelligence systems have been installed to help navigate and operate in a changing environment.<br/>
<br/>
While it's easy to say that Nissan will bring the technology to market within the next six or seven years, it's more difficult to say at what price Autonomous Drive will be available. Most remarkable about all of this is Nissan's claim that self-driving cars will be both commercially viable and available at "realistic prices for consumers." It's expecting Autonomous Drive to be available across its range within two vehicle generations.<br/>
<br/>
Nissan's motivation rests largely with the number of accidents that happen on US roads alone each year - six million accidents that cost consumers $160 million and kill more people between the ages of four and 34 than anything else. Considering the overwhelming majority of those accidents are caused by human error, this tech seems like a great idea.<br/>
<br/>
Carlos Ghosn demonstrated Nissan's resolve towards bringing the tech to market, saying, "In 2007 I pledged that &ndash; by 2010 &ndash; Nissan would mass market a zero-emission vehicle. Today, the Nissan Leaf is the best-selling electric vehicle in history. Now I am committing to be ready to introduce a new ground-breaking technology, Autonomous Drive, by 2020, and we are on track to realize it." Those are some bold words, but what Nissan is promising now is far more complex than a new drivetrain. We look forward to seeing what it comes up with.

<img alt="Tesla Model S" src='http://o.aolcdn.com/js/x.gif' data-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/green.autoblog.com/media/2013/08/tesla-1377581486.jpg" style="margin-top: 4px; margin-bottom: 4px; width: 628px; height: 417px; "/><br/>
<br/>
You can't chalk this one up to Dutch windmills and an extra push they may have theoretically given an electric car. Instead it's just plain electric power. A team of students from the Netherlands was able to <a href="http://www.greencarreports.com/news/1086453_dutch-drivers-cover-388-miles-on-single-charge-of-tesla-model-s">wring a whopping 388 miles</a> out of a <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/tesla/model+s/">Tesla Model S</a> on a single charge, but instead of any sort of special circumstances or super slow speeds, the team achieved this distance in "normal" traffic, <em>Green Car Reports</em> says. That's far more than <em>The New York Times</em> managed in a drive last winter that turned into a public <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2013/02/15/cnn-proves-tesla-model-s-can-make-infamous-road-trip-more-attem/">war of words</a>.<br/>
<br/>
With a car provided by Athlon Car Lease, the students from Eindhoven University of Technology averaged 24 miles per hour in regular traffic. The achievement is impressive given that the Tesla is rated at up to 265 miles on a single charge and the drive brings a bit of good PR to California-based Tesla, as if it needed any. Tesla <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2012/12/20/tesla-announces-european-price-model-s/">recently started European sales</a> and has its European distribution center in the Netherlands.<br/>
<br/>
The Dutch team did fall short of a Florida driver's effort last year to <a href="http://wheels.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/12/12/father-and-son-drive-423-miles-on-one-charge-in-tesla-model-s/?_r=0">get 423 miles out of a Tesla on one charge</a> late last year. Of course, that driver's average speed was just 18 miles per hour, implying that someone had a little extra time on their hands.

<img alt="Nissan Leaf" src='http://o.aolcdn.com/js/x.gif' data-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/green.autoblog.com/media/2013/08/nissanleaf.jpg" style="margin-top: 4px; margin-bottom: 4px; width: 628px; height: 417px; "/><br/>
<br/>
As <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/nissan/leaf/">Nissan Leaf</a> sales continue to heat up, the maker of the electric vehicle wants to cool concerns over hot-climate battery issues by making <a href="http://www.greencarreports.com/news/1086498_nissan-tests-new-heat-resistant-battery-for-leaf-electric-car">chemical adjustments</a> to battery packs, <em>Green Car Reports</em> says. Nissan executives met with about 10 Leaf owners in Phoenix last week and said the company is approaching a solution that will better allow Leaf lithium-ion batteries to withstand extensive heat without losing capacity faster than Leafs that live in cold-weather climates.<br/>
<br/>
Specifically, Nissan is testing new Leaf batteries under sustained temperatures of about 113 degrees Fahrenheit in an effort to replicate what some Arizona locals call a temperate month. All joking aside, the company may start using the new chemical composition in replacement batteries that would be deployed as soon as next April, <em>Green Car Reports</em> says.<br/>
<br/>
Nissan executives <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2012/09/25/nissan-addresses-leaf-battery-woes-customer-communication-short/">have been trying to address</a> high-heat fears since Arizona Leaf owners started complaining that their batteries were degrading faster than advertised, and have since replaced batteries in about two-dozen Leafs located in hotter areas. Nissan still says that a very small percentage of Leaf batteries - less than 0.1 percent - were adversely affected specifically by sustained heat and needed to be replaced, though the company did bring together an independent global advisory board to address the issue last year.

<br/>
The power of ions has overcome two models in the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/volkswagen/">Volkswagen</a> range, the resulting e-<a href="http://www.autoblog.com/volkswagen/golf/">Golf</a> and e-<a href="http://www.autoblog.com/volkswagen/up/">Up</a> slated for world premieres at the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/frankfurt-motor-show/">Frankfurt Motor Show</a>. The e-Up uses a 60-kW motor producing 155 pound-feet of torque, gets from 0-to-62 miles per hour in 12.4 seconds an on to a top speed of 81 mph. It's 18.7 kWh battery will get it a range of 100 miles, and VW figures that its ability to go 100 kilometers with 11.7 kWh of electricity will cost just three euros and two cents at current rates.<br/>
<br/>
The larger e-Golf gets more powerful internals, a 24.2-kWh battery powering an 85-kW motor producing 199 lb-ft of torque, getting it from standstill to 62 mph in 10.4 seconds and on to an electronically-limited of 87 mph. It has a range of 118 miles, its cost to do 100 km only slightly higher than the e-Up at three euros and 30 cents, or 12.7 kWh.<br/>
<br/>
Driving dynamics can be controlled through two driving modes and four regenerative brake modes. Each car also shares standard features like climate control and navigation, and both can be charged to about 80 percent of battery capacity in thirty minutes using the powerful CCS chargers in Germany. The e-Golf goes further, getting LED headlights as standard - the first VW to get the high-tech eyes.<br/>
<br/>

<a href="/2013/08/26/tesla-turning-to-samsung-sdi-for-batteries-panasonic-pumps-up-t/#continued"><img data-caption="Tesla logo as it appears on the rear of the Model S" data-mep="4037" src='http://o.aolcdn.com/js/x.gif' data-src="http://o.aolcdn.com/dims-shared/dims3/GLOB/crop/1109x746+133+0/resize/628x417!/format/jpg/quality/85/http://hss-prod.hss.aol.com/hss/storage/adam/8bde95632070b868773d87a04643c397/Tesla%20logo%20in%20situ%20on%20Model%20S.jpg" alt=""/></a><br/>
<br/>
<a href="http://www.autoblog.com/tesla/">Tesla Motors</a> has, over its short life, sourced its batteries pretty much <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2010/01/08/tesla-motors-and-panasonic-working-together-on-battery-tech/">exclusively from Panasonic</a>. Now that sales of the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/tesla/model+s/">Model S</a> are <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2013/08/07/tesla-sold-5150-models-s-evs-in-q2/">blowing up</a> &ndash; expected to be in excess of 21,000 units this year, with production ability increasing to potentially <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2013/07/12/tesla-could-make-800-model-s-evs-a-week-by-late-2014/">double that</a> &ndash; and the company's future product path is becoming <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2013/03/11/tesla-model-x-delayed-one-year/">more</a> <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2013/04/09/elon-musk-3rd-tesla-model-coming-2016-or-2017/">clear</a>, it seems time to diversify its battery supply lines.<br/>
<br/>
<blockquote class="pull-quote pull-quote-right">
<p>Tesla may eventually need more lithium cells than the entire laptop industry.</p>
</blockquote>
The automaker may eventually have a need for more lithium cells than the entire laptop industry and finds itself one earthquake (or other natural disaster) away from a huge production disruption. If previous rumors and an unidentified "source close to the matter" used by <em>The Korea Herald for </em><a href="http://www.koreaherald.com/view.php?ud=20130825000207">a recent report</a> are to be believed, Samsung SDI will be the first to offer an alternative to Tesla's traditional Japanese supplier. It currently makes the cells for the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/bmw/i3/">BMW i3</a>, the <a href="http://autoblog.com/fiat/500e/">Fiat 500e</a> and is said to have a contract with <a href="http://autoblog.com/volkswagen/">Volkswagen</a>.<br/>
<br/>
The deal is yet to be finalized, with testing still ongoing. According to the report, LG Chem was also in the running but was beat out by its Korean competitor and Chinese battery maker <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/category/byd/">BYD</a> may be working out its own deal.<br/>
<br/>
For its part, Panasonic is not sitting still. Earlier this month, <a href="http://www.globaltimes.cn/content/800773.shtml#.Uhu-2z8yOEG">it was reported</a> that the Japanese company is increasing its production of automobile-specific lithium cells, with two separate facilities in Osaka Prefecture coming online in 2014 to boost output. Battery sales are becoming increasingly important to the company's bottom line, adding 4.1 billion yen ($41.66 million US at today's rates) to its balance sheet last quarter.<br/>
<br/>
Besides dealing with the diversification issue, we expect increased competition for Tesla's high-energy capacity, 18650-format battery business should spur lower prices and improve the technology.

<br/>
We've <a href="http://www.green.autoblog.com/2009/10/20/tokyo-2009-nissans-ghosn-confirms-three-evs-for-production-i/">heard this one before</a>, the one where we hear <a href="http://autoblog.com/nissan">Nissan</a> is getting ready to expand its line-up of all-electric vehicles. We're all familiar with the <a href="http://autoblog.com/nissan/leaf">Leaf</a> and then there are the other Nissan-family EVs, the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/infiniti/">Infiniti</a> <a href="http://www.green.autoblog.com/2010/09/25/infinitis-first-electric-vehicle-will-hit-showrooms-in-2013-he/">luxury electric vehicle</a> (which Infitini boss Johann De Nysschen recently re-confirmed to <em>AutoblogGreen</em> is coming, <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2013/08/22/infiniti-boss-de-nysschen-evs-absolutely-coming-just-not-now/">someday</a>) and the <a href="http://www.green.autoblog.com/2011/07/07/nissan-to-test-nv200-electric-van-in-japan/">NV200 utility van</a>. But wait, there's more.<br/>
<br/>
<em>Automotive News</em> spoke with Carla Bailo, senior vice president for research and development at Nissan Americas, and learned that a total of five Nissan EVs are in the works. No announcements were made as to which ones are on the drawing board &ndash; could it be a sports car like the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2011/02/09/nissan-esflow-concept-is-a-sporty-rwd-leaf/">Esflow</a>? In other words, the previously only-for-luxury-buyers wireless technology could someday soon be seen on a mass market Nissan EV. When? No clue, since Bailo just said, "The other [EVs] will come in due time." We'll keep waiting, and probably hearing more rumors along these lines while we do.

<a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2013/08/26/peugeot-reveals-le-mans-derived-208-hybrid-fe-prototype/" class="entry-title" rel="bookmark" target="_blank">Peugeot reveals Le Mans-derived 208 Hybrid FE prototype</a>
</h4>
<div class="info">
<div class="about">
<p class="byline">
<span class="label">By&nbsp;</span>

<a href="http://www.autoblog.com/photos/peugeot-208-hybrid-fe/" target="_blank"><img alt="Peugeot 208 Hybrid FE - front three-quarter view, white" data-caption="Peugeot 208 Hybrid FE" data-mep="3922" src='http://o.aolcdn.com/js/x.gif' data-src="http://o.aolcdn.com/dims-shared/dims3/GLOB/crop/619x418+4+0/resize/628x417!/format/jpg/quality/85/http://hss-prod.hss.aol.com/hss/storage/adam/dc0c2c63e23b4967716659b891ce5c04/web15-peugeot-208-hybrid.jpg"/></a><br/>
<br/>
You can't leave a team of racing engineers sitting still for too long. They need something to keep them busy. Just ask the guys at <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/toyota/">Toyota</a> or <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/category/peugeot/">Peugeot</a>.<br/>
<br/>
Toyota shut down its Formula One program in 2009 and didn't get to Le Mans until last year, filling the vacuum left by Peugeot's departure at the end of the previous season. In the meantime, the engineers at <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/tag/tmg/">Toyota Motorsport GmbH</a> developed the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/tag/tmg+sports+650/">TMG Sports 650</a>. Peugeot Sport had a similar lull after shuttering its LMP1 program before it launched <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/tag/peugeot+208+t16/">Sebastien Loeb's </a><a href="http://www.autoblog.com/tag/peugeot+208+t16+pikes+peak/">208 T16</a> Pikes Peak challenger this summer. This is what they were working on in the meantime.<br/>
<br/>
It's called the Peugeot 208 Hybrid FE, and it's set to debut next month at the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/frankfurt-auto-show">Frankfurt Motor Show</a>. Working in partnership with French oil company Total, the Peugeot racing team applied many of the lessons it learned from developing the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/tag/peugeot+908/">908 HDi FAP</a> prototype to a base <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/tag/peugeot+208">Peugeot 208</a> Access.<br/>
<br/>
They bored out the 1.0-liter three-cylinder engine to 1.2 liters, gave it direct injection, replaced all the internal components and coated new ones in DLC (Diamond-Like Coating, a hardener used in motorsports engines and suspension components). All of this was done in order to increase torque by 25 percent (now up to 88 pound-feet) while keeping power steady at 68 horses. They then installed the 908's electric motor for another 22 lb-ft of torque, along with its lightweight lithium-ion battery pack. They decreased aerodynamic drag by 25 percent (thanks in part to low-rolling-resistance rubber and a narrower track) and cut 20 percent off the structure's curb weight to optimize efficiency.<br/>
<br/>
The result is a prototype that sacrifices none of the donor car's creature comforts (save for the air conditioning system) and can hit 62 mph from a standstill in 8 seconds flat, return 141.2 mpg (on Europe's liberal efficiency cycle) and emit just 49 grams of CO2 per kilometer. All of which may not be as exciting as what Toyota's racing team did with their down time, but as environmental impact becomes increasingly vital to global automakers, it could prove more important.

<a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2013/08/26/epa-says-fuel-economy-test-hybrids-accurate/" class="entry-title" rel="bookmark" target="_blank">EPA says fuel economy test for hybrids is accurate</a>
</h4>
<div class="info">
<div class="about">
<p class="byline">
<span class="label">By&nbsp;</span>
<span class="author vcard">

<p><a href="http://www.autoblog.com/photos/2013-ford-c-max-hybrid-review/" target="_blank"><img alt="EPA claims its hybrid vehicle tests remain accurate, but says Ford C-Max Hybrid exploited a loophole." data-caption="2013 Ford C-Max Hybrid, front three-quarter view" data-mep="3988" src='http://o.aolcdn.com/js/x.gif' data-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/www.autoblog.com/media/2013/08/ford-c-max-hybrid.jpg"/></a><br/>
<br/>
The EPA says it stands behind its fuel economy test for hybrid vehicles following controversy about the testing process after <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/ford/c-max/">Ford C-Max Hybrid</a> customers and automotive journalists alike <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2013/05/02/2013-ford-c-max-hybrid-review/">struggled to achieve 47 miles per gallon</a>, the advertised mpg number, <a href="http://www.autonews.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20130826/OEM11/308269980/epa-says-its-mpg-test-holds-up-for-hybrids#axzz2d56RPA00"><em>Automotive News</em> reports</a>. Ford <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2013/08/15/ford-c-max-hybrid-rerated-at-43-mpg-owners-offered-rebates/">responded to the issue</a> almost two weeks ago by claiming that a 1970s-era EPA general label rule was responsible for the inaccurate mileage numbers, rerating the C-Max Hybrid's mpg numbers and offering customers rebates. <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/ford/">Ford</a> later said it <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2013/08/19/ford-didnt-overstate-c-max-hybrid-mpg-number/">didn't overstate the C-Max Hybrid's fuel economy</a> and that it was surprised by the low numbers.<br/>
<br/>
Ford technically didn't do anything wrong because it was following the general label rule, but agency regulator Christopher Grundler says the automaker was exploiting a loophole when it came up with the hybrid <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/ford/c-max/">C-Max</a> numbers, and that the testing process remains accurate. The general label rule allows vehicles that use the same engine and transmission and are in the same weight class to share fuel economy numbers, but it doesn't take into account other factors such as aerodynamic efficiency, which affects hybrids more drastically than non-hybrid vehicles. Ford originally used the <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2013/04/01/2013-ford-fusion-hybrid-review/">Fusion Hybrid</a> economy figures for the C-Max Hybrid and claimed the engineers didn't realize that its aerodynamic efficiency would affect fuel economy as much as it did.<br/>
<br/>
To address the increasingly commonplace practice of using the same powertrain in multiple applications, Grundler says, the EPA rules need to be changed so more car buyers are not mislead.<br/>
<br/>
<a href="http://www.autoblog.com/toyota/">Toyota</a> is standing with the EPA on this one after spurring a revision of test methods in 2006 after overstating Prius mileage numbers. Toyota said in a statement that it believes "the current labeling methodology established since 2006 provides appropriate fuel economy label values for customers, when automakers apply these rules with good common sense and engineering judgement." And yes, that sounds like a well-aimed but somewhat ill-timed shot from Toyota to Ford to us, too...</p>

<a href="http://www.autoblog.com/photos/2013-volkswagen-beetle-sunshine-tour/" target="_blank"><img alt="A knit Beetle wins the 2013 Volkswagen Sunshine Tour" data-mep="3845" src='http://o.aolcdn.com/js/x.gif' data-src="http://hss-prod.hss.aol.com/hss/storage/adam/62c98628017fdf4f8d09d2b508070046/2013-volkwagen-sunshine_tour-000.jpg"/></a><br/>
<br/>
Well, now we've really seen everything. At the ninth <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/volkswagen/">Volkswagen</a> Sunshine Tour, an annual gathering of thousands of <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/volkswagen/beetle/">Beetle</a> lovers, the "most beautiful beach Beetle of the Year" award went to this <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/volkswagen/new+beetle/">New Beetle Convertible</a> enrobed in 40 kilograms &ndash; that's about 88 pounds &ndash; of colorful, knitted wool. According to VW's press release, it took Marthe and Magnus Schwarz, who met at a previous Sunshine Tour (where else?), seven months to create their unique work of stitchery. As you can see in the photo above, Marthe even went the extra mile by knitting her husband a special beard for the occasion.<br/>
<br/>
The annual Sunshine Tour takes place in Germany and is the largest private gathering of lovers of all three Beetle generations (the original Beetle, <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/volkswagen/new+beetle/">New Beetle</a> and The Beetle). People came from across Europe for the August 17 event, bringing a record 414 Beetles of all themes, colors, shapes and sizes.<br/>
<br/>
Alongside the parade of old Beetles, the new <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2013/04/19/volkswagen-ibeetle-gets-integrated-iphone-dock-little-else/">iBeetle</a> from Volkswagen made its European premiere. The Beetle Cabrio Exclusive from Volkswagen R GmbH and the limited-edition <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2013/07/17/limited-edition-vw-beetle-gsr-priced-from-29-995/">Beetle GSR</a> also made appearances. <a href="/2013/08/26/vw-new-beetle-knit-art-car-wins-sunshine-tour/#continued">Scroll down</a> to get all the "sunshiney" details in VW's press release.

<h4 class="post-head">
<a href="/2013/08/26/how-alternative-energy-makes-for-bad-public-policy/" class="entry-title" rel="bookmark">How alternative energy makes for bad public policy</a>
</h4>
<div class="info">
<div class="about">
<p class="byline">
<span class="label">By&nbsp;</span>
<span class="author vcard">
<span class="fn"><a href="/bloggers/jon-lesage/" title="Browse posts by Jon LeSage" target="_blank">Jon LeSage</a></span>

<img height="472" src='http://o.aolcdn.com/js/x.gif' data-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/green.autoblog.com/media/2013/08/white-house.jpg" vspace="4" width="628"/><br/>
<br/>
John DeCicco is starting to make a name for himself speaking out against green cars. In June, he said electric vehicles won't help much <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2013/06/17/u-of-m-researcher-says-plug-ins-hydrogen-cars-are-no-carbon-cu/">unless the energy well they tap is cleaned up</a>. He's now back with an article in a Yale University publication called <em>Environment 360</em> that we're guessing some people won't like very much. DeCicco says that government subsidies and mandates, including the electric vehicle and alternative fuel vehicle campaign championed by the Obama administration, are a big waste of taxpayer's money.<br/>
<br/>
<blockquote class="pull-quote pull-quote-right">
<p>There is "no environmentally persuasive reason to rush alternative fuel vehicles onto the road."</p>
</blockquote>
DeCicco is no stranger to the green car world. He is a University of Michigan research professor who's also been a senior fellow at the Environmental Defense Fund and who pioneered <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2013/01/17/prius-c-aceees-greenest-vehicle-ford-wins-meanest/">US green car ratings</a> for the American Council for an Energy-Efficient Economy. In his <em>Environment 360</em> article, DeCicco lists programs each of the last five presidents have supported that he's critical of: Ronald Reagan signing off on the 1988 Alternative Motor Fuels Act; George H.W. Bush helping to shape the 1992 Energy Policy Act; Bill Clinton launching the <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/tag/clean+cities/">Clean Cities</a> program; George W. Bush supporting hydrogen fuel cell vehicles and the <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/tag/renewable+fuels+standard/">Renewable Fuel Standard</a>; and Barack Obama wanting to see a million electric vehicles on US roads by 2015 and providing funding for <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2011/03/31/obama-entire-federal-fleet-purchases-to-be-alt-fuel-by-2015/">alternative fuel vehicle</a> deployment. DeCicco is blunt about what disagrees with in these Obama administration policies, in that they're "under the misguided presumption that alternative fuels emit significantly fewer greenhouse gases than gasoline, and goaded by green groups and alternative fuel business interests." He also says, that the reality is that the way we make alternative fuels today, gives "no environmentally persuasive reason to rush alternative fuel vehicles onto the road."<br/>
<br/>
For DeCicco, much of it boils down to the source of the energy. For example, about two thirds of the electricity that powers EVs comes from fossil fuels &ndash; coal and natural gas &ndash; which takes away the environmental benefits of plugging in. He thinks that energy independence and reducing greenhouse gas emissions can be carried out on three fronts. <a href="http://e360.yale.edu/feature/why_pushing_alternate_fuels__makes_for_bad_public_policy/2682/">Read the whole thing</a> for the detailed analysis and DeCicco's suggested solutions.

<a href="http://www.autoblog.com/photos/2012-fisker-karma-first-drive/#photo-3903513/" target="_blank" target="_blank"><img alt="Fisker logo on sheetmetal" class="post_top_img" src='http://o.aolcdn.com/js/x.gif' data-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/green.autoblog.com/media/2013/02/fisker-logo-628.jpg" style="margin-top: 4px; margin-bottom: 4px; width: 628px; height: 417px;"/></a><br/>
<br/>
The position of CEO of <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/fisker/">Fisker</a> has once changed hands. In early 2012, <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2012/02/28/tom-lasorda-named-fisker-ceo/">company co-founder Henrik Fisker stepped down and Tom LaSorda took the position</a>. LaSorda gave it up in August of that year <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2012/08/14/tony-posawatz-replaces-tom-lasorda-as-fisker-ceo/">to former Chevy Volt honcho Tony Posawatz</a>. It's now twelve months later, and <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/tag/tony+posawatz/">Posawatz</a> is reportedly leaving for, we assume, greener pastures. Taking his place will be company co-founder Bernhard Koehler.<br/>
<br/>
When Henrik Fisker left the company altogether in March, the company said his "departure is not expected to impact the Company's pursuit of strategic partnerships and financing to support Fisker Automotive's continued progress." Around the same time, Posawatz was <a href="http://green.autoblog.com/2013/04/01/fisker-ceo-tony-posawatz-investor-patience/">asking investors to be patient</a>, saying they were investing in more than a car. Those positive spins haven't stopped Fisker's problems from continuing &ndash; it hasn't built a car in over a year &ndash; and we recently heard that German investment group Fritz Nols AG was bidding <a href="http://www.autoblog.com/2013/08/23/fisker-to-be-purchased-by-germans-for-25m/">just $25 million</a> for all of Fisker.<br/>
<br/>
News of Posawatz's departure comes from an unconfirmed report originally from <em>Jalopnik</em>, which said the issue was money. The Fisker website and Posawatz's <em>LinkedIn</em> profile still list him at CEO, and <em>AutoblogGreen</em>'s phone calls and emails to the company and its PR agency have not yet been returned to confirm or deny the news.
