				<h2 class="entry-title">
                	                    Barn Find: Knoxville&#8217;s Lost Auto Treasures Include Shoebox Wagons                                    </h2>
                                <div class="links links-top">
                                    <div class="posted-by">
                                    	<img src="http://2475-dragzine.voxcdn.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/1/files/userphoto/salvatorealaimo.thumbnail.jpg" alt="Salvatore Alaimo" width="62" height="80" class="photo" />                                        <p>by <a href="http://www.chevyhardcore.com/author/salvatorealaimo/" title="Posts by Salvatore Alaimo" rel="author">Salvatore Alaimo</a>                                        on                                        June 20, 2013                                        </p>
                                    </div>
                                    <div class="comments-count"><a href='#fbComments'><fb:comments-count href=http://www.chevyhardcore.com/news/barn-find-knoxvilles-lost-auto-treasures-include-shoebox-wagons/>0</fb:comments-count> Comments</a></div>
                                </div>
                                <div class="clear"></div>
				<div class="entry-content">
<strong><span style="text-align:left"></span></strong><p><a href="http://cdn.speednik.com/files/2013/06/57chevythumb.jpg" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://cdn.speednik.com']);" rel="shadowbox[sbpost-455696];player=img;"><img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-328942" alt="57chevythumb" src="http://cdn.speednik.com/files/2013/06/57chevythumb-640x480.jpg" width="640" height="480" /></a>History is just one of those things that has a strange way of repeating itself. It&#8217;s no less true for our automotive heritage, and so the traditions that are connected with the rides we love are ones that we are reluctant to part with.</p>
<p><a href="http://cdn.speednik.com/files/2013/06/Wagon-2.jpg" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://cdn.speednik.com']);" rel="shadowbox[sbpost-455696];player=img;"><img class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-328540" alt="Wagon 2" src="http://cdn.speednik.com/files/2013/06/Wagon-2-300x275.jpg" width="300" height="275" /></a>Just a few years back, a colleague from the San Fernando Valley in Los Angeles had talked about the cherished, 1st-gen Vette that had belonged to his father since new. The &#8217;59 C1 turned out to be a premium-optioned car, but even with a fuelie 283 and correct 4-speed intact, the Corvette classic somehow became a garaged relic that wouldn&#8217;t see the light of day for some 30 years. The gentleman who owned the C1 eventually passed, and so the car was never restored.</p>
<p><a href="http://cdn.speednik.com/files/2013/06/Wagon-4.jpg" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://cdn.speednik.com']);" rel="shadowbox[sbpost-455696];player=img;"><img class="alignleft size-thumbnail wp-image-328537" alt="Wagon 4" src="http://cdn.speednik.com/files/2013/06/Wagon-4-300x214.jpg" width="300" height="214" /></a>Such is a similar story out of Knoxville, Tennessee, and in spite of the fact that our featured &#8217;57 Chevy wagon carries with it a bitter-sweet story, we find that it makes for a very cool barn find. The story behind our featured shoebox is that it was preserved by a Knoxville local to be restored by his son, who sadly passed just a few years back. Since then, both this &#8217;57 wagon and a &#8217;46 Willys Jeep have been stashed in a car port, wearing 1982 plates from the state of Tennessee.</p>
<p>The current owner of the cool, Bowtie wagon has indicated that the car is not a Nomad, but was fortunate enough to be packaged with the era&#8217;s higher-output 283, a raised-compression variant of Chevy&#8217;s early small-block with a 4-barrel intake option. The wagon&#8217;s original owner felt that his family didn&#8217;t share his enthusiasm for the &#8217;57 shoebox and the early Jeep, so he decided to sell both vehicles to someone who could restore and appreciate them.</p>
<p>All of us have had cars and trucks in our lives that have held a special place in our walks through the world; which car or truck from your past or current collection do you think is the most resto-worthy?!</p>
<p><a href="http://cdn.speednik.com/files/2013/06/57chevyfind.jpg" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://speednik.com']);" rel="shadowbox[sbpost-455696];player=img;"><img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-328941" alt="57chevyfind" src="http://cdn.speednik.com/files/2013/06/57chevyfind-640x480.jpg" width="640" height="480" /></a><br /><script type='text/javascript'>OA_show('content-BLOCK-1');</script></p>
