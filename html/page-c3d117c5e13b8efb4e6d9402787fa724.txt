<p>"<b>Business</b>" is a song by American rapper <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, released on July 7, 2003, as the fifth and final single from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Eminem_Show" title="The Eminem Show">The Eminem Show</a></i>. The single was not released in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Business_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Composition">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Business_(song)&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Composition" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Business" is a <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> song of four minutes and eleven seconds in length.<sup id="cite_ref-AMG_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> It sees Eminem comparing himself and Dr. Dre, the song's producer, to fictional crime-fighting duo <a href="/wiki/Batman" title="Batman">Batman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robin_(comics)" title="Robin (comics)">Robin</a>, a comparison first explored in the music video for the previous Eminem single "<a href="/wiki/Without_Me" title="Without Me">Without Me</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-raprev_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raprev-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rchristg_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rchristg-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The lyrics are backed by a "cartoonish" beat:<sup id="cite_ref-Pitchfrev_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pitchfrev-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> one of several Dr. Dre productions on <i>The Eminem Show</i> which, according to CultureDose writer Marty Brown, affords Eminem a "perfect sound-scape" to inspire emotions in the listener, calling the beat "a launchpad equally effective for humor or anger".<sup id="cite_ref-CDrev_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CDrev-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Writing for <a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_Media" title="Pitchfork Media">Pitchfork Media</a>, Ethan P. noted the "cartoonish" production to be similar in style to several of Eminem's early singles, claiming it to be fitting to the Batman and Robin theme on "Business", but noted that "this time he's actually talking about Batman and Robin!!".<sup id="cite_ref-Pitchfrev_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pitchfrev-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> DX Magazine editor J-23 called this "classic" with Dre beats.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Kris Ex On "Business": "Em names himself the gatekeeper of hip-hop and obliquely claims to be the best rapper alive: "The flow's too wet/Nobody close to it/Nobody says it, but everybody knows the shit."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
