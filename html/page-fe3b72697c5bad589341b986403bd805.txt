          <h2><a href="http://www.newluxurycarblog.com/buying-a-used-car-for-the-summer/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Buying A Used Car For The Summer">Buying A Used Car For The Summer</a></h2>
					<p class="post-info">            
            Apr 11th, 2013 by <a href="http://www.newluxurycarblog.com/author/admin/" title="Posts by Jacqueline Starz">Jacqueline Starz</a>  </p>          
				</div>

				<div class="entry">
					<p><a href="http://www.newluxurycarblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/2012_jaguar_xf_sedan_base_fq_oem_2_500.jpg" ><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-638" title="2012_jaguar_xf_sedan_base_fq_oem_2_500" src="http://www.newluxurycarblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/2012_jaguar_xf_sedan_base_fq_oem_2_500-300x189.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="189" /></a> Summer is the perfect time to get a new ride in order to enjoy the warm weather. How a bout a Jeep Wrangler that you can drive around town with the top down? Or a sweet convertible to take to the beach or go on a road trip? One of the most affordable ways to get a new ride is to buy a used vehicle. They are way more affordable than new cars plus they don&#8217;t depreciate as much in value. Let&#8217;s take a look at the easiest ways to get a used car for summer.</p>
<p>Local Dealership</p>
<p>Visit your local dealer if you are looking for a car with a warranty. These used cars have generally passed rigorous inspection points. An extended warranty can generally be purchased at an additional price. Make sure to look up the value of the vehicle on Kelley Blue Book and don&#8217;t be afraid to negotiate if the price is too high. You have the power to walk away from a deal if you don&#8217;t feel that you are getting a good offer.</p>
<p>Private Seller</p>
<p>Cars sold by private sellers are generally a lot more affordable. However, they do come with additional risks since they don&#8217;t come with a warranty. Be prepared to spend extra money to have an independent auto mechanic check out the vehicle. Inspect the interior, engine and odomoter. It&#8217;s also a good idea to order a Carfax report so you can view the auto&#8217;s history including number of owners and accidents. One of the more popular way of purchasing from a third party seller is via Craigslist which is an online classified site. It is updated every few minutes with new ads plus it&#8217;s free. If you want to go the more traditional route you can always browse the classifieds section of your local newspaper.</p>
<p>Internet Research</p>
<p>Auto shopping is easier than ever thanks to the internet. Sites like Edmunds allow you to read reviews before making a final decision. It&#8217;s a good idea to read both consumer and user reviews and form your own opinion. Expert reviews offer detailed analysis and thorough comparison to other vehicles in the same price range and niche. Consumer reviews will give you a better insight as to how it to actually use the vehicle from day to day.</p>
<p>Are you looking for an affordable car dealer? <a href="http://www.davesinclairusedcars.com/" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outbound/article/www.davesinclairusedcars.com');">Used Cars O&#8217;Fallon</a> dealer Dave Sinclair Used Car in Columbia IL offers  great selection of pre-owned autos at great prices. Get a stylish looking ride without breaking the bank.</p>

          <h2><a href="http://www.newluxurycarblog.com/used-luxury-car-buying-tips/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Used Luxury Car Buying Tips">Used Luxury Car Buying Tips</a></h2>
					<p class="post-info">            
            Feb 13th, 2013 by <a href="http://www.newluxurycarblog.com/author/admin/" title="Posts by Jacqueline Starz">Jacqueline Starz</a>  </p>          
				</div>

				<div class="entry">
					<p><a href="http://www.newluxurycarblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/2012_Jaguar_XF_.png" ><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-629" title="2012_Jaguar_XF_" src="http://www.newluxurycarblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/2012_Jaguar_XF_-300x176.png" alt="" width="300" height="176" /></a>Think it costs a fortune to buy a luxury car? Not if it&#8217;s used. Financially speaking, it doesn&#8217;t make much sense to pay full price for a new luxury vehicle. A car depreciates the moment you drive it off the lot which means that you can save a ton of money buy purchasing pre-owned. Some people may be under the assumption that it&#8217;s &#8220;better&#8221; to buy new but the fact of the matter is that quality control is highly controlled when it comes to certified vehicles from brands like Mercedes, Porsche and Volkswagen. Here are a few tips to help you get the most bang for your buck.</p>
<p>Buy Certified Pre-Owned</p>
<p>Dealers such as Mercedes and BMW offer certified programs for their <a href="http://www.valleyimports.com/inventory/used-vehicles" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outbound/article/www.valleyimports.com');">used cars</a>. These vehicles have undergone thorough inspection points and in some cases in near perfect condition. Some of these autos may come with a guarantee or extended warranty. You&#8217;ll generally have more piece of mind when purchasing from such a program instead of buying through a third party seller<em><strong>. </strong></em>Browse sites such as <a href="http://www.galpinjaguar.com/" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outbound/article/www.galpinjaguar.com');">Galpin Jaguar</a> as in order to get a ballpark figure of how much particular makes and models are selling for in your local area.</p>
<p>Obtain A Carfax Report</p>
<p>If you choose to buy from a third party seller you&#8217;ll definitely want to obtain a Carfax report. This will give you an accurate report of a vehicle&#8217;s history including mileage, number of previous owners, accidents and any flood damage. You may also want to bring an independent mechanic along in order to scope out the car before you buy it. The last thing you want to do is shell out a large amount of money for a lemon.</p>
<p>Invest The Money Instead</p>
<p>There are so many things you can do with the savings from buying  a used luxury vehicle vs a brand new one; the savings are usually staggering. You can use that money to take a vacation, put it in your kids college funds or perhaps even invest it. The fact of the matter is that most cars are very similar in appearance from year to year. The average person won&#8217;t  be able to tell the difference between a 2011 vs a 2012 model unless the manufacturer made some drastic external changes. Buying used is the smart way to go if you want to save the big bucks.</p>

          <h2><a href="http://www.newluxurycarblog.com/younger-generation-is-driving-less/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Younger Generation Is Driving Less">Younger Generation Is Driving Less</a></h2>
					<p class="post-info">            
            Dec 20th, 2012 by <a href="http://www.newluxurycarblog.com/author/admin/" title="Posts by Jacqueline Starz">Jacqueline Starz</a>  </p>          
				</div>

				<div class="entry">
					<p>Surveys have shown that younger buyers are less likely to buy a car versus the previous generations in the 80&#8242;s and 90&#8242;s. The <a href="http://www.davesinclairusedcars.com" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outbound/article/www.davesinclairusedcars.com');">used cars</a> demographic used to have a huge hold on teens and young adults; buying a vehicle used to be a rite of passage for many. However, with the advent of social media, millennials are spending more time online than ever before navigating social networking sites such as Facebook and Twitter. The car used to be a means for people to meet up on a Saturday night and hang out. Now it&#8217;s more of a hindrance since it&#8217;s easier for most to communicate via social media. Millennials are also less materialistic than previous generations; they would rather spend money on technology such as the latest iphone and ipad instead of a new car. Technology gadgets are considered more of a status symbol than of car ownership.  The economy is another factor that is influencing buying decisions; many younger consumers have less access to disposable income due to the job market and don&#8217;t want to spend huge sums of money on transportation.</p>
<p>So what can auto dealers do in order to appeal to younger buyers?  <a href="http://www.myfordofclermont.com/" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outbound/article/www.myfordofclermont.com');">Ford Ocoee</a> dealer has a large inventory of Ford cars many which offer Ford&#8217;s SYNC system which allows bluetooth capabilities and voice controlled multi-media functions. Millennials favor audio systems with USB ports in order to listen to their favorite tunes stored on mp3 players and cell phones. Access to instant information about nearby restaurants and entertainment venues as well as traffic updates is also crucial.  The key to appealing to younger generations is to manufacture vehicles with capable infotainment systems; auto companies that will make these features standard or even relatively affordable have a higher chance of reeling in the younger car buyers.</p>
