If a   repair is needed to make your vehicle operable, the service provider will tow   your vehicle to a repair facility of your choice. The emergency road service   provider will accept your personal check for up to $250. A valid membership   card, and personal identification must be presented at the time of payment. </p>
         <p>&nbsp;</p>

<h2><a name="basic" id="basic"></a><strong>Basic ERS Member Benefits</strong></h2>
<p>The following emergency services will be provided to place   your vehicle in a driveable condition. </p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Mechanical adjustments</strong> Minor/temporary adjustments will   be made in an attempt to enable your vehicle to operate safely under its own   power. </li>
    <li><strong>Battery boost</strong> A battery boost will be provided in an   attempt to start your vehicle. </li>
    <li><strong>Flat tire</strong> If your vehicle&rsquo;s spare is inflated and   serviceable, it will be installed to replace a flat tire. If no inflated spare   is available, the vehicle will be towed. </li>
  <li><strong>Fuel delivery</strong> A limited supply of fuel will be   delivered to your disabled vehicle to enable you to reach the nearest open   service station. The current pump price of the fuel may be charged, depending on   which AAA club territory you are in when service is rendered. </li>
     <li><strong>Towing</strong> When your vehicle cannot be safely driven after   attempting any of the listed emergency services, your vehicle will be towed to   the ERS facility rendering the service, or at least 3 miles in any one direction   from the point of disablement at no charge, depending on which AAA club   territory you are in when service is rendered.</li>
   <li><strong>Lockout service</strong> If your keys are locked inside the   vehicle, service will be sent to gain entry. If the service provider cannot gain   entry, a locksmith will be called and services up to $50 will be provided.Â </li>
<li><strong>Extrication/winching</strong> Your vehicle will be   extricated/winched when it can be safely reached from a normally traveled   thoroughfare. Additional expenses may be charged if special equipment or   personnel are required.</li>
</ul>
