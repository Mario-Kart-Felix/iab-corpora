</div><!-- /adman_adcode_beginning --><p>Russia&#8217;s Yo-Auto automaker is going to cut the annual production target of its gas-electric hybrid, the Yo-Mobile, from 90,000 to only 40,000 a year, its general director said Thursday.<span id="more-891"></span></p>
<p>The Yo-Mobile is a Russian hybrid electric car featuring a maximum speed of 75 miles per hour (120 km/h), a cruising range of 249 miles (400 km) and an estimated fuel economy rating of 67 miles per gallon (30 kilometers per liter).</p>
<p>Full-scale production of the vehicle will begin in 2015, months later than previously announced, director Andrei Ginzburg said, adding that the original plan was reviewed for cost optimization.</p>

<p>The Yo-Mobile is being marketed as an environmentally friendly, energy-saving car for inner-city driving.</p>
<p>General director Ginzburg added on Thursday that &#8220;if the market demands it, we will increase the manufacturing capacity.&#8221;</p>
<p>Russian media has reported that the car will use a Fiat engine, but Yo-Auto has declined to confirm such information.</p>
