	<h2>Automotive, Motorcycle, and Diesel Educational Programs</h2>
<p><img height='200' width='200' alt='diesel mechanic' style='float: right; margin: 5px;' src='http://classesandcareers.com/education/wp-content/uploads/diesel-mechanic.jpg' /></p>
<p>Do you love cars, trucks, and anything that has 4 wheels and an engine? Then you need to look at a career in automotive, motorcycle, and diesel, often called <strong>automotive service technician</strong>. Individuals in this field check, maintain, and repair vehicles that use gasoline, electricity, and other fuels to run. This can include everything from normal commuting cars and trucks to huge semi-trucks and even recreational vehicles like ATV's (all terrain vehicles). America's love affair with automobiles is still going strong and the need to keep America's cars running smoothly is also stronger than ever.</p>
<h3>Strong Earnings Potential for Those Employed in Automotive, Motorcycle, and Diesel Programs</h3>
<p>The average hourly wage for automotive for automotive service technicians in 2008 was almost $17.00 per hour. The top ten percent of technicians were earning almost $29.00 per hour. Wages varied greatly due to experience and training, according to the <a href='http://www.bls.gov/oco/ocos181.htm#earnings'>United States Bureau of Labor Statistics</a>. <div id='hidden_content_1' style='display: none;'><p></p>
<h3>Job Growth for Automotive, Motorcycle, and Diesel Service Technicians</h3>
<p>The field of automotive, motorcycle, and diesel service and repair is expected to grow at about 5 percent between now and 2018, according to the Occupational Outlook Handbook. Some of this growth will occur because Americans are keeping their cars longer and also because cars are lasting longer. These older vehicles will need to be repaired and kept running. This will create some growth in this field. &nbsp;Another factor that will create new jobs in this industry is the advanced technologies that are being used to create new more energy efficient vehicles. Those wishing to be employed in this field will be best equipped to meet the demands and competition of this field if they obtain the right training. Job opportunities will be very strong for those who obtain post secondary education and earn certifications in automotive service and repair.</p>
<h3>Great Training Programs Available in Automotive, Motorcycle, and&nbsp;Diesel Technician Field</h3>
<p>For those interested in becoming and automotive service technician, there are lots of colleges and universities that offer excellent training programs in this area. Many schools offer certification programs, associate's degrees, bachelor's degrees, and even master's degree in this field. &nbsp;It all depends on how much education you want to obtain, what you want to specialize in, and what kind of salary you want to make when you graduate.</p>
<h3>Courses Taken for Automotive Repair Technicians<strong><br /></strong></h3>
<p>Classes often focus on engine repair, brakes, fuel systems, and hydraulic applications. Courses will also instruct you in proper tool usage, repair, manual usage, trouble shooting and much more.</p>

	<h2>Auto, Motorcycle, and Diesel Career Outlook</h2>

<p>Careers in this industry offer great stability and will only  increase as the number of vehicles in America continues to grow. People  with Auto, Motorcycle and Diesel Technology degrees also advance to become shop  supervisors and shop owners, and they work as:</p>

<ul>
<li>Automotive service technician</li>
<li>Motorcycle service technician</li>
<li>Diesel service technician</li>
<li>Auto, motorcycle &amp; diesel dealer</li>
<li>Equipment renters</li>
<li>Custom designers and fabricators</li>
<li>Vehicle technology developers</li>
</ul>                             


</div>


<div id="section_3" class="info" style='display:none;'>
  

	<h2>Auto, Motorcycle, and Diesel Salary Information</h2>

<p>Average earnings for people in these fields range from  $25,000 to $40,000 per year. Many find jobs that offer commissions on top of  guaranteed wages. Many with a degree in Auto, Motorcycle and Diesel Technology  advance to become shop owners and supervisors, and their earnings range from  $40,000 to $80,000. </p>
                                 


</div>


<div id="section_4" class="info" style='display:none;'>
  

	<h2>Auto, Motorcycle, and Diesel Required Job Skills and Knowledge </h2>

<p>Most technician jobs require formal automotive, motorcycle  and diesel technology training. Course work usually takes between six months  and two years to complete, and many schools supplement course work with  on-the-job training. This industry is best for people with great mechanical  aptitude. Because of the rapid advancements in technology, you will use  computers as part of the diagnostic and repair process. For the best jobs,  employers look for technicians who have great communication and analytical  skills.</p>
