<p>The Citroën DS4 Crossover,  a high-riding five-door hatch, is the first vehicle in the French automaker&#8217;s lineup that incorporates it latest design language, which was showcased by the <a href="http://www.netcarshow.com/citroen/2010-ds_high_rider_concept/"><em>DS High Rider Concept</em></a>. Based on the recently revealed <a href="http://www.autotribute.com/6180/new-citroen-c4-revealed/"><em><strong>C4 hatchback</strong></em></a>, the DS4 has a muscular appearance that is accentuated by its coup-like profile. Its big wheel arches provide room for a range of large wheels. 19-inch alloys wheels will be featured on the top-end models. The use of LED in the headlamps, in addition to, the skillful use of chrome lends the DS4 an upscale look. </p>
<p>Inside, Citroën has created a cabin that is both luxurious and spacious. It uses of higher-class materials and decorative trim puts it a notch above the standard C4 hatchback. A tall, wide windshield provides great visibility. Its steering wheel, sculpted dashboard and center stack look work together to provide a fresh and modern look. </p>
<p>At launch, the DS4 will come with a choice of five engines. There will be two diesel options, a 110HP 1.6-liter HDi that can be specified with stop/start tech and regenerative braking technology, and a 160HP 2.0-liter HDi. The petrol range consists of three 1.6-liter engines, including a naturally aspirated version with 120HP and two turbocharged variants with 155HP and 200HP. All engines are available with manual or robotized manual EGS six-speed gearboxes.</p>
<p>European sales of the new Citroën DS4 will start in 2011. More details will be provided at the upcoming Paris Motor Show.</p>
