        <h1>2009 Ford Focus Sedan</h1>
        <span class="font12 bold">Living it up for less</span><br/>
        <span class="author"><span itemprop="author">Deborah Neal </span> on <span itemprop="datePublished">11.19.2008</span></span><br /> 
        <div itemprop="breadcrumb">
            <a href="/Reviews/C-5" class="underline blue">Reviews</a> >
<a href="/Reviews-2009/C-84" class="underline blue">2009</a> 
      </div>
                <p >
                    <span class="sectiontext">Most of us are familiar with the budget-friendly Ford Focus. It's been on the American automotive scene for nearly a decade. We'll get to the refresher course below on why the Focus is still a big seller, but first is what makes it better for 2009.</span>
                </p>
<h2 class="font12">New for 2009</h2>                <p style=margin-top:0px;>
                    <span class="sectiontext">Especially appealing if your taste exceeds your take-home, the 2009 Ford Focus five-seat sedan gets additional luxury with a new SEL model. It includes heated chrome mirrors and door handles, heated, leather-trimmed seats, plus SYNC-Ford's voice-activated mobile phone and digital music system developed in cahoots with Microsoft. 2009 Focus sedan SEL models also get 16-inch alloy wheels. SES models get the option to upgrade to 17-inch wheels, plus new suspension tuning. Also new for all 2009 Focus models, coupe and sedan, is the availability of Ford's AdvanceTrac Electronic Stability Control (ESC) system.</span>
                </p>
                    <a id="single_image" href="http://cdn.automedia.com/images/articles/rts/rts20081101fs/2009-ford-focus-sedan_01.jpg" onclick="hideArrows()"><img src="http://cdn.automedia.com/images/articles/rts/rts20081101fs/2009-ford-focus-sedan_01.jpg" width="180" height="116" alt="2009 Ford Focus Sedan" title="2009 Ford Focus Sedan" style="margin-left:5px;"/></a>
<h3 class="font12">Powertrain, Mileage, and Trims</h3>                <p style=margin-top:0px;>
                    <span class="sectiontext">Besides the new SEL trim, the 2009 Ford Focus sedan is available in S, SE, and SES levels. The base S model starts at $14,995 and sits on 15-inch wheels and gets a single-disc CD player with AM/FM radio and MP3 capability. Focus SE sedans start at $16,180 and add to the S model basics with SIRIUS Satellite Radio, power windows, locks and keyless entry. Stepping up to the Focus SES adds (to SE-model basics) Ford's SYNC Communications System and Ambient Lighting. SES pricing starts at $17,570. The new top-of-the-line SEL model starts at $17,970. It gets the basic features from the SES package and adds the heated, leather-trimmed seating, 16-inch "Euroflanged" alloy wheels, and the chrome mirror and door-handle treatment.</span>
                </p>
