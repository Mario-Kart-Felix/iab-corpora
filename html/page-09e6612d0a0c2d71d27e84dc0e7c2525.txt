            <h1 class="title">Buying a classic car</h1>
            <p>Friday 16 July 2010</p>
        </div>
        <div class="articleBody">
            <div class="mpu">
                <span id="placeholderMPU"></span>
            </div>
            <div id="articleMainContent">
        <p><img class="size-full wp-image-24125 alignleft" style="margin-bottom: 11px;" title="Buying a classic car" src="http://www.autotrader.co.uk/articleresources/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/buyingaclassiccar380x249.jpg" alt="Buying a classic car" width="380" height="249"/><strong>Buying a classic car offers an attractive </strong><strong>design and driving experience but </strong><strong>what are the potential pitfalls of buying an old car?</strong></p>
<p>There are usually well-documented things to look for when buying any classic, but you should:<br />
• Know what you’re buying – if it’s been restored, is the work of a high standard. Is it original? If not, are the modifications appropriate and installed correctly?<br />
• Check for rust everywhere – particularly the places you can’t usually see, so peel back carpets and pay special attention underneath and inside the wheelarches<br />
• Ensure it has been converted to run on unleaded petrol – it can be an expensive conversion if it hasn&#8217;t been done<br />
• Find out if the seller has any replacement parts – it could be easier than sourcing them yourself<br />
• Check for persistent blue smoke, which can indicate expensive internal oil leaks, and excessive white smoke which can point to head gasket failure – another costly repair<br />
• Know how you’re going to get it home – you might need a trailer for a non-runner</p>
<p>Whatever classic you choose to buy, there will be an owners club for it. Clubs are invaluable in helping you buy and maintain your classic – and most have good social events to help make the most of your ownership.</p>
<h3>Why buy a classic car?</h3>
<p>• You’ll own a piece of history<br />
• Unique driving experience<br />
• Enthusiastic owners’ clubs and events<br />
• Can qualify for low-cost classic car insurance and pre-1973 cars are road tax exempt<br />
• Can be a good investment, increasing in value</p>
<h3>Other considerations</h3>
<p>• No warranty<br />
• Servicing, parts and repairs can be hard to find and expensive, particularly if the manufacturer no longer exists<br />
• Maintenance or restoration costs can be considerable<br />
• Little protection offered in an accident<br />
• Many are sensitive to rain, snow and road grit, limiting their use to the summer months<br />
• A garage is essential to store and maintain a classic car properly<br />
• Most classics need constant attention and maintenance</p>
