			<h2 id="post-1211"><a href="http://auto.infoniac.com/2012/01/lexus-presents-lf-lc-hybrid-sports-coupe-concept-vehicle-at-naias-2012/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Lexus Presents LF-LC Hybrid Sports Coupe Concept Vehicle at NAIAS 2012">Lexus Presents LF-LC Hybrid Sports Coupe Concept Vehicle at NAIAS 2012</a></h2>
			<small class="meta">January 10th, 2012 </small>

			<div class="entry">
				<p><a href="http://auto.infoniac.com/2012/01/lexus-presents-lf-lc-hybrid-sports-coupe-concept-vehicle-at-naias-2012/" ><img src="http://auto.infoniac.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/lexus-lf-lc-hybrid.jpg" alt="" title="lexus-lf-lc-hybrid" width="499" height="334" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1212" /></a><br />
Recently Lexus has presented its brand new <b>LF-LC Hybrid sports coupe</b>. The presentation took place at the NAIAS 2012 in Detroit. </p>
<p>The design is very attractive, managing to catch the attention of the crowd at <b>Detroit Auto Show</b>. </p>
<p>The vehicle boasts an <b>aerodynamic body</b> and it&#8217;s probably the most futuristic hybrid car Lexus has ever launched.</p>
<p>It would be interesting to note that with <b>LED details</b> the vehicle&#8217;s headlights were inspired by jet fighter afterburners. In addition, Lexus LF-LC is currently the only car to boast a front to back glass roof.</p>
<p>Inside one will be able to discover <b>two 12.3-inch LCD screens</b> that show information about the speed and current position of the vehicle.</p>
<p>The design of the car allows it to be even more fuel-efficient.</p>
<p>So far there has been no word on when the concept will hit the market.<br />

			<h2 id="post-1176"><a href="http://auto.infoniac.com/2011/10/ford-sees-coconuts-as-alternative-to-petroleum/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Ford Sees Coconuts As Alternative to Petroleum">Ford Sees Coconuts As Alternative to Petroleum</a></h2>
			<small class="meta">October 10th, 2011 </small>

			<div class="entry">
				<p><a href="http://auto.infoniac.com/2011/10/ford-sees-coconuts-as-alternative-to-petroleum/" ><img src="http://auto.infoniac.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/coconut-fibers-form.jpg" alt="" title="coconut-fibers-form" width="350" height="263" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1177" /></a>Throughout the past couple of years, <b>Ford</b> managed to increase the use of various eco-friendly materials in its vehicles. </p>
<p>The famous car-making company started making cushions using soy foam seat, instrument panels using castor oil foam, underbody systems using recycled resins and more.</p>
<p>Recently Ford started experimenting with <b>coconut fibers</b> that are also known as <b>coir</b>. </p>
<p>The new material is expected to make <b>door and seat trim, plastic storage bins, and cup holders</b> mode durable, as well as making its vehicles more recyclable.</p>
<p>It would be interesting to note that coconut fibers can be used to make <b>lightweight products</b>, which means that a car can save more energy. </p>
<p>In addition, these fibers create more pleasant interiors due to the fact that they have a more natural-looking textured finish.</p>
<p>According to <b>Dr. Ellen Lee, technical expert for Plastics Research at Ford</b>, the company is continuously looking for ground-breaking renewable technologies able to cut the amount of petroleum used.</p>

			<h2 id="post-1134"><a href="http://auto.infoniac.com/2011/09/jaguar-c-x16-stylish-hybrid-concept-unveiled-at-frankfurt-auto-show/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Jaguar C-X16 &#8211; Stylish Hybrid Concept Unveiled at Frankfurt Auto Show">Jaguar C-X16 &#8211; Stylish Hybrid Concept Unveiled at Frankfurt Auto Show</a></h2>
			<small class="meta">September 16th, 2011 </small>

			<div class="entry">
				<p><a href="http://auto.infoniac.com/2011/09/jaguar-c-x16-stylish-hybrid-concept-unveiled-at-frankfurt-auto-show/" ><img src="http://auto.infoniac.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/jaguar_c-x16_auto_infoniac.jpg" alt="" title="jaguar_c-x16_auto_infoniac" width="570" height="300" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1135" /></a><br />
The <b>Frankfurt Auto Show</b>, which opened on September 15th and will keep attracting car lovers from around the world until September 25th, is where visitors can observe a myriad of new concept vehicles.</p>
<p>One of them is the wonderful <b>Jaguar C-X16 hybrid concept</b>. </p>
<p>The Jaguar C-X16 is a two-seater equipped with a <b>3.0 liter V6 engine</b> able to generate 300hp and an <b>electric motor that produces 95hp</b>. The latter is incorporated into the eight-speed gearbox of the sleek-looking coupe.</p>
<p>The hybrid vehicle can register acceleration <b>from 0 to 60 mph in just 4.3 seconds</b>, with the op speed being 186mph.</p>
<p>Jaguar C-X16 has its motor generator powered by a <b>1.6 kWh lithium ion unit</b> installed behind the seats. Thus the car-maker was able to obtain a <b>perfect 50/50 weight distribution</b>.<br />
<a href="http://auto.infoniac.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/jaguar_c-x16_auto_infoniac2.jpg" ><img src="http://auto.infoniac.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/jaguar_c-x16_auto_infoniac2.jpg" alt="" title="jaguar_c-x16_auto_infoniac2" width="570" height="413" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1136" /></a><br />
According to Jaguar, during the official tests its eco-friendly vehicle emitted only <b>165 grams per kilometer of carbon dioxide</b>. It was also announced that for short distances the driver will be able to run their car at <b>up to 50 mph in electric-only mode</b>.</p>
<p>Inside one will be able to discover a futuristic interior with multimodal rotary controls displayed on <b>small OLED screens</b> that can reconfigure in order to show the different functions that the driver can perform using the controls.</p>
<p>To maintain the battery pack juiced up, the company equipped its new vehicle with <b>Kinetic Energy Recovery System (KERS)</b> that charges the battery packs when the driver hits the brakes. The final version of the vehicle along with its price tag will be available in at least a year, until then we can enjoy this stylish concept.</p>

			<h2 id="post-1090"><a href="http://auto.infoniac.com/2011/07/solotrek-engineers-to-create-a-multi-passenger-hybrid-air-vehicle/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to SoloTrek Engineers to Create a Multi-Passenger Hybrid Air Vehicle">SoloTrek Engineers to Create a Multi-Passenger Hybrid Air Vehicle</a></h2>
			<small class="meta">July 7th, 2011 </small>

			<div class="entry">
				<p><a href="http://auto.infoniac.com/2011/07/solotrek-engineers-to-create-a-multi-passenger-hybrid-air-vehicle/" ><img src="http://auto.infoniac.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/multi-passenger-hybrid-air-car.jpg" alt="" title="multi-passenger-hybrid-air-car" width="332" height="222" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1091" /></a>A team of engineers headed by aircraft engineers <b>Michael Moshier and Robert Bulaga</b> is looking forward to building a <b>multi-passenger, fuel-electric hybrid air vehicle</b>. </p>
<p>The car will be the second and improved model of Vertical Take-Off-and-Landing (VTOL) single passenger vehicle, which the team named SoloTrek XFV.</p>
<p>The latest development will be based on the same duct-fan technology that the engineers used in SoloTrek. Currently the team is searching for someone to fund their project.</p>
<p>The previous $5-million project was funded by <b>DARPA</b>. The SoloTrek air vehicle was highly appreciated by aircraft enthusiasts. It also received the <b>Invention of the Year</b> award from Popular Science. </p>
<p>The latest project is expected to be very <b>helpful to the police, during recue operations and for medical aids</b>, where it could help people during different natural disasters.</p>
<p>In addition, Moshier says that their hybrid air vehicle <b>could be widely used for everyday commutation purposes in poor countries</b>, where infrastructure is a very low level. People in developed countries could use the vehicle to escape car-stuffed megapolises.</p>
