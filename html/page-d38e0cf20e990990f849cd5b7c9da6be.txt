	    <div class="KonaBody" style="font-size:15px;"> 
			<p><b>Subaru</b> will unveil the <b>XV Crosstek Hybrid</b>, its first ever gasoline-electric production vehicle at the <b>2013 New York Auto Show</b>. It will make its debut alongside a new performance concept car. The new model will feature technology based on <b>Toyota Motor Corp.</b>’s hybrid system.</p>
<p>The engine options of the new hybrid were not revealed but the current XV Crosstrek is powered by a 2.0-L four-cylinder engine which produces 148 hp and it is mated to either a CVT or a five-speed manual transmission. The XV Hybrid retains Subaru’s symmetric all-wheel drive setup. The EPA rating for the CVT-equipped Crosstrek is 25/33/28 mpg city/highway/combined, while the manual transmission Crosstrek gets 23/30/26. Equipped with the hybrid package, the fuel economy could crack 30 mpg in the city.</p>
<p>At the <strong>2011 Tokyo Motor Show</strong>, Subaru unveiled the <strong>Hybrid Tourer Concept</strong> which uses a 1.6-L four-cylinder direct-injection boxer gasoline engine which is connected to an in-house hybrid system. The Tourer concept also utilized regenerative braking and a lithium-ion battery pack. The details of the new performance car concept were not revealed.</p>
