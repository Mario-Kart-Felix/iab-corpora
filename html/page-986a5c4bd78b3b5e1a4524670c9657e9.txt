<div id="app_header">
   <h3>How To Register an Off Highway Vehicle</h3> 
</div>
            
<div id="app_content">
   
      
      
      
         <!-- English content start in DMV Template SingleField -->
         <h4>
              Off-Highway Vehicles 
            </h4>
            <p>&ldquo;Off-highway vehicles&rdquo; are vehicles such as motorcycles, trail bikes, dune buggies, all-terrain vehicles, and snowmobiles that are operated exclusively off public roads and highways. A vehicle operated in this manner must be registered as an off-highway vehicle (<acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym>) unless it is registered for regular highway use and is not otherwise exempt from registration. </p>
<img src="http://www.dmv.ca.gov/imageserver/dmv/images/fastfacts/ffvr19_offroad.gif" alt="Off-Highway Vehicles" width="581" height="83"> 
            <h4>
              Where you may use your <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> 
            </h4>
            <p>
              For areas throughout the state that are designated for <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> use, check the <a href="http://ohv.parks.ca.gov/">California State Parks Off-Highway Motor Vehicle Recreation</a> website or call 916-324-4442. 
            </p>
            <h4>
              Using your <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> on public roads and off-highway. 
            </h4>
            <p>
              Valid on-highway registration allows your vehicle to be operated both on and off the highway; however, vehicles registered only for on-highway use may be subject to a &quot;use fee&quot; if operated in an <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> park. 
            </p>
            <p>
              A vehicle registered as an <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> cannot be operated on public streets with the <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> registration. 
            </p>
            <h4>
              Your <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> license plate 
            </h4>
            <p>
              <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> license plates are green or red self-adhesive stickers with a numbering system unique to <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym>s. 
            </p>
            <h4>
              You will get a green or red sticker 
            </h4>
            <p>
              A green or red sticker is issued according to the following criteria: 
            </p>
            <p>
              <strong>Green stickers</strong> are issued for all 2002 year model and older <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym>s, including those that were previously issued a red sticker, and to 2003 and newer vehicles that comply with the California Air Resource Board emission standards for California. Green stickers allow year round use at all California <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> riding areas. 
            </p>
            <p>
              <strong>Red stickers </strong>are issued only for 2003 and newer year model <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym>s that are not certified to California <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> emission standards and which have a &quot;3&quot; or &quot;C&quot; in the eighth position of the vehicle identification number (<acronym title="Vehicle Identification Number">VIN</acronym>). Red stickers allow seasonal use only in California <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> riding areas. 
            </p>
            <p>
              For sticker placement information, and to see a sample, see the <a href="/pubs/brochures/fast_facts/ffvr16.htm">Off-Highway Vehicle License Plates brochure</a>. 
            </p>
            <p>
              <strong>NOTE:</strong> If your <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> is a motorcycle that you use solely in organized racing or competitive events on a closed course, you may obtain a permit that allows transportation of the vehicle to and from course events. <a href="/vr/fees/off_highway.htm">The permit, for which there is a fee</a>, is a reflectorized, red and white, numbered sticker that must be mounted on the vehicle in a clearly visible location. This permit remains valid until the vehicle is sold or dismantled. 
            </p>
            <h4>
              Documents needed to register an <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> that has never been registered in California 
            </h4>
            <p>
              If you purchase a new or used <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> from a California licensed dealer, the dealer will collect the fees due from you and provide the department with these fees and documents to transfer or register the <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> for you. 
            </p>
            <p>
              If you purchased an <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> that has never been registered in California from anyone other than a California dealer, you will need: 
            </p>
            <ul>
              <li>
                An <a href="/forms/reg/reg343.pdf">Application for Title or Registration (REG 343) (<acronym title="Portable Document Format">PDF</acronym>)</a>. 
              </li>
              <li>
                A vehicle verification by an authorized <acronym title="Department of Motor Vehicles">DMV</acronym> employee or law enforcement officer, vehicle verifier, or authorized auto club employee. 
              </li>
              <li>
                Evidence of ownership, which may be: 
                <ul>
                  <li>
                    The Manufacturer&#x2019;s Certificate of Origin endorsed to the nonlicensed dealer and any bills of sale needed to establish a complete chain of ownership to you.</li>
                    </ul>
                    
                    <p style="text-align:center;">or</p>
                  <ul>
                  <li>
                    An original or certified copy of the factory invoice to the nonlicensed dealer and any bills of sale needed to establish a complete chain of ownership to you.</li>
                    </ul>
                    <p style="text-align:center;">or</p>
                  <ul>
                  <li>
                    Out-of-state titling documents and any bills of sale needed to establish a complete chain of ownership to you. 
                  </li>
                </ul>
              </li>
              <li>
              <a href="/vr/fees/off_highway.htm">Off highway vehicle fees</a> and <a href="/pubs/brochures/fast_facts/ffvr10.htm">use tax</a>, if due. </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
              The Department of Motor Vehicles collects <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> licensing fees and maintains registration records pursuant to <em>Vehicle Code</em> &sect;38010. 
            </p>
            <h4>
              Transferring a California registered <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> to a new owner 
            </h4>
            <p>
              To transfer a vehicle with <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> registration, you will need: 
            </p>
            <ul>
              <li>
                California <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> Certificate of Title, or if lost, an <a href="/forms/reg/reg227.pdf">Application for Duplicate Title (REG 227) (<acronym title="Portable Document Format">PDF</acronym>)</a> properly endorsed for transfer. 
              </li>
              <li>
                <a href="/vr/fees/off_highway.htm">Transfer fee</a>. 
              </li>
              <li>
                <a href="/vr/fees/off_highway.htm">Renewal fee</a> &#x2014;if transfer of <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> registration is between June 1 and June 30 of the year of expiration, the renewal fee is due. 
              </li>
              <li>
                Use Tax. 
              </li>
            </ul>
            <h4>
              Renewing your <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> registration 
            </h4>
            <p>
              <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> registration expires on June 30 of every second year from the date fees originally became due. A computer-generated billing notice is mailed in mid-April and must be returned to <acronym title="Department of Motor Vehicles">DMV</acronym> by mail before June 30. If renewal fees are not received by June 30, late penalties will be assessed. The <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> penalty fee is 50 percent of the renewal fee. When you return the renewal notice and fee to <acronym title="Department of Motor Vehicles">DMV</acronym>, a new registration card and sticker will be issued to you. 
            </p>
            <h4>
              Planned Non-Operational (PNO) status option 
            </h4>
            <p>
              If you do not wish to renew your <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> registration, you must submit a completed <a href="/forms/reg/reg102.pdf">Certificate of Non-Operation/Planned Non-Operation Certification (PNO-REG 102) (PDF)</a> prior to the registration expiration date and pay the required <a href="/vr/fees/miscellaneous.htm">Planned Non-Operation fee</a>. 
            </p>
            <h4>
              Changing a California registered vehicle from on-highway use to an <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> 
            </h4>
            <p>
              To change your vehicle from on-highway to off-highway you will need: 
            </p>
            <ul>
              <li>
                The on&ndash;highway title or <a href="/forms/reg/reg227.pdf">Application for Duplicate Title (REG 227) (<acronym title="Portable Document Format">PDF</acronym>)</a>. 
              </li>
              <li>
                To surrender on-highway license plates and stickers. 
              </li>
              <li>
                <a href="/vr/fees/off_highway.htm">Off Highway Vehicle fee</a>. 
              </li>
            </ul>
            <h4>
              Eligibility for on-highway registration 
            </h4>
            <p>
              To be eligible for on-highway registration, 1978 year model and newer off-highway vehicles with an engine size of 50 cubic centimeters (cc) or larger must have an emission label affixed to the vehicle indicating certification by the manufacturer for on-highway use. 
            </p>
            <p>
              <strong>Exception</strong>: 2005 year model or older motorcycles with an engine size of 50 cc or less are exempt from the emission label requirements. 
            </p>
            <p>
              Emission systems cannot be converted to qualify for on-highway registration. For further information on emission standards, contact the California Air Resources Board at 1-800-242-4450. 
            </p>
            <h4>
              Registration requirements for a specially constructed <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> 
            </h4>
            <p>
              <strong>NOTE</strong>: An <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> built by a licensed manufacturer cannot be registered as a specially constructed vehicle, regardless of after-market modifications or added components. 
            </p>
            <p>
              To register an <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> that was specially constructed, you will need: 
            </p>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <a href="/forms/reg/reg343.pdf">Application for Title or Registration/Verification of Vehicle (REG 343) (PDF)</a>. 
              </li>
              <li>
                A vehicle verification done by the California Highway Patrol (CHP). You must start your application process with the <acronym title="Department of Motor Vehicles">DMV</acronym> prior to contacting the CHP for vehicle verification. 
              </li>
              <li>
                <a href="/forms/reg/reg5036.pdf">Statement of Construction (REG 5036) (PDF)</a>. 
              </li>
              <li>
                Proof of ownership, such as invoices, receipts, manufacturer&#x2019;s certificates of origin, bills of sale, or junk receipts for the major component parts (engine, frame, transmission, and body). 
                <br>
                <br>
                <p>
                  <strong>NOTE:</strong> A motor vehicle bond is required for the total value of the vehicle when proof of ownership cannot be obtained for parts valued at $5,000 or more. 
                </p>
              </li>
              <li>
                <a href="/vr/fees/off_highway.htm">Off Highway Vehicle fees</a> and use tax, if due. 
              </li>
              <li>
                Visit your <a href="/fo/offices/toc_fo.htm">local <acronym title="Department of Motor Vehicles">DMV</acronym> office</a> to begin the application process; make an <a href="/foa/welcome.do?localeName=en" title="Link to Make an Appointment Online">Appointment(s)</a> for faster service. <!--https://eg.dmv.ca.gov/foa/welcome.do?localeName=en -->
              </li>
            </ul>
            
            <h4>
              If you lose your title, registration card, or license plate 
            </h4>
            <p>
              You must apply for a duplicate title or registration card, or substitute plate if the <acronym title="Off Highway Vehicle">OHV</acronym> Certificate of Title, registration card, or plate is lost, stolen, mutilated, or illegible. There is a fee for the duplicate or substitute. 
            </p>
<p style="text-align:right; font-size:smaller;">
              HTVR 19 (REV 12/2006)
