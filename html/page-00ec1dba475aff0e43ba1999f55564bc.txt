				<div style="margin-bottom:5px;text-align:left;"><strong>Terms to Know About Road Side Automotive Service</strong></div> <p> When your automobile breaks down on the road it will be important to know who to call for quick service. Many repair and towing services offer assistance that will come to where you are on the road. Some of these companies offer repairs, jump starts, 24 hours per day aid and more. Here are some terms to know about automotive road side assistance. </p>

<li> Extrication – Extrication is the process of removing a car from around a person stuck inside the automobile that was in an accident. Road side assistance companies often offer extrication services. 

<li> Fluid Delivery – Cars, trucks and their engines require certain fluids to operate such as gasoline and more. In an emergency where you run out of fluids, a road side assistance company will offer fluid delivery. 

<li> GPS – GPS stands for global positioning system which can be helpful if you become lost while on the road. Your road side assistance provider should take your call and give you directions to where you are trying to go.

<li> Jump Start – When a car’s battery dies it may need a jump start to operate again. Jump starting involves using a second battery to distribute power to the vehicle that is having trouble starting. 

<li> Radiator – If the radiator in your car fails, you will have an emergency on your hands. The radiator cools the engine of a car and if it fails the motor will overheat. In this situation the car will not be operational and the driver will require road side assistance.  

<li> Jack – A jack is a tool used to lift a car slightly off the road so a person can change a flat tire. A flat tire is a common reason to call road assistance.
