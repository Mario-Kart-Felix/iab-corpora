 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody"><b><u>Clean Diesel Sales Show Consistent Double-Digit Increases Over Past Two Years<br/></u></b>"The 44 percent increase in U.S. clean diesel sales in September was the largest gain of any month in 2012," said <span class="xn-person" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><span itemprop="name">Allen Schaeffer</span></span>, the Executive Director of the <a href="http://www.dieselforum.org/" target="_blank">Diesel Technology Forum</a>.&#160; "And the overall 25.6 percent increase in 2012 is almost double the overall auto market increase of 13.8 percent.</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">"An important trend in U.S. clean diesel sales is the consistency of the monthly increases.&#160; Diesel sales have increased in 26 of the past 27 months with 23 of these months showing double-digit increases.&#160; Impressively, there have been 20 percent or better increases in 20 of the past 27 months," Schaeffer said.</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody"><b><u>Clean Diesel Autos and Light Duty Trucks in "Early Stages of a Renaissance in America"<br/></u></b>"While there has been some volatility in the U.S. and world auto markets, I think clean diesel cars and light duty trucks are in the early stages of a renaissance in America.&#160; Even though the combined total of diesel cars with light duty trucks sold in the U.S. is around three percent, there are several major reasons the outlook is extremely positive for dramatic sales increase," Schaeffer said.</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">"Today's clean diesels are not the same as those Americans saw in the 1970s and 1980s.&#160; Today's diesels are fast, efficient, and clean and offer consumers long-term green value for their investments.</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">"With higher and fluctuating fuel prices, Americans are seeking more <a onclick='linkOnClick(this)' href="http://www.dieselforum.org/index.cfm?objectid=1CDF6110-DA5D-11E0-8228000C296BA163" target="_blank">fuel efficient cars</a>.&#160; In addition, the new federal fuel efficiency standards that will required a 54.5 mpg average by 2025 will also boost clean diesel auto sales, as <a onclick='linkOnClick(this)' href="http://www.dieselforum.org/index.cfm?objectid=7BCD5900-B1BF-11E0-8DA1000C296BA163" target="_blank">diesel cars</a> are 20 to 40 percent&#160;more fuel efficient than gasoline versions."</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">Schaeffer said the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) forecasts that compared to 2012 the price of diesel fuel will decline by about 3 percent in 2013 to <span class="xn-money">$3.83</span>/gallon.</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">"An important consideration for consumer's comparison car shopping is the differential cost between diesel fuel and gasoline which has also declined," Schaeffer said.&#160; "When comparing diesel to premium gasoline, the cost differential today is just <span class="xn-money">17 cents</span> a gallon.&#160;With diesel fuel and gasoline prices tracking more closely, consumers choosing diesel will see a faster payback for investing in a clean diesel car, given the proven fuel efficiency and higher resale value - two of the key factors of the total cost of ownership."</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">(<a onclick='linkOnClick(this)' href="http://click.icptrack.com/icp/relay.php?r=37498145&amp;msgid=329717&amp;act=PIH1&amp;c=955064&amp;destination=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.dieselforum.org%2Findex.cfm%3Fobjectid%3D1CDF6110-DA5D-11E0-8228000C296BA163%26utm_source%3DDieselDirect02.02.12%26utm_medium%3DEmail%26utm_campaign%3DDieselDirect" target="_blank">See a list of all the Clean Diesel Vehicles Currently Available in the U.S.</a>)</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody"><b>Pike Research Predicts Major Growth in U.S. Diesel Market<br/></b>"There are currently 14 clean diesel autos and SUVs available in the U.S. and the number increases to 31 when you include light duty trucks and vans," Schaeffer said. "However, the number of clean diesels available in the U.S. will almost double in the next 18 months which should result in a significant increase in domestic sales."</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">A <span class="xn-chron">June 2012</span> <a onclick='linkOnClick(this)' href="http://www.pikeresearch.com/newsroom/clean-diesel-vehicles-to-represent-more-than-12-percent-of-global-light-duty-vehicle-sales-by-2018" target="_blank">Pike Research study</a> projects that growth of diesel light duty vehicles will be especially strong in <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">North America</span></span></span>, with annual sales expected to increase from 282,000 vehicles in 2012 to 928,000 by 2018.&#160; <a href="http://www.pikeresearch.com/newsroom/nearly-80-million-clean-diesel-vehicles-will-be-sold-worldwide-from-2012-to-2018" target="_blank">Pike Research also predicts</a> nearly 80 million clean diesel vehicles &#8211; light and medium duty - will be sold worldwide from 2012 to 2018, the study concludes.</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">New clean diesel introductions for the U.S. market that have been publicly announced and will be on display at this week's Los Angeles Auto Show include four new diesels from Audi and the Mazda's U.S.-destined SKYACTIV-D 2.2-liter clean diesel engine.</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody"><b>Clean Diesel Autos Equal More Than 50 Percent of European Market<br/></b>"<span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">Europe</span></span></span> has already experienced this evolution and the U.S. is just beginning a renewal of diesel growth," Schaeffer said.</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">"In Europe, more than half of all new cars are clean diesels. In some countries like <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">Ireland</span></span></span> and <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">France</span></span></span>, it's 60 to 70 percent. Virtually every automaker - from Toyota to GM - has diesel models for sale around the world - in <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">Europe</span></span></span>, <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">India</span></span></span>, <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">China</span></span></span> and <span class="xn-location"  itemprop="contentLocation" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Place"><span itemprop="geo" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/address"><span itemprop="addressLocality">Australia</span></span></span>," Schaeffer said.</p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">According the <span class="xn-chron">October 2012</span> <a onclick='linkOnClick(this)' href="http://us1.campaign-archive2.com/?u=d37167dbef1c508895ccc2723&amp;id=b358bada27&amp;e=1ce559082e" target="_blank">International Council on Clean Transportation European Market</a> report<i>: "</i><i>Diesel cars account for 55% of all new registrations, gasoline cars for 44%; all other technologies&#8212;hybrids, electrics, and natural gas and ethanol-fueled vehicles&#8212;combine to make up the remaining 1%."</i></p>

 					 			   		  	 		  			   																														  <p itemprop="articleBody">Internationally, Pike Research forecasts that sales of clean diesel vehicles will increase from 9.1 million in 2012 to 12.1 million annually by 2018, with clean diesels representing 12.4 percent of all light-duty vehicle sales by the end of that period.</p>
