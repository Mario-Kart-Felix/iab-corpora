</ul></div><div class="twocolumns"><div id="content"><div class="article"><div class="title"><h2>LA Auto Show: Audi&#8217;s A8 luxury sedan gets 36 mpg. Just add diesel.</h2><ul><li><span class="by vcard">By <a href="http://www.extremetech.com/author/bhoward" title="Posts by Bill Howard" rel="author">Bill Howard</a></a> on December 3, 2012 at 11:20 am</span></li><li class="comments"><a href="http://www.extremetech.com/extreme/141882-la-auto-show-audis-a8-luxury-sedan-gets-36-mpg-just-add-diesel#disqus_thread"><span>Comment</span></a></li></ul></div><div class="content"><span id="intelliTXT" name="intellitxt"><img width="640" height="353" src="http://www.extremetech.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/AudiTDI_hero_R2-640x353.jpg" class="attachment-full wp-post-image" alt="Audi TDI diesels LA Auto Show" /><div class="photo"><div class="box2"><h3>Share This article</h3><div class="share"><div class="facebook"><iframe src='http://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.extremetech.com%2Fextreme%2F141882-la-auto-show-audis-a8-luxury-sedan-gets-36-mpg-just-add-diesel&amp;locale=&amp;layout=box_count&amp;action=like&amp;width=50&amp;height=60&amp;colorscheme=light' scrolling='no' frameborder='0' style='border:none; overflow:hidden; width:50px; height:62px;' allowTransparency='true'></iframe></div><div class="twitter"><a href="http://twitter.com/share" class="twitter-share-button" data-url="http://www.extremetech.com/extreme/141882-la-auto-show-audis-a8-luxury-sedan-gets-36-mpg-just-add-diesel" data-count="vertical" data-text="LA Auto Show: Audi's A8 luxury sedan gets 36 mpg. Just add diesel." data-via="extremetech" ></a><script type="text/javascript" src="http://platform.twitter.com/widgets.js"></script></div><div class="stumbleupon"><script src='http://www.stumbleupon.com/hostedbadge.php?s=5&amp;r=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.extremetech.com%2Fextreme%2F141882-la-auto-show-audis-a8-luxury-sedan-gets-36-mpg-just-add-diesel'></script></div><div class="google1"><script type='text/javascript' src='https://apis.google.com/js/plusone.js'></script><g:plusone size='tall' href='http://www.extremetech.com/extreme/141882-la-auto-show-audis-a8-luxury-sedan-gets-36-mpg-just-add-diesel'></g:plusone></div><div class="reddit"><script type="text/javascript" src="http://www.extremetech.com/wp-content/plugins/digg-digg/include/../js/diggdigg-linkedin.js?ver=5.2.9"></script> <script type='text/javascript' src='http://platform.linkedin.com/in.js'></script><div class='dd-linkedin-share'><div data-url='http://www.extremetech.com/extreme/141882-la-auto-show-audis-a8-luxury-sedan-gets-36-mpg-just-add-diesel' data-counter='top'></div></div></div></div></div></div><p>Automakers are getting serious about diesels as one part of the fuel efficiency story. A half-dozen new diesel engine cars were introduced this week at the Los Angeles Auto Show, four of them by Audi: the flagship A8 sedans, as well as the A7, A6, and A5 midsize coupes and sedans. The Audis use a turbocharged 240-hp V6 diesel engine. On the premium A8 large sedan, Audi projects 36 mpg highway, 24 mpg city driving. The diesels get 30% better mileage and produce 30% less carbon dioxide emissions, Audi says. BMW, Mazda and Volkswagen also announced new diesel models in LA.</p><p>The Audi A8 is in the same class as the Mercedes-Benz S-Class and BMW 7-Series. That is: honking big and not cheap. But even the affluent want their neighbors to think they&#8217;re frugal when it comes to burning hydrocarbons. The current gas-burning Audi A8 models list for $73,000-$87,000 with six, eight- and 12-cylinder gasoline engines that get 18 mpg city, 28 mpg highway (21 mpg combined) for six-cylinder, to 13 mpg city, 21 mpg highway (16 mpg combined). No word on diesel pricing yet. So the Audi diesel will provide a big bump up on fuel economy and also driving range; more than 800 miles for the A8 with a 23.8 gallon tank. Even with the comparatively modest 240 hp of the diesel engine, there&#8217;s so much torque, or get-going power, that the A8 hits 60 mph in 6.4 seconds.</p><p>The diesel story is simple and most of it is compelling: Noise, vibration, hard starting, and clouds of soot are far in the review mirror. For highway driving, they&#8217;re unmatched on fuel economy. Automakers have tweaked diesel engines to dial back most emissions, though NOx (nitrous oxide) and soot particles remain an issue in the US (tougher standards than Europe). Audi is drumming up support for diesel through a social media campaign, #FuelForThought. Drivers of previous Audi diesel models would&#8217;ve saved &#8220;240,000 barrels of foreign oil&#8221; since 2009, had Audi only offered gas-engine cars, the company says.</p><div id="attachment_142015" class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 650px"><a href="http://www.extremetech.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/AudiA8_R2.jpg"><img class="size-full wp-image-142015" title="Audi A8 diesel" src="http://www.extremetech.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/AudiA8_R2-640x427.jpg" alt="Audi A8 diesel" width="640" height="427" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Audi A8 diesel</p></div><h3>The impossible dream: Free ticket into HOV lanes for diesels</h3><p>To sell more diesels, the US needs to be more evenhanded about how diesel is treated compared to hybrids, electric vehicles, and plug-in hybrids, says Audi USA president Scott Keogh. &#8220;So here’s Audi’s position &#8211; our challenge &#8211; to policymakers at the state and federal level: let’s level the playing field – or, in this case, the road – and see just how dramatically clean diesel can lower America’s fuel consumption and carbon emissions,&#8221; Keogh said at Audi&#8217;s LA Auto Show announcement. &#8220;Let’s open HOV lanes to diesel, rethink how miles per gallon is calculated, ratchet up diesel research, and make the tax on diesel equal to gas.&#8221; Keogh has some good points. Diesel has more BTUs per gallon but it&#8217;s also taxed more.</p><p>One item on Keogh&#8217;s wish list is a non-starter. Keogh thinks diesel-engine, single-occupant cars deserve a crack at high occupancy vehicle lanes to jumpstart sales. It worked well to help sell hybrids a decade ago; that and alternative-fuel tax credits. Actually, it worked too well, skewing sales toward hybrids, helping propel Toyota past 1 million US hybrid sales &#8212; and also clogging HOV lanes. If the HOV lane laden with Priuses with single occupants wasn&#8217;t moving any faster than the other lanes in 2005, well, that&#8217;s the law of unintended consequences. Many hybrid HOV special rules have been ended or allowed to lapse.</p><div id="attachment_141986" class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 650px"><a href="http://www.extremetech.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/121128_7983_LA.jpg"><img class="size-full wp-image-141986" title="VW Beetle convertible diesel" src="http://www.extremetech.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/121128_7983_LA-640x426.jpg" alt="" width="640" height="426" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">VW Beetle convertible diesel</p></div><h3>Lots more diesel engines at the LA Auto Show</h3><p>In addition to the four newly announced diesels, Audi recently put the same new diesel powerplant in the big Q7 SUV, meaning Audi and Mercedes-Benz have diesels offered in almost half their models. In the big, comfy <a href="http://www.extremetech.com/extreme/133068-mercedes-benz-gl-suv-hands-on-cutting-edge-technology-for-a-price">Mercedes-Benz GL</a> SUV, it&#8217;s also the cheapest model in the GL line. BMW is adding diesels in 2013 in the 3 Series sedan and station wagon, this time a four-cylinder. The BMW 3 Series formerly had a powerful six-cylinder diesel in the sedan. &#8220;It may be &#8216;only&#8217; a four-cylinder, but it&#8217;s going to be a rocket,&#8221; enthused Dan Creed, BMW&#8217;s VP of marketing. The midsize 5 Series also gets a diesel engine for the first time in the US. The BMW X5 currently has a diesel engine and gets a newer diesel when the next model comes out in 2013.</p><div id="attachment_142018" class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 650px"><a href="http://www.extremetech.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/121128_7989_LA_R2.jpg"><img class="size-full wp-image-142018" title="2013 Mazda6 Skyactiv diesel" src="http://www.extremetech.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/121128_7989_LA_R2-640x395.jpg" alt="" width="640" height="395" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">2013 Mazda6 Skyactiv diesel</p></div><p>Mazda announced its new Mazda6 midsize sedan with a Skyactiv (hyper-efficient) diesel to go along with its <a href="http://www.extremetech.com/extreme/141147-why-hybrids-are-dying-gas-engines-are-good-enough-on-mpg-plug-ins-are-sexier">Skyactiv gasoline</a> engines. Mazda is unique because most diesel cars sold in the US come from Europe.
