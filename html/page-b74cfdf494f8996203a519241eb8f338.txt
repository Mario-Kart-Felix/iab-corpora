                                <h1 class="page-title">Hybrids Outsell Most Segments in 2012</h1>
                              <div class="pm-cont-main" id="content">
            <div id="pm-main-pdg" class="pm-cont-pdg">

         <div class="inline-related-content"><div class="inline-related-header content-type">Related Media</div><div class="related-content-image article">  <a href="/north-america/new-civic-hybrid-accords-key-honda-2013" class="imageLink"><img alt="" src="//wardsauto.com/site-files/wardsauto.com/files/imagecache/thumb_img/uploads/2013/01/14-accord-plugin.jpg" title="" height="101" width="180" class="imagecache imagecache-thumb_img imagecache-default imagecache-thumb_img_default" /><span class="thumb-icon"></span></a><a href="/north-america/new-civic-hybrid-accords-key-honda-2013">New Civic, Hybrid Accords Key for Honda in 2013</a></div><div class="related-content-links">  <ul>    <li><a href="/vehicles-amp-technology/advanced-technology-vehicles-most-fuel-efficient-sales-lag">Advanced-Technology Vehicles Most Fuel-Efficient, But Sales Lag</a><span class="content-type"></span></li>    <li><a href="/vehicles-amp-technology/phev-buyers-have-certain-wants-needs-experts-say">PHEV Buyers Have Certain Wants, Needs, Experts Say</a><span class="content-type"></span></li>  </ul></div></div>                </div>
        
        <div class="node-body blog-body"><div></div><p>
	<em>WardsAuto</em> has been tracking sales of hybrids, electric vehicles and plug-in hybrids since the market emerged in 1999, and a study of the 2012 sales data for these alternative-fuel vehicles is particularly worthwhile.</p>
<p>
	U.S. consumers bought 427,605 hybrids last year, up from 261,507 in 2011, according to <em>WardsAuto</em> data. That&rsquo;s a 64% increase.</p>
<p>
	In fact, hybrids as a group outsold all but a handful of segments, including midsize luxury cars, small cross/utility vehicles and each of the three SUV segments.</p>
<p>
	From 1999 to 2009, U.S. market share for alternative-fuel vehicles grew to 2.8%. The segment dipped slightly in 2010 and 2011 but then came roaring back in 2012, finishing the year with 3.3% of the U.S. market. That&rsquo;s nearly a half-million vehicles, the vast majority of which are hybrids.</p>
<p>
	A market once pioneered and dominated by the Japanese now is populated by the Europeans, and not only in the luxury-car segment.</p>
<p>
	<span class='keyword_link'><a href="http://wardsauto.com/taxonomy/term/53669?intlink=incontent" title="News about Volkswagen">Volkswagen</a></span> is launching the new competitively priced Jetta Hybrid in the U.S., and it&rsquo;s arguably a hybrid of a different stripe, with a peppy engine and sophisticated transmission. It&rsquo;s a lot of fun to drive, which you haven&rsquo;t been able to say about all hybrids.</p>
<p>
	New hybrids have recently entered the market from <span class='keyword_link'><a href="http://wardsauto.com/taxonomy/term/53596?intlink=incontent" title="News about General Motors">General Motors</a></span>, <span class='keyword_link'><a href="http://wardsauto.com/taxonomy/term/53589?intlink=incontent" title="News about Ford">Ford</a></span>, <span class='keyword_link'><a href="http://wardsauto.com/taxonomy/term/53606?intlink=incontent" title="News about Honda">Honda</a></span>, BMW, Hyundai and Mercedes, and others are on the way from Acura, Infiniti, Honda, Nissan and Subaru. Even Rolls-Royce has a hybrid. Toyota, the king of the hybrids, is setting out to have hybrid versions of every vehicle in its portfolio.</p>
<p>
	Don&rsquo;t forget battery-electrics and plug-in hybrids. A more affordable version of the <span class='keyword_link'><a href="http://wardsauto.com/taxonomy/term/53626?intlink=incontent" title="News about Nissan">Nissan</a></span> Leaf is on the way, as is a battery version of the Chevy Spark. <span class='keyword_link'><a href="http://wardsauto.com/taxonomy/term/53559?intlink=incontent" title="News about BMW">BMW</a></span> is bringing both a new full-electric vehicle and an extended-range EV, both with lightweight carbon-fiber bodies. And the extended-range Chevy Volt EV will be joined in GM&rsquo;s lineup by the Cadillac ELR, a luxury version of the Volt.</p>
<p>
	Clearly, the market for hybrid-electric vehicles continues to grow.&nbsp;</p>
