                        <p><strong>Overview</strong></p>
 

<p>Nashville Auto-Diesel College (NADC) was established in 1919 as an auto mechanic training school for Nashville area residents. Over the years, the College began to attract students from well outside the local area, and eventually  expanded its scope to include all 50 states and 62 foreign countries. In 1935, the College added the diesel engine to its curriculum, and in 1946, added Collision Repair and Refinishing Technology. Today, NADC is an affiliate of Lincoln Educational Services, which operates a nationwide network of technical training schools offering a wide variety of professional programs.</p>
 
<p><strong>Points of Distinction</strong></p> 
 
<ul> 
<li>Credit given for high school training or work experience</li>
<li>Post-graduation refresher training free of charge</li>
<li>No books to buy: All books furnished by NADC </li> 
<li>Accredited by the Accrediting Commission of Career Schools and Colleges of Technology (ACCSCT)</li>
</ul> 

<p><strong>Areas of Study</strong></p> 

<p>NADC offers Associate of Applied Science Degrees, Associate of Occupational Studies Degrees, and/or Diplomas in  the following program areas of study:<p>

<ul>
<li>Automotive Technician Training</li>
<li>Collision Repair Training</li>
<li>Diesel Training</li>
<li>High Performance Training</li>
</ul> 
  
<p><strong>Admissions</strong></p> 
 
<p>Career Advisors at NADC are happy to walk any prospective applicant step-by-step through the admissions process. They can be contacted online through the school's website.</p>
      
<p><strong>Faculty</strong></p>

<p>Instructors are highly knowledgeable and ASE(Automotive Service Excellence)-certified with years of professional experience. Faculty members provide one-on-one hands-on training, using industry-standard tools and techniques. Some of them author the textbooks that their students use.</p>           
       
<p><strong>Student Body</strong></p> 

<p>The NADC student population is well in excess of 2,000 students. The student body is overwhelmingly male.</p>
  
<p><strong>Student Life</strong></p>
 
<p>Students have access to a full-service all-you-can-eat cafeteria, as well as books and tools that are completely furnished by the College. NADC provides two on-campus dormitories but there is ample off-campus apartment housing available as well. Approximately 30% of all undergraduates live on campus.</p>        </div>        <div class="profile">                        <h3>Programs of Study</h3>
