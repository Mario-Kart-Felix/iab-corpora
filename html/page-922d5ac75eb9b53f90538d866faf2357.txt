          	  <p>Without a major history of civilian car production, China is sorely lacking in both collector cars, and displays thereof. That hasn&#8217;t stopped a few enterprising individuals from doing their best, and Erik van Ingen Schenau at the <a title="China Motor Vehicle Documentation Centre" href="http://www.chinesecars.net/index.php?page=1">China Motor Vehicle Documentation Centre</a> in France has put together a list of all known collections. Erik, if you don&#8217;t know him, is the only person we know of making a serious effort to document China&#8217;s automotive history.</p>
<p>He came up with 13 museums or public collections, some of which are closed, while others are known by name only.</p>
<p>1. Beijing International Automotive Expo Center<br />
Huaxiang, Fengtai District<a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/847_1_1000-Henn-Beijing-rendering-night.jpg"></a></p>
<p><span class="pinterest-button"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.hemmings.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F10%2F06%2Fclassic-car-museums-of-china%2F&media=http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/847_1_1000-Henn-Beijing-rendering-night.jpg&description=Classic+Car+Museums+of+China" class="pin-it"  count-layout="horizontal"></a><a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/847_1_1000-Henn-Beijing-rendering-night.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15201" title="Beijing International Automotive Expo Cente exterior" src="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/847_1_1000-Henn-Beijing-rendering-night.jpg"  alt="Beijing International Automotive Expo Cente exterior" width="450" height="274" \/></a></span></p>
<p>The BIAEC will include the Automuseum, but is currently under construction, expected to open in 2010. It was originally scheduled to be ready for the 2008 Olympics. <a title="Beijing International Automotive Expo" href="http://maps.google.com/maps?f=q&amp;source=s_q&amp;hl=de&amp;geocode=&amp;q=&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;ll=39.827374,116.296045&amp;spn=0.007069,0.016479&amp;t=h&amp;z=17">Google Maps</a> shows the main building looking nearly completed, but a very quiet construction zone, and no sign of the vast park shown in the drawings.</p>
<p><span id="more-15197"></span></p>
<p>The interior is blatantly Guggenheim-derived, although the exterior can best be described as &#8220;Space Slug.&#8221; <a title="Henn Arkitekten" href="http://www.henn.com/">Henn Arkitekten</a> are the designers; exterior renderings show the word &#8220;SPEED&#8221; inscribed on the side in blue letters about 40 feet high, which the Guggenheim can&#8217;t match. We also approve heartily of the figure-eight track shown in their presentation.</p>
<p><span class="pinterest-button"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.hemmings.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F10%2F06%2Fclassic-car-museums-of-china%2F&media=http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/847_3_1000-Henn-Beijing-Internal.jpg&description=Classic+Car+Museums+of+China" class="pin-it"  count-layout="horizontal"></a><a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/847_3_1000-Henn-Beijing-Internal.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15202" title="847_3_1000 Henn Beijing Internal" src="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/847_3_1000-Henn-Beijing-Internal.jpg"  alt="Beijing International Automotive Expo Center interior" width="450" height="274" \/></a></span></p>
<p>2. Beijing Bayi Film Studio Car Collection<br />
Guangnamen Liuliqiao</p>
<p>Located in Beijing&#8217;s Fengtai District, Bayi has done a number of historical pieces. Beyond that, we got nothing. If you speak Chinese, maybe you can turn something up; otherwise, do us a favor, and drop in at No.1a, Liuliqiao Beili, next time you&#8217;re in Beijing.</p>
<p>3. Luo Wenyou Classic Car Museum<br />
Yangsong Huairou, Beijing Municipality</p>
<p><span class="pinterest-button"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.hemmings.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F10%2F06%2Fclassic-car-museums-of-china%2F&media=http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Luo-Wenyou-building_1.jpg&description=Classic+Car+Museums+of+China" class="pin-it"  count-layout="horizontal"></a><a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Luo-Wenyou-building_1.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15211" title="Luo Wenyou building_1" src="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Luo-Wenyou-building_1.jpg"  alt="Luo Wenyou building_1" width="450" height="337" \/></a></span></p>
<p><a title="Luo Wenyou personal website" href="http://www.laoyeche.org/">Luo Wenyou</a> seems to be Beijing&#8217;s classic-car collector of note, and the museum features cars from his collection. It opened to the public in May 2009 with about 160 cars, mostly Chinese. They focus on Hongqi, and one of their star cars is a 10 meter &#8211; 33 foot &#8211; Hongqi limousine. As Erik says, &#8220;Cars are more or less restored;&#8221; we&#8217;d guess Chinese restorers are still getting up to speed. The collection looks nifty, though, definitely worth a visit.</p>
<p><span class="pinterest-button"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.hemmings.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F10%2F06%2Fclassic-car-museums-of-china%2F&media=http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Luo-Wenyou-3_1.jpg&description=Classic+Car+Museums+of+China" class="pin-it"  count-layout="horizontal"></a><a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Luo-Wenyou-3_1.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15210" title="Luo Wenyou polishes one of his cars" src="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Luo-Wenyou-3_1.jpg"  alt="Luo Wenyou polishes one of his cars" width="450" height="247" \/></a></span></p>

<p>4. Shanghai Auto Museum<br />
Anting, Shanghai Municipality</p>
<p><span class="pinterest-button"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.hemmings.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F10%2F06%2Fclassic-car-museums-of-china%2F&media=http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Shanghai-Cadillac_1.jpg&description=Classic+Car+Museums+of+China" class="pin-it"  count-layout="horizontal"></a><a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Shanghai-Cadillac_1.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15227" title="Cadillac at Shanghai International Automobile City" src="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Shanghai-Cadillac_1.jpg"  alt="Cadillac at Shanghai International Automobile City" width="450" height="337" \/></a></span></p>
<p>Over in Shanghai, the provincial (?) government runs a car museum with, oddly, 40 cars from <a title="Blackhawk Collection" href="www.blackhawkcollection.com">Blackhawk Collection</a>, which I assume means you can buy one if you want. Open since 2007, it claims, dubiously, to be the oldest operating public auto museum in China, and is part of <a title="Shanghai Auto City" href="www.shautomuseum.gov.cn" target="_self">Shanghai International Auto City</a>.</p>
<p><span class="pinterest-button"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.hemmings.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F10%2F06%2Fclassic-car-museums-of-china%2F&media=http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Shanghai-3_1.jpg&description=Classic+Car+Museums+of+China" class="pin-it"  count-layout="horizontal"></a><a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Shanghai-3_1.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15230" title="Shanghai International Automobile City building" src="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Shanghai-3_1.jpg"  alt="Shanghai International Automobile City building" width="450" height="185" \/></a></span></p>
<p>5. SAIC Headquarters Car Collection<br />
Shuntiancun, Shangai</p>
<p>Word is that SAIC, Shanghai Automotive Industry Corporation, China&#8217;s largest car company and parent of MG, has a small collection of cars made in Shanghai at SAIC HQ. You might want to call before making the trip (021ï¼22011688), although it&#8217;s only about 40 miles (Shanghai is HUGE) from the Auto Museum.</p>
<p>6. Shanghai Film Studio Car Collection<br />
Songjiang, Shanghai</p>
<p><span class="pinterest-button"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.hemmings.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F10%2F06%2Fclassic-car-museums-of-china%2F&media=http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Shanghai1.jpg&description=Classic+Car+Museums+of+China" class="pin-it"  count-layout="horizontal"></a><a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Shanghai1.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15232" title="Shanghai" src="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Shanghai1.jpg"  alt="Shanghai" width="450" height="337" \/></a></span></p>
<p>As American studios once did, 60-year-old Shanghai Film maintains its own collection of movie cars. I do know they offer studio tours, but not much more than that. They don&#8217;t seem to have a website; this is a <a title="Brian Yang blog" href="http://www.alivenotdead.com/brianyang/Pause--profile-546082.html">production still</a> from <em>Alive Not Dead</em>, presumably using one of SFS&#8217;s cars.</p>
<p>7. Nanjing Auto Museum<br />
Jiangning</p>
<p><span class="pinterest-button"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.hemmings.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F10%2F06%2Fclassic-car-museums-of-china%2F&media=http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/02-car-museum.jpg&description=Classic+Car+Museums+of+China" class="pin-it"  count-layout="horizontal"></a><a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/02-car-museum.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15237" title="Nanjing Auto Museum" src="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/02-car-museum.jpg"  alt="Nanjing Auto Museum" width="450" height="312" \/></a></span></p>
<p>I&#8217;d call the theme of the Nanjing Auto Museum &#8220;Modern Goodwood Parking Garage.&#8221; The designer, <a title="3gatti Architecture" href="http://www.3gatti.com/Francesco-Gatti/index.htm">Francesco Gatti</a>, who won an international design competition, wants you to think origami: &#8220;A building to be dedicated to the automobile, where the automobile is also the vehicle to visit the space. You visit the first external ramp of the museum with your own private car, like a SAFARI, you park your car on the roof and visit by foot the internal ramp going down. The building could seem to appear as an urban car exhibitor, with its corners and angles filled with tempting shining exposed automobiles.&#8221; Scheduled for completion in 2010. Related: Fabrizio Gallanti&#8217;s <em><a title="Auto e Architettura Non Vanno d&rsquo;Accordo" href="http://www.abitare.it/featured/autoarchitettura-come-no/">Auto e Architettura Non Vanno d&rsquo;Accordo</a></em> (Cars and Arcitecture Don&#8217;t Get Along Well)</p>

<p>8. FAW Collection</p>
<p>Changchun</p>
<p><span class="pinterest-button"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.hemmings.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F10%2F06%2Fclassic-car-museums-of-china%2F&media=http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/FAW-3.jpg&description=Classic+Car+Museums+of+China" class="pin-it"  count-layout="horizontal"></a><a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/FAW-3.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15239" title="FAW collection assembly display" src="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/FAW-3.jpg"  alt="FAW collection assembly display" width="450" height="337" \/></a></span></p>
<p>North China is FAW territory, and FAW famously has a warehouse of their old vehicles at their corporate group HQ, which sounds a lot like GM. Unlike GM, they&#8217;ve opened an immense collection of Hongqi prototypes and other cars used for test purposes by FAW to the public. Restored cars are increasingly part of the collection. Unlike most other Chinese automakers, FAW also has <a title="FAW 60th anniversary" href="http://www.faw.com.cn/gq60zt/">a nice historical section on their website</a>, celebrating their 60th anniversary. Expect to find many of these cars &#8211; like this 1956 CA71 &#8211; in the collection.</p>

<p>9. Changchun International Automotive Museum</p>
<p><span class="pinterest-button"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.hemmings.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F10%2F06%2Fclassic-car-museums-of-china%2F&media=http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Changchun-International-Automotive-Museum.jpg&description=Classic+Car+Museums+of+China" class="pin-it"  count-layout="horizontal"></a><a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Changchun-International-Automotive-Museum.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15250" title="Changchun International Automotive Museum" src="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Changchun-International-Automotive-Museum.jpg"  alt="Changchun International Automotive Museum" width="450" height="337" \/></a></span></p>
<p>It&#8217;s not clear if Changchun International Automotive Museum actually has cars, but South Dongfeng Street is also home to the FAW collection above, making this Changchun&#8217;s auto museum row. It officially opened on January 10, 2007, on the site of the late Changchun Movie City Zone, the centerpiece of the Auto-Culture area of the Changchun Automobile Park.</p>
<p>10. Dalian Classic Car Museum<br />

<p><span class="pinterest-button"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.hemmings.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F10%2F06%2Fclassic-car-museums-of-china%2F&media=http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Dalian-Classic-Car-Museum-2.jpg&description=Classic+Car+Museums+of+China" class="pin-it"  count-layout="horizontal"></a><a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Dalian-Classic-Car-Museum-2.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15266" title="Dalian Classic Car Museum front" src="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Dalian-Classic-Car-Museum-2.jpg"  alt="Dalian Classic Car Museum front" width="450" height="337" \/></a></span></p>

<p>11. Sanhe Art Museum<br />
Chengdu</p>
<p><span class="pinterest-button"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.hemmings.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F10%2F06%2Fclassic-car-museums-of-china%2F&media=http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Sanhe-CA-770.jpg&description=Classic+Car+Museums+of+China" class="pin-it"  count-layout="horizontal"></a><a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Sanhe-CA-770.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15277" title="Sanhe CA 770" src="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Sanhe-CA-770.jpg"  alt="Sanhe CA 770" width="450" height="337" \/></a></span></p>
<p>Erik says the Sanhe, established in 2005 in Sanhe Automobile Park, has a wonderful collection of classics and&#8230;wait for it&#8230;restored Hongqis. Sanhe (AKA Chengdu Xing Sanhe Classic Car Rebuilding Co., Ltd. ), part of a large high-end dealer group, are Hongqi restoration specialists, and <a title="Sanhe Group restorations" href="http://www.sanhegroup.com/libs/fwzx.php?sin=shlycfxfw&amp;pagetitle">consider themselves the only professional car restorers in China</a>. I&#8217;m picking them to host the first collector-car auction outside of Hong Kong.</p>

<p>China&#8217;s got Excaliburs! Or a reasonable facsimile thereof.</p>
<p><span class="pinterest-button"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.hemmings.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F10%2F06%2Fclassic-car-museums-of-china%2F&media=http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Sanhe-Citroen.jpg&description=Classic+Car+Museums+of+China" class="pin-it"  count-layout="horizontal"></a><a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Sanhe-Citroen.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15278" title="Sanhe Citroen" src="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Sanhe-Citroen.jpg"  alt="Sanhe Citroen" width="450" height="337" \/></a></span></p>
<p><span class="pinterest-button"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.hemmings.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F10%2F06%2Fclassic-car-museums-of-china%2F&media=http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Sanhe-1.jpg&description=Classic+Car+Museums+of+China" class="pin-it"  count-layout="horizontal"></a><a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Sanhe-1.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15276" title="Sanhe 1" src="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/Sanhe-1.jpg"  alt="Sanhe 1" width="450" height="337" \/></a></span></p>

<p>12. Kunming Auto Museum</p>
<p>The Kunmie, as I like to call it, was visible in 2003, but there hasn&#8217;t been a trace of it in a while. Don&#8217;t promise the kids a trip.</p>
<p>13. Emei Film Studio Car Collection<br />
Chengdu</p>
<p>Another busy studio, Emei celebrated their 50th anniversary in 2008 with their first feature film in a decade &#8211; they produce much more TV than film these days. I&#8217;m only telling you this because I couldn&#8217;t find any information about their cars.</p>
<p>14. Classic Auto-Cycle Museum<br />
Hong Kong</p>
<p><span class="pinterest-button"><a href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fblog.hemmings.com%2Findex.php%2F2009%2F10%2F06%2Fclassic-car-museums-of-china%2F&media=http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/CAM-museum.jpg&description=Classic+Car+Museums+of+China" class="pin-it"  count-layout="horizontal"></a><a href="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/CAM-museum.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15290" title="CAM museum" src="http://images.hemmings.com/wp-content/uploads//2009/09/CAM-museum.jpg"  alt="CAM museum" width="350" height="200" \/></a></span></p>
<p>Erik didn&#8217;t include it, but Hong Kong&#8217;s CAM is hard at work, attempting to convert a former Fanling Magistrates&#8217; Courthouse (shown) into a museum, which would house selections from club members&#8217; collections. Club members? Yep: CAM is a also a car club, whose core started as motorcycle racers, but rapidly expanded into the museum project, with support from numerous other HK car clubs. As you&#8217;d expect, they&#8217;re heavy on the vintage bikes; both cars and bikes lean toward the British, not a surprise in the ex-colony. <a title="Calssic Auto-Cycle Museum" href="http://www.cam-hk.com/index.html">Check out their slick website</a> for more.</p>
