<div class="entry" id="entry-6a00d83451b3c669e2016764438aac970b">
	
		<h1 class="entry-header">2013 Hyundai Elantra Sedan: What's Changed</h1>
	
	<div class="entry-content">
		<div class="entry-body">
			<p><img alt="Hyundai Elantra" border="0" class="asset  asset-image at-xid-6a00d83451b3c669e20168e944809d970c" src="http://blogs.cars.com/.a/6a00d83451b3c669e20168e944809d970c-800wi" title="Hyundai Elantra" /></p>
<p>The <a href="http://blogs.cars.com/kickingtires/2012/02/2013-hyundai-elantra-coupe-at-the-2012-chicago-auto-show.html" target="_self">Hyundai Elantra coupe</a> and <a href="http://blogs.cars.com/kickingtires/2012/02/2013-hyundai-elantra-gt-at-the-2012-chicago-auto-show.html" target="_self">Hyundai Elantra GT</a> are the big news for the 2013 lineup, but the Elantra sedan gets some updates, too.</p>
<p>The 2013 Hyundai Elantra sedan is already on sale with a base MSRP of $16,695, excluding a $775 destination fee. That&#39;s a big jump from the $15,345 starting price of the 2012 Elantra, but for good reason: The base Elantra now comes standard with air conditioning, cruise control, telescoping steering wheel and UV windshield glass, as well as 16-inch wheels (instead of 15-inchers). If you take into account all those added features — which make the base 2013 Elantra the equivalent of the 2012 Elantra GLS with the Comfort Package — the price is $100 more than last year&#39;s Elantra with the same equipment.</p>

		</div>
		
			<a id="more"></a>
				

<p>The 2012 Elantra GLS with a manual transmission and no air conditioning was hard to find, according to Hyundai, which <a href="http://blogs.cars.com/kickingtires/2011/11/hyundai-stops-marketing-accent-elantra-base-models.html" target="_self">removed the trim</a> from its consumer website late last year.</p>
<p>The increase gives the Elantra a higher asking price than the <a href="http://www.cars.com/chevrolet/cruze/2012/" target="_self">2012 Chevrolet Cruze</a>, <a href="http://www.cars.com/honda/civic/2012/" target="_self">Honda Civic</a>, <a href="http://www.cars.com/kia/forte/2012/" target="_self">Kia Forte</a> and <a href="http://www.cars.com/toyota/corolla/2012/" target="_self">Toyota Corolla</a>, though, again, the Elantra is better equipped than most of those entries.</p>
<p>The 2013 Elantra GLS with an automatic transmission costs $17,695, or $100 more than last year, and has no new features. The Preferred Package, a $750 option ($150 more than last year), now includes heated front seats, which were previously unavailable on the GLS trim.</p>
<p>The Limited trim now gets a power driver&#39;s seat with lumbar support standard, and it starts at $20,945. That&#39;s $350 more than the 2012 Elantra Limited. The Limited Technology Package adds a dual zone automatic climate control system with a clean-air ionizer and auto defog system to the option package and costs $2,350 ($200 more than last year).</p>
		
	</div>
