        <div class="title"><div class="bg">Roadside Assistance, AAA Membership)</div></div>                    

        <div class="bulk-text">
		Roadside assistance is 
      a&nbsp;type of insurance policy that&nbsp;helps you if 
      you&nbsp;have&nbsp;car troubles.&nbsp;It&nbsp;offers various 
      services&nbsp;like changing&nbsp;a flat&nbsp;(punctured) tire, bringing 
      you&nbsp;a small amount of gas if you run <!--310512221973-->       out, opening your car&nbsp;if you lost your key or locked yourself out, and
		towing your car if you&nbsp;have an accident or your car won't start. 
		<br />

		<br />
		      You can subscribe to roadside assistance services from independent automobile clubs like
		AAA, or&nbsp;subscribe to it&nbsp;through your car insurance company.  
		Some luxury cars also include emergency roadside assistance as long as
	    the vehicle is within warranty period.    
		</div>
		<br />
		
		<div class="bulk-text-header">
		AAA Membership
		</div>				
		<div class="bulk-text">
		The 
		<a href="http://www.aaa.com/scripts/WebObjects.dll/ZipCode.woa/wa/route">AAA</a>&nbsp;(American Automobile 
      Association)&nbsp;is an independent automobile club for roadside 
      assistance and other travel related services. Their 
      <!--310512221973-->toll free number is (800) AAA-HELP.&nbsp;A membership to AAA 
		is&nbsp;very useful. There are many AAA bureaus&nbsp;throughout the&nbsp;United States. 
		<br /><br />

		In addition to providing the various roadside assistance services as described above, you can 
		also&nbsp;request maps and other travel information from them. If you are a member of AAA, you may get
		discounts at some hotels/motels, rental car companies, museums, etc. 
		<br />
		<br />
		AAA offers two major 
      programs: AAA and AAA Plus. The major difference between&nbsp;the 
      two&nbsp;is that the&nbsp;standard program will&nbsp;tow the car up to 3 
      miles, while AAA Plus&nbsp;allows towing&nbsp;up to 100 miles. AAA charges 
      annual membership fees to <!--310512221973-->their members and 
		covers you as long as you are in the car. It does not matter whether it is your own car,
		a rental car, you are driving a friend's car, or you're simply riding in someone's car. 		
		</div>
		<br />			
		
		<div class="bulk-text-header">

		Caution
		</div>				
		<div class="bulk-text">				
It is better to&nbsp;get 
      an&nbsp;AAA membership than emergency roadside assistance through your 
      auto insurance company. Every time you use insurance company's roadside 
      assistance service, it&nbsp;is considered an insurance claim and will 
      appear in your vehicle driving history. This will affect your insurance 
      premiums with most insurance companies&nbsp;in the future. However, if you 
      go with AAA or another non-insurance organization, they will NOT report 
      it&nbsp;to your&nbsp;vehicle driving history. Moreover, if you use your 
      insurance's roadside assistance service too much, they may even cancel <!--310512221973-->your car insurance. It is&nbsp;definitely best&nbsp;to not have&nbsp;your roadside assistance and car insurance with&nbsp;the same company. </div>

		<br />
		
		<div class="bulk-text-header">
		How It Works
		</div>				
		<div class="bulk-text">
		Regardless of 
      which&nbsp;roadside assistance service you choose, most&nbsp;are 
      simply&nbsp;dispatchers&nbsp;that subcontract with a number of towing 
      companies and garages. Therefore, whether you have AAA membership or your 
      luxury car (Lexus/Acura/BMW/Mercedes, etc.) provides roadside <!--310512221973-->  
		     
		    assistance, chances are very high that the person&nbsp;who helps
		you will&nbsp;be the same. Depending on the circumstances, the problem, and your location, the		
		service may either be very quick or you may have to wait for several
		hours before getting help. Always keep a cell phone handy while driving. Cell phones are&nbsp;your best assistance
		when you are stuck on the road.
		</div>
