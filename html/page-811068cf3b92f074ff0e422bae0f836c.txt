<div class="eventsBar2"><strong>About Eco Auto</strong></div><br/>

<div class="text" align="left"><strong>I'm Ron Gompertz, founder of Eco Auto Inc. </strong>We're based in the university town of Bozeman, Montana. We specialize in all-electric vehicles for fleet and personal use in the states of Montana, Wyoming, Idaho and the Dakotas.</div>

<div class="text" align="left">We've driven just about every electric vehicle available, and identified those that can handle this region's cold winters and hot summers. We believe "green" cars don't need to cost a lot of green, and driving an electric vehicle shouldn't require big sacrifices in ride, comfort and safety.</div>

<div class="text" align="left">Electric cars are a revolution in motion and an evolution in personal transportation. If you're thinking about electrifying your ride, give us a call.</div>

<div class="text">And if you're interested in energy-efficient buildings, check out our<a href="http://www.bozemanhouse.com" target="_blank"> green building website.</a></div><br/>

<div class="text" align="left">On April 23rd, 2007, Montana's Governor Schweitzer signed into law Montana SB 185 creating a new motor vehicle category adopting in Montana called "Medium Speed Electric Vehicles." Small, lightweight, inexpensive neighborhood electric cars equipped with crash safety features are now legal to drive up to 45 MPH in Montana. Since the passing of SB 185, other states including Washington, Minnesota, Oklahoma and Tennessee have joined Montana in adopting similar "Medium Speed" electric car laws.</div></ br></ br>

<div class="eventsBar">"SubiEVee" Electric Car</div>

<div class="text"><br>Our AWD Subaru electric prototype is nearing completion. We began with a new 2008 Forester Sport and removed all the unnecessary combustion engine components - engine, gas tank, exhaust system, radiator. We replaced them with a powerful electric motor, computer equipment and a 320 volt lithium battery pack. <a href="http://www.ecoautoinc.com/subievee.php" target="_blank"><strong>Specifications</strong></a> photos and details.<br></div>

<div class="text" align="left"><img src="images/pluggedin.jpg"></div>

<div class="text" align="left">Plugged-in to either 220 or 110 volts, the SubiEVee charges in 4 to 8 hours.<br /><br />


<div class="eventsBar">Why Electric Vehicles?</div><br />

<div class="text" align="left">Reducing human-caused CO2 emissions has become a global priority, and there is a sense of urgency and purpose building. One of President Obama's priorities is to establish the US as a leader in alternative energy. Montana Governor Schweitzer's "20x10" initiative challenges state agencies to reduce CO2 emissions by 20% by next year. Over 500 US cities have signed onto the <a href="http://www.usmayors.org/climateprotection" target="_blank">Mayor's Climate Protection Agreement</a>. "Energy Independence" has become a national priority and "Green" has gone mainstream. Cumulatively, our individual decisions: the cars we drive, the sort of houses we live in and the lifestyles we lead, do make a big difference.</div>

<div class="text" align="left">Last year, American <a href="http://www.bozemanhouse.com" target="_blank">homes</a> and cars together emitted over 3 billion metric tons of CO2 into the atmosphere. The US ranks as the highest greenhouse gas emitter on the planet, and the Northern Rocky Mountain states are among the highest in the US. Wyoming has the distinction of being <a href="http://eredux.com/states/" target="_blank">the #1 CO2 emitter per capita</a> while North Dakota is #2 and Montana #7. As a nation and a global commmunity we face great challenges, but also unprecedented opportunities.</div>

<div class="text" align="left">While traditional car dealerships had a vested interest in maintaining the status-quo, Eco Auto offered an alternative way of thinking about how we move around each day. Eco Auto was the first and only specialty company offering zero-emission, no-gas electric vehicles in the Northern Rocky Mountain region.</div>
