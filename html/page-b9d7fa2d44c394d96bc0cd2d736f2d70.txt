<div id="content"><h1>Minivan Comparison</h1> <div class="image right" style="width: 427px"><a title="minivans" rel="" href="/image/144502~row-of-minivans.jpg"><img src="http://cf.ltkcdn.net/cars/images/std/144502-425x251-row-of-minivans.jpg" alt="minivans" /></a></div><div id="byline"><div class="author">By <a rel="author" href="http://www.lovetoknow.com/member/86~kate-miller-wilson">Kate Miller-Wilson</a></div></div>

With all the <a href="/minivans/compare-minivan-features" title="Compare Minivan Features">features</a> available, the safety ratings, the brand reliability, and other factors, it can be difficult to compare one model against another. The key is breaking down the options and beaming how each minivan model stacks up. To do this, you'll need to know what makes each minivan special and which aspects of each model give reviewers pause.</p>

<h3>Chrysler Town and Country</h3>
<p>Chrysler's Town and Country model has pioneered some of the popular minivan gadgets, including stowable seating and sliding doors on the driver's side. However, this model has been slow to adapt some of the safety features of other minivans, including back-up cameras and stability control systems. During more than two decades on the market, the Town and Country has been plagued with mechanical problems and it often receives low ratings for reliability. Reviewers praise the zippy performance of its V6 engine.</p>
<script type="text/javascript">w(ltk_ad_custom2);</script>
<h3>Dodge Grand Caravan</h3>
<div class="image right" style="width: 327px"><a href="/image/143647~dodge_caravan.jpg" rel="" title="caravan"><img alt="caravan" src="http://cf.ltkcdn.net/cars/images/std/143647-325x211-dodge_caravan.jpg" /></a></div>
<p>The Dodge Grand Caravan shares a mechanical platform with the Chrysler Town and Country, which means this van is also known for having reliability problems. Typically a bit more affordable than the Town and Country, the Grand Caravan's engine performance also tends to be less impressive. Consumers complain about the van's sluggishness. The Grand Caravan features many of the same options as the Town and Country, including seats that can be configured in several ways, entertainment packages, power sliding doors, and available leather seats.</p>
<h3>Honda Odyssey</h3>
<p>When it comes to available features, reviewers often describe the Odyssey as unremarkable. You can get just about any typical minivan options on this van, but this model isn't known for pioneering new gadgets. On the other hand, Honda Odysseys are famous for their excellent reliability and impressive value retention. Consumers are generally pleased with the performance of the Odyssey's V6 engine, and larger families appreciate the available eight-passenger seating arrangement.</p>
<h3>Kia Sedona</h3>
<p>Older Kia Sedona models were heavy and did not perform well when compared to other minivans. However, this van received a complete redesign in 2006, including a lighter body to enhance performance. Reviewers praise the Sedona's roomy interior, although they note that the third seat may only be comfortable for children. All the standard minivan options are available on the Sedona, including an entertainment package, back-up camera, navigation system, and leather seats. Many consumers find that the Sedona is a good value for the money.</p>
<h3>Mazda MAZDA5</h3>
<div class="image right" style="width: 327px"><a href="/image/71118~2009_Mazda5.jpg" rel="" title="mazda 5"><img alt="mazda 5" src="http://cf.ltkcdn.net/cars/images/std/71118-325x216-2009_Mazda5.jpg" /></a></div>
<p>By far the most affordable minivan on the market, the MAZDA 5 is also the smallest and the most fuel-efficient. The limited seating capacity and cargo room make this van an unpopular choice for larger families. However, among smaller families concerned about the environment and cost of ownership, the MAZDA5 has a loyal following. The MAZDA5 is easy to maneuver, but the only engine offered for this model is a four cylinder. Reviewers report that this smaller engine keeps the MAZDA5 from pulling ahead of the competition in terms of performance.</p>
<h3>Nissan Quest</h3>
<p>Older models of the Nissan Quest are unremarkable, according to reviewers. However, the 2011 redesign placed this minivan in competition with class leaders. This version sports a peppy V6 engine, easy-to-operate controls, a comfortable interior, and a stylish exterior. On the negative side, this minivan doesn't offer the seating flexibility of some of its competitors. The seats don't fold flat, and the seating capacity is limited to seven passengers.</p>
<h3>Toyota Sienna</h3>
<div class="image right" style="width: 327px"><a href="/image/143690~toyota_sienna3.jpg" rel="" title="sienna"><img alt="sienna" src="http://cf.ltkcdn.net/cars/images/std/143690-325x241-toyota_sienna3.jpg" /></a></div>
<p>Despite the quality issues that plagued Toyota during 2009 and 2010, the Sienna has a nearly unblemished record for reliability. This minivan is also very popular with consumers for its impressive safety ratings and available all wheel drive. The Sienna offers all the usual minivan options like navigation systems, folding seats, and power lift gates. Many Sienna models seat up to eight passengers, and you can purchase this van with a zippy V6 or an adequate four-cylinder engine.</p>
<h3>Volkswagen Routan</h3>
<p>The Volkswagen Routan is a cousin of the popular Chrysler Town and Country, but reviewers complain that it lacks some of the features that have made consumers so fond of the American model. The Routan does not offer stowable seating in the second row, which limits drivers' abilities to haul a large amount of cargo. Since the Volkswagen Routan has only been on the market since 2009, reliability ratings for this minivan are limited.</p>
<h2>Three Best-Rated Minivans</h2>
<p>According to <a href="http://usnews.rankingsandreviews.com/cars-trucks/rankings/Minivans/">US News Car Reviews</a>, the following are the three best-rated minivans on the market:</p>
<ol><li>Toyota Sienna - This model gets excellent marks for its safety and very good marks for its performance, exterior style, and interior comfort. It has better than average reliability as well.</li>
<li>Honda Odyssey - This minivan scores even better than the Sienna for safety and performance. It has better than average exterior style and very good interior comfort. Reliability is slightly lower than the Sienna.</li>
<li>Kia Sedona - This inexpensive model scores great marks for safety, good marks for performance, and slightly lower marks for exterior style and interior comfort. Reliability was average.</li></ol>
<h2>Understanding Ratings</h2>
<p>As you compare minivan models, it's important to understand that reviews and ratings are based on several different factors. Choosing the right minivan for you means seeing how models measure up in the areas that are important to you. Some of the things you may want to consider as you compare models include the following:</p>
<ul><li>Price</li>
<li>Available features</li>
<li>Gas mileage</li>
<li>Performance</li>
<li>Reliability</li>
<li>Safety</li>
<li>Size and seating capacity</li>
<li>Handling</li>
<li>Driver satisfaction</li>
<li>Interior comfort</li>
<li>Exterior style</li></ul>
<h2>Resources for Comparing Minivans</h2>
<p>In addition to looking up safety test results on the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration website, you can use these resources to compare models:</p>
<ul><li><a href="http://www.fueleconomy.gov/feg/Find.do?action=sbsSelect">Fueleconomy.gov</a> - This EPA-run site allows you to compare real fuel economy ratings between several different models.</li>
<li><a href="http://www.motortrend.com/new_cars/09/minivan_van/comparisons/">Motor Trend</a> - This car magazine lets you compare several models with a focus on vehicle performance.</li>
<li><a href="http://www.kbb.com/">Kelley Blue Book</a> - This vehicle valuation service can help you find out about the true cost of owning each model over a period of five years.</li></ul>
<h2>A Minivan That Will Serve You Well</h2>
<p>When you compare <a href="/Best_Used_Minivans" title="Best Used Minivans">minivans</a>, it's essential to prioritize the features that are most important to your family. Analyze how you plan to drive this van, how much you can budget for gas, and how many passengers you'll need to carry. Additionally, if you're concerned about costly vehicle repairs, it's a good idea to consider the historical reliability of a particular model. If you do your research and take these considerations into account, you're sure to settle on a minivan that will serve your family well for many years to come.</p><div id="more"><div class="content-bottom"><div class="prev"><a href="/Mini_Cargo_Van"><img src="http://cf.ltkcdn.net/3.1.14.111/images/btn-prev.png" alt="Previous Topic" /></a></div>
