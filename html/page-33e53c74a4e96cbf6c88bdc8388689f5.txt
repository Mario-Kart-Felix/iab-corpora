			<h2><a href="http://www.automotiveinsiderblog.com/reviews/ford-focus-hybrid-review/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link: Ford Focus Hybrid Review">Ford Focus Hybrid Review</a></h2>
<small>April 6th, 2010  | </small>
		
				<p><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-174" title="focus" src="http://www.automotiveinsiderblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/focus-300x162.jpg" alt="" hspace="10" vspace="10" width="300" height="162" />The Ford Focus, with its high gas mileage and quiet operation, is considered by many to be an honorary hybrid. According to the automobile industry rumor mills, the Focus can soon enough shed that “honorary” qualifier – insiders say to expect a hybrid Ford Focus based on the new Focus platform seen at the Detroit Auto Show, but not to expect it anytime soon. The current timeline is projecting a year and a half to three year wait for the car to hit showrooms.</p>
<p>The Ford Focus Hybrid is expected to retain its rubber burning abilities, and predictions abound regarding a hybrid powertrain which will feature Ford’s famed 2.0 liter Ecoboost four cylinder. Reports have this mill driving the front wheels and an electric motor controlling the rear – estimates have the Ford Focus Hybrid topping out at a speed of 155 miles per hour and reaching 60 miles per hour in less than five seconds.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, the Ford Focus is retaining its popularity quite well among auto enthusiasts. The 2010 Ford Focus handles precisely, has a firm and comfortable suspension, and shifts quietly and smoothly. Its slick interiors feel surprisingly luxurious, even with the standard cloth seats; the information center and instrument layout are thoughtfully placed and very useful. In fact, the only gripes about the Focus are minor and somewhat cosmetic in nature; the speedometer inexplicably only marks every twenty miles instead of every ten, the “Shift Up” light is aggravating and never turns off, it is not outfitted with a telescoping steering wheel, and there is no trunk release hatch – the trunk must be opened using a key or the keyless entry. Aside from these minor issues, the 2010 Ford Focus is an excellent value for a small sedan or coupe at its price point.</p>
