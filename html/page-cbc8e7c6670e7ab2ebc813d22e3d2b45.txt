</div></div></div><div class="text parbase section"><h3>You and the School of Business</h3>
<p>You can be confident that our curricula are designed to offer academic quality and professional relevance. Not only are many of our business degree programs accredited by the Accreditation Council for Business Schools &amp; Programs (ACBSP), but they also address needs within U.S. businesses. Our coursework helps develop skills important to job success, such as idea development, analytical thinking, planning and effective communication.</p>
<p>As a student, you’ll experience firsthand how to apply business principles to the working world from <a class="openNewWindow" href="http://www.phoenix.edu/forward/community/2013/02/school-of-business-faculty-well-equipped-for-the-classroom-and-boardroom.html">faculty members</a> who average 20.5 years of working experience. In addition to 460 <a href="/faculty/our_faculty/faculty-profiles-business.html">faculty members</a> who are or have been chief executive officers, there are also 188 chief financial officers, 90 chief operating officers and 1,754 presidents who instruct our students.</p>
<p>We also provide a unique benefit through our partnerships with national associations in manufacturing, retail, hospitality and energy to develop multiple educational options for their corporate members. In fact, many nationally recognized corporations, such as AT&amp;T and Hitachi, have formed corporate partnerships with us to educate their employees. Many of these partners post open jobs in <a href="/career-services.html">Phoenix Career Services portal</a> for our students and alumni to consider.</p>
<p>If you’d like to know more about how to strengthen skills needed for careers, talk with an <a href="/request-information.html">Enrollment Advisor.</a></p>
