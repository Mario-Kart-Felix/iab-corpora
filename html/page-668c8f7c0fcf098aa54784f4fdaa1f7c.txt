<p>Honda announced it’s recalling 807,000 of its family-minded SUVs and minivans because of a defect that could cause the vehicles to inadvertently roll away after being parked. This is the 18th time so far this year that the Japanese automaker has recalled vehicles for safety considerations and/or other defects.</p> 
<p>The recall involves approximately 259,000 Honda Pilots and 318,000 Odysseys from the 2003-04 model years and 230,000 MDXs from 2003-06. At issue is a worn part in the ignition system that can allow the ignition key to be removed without the transmission being shifted into Park. If the parking brake is not set, Honda says the vehicle might roll away and a crash could occur. The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) reportedly began investigating the matter after receiving complaints from 42 owners. Honda reports two such incidents resulted in minor injuries.</p> 
<p>This has not been a particularly good year for the automaker with regard to recalls, especially for a brand that’s well known for reliability. By our count, well over three million Honda and Acura models have been recalled – voluntarily we should add – during 2012. In recent months the company recalled 572,000 Honda Accords for power steering hose issues and 268,000 CR-Vs for faulty power window switches and 820,000 Civic and Pilot models for potential low-beam headlamp failures.</p> 
<p>And yet, with Honda sales up by 23.4 percent and Acura ahead by 27.2 percent through the first 11 months of the year it’s hard to say whether the adverse publicity has had any effect whatsoever on its bottom line. It’s always better for an automaker to be quickly and quietly proactive in this regard than to be dragged kicking and screaming into the public arena by the NHTSA.</p> 
<p>Honda says it will begin notifying affected SUV and minivan owners of the latest recall in early February 2013; owners can also log onto to <a href="http://www.recalls.honda.com/">www.recalls.honda.com </a>and <a href="http://www.recalls.acura.com/">www.recalls.acura.com</a> to see if their vehicles are affected by this or any other corporate recall or phone (800) 999-1009 for Honda vehicles or (800) 382-2238 for Acura models.</p>
