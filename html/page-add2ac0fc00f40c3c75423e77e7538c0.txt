<dt class="wp-caption-dt"><img class="size-full wp-image-5" src="http://online.wsj.com/media/autoshow_G_20100423055700.jpg" alt="" width="553" height="369" /></dt>

<p><em>As the Beijing auto show kicked off Friday, it was a day for electric cars and hybrids. Some of the news:</em></p>
<p><strong>Mitsubishi Motors</strong>, which became the world&#8217;s first auto maker to launch mass-produced electric cars when it rolled out the i-MiEV in Japan last year, plans to introduce it’s the small i-MiEV to China by 2012, said Osamu Masuko, its president. The Japanese firm’s push into China will be part of an overseas roll-out of the car that will include some countries in Europe and Asia. &#8220;We wanted to bring it [to China] in 2011. But we don&#8217;t have enough capacity to produce the batteries,&#8221; Masuko said in an interview. Masuko said Mitsubishi will export the i-MiEV to China from Japan, but may have to consider producing its electric cars in China in the future if the Chinese government says local production is a prerequisite for subsidies for electric cars.</p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p><strong>Honda</strong> plans to launch two of its hybrid vehicles, the Insight and CR-Z, in China in 2012, President Takanobu Ito said. The Japanese auto maker will also launch the Acura brand hybrids in China within the next three years, Ito said. Honda unveiled its CR-Z model in Japan earlier this year, making it the first mass-market hybrid sports car to hit dealerships. Honda is the No. 2 hybrid car maker by volume after Toyota Motor Co., and its Insight hybrid car was the first mass-produced hybrid automobile sold in the U.S.</p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p><strong>Toyota</strong> wants to begin a trial launch of its plug-in hybrid vehicles in China as soon as possible, said Takeshi Uchiyamada, an executive vice president with the Japanese auto maker.</p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p><strong>BYD Co.</strong> plans to launch its all-electric car, the e6, in Shenzhen this year by supplying e6 taxis in the southern Chinese city, said an executive of the Chinese battery and car maker. The executive said e6 taxis may be rolled out in Shenzhen by the end of the first half and sales will be expanded to other cities later. BYD, which is partly owned by Warren Buffett&#8217;s MidAmerican Energy Holdings Co., has put a big focus on developing electric cars. BYD Chairman Wang Chuanfu said last month that the company is on track to launch the e6 in the U.S. during the second half of 2010. BYD has said it plans to sell the e6 model for slightly more than $40,000.</p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p><strong>Daimler</strong> Chief Executive Dieter Zetsche said the German auto maker plans to launch an electric car for the Chinese market together with its local cooperation partner BYD Co. in 2013. &#8220;It will be a Chinese car for the Chinese market,&#8221; Zetsche said. Asked about the size of the new vehicle, Zetsche said it could be &#8220;targeted at the taxi segment.&#8221; Daimler and BYD announced their cooperation in the field of batteries and alternative drivetrains about two months ago.</p>
<p>&#8212;</p>
<p><strong>FAW Group</strong>, China&#8217;s second-largest auto maker by sales which has joint ventures with Toyota and Volkswagen,  said it plans to launch hybrid and pure electric cars on a &#8220;small scale&#8221; by October. The company said it plans to build up a system by 2012 for manufacturing, marketing, and services, including after-sales service, for electric cars, to make its new-energy cars globally competitive.<br />
