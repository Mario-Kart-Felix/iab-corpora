        <h1>Automotive Technology Schools: Getting Your Hands Greasy</h1>
            <p>Interested in attending an automotive technology school? If you love cars and don&rsquo;t mind getting your clothes dirty and your hands greasy, you might consider enrolling in an automotive technology program. There are millions of cars on the road today, and they are constantly in need of inspection, repair, and maintenance. Automotive technology colleges prepare you to perform basic maintenance, like oil changes and tire rotations, in addition to diagnosing and addressing more complex problems.</p>
<div id="ArticleReadMoreLink"><a href="javascript:ReadMoreOnClick()" id="ReadMore">read more</a> </div>
<div id="ArticleReadMoreDiv">
<h2>Getting an Automotive Technology Degree</h2>
<p>Automotive technology training is offered at high schools, vocational schools, and career colleges. The majority of employers prefer applicants who have received formal schooling. The quality of training programs at the high school level vary widely, however, so you may need to complete additional training to qualify for entry-level positions as an automotive technician.</p>
<p>Automotive technology classes that you might be required to take can include the following:</p>
<ul class="fullwidth">
    <li>Intro to automotive technology</li>
    <li>Automotive engine repair</li>
    <li>Automotive electrical systems</li>
    <li>Automotive brake systems</li>
    <li>Automotive electronics</li>
    <li>Engine performance analysis</li>
</ul>
<p>In addition to completing the above automotive technology courses, as part of a formal <a href="/search.aspx?parametric=1&amp;sub=Automotive%20%26%20Motorcycle&amp;lpref=Campus%2BOnline&amp;scat=Automotive%20-%20Technology">automotive technology degree program</a>, you might also have to undergo on-the-job training in the form of an internship. Automotive technology schools generally award certificates or associate&rsquo;s degrees. These programs can take anywhere from six months to two years to complete. There are also automotive engineering technology degrees available at the bachelor&rsquo;s level, which take four years to complete and provide more in-depth technical knowledge.</p>
<p>Many automotive technology schools update their curriculum frequently to keep current with the evolution of automotive technology and equipment advances. To enhance their employees&rsquo; knowledge, it&rsquo;s not uncommon for companies to send their automotive service technicians to training centers so that they can learn how to repair new models and receive specialized training.</p>
<p>If you&rsquo;re planning to look for work as an automotive technician in a large, metropolitan area, you might want to obtain the <a href="http://ase.com/Tests/ASE-Certification-Tests.aspx" target="_blank">National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE)</a> certification, which has become the industry standard. To qualify to sit for the certification exam, you must have at least two years of experience in a particular area of automotive service.</p>
<h2>Employment Outlook for Automotive Technology Program Graduates</h2>
<p>After graduating from an automotive technology college&mdash;depending on the level of degree you have earned, as well as your experience&mdash;it is possible to work as an engine builder, automotive engineer, smog check technician, vehicle designer, performance engine tuner, race team technician, retail service technician, and more. Although the employment of automotive technicians is expected to grow at a slower than average rate, job opportunities for technicians with formal training, strong computer skills, and electronics knowledge should be good, according to the <a href="http://www.bls.gov/oco/ocos181.htm" target="_blank">Bureau of Labor Statistics</a>. Workers with expertise in specific areas, such as hybrid-fuel technology or certain vehicle models, will especially be in high demand.</p>
<p>As an automotive technician, you could end up working in an auto repair and maintenance shop. Businesses that employ automotive technicians include auto parts and tire shops, gas stations, automotive equipment rental companies, and a range of other organizations, which design, test, and repair automobiles. Setting up your own repair shop and working as a self-employed automotive technician is also a possibility. The BLS claims that the median hourly rate for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $16.88 in May 2008, with the highest 10 percent having earned more than $28.71 per hour. If you love working with cars and seek a flexible, hands-on career, explore the educational opportunities offered at automotive technology schools &ndash; you could be cruising your way down a new career path in less time than you think!</p>
