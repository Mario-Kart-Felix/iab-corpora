<div class="stext"><div class="copy">Why would grown men want to ride or drive a three-wheeled vehicle when sports cars and motorcycles have evolved to near engineering perfection? One explanation: Mattel's Big Wheel.</div><div class="more"></div></div><div class="links"><ul><li>2-Wheeled Vehicles</li><li><a href="http://autos.aol.com/motorcycles/" title="Motorcycles">Motorcycles</a></li><li><a href="http://autos.aol.com/forum/board/5a11a536e312429482c3ab1ac3597cbe/" title="Motorcycle Forum">Motorcycle Forum</a></li><li><a href="http://autos.aol.com/gallery/top-electric-scooters/" title="Electric Scooters">Electric Scooters</a></li></ul></div>

<div class="description">
<div class='stext'><div class='copy'>The theory goes that Big Wheels were the source of such fun that some men will forever attempt to recapture the boyhood excitement of careening around driveways on three plastic wheels. </div>
<a class="knot-anchor" href="http://www.blogcdn.com/autos.aol.com/media/import/47bdf533-001b2-02eb8-400cb8e1.jpg" data-media-id="54295" data-photo-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/autos.aol.com/media/import/47bdf533-001b2-02eb8-400cb8e1.jpg">Mattel Big Wheel</a>
</li><li class="knot-slide-data">
<div class="description">
<div class='stext'><div class='copy'>As appealing as the Big Wheel theory may be, it cannot account for what is considered the first three-wheeled gasoline-powered vehicle. Karl Benz of Mercedes-Benz fame built the Patent Motorwagen in 1886. He didn't get his idea from Mattel, but probably from steam engines invented in the 1700s. The German visionary switched to four-wheeled conveyances in the mid 1890s.<br/><br/>Next Slide: Morgan 3-Wheeler</div></div><div class='links'><ul><li>2-Wheeled Vehicles</li><li><a href='http://autos.aol.com/motorcycles' title=''>Motorcycles</a></li><li><a href='http://autos.aol.com/forum/board/5a11a536e312429482c3ab1ac3597cbe' title=''>Motorcycle Forum</a></li><li><a href='http://autos.aol.com/gallery/top-electric-scooters' title=''>Electric Scooters</a></li></ul></div>
</div>
<a class="knot-anchor" href="http://www.blogcdn.com/autos.aol.com/media/import/47bdf5a5-0010a-02eb8-400cb8e1.jpg" data-media-id="54296" data-photo-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/autos.aol.com/media/import/47bdf5a5-0010a-02eb8-400cb8e1.jpg">Benz Patent Motorwagen</a>
</li><li class="knot-slide-data">
<div class="description">
<div class='stext'><div class='copy'>Models from Morgan also predated the Big Wheel by decades. These Brits produced various three-wheeled vehicles from 1913 through 1952. Many featured lightweight ash wood frames, minimalist bodywork and V-twin engines. The wide-set front wheels and low-slung frames helped enhance cornering stability.<br/><br/>Next Slide: BMW Isetta &amp; Reliant Robin</div></div><div class='links'><ul><li>2-Wheeled Vehicles</li><li><a href='http://autos.aol.com/motorcycles' title=''>Motorcycles</a></li><li><a href='http://autos.aol.com/forum/board/5a11a536e312429482c3ab1ac3597cbe' title=''>Motorcycle Forum</a></li><li><a href='http://autos.aol.com/gallery/top-electric-scooters' title=''>Electric Scooters</a></li></ul></div>
</div>
<a class="knot-anchor" href="http://www.blogcdn.com/autos.aol.com/media/import/47bdf5a6-002bb-02eb8-400cb8e1.jpg" data-media-id="54297" data-photo-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/autos.aol.com/media/import/47bdf5a6-002bb-02eb8-400cb8e1.jpg">Morgan 3-Wheeler</a>
</li><li class="knot-slide-data">
<div class="description">
<div class='stext'><div class='copy'>Throughout the 1900s, dozens of manufacturers sold three-wheelers with varying degrees of success. The BMW Isetta was a huge post-World War II success due to its frugal nature. You may also recognize the trusty Reliant Robin from various BBC television shows or the British cinema. A list of hundreds of contraptions with three wheels can be found at <a href="http://www.3wheelers.com/azlist1.html" target="_blank">www.3wheelers.com</a>.<br/><br/>Next Slide: BRP Can Am Spyder</div>
</div><div class='links'><ul><li>2-Wheeled Vehicles</li>
<li><a href="http://autos.aol.com/motorcycles/">Motorcycles</a></li>
<li><a href="http://autos.aol.com/forum/board/5a11a536e312429482c3ab1ac3597cbe/">Motorcycle Forum</a></li>
<li><a href="http://autos.aol.com/gallery/top-electric-scooters/">Electric Scooters</a></li></ul></div>
</div>
<a class="knot-anchor" href="http://www.blogcdn.com/autos.aol.com/media/import/47bdf5a7-002bb-02eb8-400cb8e1.jpg" data-media-id="54298" data-photo-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/autos.aol.com/media/import/47bdf5a7-002bb-02eb8-400cb8e1.jpg">BMW Isetta &amp; Reliant Robin</a>
</li><li class="knot-slide-data">
<div class="description">
<div class="stext"><div class="copy">Fast-forward to the present day, and currently there are a number of fully modern three-wheeled vehicles available. Some bear a resemblance to motorcycles, while others are more car-like. Some just defy description. Each is presented for your motoring pleasure. Or peril.</div><div class="more"></div></div><div class="links"><ul><li>2-Wheeled Vehicles</li><li><a href="http://autos.aol.com/motorcycles/" title="Motorcycles">Motorcycles</a></li><li><a href="http://autos.aol.com/forum/board/5a11a536e312429482c3ab1ac3597cbe/" title="Motorcycle Forum">Motorcycle Forum</a></li><li><a href="http://autos.aol.com/gallery/top-electric-scooters/" title="Electric Scooters">Electric Scooters</a></li></ul></div>
</div>
<a class="knot-anchor" href="http://www.blogcdn.com/autos.aol.com/media/import/47bdf5ab-0023d-02eb8-400cb8e1.jpg" data-media-id="54299" data-photo-src="http://www.blogcdn.com/autos.aol.com/media/import/47bdf5ab-0023d-02eb8-400cb8e1.jpg">BRP Can Am Spyder</a>

You may know BRP as the manufacturer of Ski-Doo snowmobiles, Sea-Doo personal watercraft and Can Am ATV off-road vehicles.</div>
