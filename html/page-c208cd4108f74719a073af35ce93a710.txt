		<h1 class="entry-title">Motorcycle Mechanic Schools in OH</h1>

	    <div class="entry-content">
		    <p>A variety of motorcycle mechanic schools in Ohio exist. Because of the types of schools &amp; training programs, there are some decisions as a student you will need to make before you become a motorcycle technician. It will simply be a matter of looking at what schools offer what courses in order to determine a career path.</p>
<h2>List of Ohio Motorcycle Mechanic Schools</h2>
<p><a href="http://www.ohiotech.edu/powersport" target="_blank"><strong>Ohio Technical College (OTC) &#8211; Powersport Institute &#8211; Motorcycle Training Program</strong><br />
</a>Degrees: PowerSport Technology &#8211; Associate of Applied Science (A.A.S.)<br />
Phone: (800) 322-7000<br />
Location:<br />
1374 E. 51st Street<br />
Cleveland, OH 44103</p>
<h2>Motorcycle Mechanic Specialties</h2>
<p><a href="http://findautomotiveschools.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/Motorcycle-Mechanic-Schools-in-Ohio.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-1488" alt="Motorcycle-Mechanic-Schools-in-Ohio" src="http://findautomotiveschools.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/Motorcycle-Mechanic-Schools-in-Ohio-300x184.jpg" width="300" height="184" /></a>Students will need to choose the specialty at some point within their education. There are many motorcycle mechanic schools in Ohio and not all of them offer the same specialties. As a result, it is important for student to determine what their specialty is going to be prior to enrolling at a motorcycle school.</p>
<p>Core classes are something that everyone will take. Most motorcycle technology programs offer eight core classes. This will focus on suspension systems, brake systems, electrical systems, fuel systems, the fundamentals of electricity, two-stroke engines, four stroke engines, and service.</p>
<p>After the core classes have been completed, students will need to choose an actual specialty to focus on. This specialty will typically dictate what their career will consist of. Those who wish to work on Ducati and other European models will enroll in a European concentration. Those who wish to work on Yamaha, Honda, Suzuki, and other Asian models will enroll in an Asian concentration.</p>
<p>Another common concentration is off-road power. This will discuss the mechanical systems of dirt bikes, ATV vehicles, and personal watercraft. It will teach students about diagnostics and repair procedures.</p>
<p>Another concentration that students will go to, often after they have obtained knowledge and other specialties, is applied service management. This is ideal for students who wish to go into management or to own their own repair shop. Curriculum includes courses on shop management, accounting, business principles, and more.</p>
<p>Every student will need to choose a specialty. Determining a specialty will make it easier to choose the right motorcycle mechanic school in Ohio.</p>
<h2>Certifications Motorcycle Mechanics Can Get</h2>
<p>Many different certifications exist for motorcycle mechanics. Certifications can provide a motorcycle mechanic the edge they need within the workforce. While Ohio does require a basic state certification, many employers will dictate other certifications that must be held in order to apply for position as well.</p>
<p>The following organizations offer certifications and it can be advantageous to look into each of them:</p>
<ul>
<li>ASE</li>
<li>AYES</li>
<li>NATEF</li>
</ul>
<p>Each of these offers certifications and often it is a matter of paying a fee and taking an exam in order to receive the certification.</p>
<p>A variety of manufacturers will also offer certifications. For example, <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Harley-Davidson</strong></span> offers a 900 hour course, as discussed in the <a href="http://cdn.uti.edu/catalogs/mmi_catalog.pdf" target="_blank">MMI</a> (Motorcycle &amp; Marine Mechanics Institute) course catalog. <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Suzuki also offers their FAST training</strong></span>, which stands for factory authorized Suzuki training. <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Kawasaki even offers a K Tech specialist</strong></span> which is a total of 300 hours to provide an entry-level certificate. Not only will students learn about motorcycles with the K-Tech specialist certification but also ATVs, mules, and personal watercraft.</p>
<p>Other certifications include the <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>HonTech certification from Honda</strong></span> where students will attend 300 course hours as well as 90 days at a Honda dealership in order to receive a bronze designation. There is also the <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>BMW Motorrad program</strong></span> which discusses the history of BMW motorcycle models, VIN identification, ABS, and much more.</p>
<h2>Scholarships Available to Mechanics in Ohio</h2>
<p>Many different scholarships are available to mechanics in Ohio. By taking advantage of the different <a href="http://www.utifoundation.net/uti-foundation/scholarships-grants" target="_blank">scholarships and grants</a>, it can be easier to afford the tuition at a motorcycle mechanics institute. Some of the better scholarships to apply for include AutoZone, the UTI Foundation Scholarships and also the <a href="http://www.ridetowork.org/motorcycle-mechanic-scholarship" target="_blank">Ride to Work</a> scholarship offered each year.</p>
<h2>Growth of Mechanic Jobs Overview &amp; Top Employers</h2>
<p>The growth of mechanic jobs throughout the country is increasing. The Bureau of Labor Statistics has estimated a 21 percent growth over the next 10 years. This not only shows job stability but also the ability to find more jobs around the nation – including in Ohio. There will be more opportunities located in the metropolitan areas of Cincinnati, Cleveland, and other heavily populated areas.</p>
<h2>Motorcycle Mechanic Salary in OH</h2>
<p>According to the <a href="http://www.bls.gov/oes/current/oes493052.htm" target="_blank">Bureau of Labor Statistics</a>, the average motorcycle mechanic salary in Ohio is $28,800. It may be possible for a motorcycle technician to earn more based upon their experience, certifications and eagerness to work longer hours. The employer is also likely to have an impact on salary earnings.</p>
<p>Top employers throughout the state include Harley-Davidson, American Honda Motor Company, and HMF Engineering. It is important to look at the different top employers within Ohio as they can often offer more opportunities, the ability to grow within the company, as well as hire compensation packages.</p>
<p>Every motorcycle technician, upon graduating from a motorcycle repair school, should consider their options. While there are many options throughout Ohio to consider, it is important to make some decisions in terms of specialty as well as where they will go to work.</p>
