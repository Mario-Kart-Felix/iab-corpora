<p>The <b>Dodge Caravan</b> is a family <a href="/wiki/Minivan" title="Minivan">minivan</a> manufactured by <a href="/wiki/Chrysler" title="Chrysler">Chrysler Group LLC</a> and sold under its <a href="/wiki/Dodge" title="Dodge">Dodge</a> brand. The Caravan was introduced for the 1984 model year along with its <a href="/wiki/Badge_engineering" title="Badge engineering">nameplate variant</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Voyager" title="Plymouth Voyager">Plymouth Voyager</a> (1984-2001). In 1987, the <b>Dodge Grand Caravan</b> long-wheelbase (LWB) model was introduced and sold alongside the short-wheelbase (SWB) Caravan. The SWB model was discontinued after the 2007 model year; and, since the 2008 model year, the van has been sold only under the <b>Grand Caravan</b> nameplate.</p>
<p>Other nameplate variants of the Caravan include the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Voyager" title="Chrysler Voyager">Chrysler Voyager</a> (1988–present), the upmarket <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Town_%26_Country" title="Chrysler Town &amp; Country">Chrysler Town &amp; Country</a> (1989–present), and the <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Routan" title="Volkswagen Routan">Volkswagen Routan</a> (2008–2012).</p>
<p>Chrysler manufactures and markets its minivans worldwide. Combining the nameplate variants, the Chrysler minivans rank as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_automobile_sales_by_model" title="List of automobile sales by model">13th best-selling automotive nameplate</a> worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-autonews_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autonews-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Currently, the Caravan and its sister vehicles, the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Town_and_Country" title="Chrysler Town and Country" class="mw-redirect">Chrysler Town and Country</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Grand_Voyager" title="Chrysler Grand Voyager" class="mw-redirect">Chrysler Grand Voyager</a> (Sold as a Lancia in mainland Europe), are treated and sold as separate vehicles. However, it is speculated that the three vehicles will be combined in the near future and sold under a single nameplate, depending on region (The USA and the UK as the Town and Country, Canada as the Caravan and mainland Europe as the Lancia Voyager).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chrysler introduced the Dodge and Plymouth minivans in November 1983 for the 1984 model year, using an extended derivative of the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_K_platform" title="Chrysler K platform">Chrysler K platform</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_S_platform" title="Chrysler S platform">Chrysler S platform</a>. Chrysler followed with a long wheelbase (LWB) model, the <b>Grand Caravan</b> in 1987, also based on the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_S_platform" title="Chrysler S platform">Chrysler S platform</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lee_Iacocca" title="Lee Iacocca">Lee Iacocca</a> and Hal Sperlich had conceived their idea for a modern minivan during their earlier tenure at Ford Motor Company. <a href="/wiki/Henry_Ford_II" title="Henry Ford II">Henry Ford II</a> had rejected Iaccoca's and Sperlich's idea (and a prototype) of a minivan in 1974, then rumored to carry the name "Maxivan" (though that was already in use for the extended body version of the full-size Dodge van). Iaccoca followed Sperlich to Chrysler Corporation, and together they created the T115 minivan – a prototype that was to become the Caravan and Voyager, known colloquially as the "Magic-wagons" (a term used in advertising)<sup id="cite_ref-allpar.com_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allpar.com-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Chrysler minivans launched a few months ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Renault_Espace" title="Renault Espace">Renault Espace</a> (the first MPV/minivan in Europe, initially presented to executives as a <a href="/wiki/Talbot" title="Talbot">Talbot</a> in 1979,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> but not launched until 1984), making them the first of their kind – effectively creating the modern <a href="/wiki/Minivan" title="Minivan">minivan</a> segment in the US. The original Matra design for the Renault Espace was created when Matra was owned by Chrysler Corporation, so Chrysler may also be credited with originating the minivan concept in Europe as well.<sup id="cite_ref-allpar.com_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allpar.com-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>Since their introduction in the fall of 1983, the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_minivans" title="Chrysler minivans" class="mw-redirect">Chrysler minivans</a> have outsold other minivans in the United States,<sup id="cite_ref-Chryslerpress_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chryslerpress-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> with over 13 million Chrysler, Dodge and Plymouth minivans<sup id="cite_ref-Chryslerpress_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chryslerpress-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> in over 80 countries<sup id="cite_ref-Chryslerpress_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chryslerpress-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> sold as of October 2008. Only recently has Chrysler ceded the top-selling minivan spot to the <a href="/wiki/Honda_Odyssey_(North_America)" title="Honda Odyssey (North America)">Honda Odyssey</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Chryslerpress_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chryslerpress-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> if Dodge Grand Caravan and Chrysler Town &amp; Country sales are considered separately.</p>
<ul>
<li>Magician <a href="/wiki/Doug_Henning" title="Doug Henning">Doug Henning</a> was used as a spokesman to introduce the Caravan as a vehicle with the space of a cargo van that would fit in a standard garage and a low step-in height for small children.</li>
<li>A first year production Dodge Caravan is displayed at the <a href="/wiki/Henry_Ford_Museum" title="Henry Ford Museum" class="mw-redirect">Henry Ford Museum</a> in Detroit.</li>
<li>The "America on the Move" exhibit at the <a href="/wiki/National_Museum_of_American_History" title="National Museum of American History">National Museum of American History</a> opened in 2003. The display also marked the 20th anniversary of the minivan and the year Chrysler sold its 10 millionth minivan worldwide.</li>
<li>In 2004, Chrysler held a <i>Stand By Your Van Contest</i>, where contestants had to place one hand on the new van. The person whose hand was on the longest won a brand new Caravan and was one of the first consumers to experience the new <i>Stow 'n Go</i> seating. The event was held during the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Auto_Show" title="Chicago Auto Show">Chicago Auto Show</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>Special anniversary editions of the Chrysler minivans have included, in 1994, the "10 Year Anniversary Edition" Dodge SE with badges and special two-tone paint, as well as the 2004 Anniversary Edition SXT Caravan.</p>

<p>Generation I Caravans used the Chrysler S platform, which was closely related to the K-cars, <a href="/wiki/Dodge_Aries" title="Dodge Aries" class="mw-redirect">Dodge Aries</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Reliant" title="Plymouth Reliant">Plymouth Reliant</a>. There were three trim levels on the Generation I Caravan: base, mainstream <i>SE</i>, and upscale <i>LE</i>. The Caravan was on <i><a href="/wiki/Car_and_Driver" title="Car and Driver">Car and Driver</a></i> magazine's <a href="/wiki/Car_and_Driver_Ten_Best" title="Car and Driver Ten Best" class="mw-redirect">Ten Best list</a> for 1985. All trim levels were also available in a slightly longer body, called the <b>Grand Caravan</b>, which allowed significantly more cargo space behind the rear seat. Most of the interior trim, controls, and instrumentation were borrowed from the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_K_platform" title="Chrysler K platform">K-car</a>, leading to a very "car-like" interior <a href="/wiki/Look_and_feel" title="Look and feel">look and feel</a>. Coupled with the lower floor that the front-wheel-drive design allowed, this helped to make the Caravan seem more like a large station wagon than a van, enticing many buyers who would not have otherwise been comfortable driving a van.</p>
<p>The vans came equipped for seven passengers in three rows of seating, putting it between a typical twelve-passenger/four-row full-size van and a six-passenger/two-row large sedan. 2 bucket seats with attached armrests and open floor space between them in the front, a 2 person bench seat in the second row, and a 3 person bench seat in the back row. The two bench seats in the rear were independently removable, and the large 3 person bench could also be installed in the 2nd row location via a second set of attachment points on the van's floor, ordinarily hidden with snap-in plastic covers. This configuration allowed for conventional 5 person seating with a sizable cargo area in the rear. The latching mechanisms for the benches were very intuitive and easy to operate. The act of moving the seats, however, typically required 2 adults, and lifting them back into the van was a very awkward procedure. An 8-passenger model with a bench front seat accommodating a middle front passenger was offered in the SE trim level in 1985 only, and was not repeated due to poor sales.</p>
<p>Safety features were typical of vans of the era, and consisted of 3-point seat belts for the front two passengers, with simple lap belts for the rear 5. Seats on base models and cloth-trimmed SEs had no headrests, which were not mandated due to the van's "light truck" legal status. However, the two front seats were equipped with non-adjustable headrests on the LE model and in conjunction with vinyl upholstery on the SE. Side-impact reinforcements were mandated, and were at all seating positions front and rear. Neither <a href="/wiki/Airbags" title="Airbags" class="mw-redirect">Airbags</a> nor <a href="/wiki/Anti-lock_braking_system" title="Anti-lock braking system">ABS</a> were available.</p>
<p>Access to the rear rows of seating was by a large sliding door on the passenger side. The sliding door allowed easier access in a confined suburban garage, compared to the double doors found on most full-size vans of the era. Because only one sliding door was offered, the smaller 2nd row bench seat was installed off center, shifted to the drivers side of the van, to allow easy passenger access to the 3rd row seat. The cargo area behind the rear seat was very limited on the standard short body model, however, the seat could be adjusted forward in 2 increments, the first of which removed roughly 6 inches (150&#160;mm) of legroom from the back row passengers, and the second of which would push the bench all the way to the back of the 2nd row, making the seats unusable. The seatback of the rear bench could also be folded forward, providing a flat cargo shelf. The smaller 2nd row bench was not adjustable, nor foldable; it could only be removed entirely.</p>
<p>Cargo access to the rear was via a hatchback, similar to the one on the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_K_platform" title="Chrysler K platform">K-Car</a> station wagons. The hatch was hinged at the top and held open by gas struts, and it was the source of much confusion among Caravan owners. It could only be released by the key, or by a button on the driver's control panel; it did not have a traditional door handle. Once the hatch had been "popped", the key could be removed, and the hatch lifted by a handhold above the license plate holder. However, many owners did not identify the handhold area, and instead used the turned key as a handle to lift the hatch, resulting in many broken keys.</p>
<p>A cargo version of the Caravan, called the <b>Mini Ram Van</b>, was also introduced in 1984. It was renamed as the <b>Caravan C/V</b> for 1989, and was discontinued after 1995. It was available either with the short- or long-wheelbase models. Unique to the Caravan C/V was the option of either having the traditional hatch door in the back or the optional "barn doors" (with or without windows), swing-out versions similar to those of more traditional cargo vans. These doors were made of fiberglass and required the C/V vans to be "drop shipped", as these doors were custom installed by another vendor. Another version of the Caravan was the camper. The camper wasn't very popular and dropped within 2 years.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transmissions">Transmissions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transmissions">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transmissions" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Both a three-speed TorqueFlite <a href="/wiki/Automatic_transmission" title="Automatic transmission">automatic transmission</a> and a five-speed manual were available with all <a href="/wiki/Inline-4" title="Inline-4" class="mw-redirect">inline-4</a> engines, including the turbocharged 2.5 L (this was a rare combination). The Plymouth Voyager which was a rebadged version of the Caravan was also available with a manual transmission. The Chrysler Town &amp; Country which was a more luxurious repackaged version of the Caravan had no manual transmission option. <a href="/wiki/V6_engine" title="V6 engine">V-6</a> engines were only offered with the venerable fully hydraulically operated <a href="/wiki/TorqueFlite" title="TorqueFlite">TorqueFlite</a>, until the computer controlled <a href="/wiki/Ultradrive" title="Ultradrive">Ultradrive</a> 4-speed automatic became available in 1989. The <a href="/wiki/Ultradrive" title="Ultradrive">Ultradrive</a> offered much better fuel economy and responsiveness, particularly when paired with the <a href="/wiki/Inline-4" title="Inline-4" class="mw-redirect">inline-4</a> engine. However, it suffered from reliability problems, usually stemming from what is known as "gear hunt" or "shift busyness", resulting in premature wear of the internal clutches. It also requires an uncommon type of automatic transmission fluid and is not clearly labeled as such, leading many owners to use the more common <a href="/wiki/Dexron" title="Dexron" class="mw-redirect">Dexron II</a> rather than the specified "Mopar ATF+3", resulting in transmission damage and eventual failure.</p>
<p>Subsequent years would see many design changes to the <a href="/wiki/Ultradrive" title="Ultradrive">Ultradrive</a> to improve reliability, and many early model transmissions would eventually be retrofitted or replaced with the updated versions by dealers, under warranty. These efforts were mostly successful, and most 1st generation Caravans eventually got an updated transmission. This car features on <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_in_the_Middle" title="Malcolm in the Middle">Malcolm In The Middle</a> as the family car, and remained through the series until the very end.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Engines">Engines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Engines">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Engines" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For the first 3 years of production, only 2 engines were offered in the Caravan, both were <a href="/wiki/Inline-4" title="Inline-4" class="mw-redirect">inline-4</a> engines with 2 barrel <a href="/wiki/Carburetors" title="Carburetors" class="mw-redirect">carburetors</a>. The base <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_K_engine#2.2" title="Chrysler K engine" class="mw-redirect">2.2L</a> was borrowed from the Chrysler <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_K_Platform" title="Chrysler K Platform" class="mw-redirect">K-cars</a>, and produced 96&#160;hp (72&#160;kW) horsepower. The higher performance fuel-injected version of the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_K_engine#2.2" title="Chrysler K engine" class="mw-redirect">2.2L</a> engine later offered in the Chrysler <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_K_Platform" title="Chrysler K Platform" class="mw-redirect">K-cars</a> was never offered in the Caravan, and the 2-bbl version would remain the base powerplant until mid-1987. Alongside the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_K_engine#2.2" title="Chrysler K engine" class="mw-redirect">2.2L</a>, an optional Mitsubishi <a href="/wiki/Astron_2.6" title="Astron 2.6" class="mw-redirect">2.6L</a> engine was available producing 104&#160;hp (78&#160;kW) horsepower, which did a better job motivating the van.</p>
<p>At launch, the Caravan's low <a href="/wiki/Power-to-weight_ratio" title="Power-to-weight ratio">horsepower to weight ratio</a> had not been much of a concern. Its main competitors were the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Van" title="Toyota Van" class="mw-redirect">Toyota Van</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Type_2_(T3)" title="Volkswagen Type 2 (T3)">Volkswagen Vanagon</a>, both of which had similarly sluggish performance. However, by 1986, new competition from Ford's <a href="/wiki/Ford_Aerostar" title="Ford Aerostar">Aerostar</a> and Chevrolet's <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet_Astro" title="Chevrolet Astro">Astro</a> had highlighted how underpowered the Caravan was. Therefore in mid-1987, the base <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_K_engine#2.2" title="Chrysler K engine" class="mw-redirect">2.2L</a> <a href="/wiki/Straight-4" title="Straight-4" class="mw-redirect">I4</a> was replaced with a <a href="/wiki/Fuel-injected" title="Fuel-injected" class="mw-redirect">fuel-injected</a> <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_K_engine#2.5" title="Chrysler K engine" class="mw-redirect">2.5L</a> <a href="/wiki/Straight-4" title="Straight-4" class="mw-redirect">I4</a>, which produced a respectable 100&#160;hp (75&#160;kW), while the <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Astron_engine" title="Mitsubishi Astron engine">Mitsubishi <i>G54B</i></a> <a href="/wiki/Straight-4" title="Straight-4" class="mw-redirect">I4</a> was replaced with the new <a href="/wiki/Fuel-injected" title="Fuel-injected" class="mw-redirect">fuel-injected</a> <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_6G7x_engine#6G72" title="Mitsubishi 6G7x engine" class="mw-redirect">3.0L Mitsubishi</a> <a href="/wiki/V6_engine" title="V6 engine">V-6</a> producing 136&#160;hp (101&#160;kW), which was a popular upgrade. This gave the base Caravan noteworthy performance among its (mostly heavier, rear-wheel drive) peers.</p>

<p>Shortly thereafter in 1989, an even more powerful engine became an option, with a <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_K_engine#2.5_Turbo" title="Chrysler K engine" class="mw-redirect">turbocharged version</a> of the base 2.5L producing 150&#160;hp (112&#160;kW). This engine would not prove popular in the face of new V-6 engine options, though. Revisions to the Mitsubishi V-6 upped its output to 142&#160;hp (106&#160;kW) that same year, and in 1990 a new 150&#160;hp (110&#160;kW) <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_3.3_engine#3.3" title="Chrysler 3.3 engine" class="mw-redirect">3.3L V-6</a> was added to the option list. Buyers flocked to the V-6 engines in droves, sales of the 2.5 turbo dwindled to nothing, and it was quietly dropped at the end of the year. In these years, a sportier and more luxurious ES model debuted (short wheelbase only) to highlight the new engines, the turbo 2.5 in particular. The ES continued throughout 2003, before it was finally discontinued and replaced with the SXT.</p>

<p>From 1990 through 1994, the Caravan used the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_S_platform" title="Chrysler S platform">Chrysler AS platform</a>; they were the last minivans derived from the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_K_platform" title="Chrysler K platform">Chrysler K platform</a>. The increasing popularity of the short wheelbase Caravan had a dramatic effect on the captive import from Mitsubishi, called the <a href="/wiki/Dodge_Colt_Vista" title="Dodge Colt Vista" class="mw-redirect">Dodge Colt Vista</a>, and was no longer imported to North America starting in 1991.</p>
<p>This generation of vans brought additional innovations, such as:</p>
<ul>
<li>"Quad Command" bucket seating (1990)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dodge_Caravan#Seating_innovation" title="Dodge Caravan">Integrated child safety seats</a> (1991), improved design with recliners (1993)</li>
<li>Available <a href="/wiki/Anti-lock_brakes" title="Anti-lock brakes" class="mw-redirect">anti-lock brakes</a> (1990)</li>
<li>First driver's side <a href="/wiki/Airbag" title="Airbag">airbag</a> in a minivan (1991) and first dual front airbags (1993)</li>
<li>First minivan to meet 1998 U.S. federal safety standards (1993)</li>
<li>All-wheel drive was introduced for the minivan. (1990)</li>
</ul>
<p>Changes for this generation included revised trim for 1992, including roof-racks and doorhandles, among other components (prior to this, said components were largely like those found on the previous generation). 1993 marked the final year for optional woodgrain and wire wheels on higher level models. 1994 saw a redesigned interior, with slightly different seat contours/fabrics, along with a new dash, in order to accommodate a passenger-side air bag. 1994 also saw exterior trim changes, such as a body colored grill and moldings on certain models, as well as the addition of a one year only "10th Anniversary Edition" model. Available as an option on a mid-level Caravan and Grand Caravan (also offered on Voyager models as well), the popular "10th Anniversary" package featured 2-tone paint schemes, which included a contrasting light gray colored lower break, along with a gold fender badge. Only select colors were available on this model.</p>

<p>With Generation III, the Chrysler minivans were available in long- and short– wheelbase models; three- and four-door configurations; and eight different powertrains, including electric and compressed natural gas; on a single, flexible platform.</p>
<p>In development for nearly 5 years from early 1990 to December 26, 1994 (final design was approved on September 23, 1991 and frozen in May 1992),<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> the 1996 model was introduced on January 4, 1995 at the 1995 <a href="/wiki/North_American_International_Auto_Show" title="North American International Auto Show">North American International Auto Show</a> <b>(NAIAS)</b> using the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_S_platform" title="Chrysler S platform">Chrysler NS platform</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> It included a number of innovations, including a driver's side sliding door (optional initially, to become standard equipment later), a first for Chrysler and a non-compact minivan for the United States &amp; Canada (the <a href="/wiki/Honda_Odyssey_(North_America)" title="Honda Odyssey (North America)">Honda Odyssey</a> had introduced the first four-door non-<a href="/wiki/Compact_MPV" title="Compact MPV">compact</a> minivan for the United States &amp; Canada in 1994, although the first-generation Odyssey had rear-hinged doors). With Generation III, Chrysler introduced a seat management system marketed as <a href="/wiki/Dodge_Caravan#Seating_innovation" title="Dodge Caravan"><i>Easy Out Roller Seats</i></a>. A conventional door handle and lock was added to the rear hatch, eliminating the confusing pop-and-lift maneuver which had been required on earlier models.</p>
<p>Base models of the Caravan were offered in most states with either a 2.4 L four-cylinder or the 3.0 L <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_6G7_engine#6G72" title="Mitsubishi 6G7 engine">Mitsubishi</a> V6, except in several northeastern states, where the Mitsubishi <a href="/wiki/V6_engine" title="V6 engine">V6</a> didn't meet emissions standards. In those locales, the 3.3 L <a href="/wiki/Internal_combustion_engine" title="Internal combustion engine">engine</a> was offered as the V6 option from 1997 through 2000. The <a href="/wiki/Manual_transmission" title="Manual transmission">manual transmission</a>, never popular, was dropped; only some early 1996 models and European Chrysler Voyager were the only ones equipped with manuals. The 2.4 L four-cylinder engine produced more power than the Mitsubishi 3.0 had in the first two generations.</p>
<p>The 1996 Caravan, along with the <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Voyager" title="Plymouth Voyager">Plymouth Voyager</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Town_%26_Country" title="Chrysler Town &amp; Country">Chrysler Town &amp; Country</a> won the North American Car of the Year award. The Caravan itself won <i><a href="/wiki/Motor_Trend" title="Motor Trend">Motor Trend</a></i> magazine's <a href="/wiki/Car_of_the_Year" title="Car of the Year">Car of the Year</a> 1996 and appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Car_and_Driver" title="Car and Driver">Car and Driver</a></i> magazine's <a href="/wiki/Car_and_Driver_Ten_Best" title="Car and Driver Ten Best" class="mw-redirect">Ten Best list</a> for 1996 and 1997. 1999 also saw the addition of a one year only 15th anniversary "Platinum Edition", to mark Caravan's 15th year of production. This package was offered on various trim levels, and included Platinum Metallic paint, and fender badges. The 2000 model year offered packages which included the "2000+" and "Millennium" package, however these were little more than unique fender badges on vans with popular equipment.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1998, the 1999 Dodge Grand Caravan got new front styling on all trims above SE, and the Sport and ES models received even more sportier styling. The ES model was the first minivan to receive the "AutoStick" transmission and 17&#160;inch wheels. A <a href="/wiki/Cargo_net" title="Cargo net">cargo net</a> between the driver and front passenger seats was added. Some other changes during this generation included new colors, and interior fabric.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Color keyed door and lift-gate handles were standard across the whole range, in addition to a new keyless entry remote. Base and SE models had options for a spoiler as well as color keyed bumpers and trim (grey or color molded bumpers and trim were standard). The 3–Door with 1 sliding door is discontinued and it's replaced with 2 sliding doors as a 4–Door. Chrysler had updates of the <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Voyager" title="Plymouth Voyager">Plymouth Voyager</a> in 1996 for the 1997 model year and the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Town_%26_Country" title="Chrysler Town &amp; Country">Chrysler Town &amp; Country</a> in 1997 for the 1998 model year, prior to the 1998–2007 <a href="/wiki/DaimlerChrysler" title="DaimlerChrysler" class="mw-redirect">DaimlerChrysler</a> era; it was the only exterior update of the NS Dodge Caravan.</p>

<p>Other plans for this model year included three minivan concepts all to be made in the Windsor Assembly, the Dodge Caravan R/T, Voyager XG, and the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Pacifica_(1999_concept_vehicle)" title="Chrysler Pacifica (1999 concept vehicle)">Chrysler Pacifica</a>. The Caravan R/T was to include the most powerful engine ever for a minivan, rated at 325 horsepower (242&#160;kW). It had two <a href="/wiki/Dodge_Viper" title="Dodge Viper" class="mw-redirect">Dodge Viper</a> hoodscoops, a brushed aluminum instrument panel, racing-style pedals, and black and white rubber flooring.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. The skylight feature was used by <a href="/wiki/Nissan" title="Nissan" class="mw-redirect">Nissan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Quest" title="Nissan Quest">Quest</a>. The Pacifica actually did come to be in 2004, based on the fifth generation Caravan, except that it became a <a href="/wiki/Crossover_SUV" title="Crossover SUV" class="mw-redirect">crossover SUV</a> rather than a minivan.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dodge_Caravan_EPIC"><span id="Dodge_Caravan_EPIC">Dodge Caravan EPIC</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Dodge Caravan EPIC">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Dodge Caravan EPIC" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Dodge_EPIC" title="Dodge EPIC">Dodge EPIC</a></div>
<p>In 1999, Dodge introduced the Caravan EPIC, a fully electric minivan. The EPIC was powered by 28 12-volt NiMH batteries and was capable of traveling up to 80 miles (130&#160;km) on a single charge. The EPIC was sold as a fleet-only lease vehicle. Production of the EPIC was discontinued in 2001. Only a few hundred of these vehicles were produced and sold. After the leases expired they were returned and crushed. Approximately 10 vans remain in private hands today.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Crash_test_results">Crash test results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Crash test results">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Crash test results" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 1996–2000 Dodge Grand Caravan received a "Marginal" rating in the <a href="/wiki/Insurance_Institute_for_Highway_Safety" title="Insurance Institute for Highway Safety">Insurance Institute for Highway Safety</a>'s 40&#160;mph offset test. The structural performance and restraints were graded "Acceptable", but the foot injuries were very high.</p>
<p>In the NHTSA crash tests, it received 4 stars for the driver and front passenger in the frontal-impact. In the side-impact test, it received 5 stars for the driver, and 3 stars for the rear occupant, and resulted in a fuel leak that could cause a fire hazard.</p>

<p>Unveiled at the 2000 <a href="/wiki/North_American_International_Auto_Show" title="North American International Auto Show">North American International Auto Show</a> (NAIAS) on Monday, January 10, 2000,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> the redesigned 2001 Dodge Caravan and 2001 Chrysler Town&amp;Country were released for sale in August 2000. The release was part of a promotional tie-in with <a href="/wiki/Nabisco" title="Nabisco">Nabisco</a>, which unveiled their new "<a href="/wiki/Oreo" title="Oreo">Mini Oreos</a>" inside the van during the unveiling.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> The first vans rolled off the line at the <a href="/wiki/Windsor_Assembly" title="Windsor Assembly">Windsor Assembly</a> Plant on July 24. The Caravan comes in trim levels: <i>SE</i>, <i>Sport</i>, <i>SXT</i>, <i>AWD Sports</i>, "base" model, <i>AWD Choice</i>, <i>eL</i>, <i>C/V</i>, <i>ES</i>, <i>EX</i>, <i>AWD Wagon</i>, and <i>SXT All-Wheel-Drive</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In development from February 1996 to December 1999, the Generation IV minivans were based on the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_S_platform" title="Chrysler S platform">Chrysler RS platform</a> and featured a larger body frame with modified headlights and taillights. In addition to other detailed changes, power sliding doors and a power hatch became available as options. The <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Motors" title="Mitsubishi Motors">Mitsubishi</a> 3.0 L V6, which no longer met emissions standards in California and the northeastern U.S., was discontinued, and a more powerful 3.8L engine, based on the 3.3L, became available. All Wheel Drive continued to be offered on high end models. Other innovative available features included remote operated sliding doors and rear hatch, which could be opened and closed as the push of a button, either inside the vehicle, or with the keyless entry fob. Design work was done by Brandon Faurote from January 1997 and reached production approval in 1998.</p>
<p>In the 2002 model year, <a href="/wiki/DaimlerChrysler" title="DaimlerChrysler" class="mw-redirect">DaimlerChrysler</a> stopped using the "DODGE" badges on the front doors, like with all Dodge vehicles.</p>
<p>In 2003, the Caravan C/V and Grand Caravan C/V returned after having been discontinued in 1995. The C/V featured the option of deleted side windows (replaced by composite panels), optional rear seats, a cargo floor made of plastic material similar to pickup truck bedliners, rubber flooring in lieu of carpeting and normal hatch at the rear. Minor changes were made to the Grand Caravan ES including many of the features included in Option Group 29S becoming standard, the 17&#160;inch Titan Chrome wheels no longer being an option replaced with standard 16&#160;inch chrome wheels, and the disappearance of the AutoStick Transmission option. This year also saw the appearance of an optional factory-installed rear seat DVD system with single disc player mounted below the HVAC controls.</p>
<p>2004 offered an exclusive one year only "Anniversary Edition" package to mark Caravan's 20th year in production. This package was offered on higher level SXT models, and included chrome wheels, body color moldings, special interior accents and a unique fender badge.</p>
<p>2005 changes to the Caravan included a revised grille, new foglight fascia, and a system of in-floor folding second and third row seats, marketed as <a href="/wiki/Dodge_Caravan#Stow_.E2.80.99N_Go_seating" title="Dodge Caravan"><i>Stow 'n Go seating</i></a>.</p>
<p>2009 Production of the IV generation continues in China <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://www.dodge.cn/caravan/index.html">http://www.dodge.cn/caravan/index.html</a></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="IIHS_results">IIHS results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: IIHS results">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: IIHS results" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 2001 model of this version earned a "Poor" rating in the <a href="/wiki/Insurance_Institute_for_Highway_Safety" title="Insurance Institute for Highway Safety">Insurance Institute for Highway Safety</a> 's 40&#160;mph offset test. It did protect its occupants reasonably well, and the dummy movement was well controlled, however, a fuel leak occurred. Chrysler corrected this problem starting with the 2002 models, moving it up to an "Acceptable" rating.</p>
<p>The 2006 model year brought optional side curtain airbags and a stronger B-pillar, which was tested by the <a href="/wiki/Insurance_Institute_for_Highway_Safety" title="Insurance Institute for Highway Safety">Insurance Institute for Highway Safety</a>'s side impact crash test. With the side airbags, it got an "Acceptable" rating. For the driver, there is a chance of serious neck injuries, rib fractures and/or internal organ injuries. The rear passengers, however, could leave this accident unharmed, as there is a low risk of significant injury in a crash of this severity for them.</p>

<p>Beginning with Generation V in model year 2008, Chrysler only made the long wheelbase Grand Caravan. This van debuted at the 2007 <a href="/wiki/North_American_International_Auto_Show" title="North American International Auto Show">North American International Auto Show</a> and had exterior styling by <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Gilles" title="Ralph Gilles">Ralph Gilles</a>.</p>
<p>A new six-speed <a href="/wiki/Automatic_transmission" title="Automatic transmission">automatic transmission</a> became standard with the 3.8 L <a href="/wiki/V6_engine" title="V6 engine">V6</a> and the new 4.0 L V6. The four-speed <a href="/wiki/Automatic_transmission" title="Automatic transmission">automatic transmission</a> is standard with the 3.3 L Flex-Fuel V6.</p>
<p>Chrysler introduced a seat management system marketed as <a href="/wiki/Dodge_Caravan#Swivel_.27n_Go_seating" title="Dodge Caravan"><i>Swivel'n Go</i></a> seating, the MyGIG entertainment system (a stereo with built in hard drive for recording, storing, and playing music), second and third row video screens, powered second row windows, standard side curtain airbags, and dashboard-mounted transmission controls. The gear shift lever moved to the instrument panel, the location used by competitors.</p>
<p>With discontinuation of the short-wheelbase Caravan, Dodge offered the <a href="/wiki/Dodge_Journey" title="Dodge Journey">Journey</a> on nearly an identical wheelbase and as a <a href="/wiki/Crossover_(automobile)" title="Crossover (automobile)">crossover</a> rather than a <a href="/wiki/Minivan" title="Minivan">minivan</a>. Although the SWB model, which had accounted for half of all sales in Canada,<sup id="cite_ref-CAdriver_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CAdriver-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> cost approximately $2,000 less and offered a four-cylinder engine option with improved fuel economy, Chrysler executives stated the SWB Caravan was discontinued to accommodate new features offered in the Grand Caravan, consistent with the demands of the majority of the minivan market.<sup id="cite_ref-usatoday_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The market shifted briefly away from minivans and SUVs with the gasoline price spikes of the earlier part of 2008. This trend began to reverse itself towards the fall of 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-usatoday_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2009 and 2010 the Dodge Grand Caravan continued to be the top selling minivan in Canada, with over 60% of the market's monthly sales. Fiat has stated that the Dodge Grand Caravan, assembled in Windsor, Ont., will remain in the company’s product line until at least 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>A noticeable cosmetic update in the 2009 model year saw the "DODGE" badge moved to the left side of trunklid (in the spot where "GRAND CARAVAN" was previously), while "GRAND CARAVAN" had been moved to the right. The previously unavailable <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Stability_Control" title="Electronic Stability Control" class="mw-redirect">Electronic Stability Control</a> was made standard on this generation.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>The Grand Caravan was facelifted starting the 2011 model year, which included major changes in both styling and functionality. The suspension was heavily re-tuned, with both Dodge and Chrysler minivans gaining a larger front sway bar and new rear sway bar, increased rear roll center height, adjusted spring rates, a new steering gear, a revised front static camber setting, and lowered ride height.</p>
<p>All three of the former engine choices were replaced by the new <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Pentastar_engine" title="Chrysler Pentastar engine">Pentastar</a> 3.6-liter V6 with six-speed automatic transmission, now the sole powertrain choice for all models. Interior trim was restyled on both vans, in addition to major exterior revisions highlighted by the new "double-crosshair" grille on the Grand Caravan and a new chrome grille for the Town &amp; Country.</p>
<p>Other changes included extra sound insulation, acoustic glass, new seats, softer-touch surfaces, new <a href="/wiki/LED" title="LED" class="mw-redirect">LED</a> ambient lighting and center console, and halogen projector headlamps with LED accents. The Chrysler models were adjusted so that instead of competing against equivalent Dodge trim levels, they were above Dodge in trim and features.<sup id="cite_ref-allpar-2011_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allpar-2011-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>For 2012, a new, basic style trim called "AVP" is introduced, while some features previously unavailable for "SE" (like touch navigation panel) become available as options (The "SE" now also receives a floor console, similar to the one available for "SXT"). Finally, just like on all other 2012 Dodge models, the front logo design now bears the two slanted rectangles in red.</p>
<p>For 2013, the "AVP" trim starting MSRP reduced by $1,000 from last year.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Volkswagen_Routan">Volkswagen Routan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Volkswagen Routan">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Volkswagen Routan" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Beginning with Generation V, Volkswagen began marketing the <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Routan" title="Volkswagen Routan">Routan</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Badge_engineering" title="Badge engineering">rebadged</a> variant of the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_RT_platform" title="Chrysler RT platform" class="mw-redirect">Chrysler RT platform</a> minivan with revised styling and content, for the North American market.</p>
<p>The Routan, made at <a href="/wiki/Windsor_Assembly" title="Windsor Assembly">Windsor Assembly</a> alongside the Grand Caravan, debuted in 2008 at the Chicago Auto Show. Sales began in autumn of 2008, and features neither Chrysler's <a href="/wiki/Dodge_Caravan#Stow.27n_Go_seating" title="Dodge Caravan">Stow'n Go</a> nor <a href="/wiki/Dodge_Caravan#Swivel_.27n_Go" title="Dodge Caravan">Swivel'n Go</a> seating systems. Total sales as of September 2010 were reported as being under 10,000 units, far lower than the target of 5% of the minivan market.<sup id="cite_ref-allpar-2011_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allpar-2011-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> As a result, in 2012, the Routan was discontinued.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Seating_features">Seating features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Seating features">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Seating features" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Dodge Caravan has incorporated various seating systems for their minivans to enhance interior flexibility.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Integrated_child_safety_seats">Integrated child safety seats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Integrated child safety seats">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Integrated child safety seats" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1991, Dodge introduced a second row bench seat integrating two child booster seats on 1992 models. These seats continued as an available option through Generation V until they were discontinued in 2010.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Easy-Out_Roller_Seats">Easy-Out Roller Seats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Easy-Out Roller Seats">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Easy-Out Roller Seats" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1995, Dodge introduced a system of seats to simplify installation, removal, and re-positioning, marketed as <i>Easy-Out Roller Seats</i>. When installed, the seats are latched to floor-mounted strikers. When unlatched, eight rollers lift each seat, allowing it to be rolled fore and aft. Tracks have locator depressions for rollers, thus enabling simple installation. Ergonomic levers at the seat backs release the floor latches single-handedly without tools and raise the seats onto the rollers in a single motion. Additionally, seat backs were designed to fold forward. Seat roller tracks are permanently attached to the floor and seat stanchions are aligned, facilitating the longitudinal rolling of the seats. Bench seat stanchions were moved inboard to reduce bending stress in the seat frames, allowing them to be lighter.</p>
<p>When configured as two and three person benches (available through Generation IV), the Easy Out Roller Seats could be unwieldy. Beginning in 2000, second and third row seats became available in a 'quad' configuration – bucket or captain chairs in the second row and a third row three-person 50/50 split "bench" — with each section weighing under 50&#160;lb (23&#160;kg). The Easy-out system remained in use through Generation V – where certain models featured a two-person bench <i>and</i> the under-floor compartments from the <i>Stow'n Go</i> system.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Routan" title="Volkswagen Routan">Volkswagen Routan</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Badge_engineering" title="Badge engineering">rebadged nameplate variant</a> of the Chrysler minivans, uses the Easy Out Roller Seats on its second row seating.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stow_.E2.80.99N_Go_seating">Stow ’N Go seating</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Stow ’N Go seating">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Stow ’N Go seating" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, Dodge introduced a system of second- and third-row seating that folded completely into under-floor compartments. It was marketed as <i>Stow ’N Go</i> and was available exclusively on long-wheelbase models.</p>
<p>In a development program costing $400 million,<sup id="cite_ref-ameinfo_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ameinfo-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> engineers initially used an <a href="/wiki/Erector_Set" title="Erector Set">Erector Set</a> to visualize the complex interaction of the design<sup id="cite_ref-pressrel1_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressrel1-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> and redesigned under-floor components. The system included the spare tire well, fuel tank, exhaust system, parking brake cables, rear climate control lines, and rear suspension<sup id="cite_ref-pressrel1_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressrel1-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> but precluded <a href="/wiki/Four-wheel_drive" title="Four-wheel drive">all-wheel drive</a> (AWD).</p>
<p>The system, in turn, creates a combined volume of 12 cubic feet (340&#160;L) of under-floor storage when second-row seats are deployed. With both rows folded, the vans have a flat-load floor and a maximum cargo volume of 160.7 cubic feet (4,550&#160;L).<sup id="cite_ref-ameinfo_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ameinfo-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-www.media.chrysler.com_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-www.media.chrysler.com-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Stow 'n Go system received the Popular Science Magazine's "Best of What's New" for 2005 award,<sup id="cite_ref-autointell_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autointell-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> and was never offered on the <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Routan" title="Volkswagen Routan">Volkswagen Routan</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Badge_engineering" title="Badge engineering">rebadged nameplate variant</a> of the Chrysler minivans.</p>
<p>For model year 2011 Chrysler revised the system, rebranding it as "Super Stow 'n Go". New pivoting head restraints with taller seatbacks and a revised folding mechanism (marketed as "single action") improved stowage ease — with the head restraints folding on themselves automatically and the entire seat automatically folding down to a position just over its floor recess.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Swivel_.27n_Go_seating">Swivel 'n Go seating</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Swivel 'n Go seating">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Swivel 'n Go seating" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dodge introduced a seating system in 2007, marketed as <i>Swivel'n Go</i>. In the seating system, two full size second row seats swivel to face the third row. A detachable table can be placed between the second and third row seats. The Swivel'n Go seating system includes the 3rd row seating from the Stow'n Go system. The system is offered on the Dodge Grand Caravan and <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Town_%26_Country" title="Chrysler Town &amp; Country">Chrysler Town &amp; Country</a>, but not the <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Routan" title="Volkswagen Routan">Volkswagen Routan</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Badge_engineering" title="Badge engineering">rebadged nameplate variant</a> of the Chrysler minivans.</p>
<p>These Swivel 'n Go Seats are manufacted by Intier Corp. a division of Magna. The tracks, risers and swivel mechanisms are assembled by Camslide, a division of Intier. The swivel mechanism was designed by and is produced by Toyo Seat USA Corp.</p>
<p>The system is noted for its high strength.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The entire load of the seat in the event of a crash is transferred through the swivel mechanism, which is almost twice as strong as the minimum government requirement.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The swivel mechanism includes bumpers that stabilize the seat while in the lock position. When rotated the seat comes off these bumpers to allow easy rotation.</p>
<p>The seat is not meant to be left in an unlocked position or swiveled with the occupant in it, although this will not damage the swivel mechanism.</p>
<p>"Swivel 'n Go" was dropped after the 2010 model year and is no longer an option on 2011 and later Chrysler and Dodge vans</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Production">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dodge_Caravan&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Production" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

Both wheelbase models were produced in <a href="/wiki/Fenton,_Missouri" title="Fenton, Missouri">Fenton, Missouri</a> at <a href="/wiki/Saint_Louis_Assembly" title="Saint Louis Assembly">Saint Louis Assembly</a> by members of the <a href="/wiki/United_Auto_Workers" title="United Auto Workers" class="mw-redirect">United Auto Workers</a> Local 110 until the end of October 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
