			<p><strong>Does anyone know what their Towing and Roadside Assistance covers? </strong></p>
<p>Towing and Roadside Assistance is usually packaged together in the  same coverage with most insurance companies.  Here are the general  definitions.</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Towing</strong></span>: covers expenses for having your car towed to another location as a result of Mechanical Breakdown or an Accident</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Roadside Assistance</strong></span>: covers Lockout  Assistance (but probably won&#8217;t cover re-key services), Flat Tire Change,  Emergency Fuel Delivery (usually 1 or 2 gallons), Dead  Battery/Jump-Start and Roadside Winching (vehicle stuck in mud, snow,  etc.)</p>
<p><strong>Towing Questions &#8211; </strong> Check with your carrier to find out their coverage specifics; each company has their own rules.</p>
<ol>
<li>Does your towing coverage follow the car or the person?  In other  words, if you are in someone else&#8217;s car and they need a tow, can your  coverage be used?</li>
<li>Is there a maximum amount of tow/roadside assistance claims you can make?</li>
<li>How far (how many miles) will the towing company take your car without you paying out of pocket?</li>
<li>Do you have to pay the Towing company and then submit receipts to your insurance company to be reimbursed?</li>
<li>Do you have to call a special number to make a tow claim?</li>
</ol>
