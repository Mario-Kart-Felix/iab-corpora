<p>Cadillac introduced a fresh <a href="/companies/cts/">CTS</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="CTS" data-exchange="NYSE" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/97041" data-quotes-closing="14.23" data-quotes-now="13.87"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/cts/">CTS</a></span></span>&nbsp;<a href="http://www.foxnews.com/leisure/2013/03/26/2014-cadillac-cts-hits-town-in-new-york/">mid-sized luxury car</a>. Land Rover imported <a href="http://cardealermagazine.co.uk/publish/new-york-blog-day-two/73254">James Bond.</a> But Honda stole the 2013 New York <a href="http://www.forbes.com/international/">International</a> Auto Show this week with a vacuum cleaner.</p> 
<p>A built-in vacuum cleaner, to be exact. Honda said the 2014 Odyssey minivan is now equipped with an integrated vacuum system, called the HondaVac. If the name seems familiar, it’s because the Honda version was developed with the experts at Shop-Vac, the folks who’ve saved your basement more than once.</p> 
<p>Basically, it looks just like a hand-held vacuum, if one was designed to fit into a car. As <a href="http://rumors.automobilemag.com/new-york-2013-2014-honda-odyssey-boasts-built-in-vacuum-cleaner-213171.html#ixzz2OkWsfba2">Automobile explained it</a>, “The vacuum canister is tucked into the driver’s side cargo area wall, as are a variety of nozzle attachments. Like most other Shop-Vac wet/dry vacs, the HondaVac boasts both a replacable filter and canister bag.&nbsp;The built-in vac doesn’t need to be plugged in or recharged, and can run for eight minutes off accessory power.”</p> 
<p>If you start the engine, Automobile says, the HondaVac can run without interruption. The HondaVac is only available on the 2014 Odyssey Touring model, whose price hasn’t been announced. But the 2013 Touring Elite model starts just above $44,000, according to <a href="http://automobiles.honda.com/odyssey/">Honda’s Web site</a>.</p> 
<p>Of course, people who can afford vehicles in that price range can also buy any vacuum of their choice. Or, they also can pay to have their minivan professionally vacuumed. But imagine the convenience of having a vacuum at your elbow, to sweep up Cheerios and dog hair.</p> 
<p>The idea of a built-in vacuum has gotten Honda plenty of attention. The HondaVac has been featured everywhere from NBC News to <a href="http://www.foxnews.com/leisure/2013/03/26/honda-odyssey-minivan-with-its-own-vacuum/">Fox News</a>, which calls it “the minivan with its own vacuum.” Honda can use the buzz: the past couple of years have been difficult for the Japanese car company, which had to restyle the Civic when the most recent version was a dud. And, Honda is still fighting to rebuild its market share in the wake of the Japanese earthquake and tsunami two years ago.</p> 
<p>With the HondaVac, Honda has managed to suck up (sorry) much of the attention from other carmakers at the show. Although car enthusiasts might snicker, the challenge for a car company at any show is to break through the automotive press and onto television news casts, capture mainstream Web sites, and get people to write Tweets and <a href="/companies/facebook/">Facebook</a> <span class="quotecard_hook" data-ticker="FB" data-exchange="null" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/15317" data-quotes-closing="0.0" data-quotes-now="0.0"><span class="wrapper"><a href="/companies/facebook/">Facebook</a></span></span> posts.</p> 
<p>To that end, the HondaVac could wind up joining the Taxi of Tomorrow and the flying car in the New York show pantheon. But whether a vacuum will be enough to make Honda clean up (sorry again) at the sales desk isn’t clear.&nbsp;Odyssey is the third best-selling minivan in the United States, behind Toyota Sienna and Dodge Caravan. And, minivan sales are only about one-third of what they were in the year 2000.</p> 
<p>But sometimes, all it takes is curiosity to get people into showrooms. Or, breakfast cereal on the carpet.</p>
