                    <div class="node-body article-body"><div></div><p>                              	BALTIMORE &ndash; <span class='keyword_link'><a href="http://wardsauto.com/taxonomy/term/53564?intlink=incontent" title="News about Chrysler">Chrysler</a></span> is keeping close to the vest which brand will get its next-generation minivan, but a decision has been made, says the auto maker&rsquo;s minivan vehicle-line executive.</p>                              <p>                              	Designers and engineers presented top management with several options before the final call was determined, Phil Jansen tells <em>WardsAuto</em> on the sidelines of a media drive here for the <span class='keyword_link'><a href="http://wardsauto.com/taxonomy/term/53581?intlink=incontent" title="News about Fiat">Fiat</a></span> 500L.</p>                              <p>                              	&ldquo;Designers start off wanting to know the vision of the vehicle early in the phase of the program, regardless of what brand it is,&rdquo; he says. &ldquo;We will do designs for many different brands and styles.</p>                              <p>                              	&ldquo;The minivan has a set of requirements we must deliver, and we can do it for a lot of different brands,&rdquo; he adds. &ldquo;(But) once we know the brand, then we start to focus on performance and features.&rdquo;</p>                              <p>                              	CEO Sergio Marchionne has said either the Dodge Grand Caravan or <span class='keyword_link'><a href="http://wardsauto.com/taxonomy/term/53564?intlink=incontent" title="News about Chrysler">Chrysler</a></span> Town &amp; Country will be axed. There has been no formal announcement of the decision or indication of when plans will be revealed.</p>                              <p>                              	But questions over the future of the Grand Caravan have been raised, as the Dodge brand is set to lose several models over the next few years, according to a <em>WardsAuto/</em>Automotive Compass production forecast.</p>                              <p>                              	Regardless of which marque gets the model, there still is a market for minivan buyers, Jansen says. Sales are far from peak levels, but the auto maker believes there remains a significant base of buyers who seek the functionality of a minivan.</p>                              <p>                              	&ldquo;Look at what others have done recently,&rdquo; he says &ldquo;Look at <span class='keyword_link'><a href="http://wardsauto.com/taxonomy/term/53606?intlink=incontent" title="News about Honda">Honda</a></span> and what they&rsquo;ve done with the Odyssey. Families still exist. Families need basic cargo (capacity).&rdquo;</p>                              <p>                              	Jansen says there are two types of minivan buyers: Young families and older drivers who want cargo- and passenger-hauling capability, but not necessarily the ruggedness of an SUV.</p>                              <p>                              	The next-generation minivan will need to blend fuel economy with performance, executives say. Jansen gives no hint whether the new model will be smaller, but trends in Europe &ndash; particularly the just-revealed <span class='keyword_link'><a href="http://wardsauto.com/taxonomy/term/53581?intlink=incontent" title="News about Fiat">Fiat</a></span> 500L Living, a 7-passenger version of the B-segment compact &ndash; may point in that direction.</p>                              <p>                              	&ldquo;I don&rsquo;t care if you have it as an A-segment or B-segment, you want similar levels of doing the fundamentals right,&rdquo; he says. &ldquo;There are lots of minivans, especially in Europe, that are much smaller and (have a) 7-passenger, narrower setup. That is a successful market in Europe.</p>                              <p>                              	&ldquo;You can definitely make it smaller. The question is, is that what should be done?&rdquo;</p>                              <p>                              	Although the minivan has ceded plenty of ground to the cross/utility vehicle, there still is room for both, Jansen contends.</p>                              <p>                              	&ldquo;Is it a growing segment? Probably not. Is it a mature segment? Yes. Is the market going away? It&rsquo;s anyone&rsquo;s guess,&rdquo; he says. &ldquo;(But) Sergio has made a very clear statement that we think the market is not going away We also don&rsquo;t think the market is going to grow two or three times its current size.&rdquo;</p>                              <p>                              	<a href="mailto:afoley@wardsauto.com">afoley@wardsauto.com</a></p>                              </div>                          </div>
