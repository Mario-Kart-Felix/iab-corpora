The Murphy Auto Museum is a non-profit museum that boasts a collection of over 50 cars displayed by many collectors from the tri-county area surrounding Oxnard, California. It houses a wide variety of classic and special interest vehicles. <br /><br />
 
Many great cars in this area have been tucked and are seldom see the light of day and are, therefore, infrequently seen by the general public. When Dan Murphy retired from his neurosurgery practice in Ventura, he set about to form a non-profit museum that would house these great cars.  Dan Murphy opened the museum on August 2, 2002.<br /><br />

The museum is located in a 16000 sq. ft. building, including an "old car showroom" area.  Thanks to the assistance of Holley Gene Leffler, the museum now has an extensive vintage clothing display representing all decades from the 1890s to the 1970s. <br /><br />
 
The museum auto collection continues to vary as they add new collectors to their family of supporters.
