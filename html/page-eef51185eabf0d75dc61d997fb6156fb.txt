          <P ALIGN=LEFT>
           <B><I><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="RED">ELECTRIC
            VEHICLES</FONT></FONT></FONT></I></B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="RED"> -</FONT></FONT></FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="RED"> 
           How they work </FONT></FONT><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">Electric
            vehicles are probably the simplest form of self propelled mechanical 
           transportation. In it most basic form, the drive train of an electric 
           vehicle consists of a battery attached to an off and on switch which 
           is attached to an electric motor. The electric motor drives the 
           wheels. Most electric vehicles have a more elaborate method to 
           control the amount of electricity going to the motor as well as a 
           system of gears to drive the wheels in a most efficient manner. In 
           addition to the above, &quot;solar powered&quot; vehicles incorporate 
           a solar collector which converts solar energy into electricity which 
           is used to slowly recharge the battery.</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <BR>
           <B><I><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="RED">WHAT
            THEY CAN AND CAN NOT DO</FONT></FONT></FONT></I></B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2"> 
           : </FONT></FONT></FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">Many
            people have the impression that electric cars are pollution free and 
           will eventually replace fossil fuel powered engines. This 
           understanding is rather inaccurate. The fact is that at the present 
           time, electric vehicles can not compete with main stream gasoline, 
           diesel, compressed natural gas and other vehicles in terms of speed, 
           range and overall safety. In addition, electric vehicles create 
           pollution in a number of</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">different
            ways. First, most of the electricity we use in the United States is 
           from fossil fuel burning power plants which creates pollution, though 
           usually away from the urban centers. In some cases, electricity 
           produced at nuclear power plants, though</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">these
            also pose potential risks. Solar power plants are in their infancy, 
           though they may be useful in sunny, areas with little cloud cover. If 
           everyone was to have solar arrays on their property, some people may 
           object to them, similar to how people objected to the early 10 foot 
           satellite dishes. Wind power may also offer some potential to 
           generate electricity, though windmills pollute in terms of visual as 
           well as noise pollution. In January of this year, I drove from 
           Hamburg Germany north towards Denmark and passed a large array of 
           modern windmill electric generators. The first thing that struck me 
           was the loud and continuous wiring sound they put out. Here in New 
           York City, there have been several attempts over the past 25 years to 
           harness wind power. There were windmills on East 10 street between 
           Avenue A and First Avenue, East 5 Street between Avenue A and Avenue 
           B and on East 9 street and the Bowery. In every case, the owners</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">abandoned
            the windmills after a year or so presumable since they were not cost 
           effective. Nevertheless such a system may be useful in remote areas. 
           Many of the materials used in electric cars today are toxic. For 
           example the lead, which is the most common element used in 
           conventional deep cycle electric vehicle batteries can be a serious 
           health hazard. In the case of solar panels, many of the panels are 
           manufactured using highly toxic chemicals and the panels themselves 
           can, under the wrong circumstances, pose a health</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">hazard.
            Many people are concerned about power lines and the possible health 
           effects. In an electric car, you are surrounded by massive amounts of electro-magnetic
            radiation. To this date,</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">health
            hazards of this has not been proven. The primary obstacle facing 
           electric vehicles today is the limited storage capacity of 
           economically viable batteries. The most common and probably the best 
           compromise battery is the &quot;deep cycle&quot; lead acid 
           battery...the same type of battery that has been in use for electric 
           vehicles since the 1880's. There are other batteries that do offer 
           greater electrical storage capacity but at either a much higher 
           price, or less overall life or low power output (though higher total 
           storage capacity). Today, most electric cars have a range of about 40 
           miles per charge at a speed of about 35 to 45 miles per hours. Most 
           smaller electric cars use between 6 and 12 6 volt deep cycle, lead 
           acid batteries. The recharging time is normally 8 hours and once per 
           week, the charging time is 12 hours to even out the charge among the 
           battery array. Deep cycle lead acid batteries, with proper care, can 
           last about 3 years or about 600 charge-discharge cycles. Factors such 
           as ambient temperature, moisture in the air, terrain and how a person 
           drives can substantially decrease these figures. Overall, 
           conventional batteries are not are predictable in terms of range and 
           speed as say, a gallon of gasoline. To increase range, many car 
           companies have experimented by building lighter or smaller vehicles. 
           Generally a lighter or smaller vehicle can hold less passengers and 
           in most cases, does not offer the safety of a larger vehicle. Solar 
           panels have been used to recharge electric vehicles, though there is 
           not enough energy from sun light per square foot to directly propel a 
           conventional 2500 pound car. It is possible to slowly store solar 
           energy in the batteries to allow the car to move for a limited time.</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <BR>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">SO
            WHY BOTHER WITH ELECTRIC VEHICLES? </FONT></FONT></FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">Electric
            vehicles offer a unique driving experience. With their low center of 
           gravity, most</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">electric
            cars, offer superb handling as well as rapid initial</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">acceleration.
            Electric motors offer almost continuous torque even</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">at
            lower speeds. This allows a vehicle to do things that</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">conventional
            fossil fuels vehicles can not do. For example, if you were to drive 
           up to a street curb with a conventional gasoline</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">powered
            car, stop and then try to climb the curb, you would have to rev up 
           the motor and only then would you be able to drive over</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">the
            curb. With an electric vehicle, you would be able to climb the curb 
           with out any real effort. Today, most railroads around the</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">world
            use diesel electric locomotives with the diesel engine generating 
           the electricity to power the electric motors which actually turns the 
           wheels. American military tanks use the same technique. Electric 
           vehicles are also ideal for use in areas that have very high gasoline 
           costs or where conventional fuel is simply</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">not
            available. During world war two, many countries started to produce 
           electric vehicles for the population. Electric cars were</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">manufactured
            during the war in Spain, Belgium, France, Japan and others. In 1946, 
           my father had seen several electric powered and steam powered private 
           vehicles in Tokyo that clever citizens had converted from gasoline 
           when gasoline was no longer available.</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><BR>
           <BR>
           <B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT SIZE="2">Presently,
            I support multi-powered or hybrid cars which utilize a combination 
           of gasoline and electric motors. In such vehicles, the electric power 
           can be used at low speeds or in cities limiting the amount of 
           pollution in urban areas, while at higher speeds, the gasoline engine 
           can take over. This configuration was actually first tried in 1901 
           and a number of times thereafter, but did not take hold partially 
           since there is a double cost involved to manufacture the vehicle. 
           Electric vehicles will probably have a limited niche market for the 
           foreseeable future.</FONT></FONT></FONT></B></TD>

           DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS</FONT></FONT></FONT></B></P>

           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">&nbsp;
            </FONT></FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">Electric
            vehicles are fascinating to build and drive, even taking into 
           consideration the limitations as outlined above. Electric vehicles 
           are so simple in overall construction that they can easily be built 
           by people with basic construction skills. In the simplest form, such 
           as in a high school class, a very basic electric go cart can be built 
           that can consist of a simple wood frame built out of 2&quot; x 
           4&quot;s, basic wheels, an electric motor, a car battery and a 
           momentary electrical switch to turn the electricity on and off as a 
           gas pedal. A more sophisticated system would use a go-cart body made 
           out of steel (or perhaps converted from an existing gasoline 
           go-cart), with the same elements as the wooden cart would use. Simple 
           electric vehicles as just described can probably be built in two 
           weeks with minimal skills.</FONT></FONT></B></FONT></P>

           <FONT COLOR="RED"><B><I><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">DESIGNING:</FONT></FONT></I></B></FONT><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"> </FONT></FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">As
            with all design projects, it is essential that the designer has a 
           clear </FONT></FONT></B></FONT><B><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">un</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">derstanding
            of what the function of the final vehicle will have. For example, 
           how many passengers will the vehicle carry? Will there be storage 
           space? Where or how will the vehicle be recharged? Will it be parked 
           on the street? What are the local regulations pertaining to electric 
           vehicles? If the vehicle will be charged from the power grid, is the 
           available outlet adequate for charging the vehicle?</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           </P>
          <P ALIGN=CENTER>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><A HREF="shark5.jpg"><!-- $MVD$:picsz("0","0") --><IMG SRC="shark5.jpg" WIDTH="222" HEIGHT="153" VSPACE="0" HSPACE="0" ALT="[IMAGE]" BORDER="0" LOOP="0"></A></FONT></FONT></FONT></P>

           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">&nbsp; </FONT></FONT><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">Once
            everything of the final vehicle has been written down and 
           considered, you can start to design the vehicle. With the limited 
           storage capacity of batteries, it is important to design the vehicle 
           using as much light weight materials that can be safely incorporated 
           into the vehicle with cost and skill considerations in mind. It 
           should be noted that many light weight materials are particularly 
           difficult to work with and often times a person who is not acquainted 
           with the particular engineering of the material will actually build a 
           vehicle that is heavier using the light weight materials, than if 
           they had used heavier, but easier to use materials. I have seen 
           college student constructed solar powered racing cars using much 
           touted, though still questionable, carbon fiber that probably weighed 
           more then if they had used aluminum or steel tubing. Steel, 
           particularly thin walled steel, is the easiest to work with. 
           Aluminum, while weighing about 60 percent less then steel, is very 
           difficult to weld and requires</FONT></FONT></B></FONT></P>
          <P ALIGN=CENTER>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><A HREF="shark6.jpg"><!-- $MVD$:picsz("0","0") --><IMG SRC="shark6.jpg" WIDTH="238" HEIGHT="164" VSPACE="0" HSPACE="0" ALT="[IMAGE]" BORDER="0" LOOP="0"></A></FONT></FONT></FONT></P>
          <P ALIGN=LEFT>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">considerable
            engineering in order to fully take advantage of the wight 
           difference. Wood can often be used for some components, though in an 
           accident, it usually does not offer the same protection that metals 
           offer. Steel and wood are by far the cheapest materials to work with 
           and this should be taken into</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">consideration
            if cost of the finished vehicle is a factor.</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">&nbsp;
            After everything is considered, then and only then should the actual 
           design phase proceed. In a classroom environment, I think that it is 
           important for a professor to summarize to the students what materials 
           can be used and then, with out suggesting a design, let the students 
           individually design their vehicle on paper as a homework assignment. 
           At this point, each member of the class</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <BR>

           In the case of the DIDIK SUN SHARK, about 500 feet of wire and over 
           &nbsp;100 moving mechanical components were installed prior to the

            review every other design and then redesign a vehicle using the new 
           ideas gained by seeing what other have designed. This way new ideas 
           can be incorporated into the design. Who can predict what can be 
           developed this way? Again everyone should compare notes and then a

           In my vehicles, I make it a point to have as many lights and 
           reflectors on the vehicle as possible so that conventional car 
           drivers can see the vehicle from a distance. I also paint the 
           vehicles bright colors for added visibility.</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>

           <B><I><FONT COLOR="RED"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">SKILLS</FONT></FONT></FONT></I></B><FONT COLOR="RED"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">:</FONT></FONT></FONT><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"> </FONT></FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">In
            building a prototype vehicle, one must consider the skill of the 
           builders. Probably any shop class or machine shop</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">could
            build a reasonably high quality electric powered car. The basic 
           skills that will be needed using off the shelf components or surplus 
           components includes welding skills, metal cutting skills,</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">drafting
            skills and general design skills. Welding is essential if the frame 
           of the vehicle is to be made out of steel. Surprisingly, it is very 
           easy to master basic welding, </FONT></FONT></B></FONT><B><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">pa</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2">rticularly
            if using a wire fed &quot;mig&quot; type welder. Such basic mig

           Freight Tools, out in California for as little as $129 though prices 
           can also be substantially higher. It will also be necessary to have 
           access to a metal cut-off saw. A cut off saw capable of using 12 or 
           14 inch blades is ideal. Once basic welding techniques are mastered, 
           many seemingly hard projects will look much easier. If</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>

          <P ALIGN=LEFT>
           <FONT COLOR="RED"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">MATERIALS:</FONT></FONT></FONT><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1"> </FONT></FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">The
            basic materials needed to build a steel framed,</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">electric
            vehicle with a body includes:</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           </P>
          <P ALIGN=LEFT>
           <FONT COLOR="RED"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">Steel
            tubing.</FONT></FONT></FONT><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1"> </FONT></FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">I
            prefer to use basic 1/2&quot; to 1&quot; EMT electrical tubes. They 
           are light weight, easy to cut, easy to handle and are very 
           inexpensive. For example, a 3/4&quot; x</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">10
            foot EMT tube at Home Depot costs about $2.50 in New York City. If 
           you prefer, you can also purchase standard square steel tubes from 
           steel distributors such as Eastern Steel in New York City for very 
           low prices. The standard length of steel tubes form distributors is 
           20 feet or 24 feet.</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <BR>
           <FONT COLOR="RED"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">Electric
            Motor.</FONT></FONT></FONT><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1"> </FONT></FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">Though
            some people have used ac motors, I</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">much
            prefer to use DC motors. One to three HP DC motors can</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">be
            purchased in scrap yards (not to be confused with auto junk</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">yards)
            for under $100. They can also be purchased from a</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">variety
            of surplus outlets throughout the country.</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <BR>
           <FONT COLOR="RED"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">Drive.</FONT></FONT></FONT><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1"> </FONT></FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">You
            can use a number of systems to couple the</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">electric
            motor to the wheels including sprocket gear and</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">chain,
            pulley and belt or similar. The pulley and belt system</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">is
            probably the easiest system to use.</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <BR>
           <FONT COLOR="RED"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">Body.</FONT></FONT></FONT><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1"> </FONT></FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">There
            are several ways to shape the body. One way</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">is
            to use a very large styrofoam block that can be sanded</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">using
            an electric sander to the correct shape and then covered</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">first
            with Elmers type white glue and then covered with</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">fiberglass.
            Another, cheaper and less messy way is to use</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">chicken
            wire mesh to shape the body over a basic metal frame.</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">You
            can then cover the wire mesh with fiberglass sheets,</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">though
            I usually cover the mesh first with very thin layer of</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">plaster
            and fabric strips to give the mould strength and then</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">cover
            it with the fiberglass. The finish can be made perfect</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">with
            the help of an auto body expert who will smooth out</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">imperfections
            and then paint the resulting vehicle to high</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           <FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="1">standards.</FONT></FONT></B></FONT><BR>
           </P>
          <P ALIGN=LEFT>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><B><FONT COLOR="RED">Windshield.</FONT></B><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B> 
           The windshield should be made out of a substance with safety in mind. 
           While conventional car windshields are useful, I have found them to 
           be generally too heavy for my vehicles. Instead, I prefer to use 
           &quot;unbreakable&quot; Lexan type plastic. I normally use the 
           1/16&quot; thick lexan and for strength, design the windscreen so 
           that it is curved. It is also possible to mould the windscreen using 
           either a vacuum or compression system, though this requires a rather 
           high degree of construction skill.</B></FONT></FONT></FONT></P>
          <P ALIGN=CENTER>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><A HREF="shark2.jpg"><!-- $MVD$:picsz("0","0") --><IMG SRC="shark2.jpg" WIDTH="236" HEIGHT="163" VSPACE="0" HSPACE="0" ALT="[IMAGE]" BORDER="0" LOOP="0"></A></B></FONT></FONT></FONT></P>
          <P ALIGN=LEFT>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B>When
            designing an electric vehicle, it is important to consider exactly 
           how the vehicle will be used. In the case of the DIDIK SUN SHARK, it 
           was designed to be able to go through a standard 28&quot; doorway.</B></FONT></FONT></FONT><BR>
           </P>
          <P ALIGN=LEFT>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B>Electric
            wiring and switches can be purchased at most electrical supply houses.</B></FONT></FONT></FONT><BR>
           <BR>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><B><FONT COLOR="RED">CONSTRUCTION</FONT></B><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B>:
            After the vehicle has been designed on paper, and the components 
           purchased, you can proceed with the actual construction. Safety 
           should never be compromised. Always wear ear plugs, face shield, 
           proper protective clothing and work in a well ventilated, properly 
           lighted room.</B></FONT></FONT></FONT></P>
          <P ALIGN=LEFT>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B>First
            cut out the immediate metal frame parts and weld them together. I 
           have found that a department store type rolling coat rack can be 
           utilized as a welding jig with the use of vice grips to hold the 
           metal that is being welded together. After the frame has been welded 
           together add the steering mechanism and electric</B></FONT></FONT></FONT><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"> dr</FONT></FONT></FONT></B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B>ive
            components. Before the body can be incorporated on to the</B></FONT></FONT></FONT><BR>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B>vehicle,
            it is essential that the vehicle is in complete working order. After 
           the body has been built, it is normally more</B></FONT></FONT></FONT><BR>
           </P>
          <P ALIGN=CENTER>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B><A HREF="shark1.jpg"><!-- $MVD$:picsz("0","0") --><IMG SRC="shark1.jpg" WIDTH="250" HEIGHT="173" VSPACE="0" HSPACE="0" ALT="[IMAGE]" BORDER="0" LOOP="0"></A></B></FONT></FONT></FONT></P>
          <P ALIGN=LEFT>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><I>DIDIK
            SUN SHARK without the solar panel installed.</I></FONT></FONT></FONT></P>
          <P ALIGN=LEFT>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B>difficult
            to make possible modifications to the mechanical components. Initial 
           test drives should not be conducted on public</B></FONT></FONT></FONT><BR>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B>roads
            but rather on private property, at the slowest possible speed. As 
           with all prototypes, it will probably be necessary to made minor and 
           possibly major modifications before the vehicle is ready for general 
           use. No matter how much preparation is made in</B></FONT></FONT></FONT><BR>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B>designing
            the vehicle, one can never predict the effects of every possible 
           design feature.</B></FONT></FONT></FONT><BR>
           <BR>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B>Construction
            of a basic vehicle, capable of holding two people should not take 
           more than 60 hours of actual construction time, though it may take as 
           little as 25 hours.</B></FONT></FONT></FONT><BR>
           <BR>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><B><FONT COLOR="RED">LEGAL
            REQUIREMENTS</FONT></B><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B> The federal 
           government as well as all states have strict rule for motor vehicles 
           manufacturers. Almost all states have special provisions for electric 
           cars. Further, most states allow human powered vehicles with power 
           assists to operate on local roads without having to be registered. 
           Further, some</B></FONT></FONT></FONT><BR>
           <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica,Monaco"><FONT SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#007F7F"><B>states
            have special regulations that are easier to conform to for slow 
           moving vehicles or specialty vehicles such as snow mobiles, or all 
           terrain vehicles. These provisions may be useful to know when 
           designing the vehicle.</B></FONT></FONT></FONT></P>
