<p><b>Car tuning</b> is modification of the performance or appearance of a vehicle. For actual "tuning" in the sense of automobiles or vehicles, see <a href="/wiki/Engine_tuning" title="Engine tuning">engine tuning</a>. Most vehicles leave the factory set up for an average driver's expectations and conditions. Tuning, on the other hand, has become a way to personalize the characteristics of a vehicle to the owner's preference. Cars may be altered to provide better fuel economy, produce more power, or to provide better handling.</p>
<p>Car tuning is related to <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">auto racing</a>, although most <a href="/wiki/Performance_car" title="Performance car">performance cars</a> never compete. Tuned cars are built for the pleasure of owning and driving. Exterior modifications include changing the aerodynamic characteristics of the vehicle via side skirts, front and rear bumpers, spoilers, splitters, air vents and light weight wheels.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cars have always been subject to after-market modification. The golden age of car tuning was the decades between World War II and the beginning of air pollution restrictions. Both moderate and radical modification have been commemorated in the popular songs <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rod_Race" title="Hot Rod Race">Hot Rod Race</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rod_Lincoln" title="Hot Rod Lincoln">Hot Rod Lincoln</a>. The names of <a href="/wiki/Abarth" title="Abarth">Abarth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a> appear on models styled after the cars they modified. <a href="/wiki/Cosworth" title="Cosworth">Cosworth</a> went, with support from Ford, from modifying <a href="/wiki/Ford_of_Britain" title="Ford of Britain">English Ford</a> <a href="/wiki/Flathead_engine" title="Flathead engine">Flathead engines</a> for <a href="/wiki/Lotus_Seven" title="Lotus Seven">Lotus Sevens</a> to dominating <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a>.</p>
<p>In the 1970s and 80s, many Japanese performance cars were never exported outside the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_domestic_market" title="Japanese domestic market">Japanese domestic market</a>. In the late 1980s and early 1990s, <a href="/wiki/Grey_import_vehicle" title="Grey import vehicle">grey imports</a> of Japanese performance cars, such as the <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Skyline" title="Nissan Skyline">Nissan Skyline</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-0-60_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0-60-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-0-60_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0-60-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> began to be privately imported into Western Europe and North America. In the United States, this was in direct contrast to the domestic car production around the same time, where there was a very small performance aftermarket for domestic compact and economy cars; the focus was instead on <a href="/wiki/Sporty_car" title="Sporty car" class="mw-redirect">sporty cars</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Ford_Mustang" title="Ford Mustang">Ford Mustang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet_Corvette" title="Chevrolet Corvette">Chevrolet Corvette</a>, or on classic <a href="/wiki/Muscle_car" title="Muscle car">muscle cars</a>.</p>
<p>Because of their light weight and the increasing availability of low-cost tuning equipment, economy and compact cars exhibit high performance at a low cost in comparison to dedicated sports cars. As professional sporting and racing with such vehicles increased, so did recreational use of these vehicles. Drivers with little or no automotive, mechanical, or racing experience would modify their vehicles to emulate the more impressive versions of racing vehicles, with mixed results.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Areas_of_modification">Areas of modification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Areas of modification">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Areas of modification" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The essence of modification of a tuner car is an attempt to extract the greatest possible performance—or the appearance of high performance—from the base motor vehicle through the addition, alteration or outright replacement of parts. Although this largely involves modifying the engine and management systems of the vehicle to increase the power output, additional changes are often required to allow the vehicle to handle this power, including stiffened suspension, widened tires, better brakes, improved steering and transmission modifications such as the installation of a short shifter. Although largely invisible from outside the vehicle, certain modifications such as low profile tires, altered suspension, and the addition of spoilers can change the overall appearance of the car, As well as adding downforce to increase traction.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Audio">Audio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Audio">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Audio" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A stock audio system is one specified by the manufacturer when the vehicle was built in the factory. A custom audio installation can involve anything from the upgrade of the radio to a full-blown customization based around the audio equipment. Events are held where entrants compete for the loudest, highest quality reception or most innovative sound systems.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Interior">Interior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Interior">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Interior" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>All cars competing in each class must adhere to a strict set of regulations. As in some well known racing events, like NASCAR and NHRA, sanctioned events often require a minimum vehicle weight. In such cases the interior is stripped and lead weights or similar are added to meet that requirement.</p>
<p>Along with weight requirements, safety requirements are present . Requirements differ for different classes. Roll cages, fire extinguishers, reinforced bucket seats, seat harnesses, and the like are some of the required safety modifications. Roll cages may be difficult to install when the original equipment interior is present. Some tuners will have "gutted" interiors. Some even go as far as to not have a sound system or even an A/C.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Engine_tuning">Engine tuning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Engine tuning">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Engine tuning" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="rellink relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Engine_tuning" title="Engine tuning">Engine tuning</a></div>
<p>Engine tuning is the process of modifying the operating characteristics of an engine. In a typical engine set-up, there are various mechanical and electronic elements such as the intake manifold, spark plugs, <a href="/wiki/Mass_flow_sensor" title="Mass flow sensor">Mass air flow/ Volume air flow</a>, etc. Modern engines employ the use of an <a href="/wiki/Engine_Control_Unit" title="Engine Control Unit" class="mw-redirect">ECM</a> to provide the best balance between performance and emissions. Via the <a href="/wiki/On-board_diagnostics" title="On-board diagnostics">OBD</a> communications protocol, the electronically controlled aspects of the engine can be modified in the process known as 'mapping'. Mapping can either be performed by changing the software within the ECU (chip tuning via firmware modification), or by providing false data via plug-in hardware (<a href="/wiki/Piggybacking_(data_transmission)" title="Piggybacking (data transmission)">piggybacking</a>). Mechanical components can also be replaced, such as turbochargers or superchargers.</p>
<p>Other standalone engine management systems are available. These systems replace the factory computer with one that is user programmable.</p>
<p>Poorly executed modifications can have a detrimental effect on performance as well as mechanical and electronic components. An example would be the use of an air compressor such as a turbocharger to increase the volume of air used in power stroke of the <a href="/wiki/Otto_cycle" title="Otto cycle">otto cycle</a>. In a typical chemical reaction, the air-fuel ratio must be a minimum of 14:1 (see <a href="/wiki/Stoichiometry" title="Stoichiometry">Stoichiometry</a>). If higher ratios are used, higher pressures and temperatures are observed in the cylinders. Depending upon the build of the engine, such operating parameters can lead to premature failure such as warped cylinder heads and walls (temperature related), cracked connecting rods and crankshafts (excessive torque applied), engine seizing and blowouts.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Suspension_tuning">Suspension tuning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Suspension tuning">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Suspension tuning" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)" title="Suspension (vehicle)">Suspension</a> tuning involves modifying the springs, shock absorbers, swaybars, and other related components of a vehicle. Shorter springs offer greater stiffness and a lower center of gravity at the possible cost of unwanted changes of suspension geometry. Stiffer <a href="/wiki/Shock_absorber" title="Shock absorber">shock absorbers</a> improve the dynamic weight shifting during cornering and normally have shorter internals to stop them from bottoming out when shorter springs are used. Stiffer <a href="/wiki/Sway_bar" title="Sway bar">sway bars</a> reduce body roll during cornering, thus improving the grip that the tires have on the surface by reducing suspension geometry changes caused by roll; this also improves handling response due to faster weight shifting (similar to stiffer springs.) The danger with overly stiff swaybars is the lifing of the inner wheel, which changes traction of that end at a discontinuous rate. By increasing the roll resistance of on end, <a href="/wiki/Weight_transfer" title="Weight transfer">weight transfer</a> is concentrated at that end, causing it to slip more than the other. This effect is used to control the over/under steer characteristic as well as to reduce roll. Other components that are sometimes added are <a href="/wiki/Strut_bar" title="Strut bar">strut bars</a>, which improve the body stiffness and help better maintain the proper suspension geometry during cornering. On some cars certain braces, anti-roll bars, etc., can be retrofitted to base model cars from sports models.</p>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/Offroad_vehicle" title="Offroad vehicle" class="mw-redirect">offroad vehicles</a>, the emphasis is on lengthening the suspension travel and installing larger tires. Larger tires, with or without larger wheels, increase ground clearance, ride over short obstacles and holes more smoothly, provide more cussioning and decrease ground loading which is important on soft surfaces.</p>
<p>These suspension modifications are in contrast to <a href="/wiki/Lowrider" title="Lowrider">Lowriders</a> with hydraulic or pneumatic suspensions. Lowriders use another type of suspension tuning in which the height of each individual wheel can be rapidly adjusted by a system of rams which, in some cases, makes it possible to "bounce" the wheels completely clear of the ground.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Body_tuning">Body tuning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Body tuning">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Body tuning" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Body tuning involves adding or modifying <a href="/wiki/Spoiler_(automotive)" title="Spoiler (automotive)">spoilers</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Body_kit" title="Body kit">body kit</a> in order to improve the aerodynamic performance of a vehicle. Through the generation of downforce, cornering speeds and tire adhesion can be improved, often at the expense of increased drag. To lighten the vehicle, bodywork components such as hoods and rear view mirrors may be replaced with lighter weight components.</p>
<p>Often, body modifications are done mainly to improve a vehicle's appearance, as in the case of non-functioning scoops, wide arches or other aesthetic modification. Aftermarket spoilers or body kits rarely improve a car's performance. The majority, in fact, add weight and increase the <a href="/wiki/Drag_coefficient" title="Drag coefficient">drag coefficient</a> of the vehicle, thus reducing its overall performance.</p>
<p>Increasing the wheel track width through spacers and wide body kits enhance the cars cornering ability. Lowering the center of gravity via suspension modifications is another aim of body tuning. Often, suspension tuners unfamiliar with <a href="/wiki/Hooke%27s_Law" title="Hooke's Law" class="mw-redirect">spring dynamics</a> will cut stock springs, producing a harder, bouncy ride. It is also common to lower the car too far, beyond the optimal height for performance, purely for appearance.</p>
<p>Competition cars may have light weight windows, or the windows may be completely removed, as auto glass adds significant weight high up. Plastic windows are much more vulnerable to scratches which reduces service life.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tires">Tires</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Tires">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Tires" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tire" title="Tire">Tires</a> have large effects on a car's behavior and are replaced periodically, therefore tire selection is a very cost effective way to personalize an automobile. Choices include tires for various weather and road conditions, different sizes and various compromises between cost, grip, service life, rolling resistance, handling and ride comfort.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Detuning">Detuning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Detuning">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Detuning" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Detuning is returning a modified car to its original factory status or reducing its performance in a particular area of tuning. For example, a car may be "detuned" to allow increased traction where the track grip is not sufficient to handle the increased power of the tuned engine.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Styles_of_modification">Styles of modification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Styles of modification">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Styles of modification" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Modified cars can be significantly different from their stock counterparts. A common factor among owners/modifiers is to emulate the visual and/or performance characteristics of established styles and design principles. Sometimes these similarities are unintentional. Some of the many different styles and visual influences to car modification are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Rat style: The characteristics of the <a href="/wiki/Rat_rod" title="Rat rod">rat rod</a> style of hot rod and custom cars, imitating the "unfinished" appearance of some hot rods in the 40s, 50s and 60s.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hot_rod" title="Hot rod">Hot rod</a> style largely consists of period specific vehicles, components and finishes to reproduce characteristics of early hot rods from the 1930s and 1940s. This style may also include the associated styles of Street Rods and <a href="/wiki/Custom_cars" title="Custom cars" class="mw-redirect">Custom cars</a>.</li>
<li>Modern styles include the recently established modification styles such as the <a href="/wiki/Import_scene" title="Import scene">Import scene</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lowrider" title="Lowrider">Lowriders</a>, European(Euro-style), DUB, <a href="/wiki/Cal_Look" title="Cal Look" class="mw-redirect">Cal Look</a>; most of which are largely visually oriented.</li>
<li>Cultural/media styles: The characteristics specific to cultures, stereotypes and media, such as <a href="/wiki/Boy_racer" title="Boy racer">Boy racers</a> and film specific cars. <a href="/wiki/The_Fast_and_the_Furious_(series)" title="The Fast and the Furious (series)">The Fast and the Furious (film series)</a> provides an often emulated modification style.</li>
<li>Production car styles, in which the characteristics of current-model and luxury cars, <a href="/wiki/Sports_car" title="Sports car">Sports cars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Supercar" title="Supercar">Supercars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muscle_car" title="Muscle car">Muscle cars</a> are emulated, largely with the intent of improving or updating a vehicles appearance and/or technology to current market preferences.</li>
<li>Purpose built or racing car styles such as <a href="/wiki/Touring_car_racing" title="Touring car racing">Touring car racing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rallying" title="Rallying">Rallying</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drifting_(motorsport)" title="Drifting (motorsport)">Drifting (motorsport)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drag_racing" title="Drag racing">Drag racing</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sleeper_(car)" title="Sleeper (car)">Sleeper (car)</a>: This is where a car owner will put every effort into performance and try to keep the car looking standard, usually in order to not raise suspicion.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/B%C5%8Ds%C5%8Dzoku" title="Bōsōzoku">Bōsōzoku</a>: This Japanese style features wide bodykits and huge wings.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_requirements">Legal requirements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Legal requirements">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Legal requirements" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many countries or municipalities have legal requirements which govern vehicle modifications. For example, all vehicles in Victoria, Australia, must conform to construction standards to ensure vehicle safety.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> There are also restrictions for P Plate drivers which can prevent young drivers from driving modified vehicles.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Developed countries have "smog rules", which generally forbid any modifications to engines or related components, unless the modifications themselves are type smog certified, like production car models. One of a kind modifications, even if they do not actually result in increased emissions, prevent legal use on public roads.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sanctioning_organizations">Sanctioning organizations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sanctioning organizations">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Car_tuning&amp;veaction=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sanctioning organizations" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many organizations involved in competitive motorsports establish safety guidelines that far exceed legal requirements placed on street legal vehicles. The <a href="/wiki/NHRA" title="NHRA" class="mw-redirect">NHRA</a>, <a href="/wiki/International_Hot_Rod_Association" title="International Hot Rod Association">IHRA</a> and SOLO programs all require that vehicles pass inspection to ensure that all regulations are being complied with.</p>
