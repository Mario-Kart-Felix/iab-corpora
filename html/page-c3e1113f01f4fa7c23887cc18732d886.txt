          <p align="left" class="style14">I have to admit for myself that after looking at the pros and cons it does seem a bit vague so let me touch on a few points. The fuel mileage of a Prius is awesome if you rely of city driving. Now look at a Ford Focus, pretty good gas mileage at half the up front cost. Emissions, well, the hybrid has about 25% less emissions than a gas only, so if you buy into the Al Gore global warming deal, this may be a no brainer for you. Moving on to more relevant issues like cost of operation and repairs. The cost of maintenance short term or under warranty would be about the same as a gasoline only car. After the warranty is up... watch out, the cost goes up as the hybrid has 2 motors and electric and a gasoline and in most cases other than brakes and tires, your off to the DEALER for repairs. As far as driving, in a dry climate, not much of an issue. Snow driving in a hybrid on the other hand can be quite a different story. Lets take the Honda insight for example, the battery is very heavy and located toward the rear of the vehicle and causes the rear of the car to want to slide out similar to when you would operate the park brake to make it slide only this will happen without encouragement.</p>
          <p align="left" class="style14">For me, The Price alone out ways any of my thoughts of buying a hybrid brand new. I have owned and operated an Auto Repair Service Center and come across may different vehicles and seen many different hybrids already in need of costly repairs. I feel i can buy two non hybrids for the price of a hybrid. So for me, The Hybrid is not my choice for the future. Just my opinion, nothing else. </p>
