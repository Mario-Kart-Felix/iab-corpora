				<p><a href="http://paultan.org/2013/01/03/suzuki-wagon-r-two-for-tokyo-auto-salon/wagonr-3/" rel="attachment wp-att-148113"><img src="http://s5.paultan.org/image/wagonr-3-e1357195575262-630x293.jpg" alt="wagonr-3" width="630" height="293" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-148113" /></a></p>
<p>Suzuki&#8217;s next to join the Tokyo Auto Salon party &#8211; it&#8217;ll be displaying two <a href="http://paultan.org/2008/09/29/suzuki-wagon-r-launched-in-japan/" target="_blank">Wagon R</a> design studies at the aftermarket exhibition, both based on the recently-launched fifth generation kei car.</p>
<p>The Wagon R Stingray Customize you see above is based on the more upmarket Stingray version, and features big brushed alloys, unique brown paintwork, headlamps adorned with teal-coloured accents, LED strips and a road-hugging bodykit. Some suspension tweaks, too.</p>
<p>The one in white is based on the regular Wagon R. Called the Wagon R RR Concept, it comes with sports suspension, smoked headlamps, decals, black wheels, a bodykit and twin central tailpipes.</p>
<p>The Suzuki Wagon R has been the best-selling kei car in Japan since 2003.</p>
