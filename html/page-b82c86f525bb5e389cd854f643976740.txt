<p><b>Diesel cars</b>&nbsp;are cars that have a diesel engine. Diesel is the second most used fuel, with gasoline being first. Diesel cars are extremely popular in Europe, and are starting to make a move back to the United States, where they used to be popular in the early 1970s. Diesel has a negative connotation attached, that it is a dirty fuel that causes your car to run very loud. That was 25 or more years ago, and now they have fixed it pretty good. Diesel is now starting to pass the very strict emissions tests and are populating the roads more. Diesel cars are in demand due to their increased fuel economy numbers. If a gasoline car gets 30 miles per gallon, an equivalent diesel car will get 40 miles per gallon. This is a big difference, and can amount to hundreds of miles over the course of the year, which can amount to savings of hundreds of dollars as well. Diesel engines provide more torque for your car, which helps in its acceleration. This comes at the expense of power, but diesel engines have a turbo charged engine which can match up fist against fist versus gasoline. Diesel cars are a little more expensive than gasoline, but the engine in it is the reason why. The engines last much longer than their gasoline counterparts, and this helps the car hold its value more in the long run. If you would like more information about diesel cars, please check out the rest of our articles here at CarsDirect.</p>
