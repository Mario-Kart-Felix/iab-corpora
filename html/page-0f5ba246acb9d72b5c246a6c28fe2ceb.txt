		<h2 class="single with-review">What to Know About Roadside Assistance Insurance</h2>

<span class = "" style = "height: 40px;  "><iframe src="http://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=http://www.carinsurancecomparison.com/what-is-roadside-assistance-insurance/&layout=standard&send=false&show_faces=false&width=&action=like&colorscheme=light&font=" scrolling="no" frameborder="0" allowTransparency="true" style="border:none; overflow:hidden; width:px; height:40px"></iframe></span><p><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-2419" title="roadside-assistance-insurance" src="http://www.carinsurancecomparison.com/Images/roadside-assistance1-200x300.jpg" alt="Roadside assistance insurance" width="200" height="300" />It is never a good feeling to be driving along the highway and have a tire blowout or discover you locked your keys in your car while you are away from home. Roadside assistance insurance can give you peace of mind by providing you with a variety of assistance that can range from helping you unlock your door to towing a broken down vehicle.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.carinsurancecomparsion.com">For complete coverage</a> get car insurance rates with the FREE quote tool now! Just enter your ZIP code into the box at the top of the page.</p>
<p>This type of insurance <a href="http://www.edmunds.com/driving-tips/roadside-assistance-who-ya-gonna-call.html" target="_blank">provides basic help</a> for things that go wrong on the road. Typical coverage includes emergency roadside assistance and towing if needed, assistance with your battery (such as a jump start), help changing a flat tire, and lockout assistance. Roadside assistance can also be used if your car runs out of gas and you need a small supply of fuel brought to you.</p>
<h3>Details of Roadside Assistance</h3>
<p>Oil, various fluids, and water are also usually covered under roadside assistance auto insurance delivery. Reasons for roadside emergencies can vary widely, from an overheating car to an alternator that goes out. Another time roadside assistance comes in handy is if your car ends up in a ditch or gets stuck in the mud or the snow; they will extract your car for you.</p>
<p>Additional roadside assistance perks can also include discounts for various hotels and car rental companies and free maps and driving directions. Some roadside assistance car insurance even includes benefits such as reimbursement for emergency travel expenses, bail bond expenses for traffic violations, and legal defense assistance for traffic violations. You may even receive personal protection benefits such as the offer of a reward if your car gets stolen or gets hit by a hit and run driver.</p>
<p>There are many types of  insurance benefits that you can get with your policy or membership.</p>
<blockquote><p>The most valuable part of it is probably knowing that if you ever get stranded you are only a phone call away from safety.</p></blockquote>
<p>Keep the phone number in the glove box of your car and consider programming it into your cell phone as well. That way you will always have it ready if you need it.</p>
<h3>What Roadside Assistance Car Insurance Does Not Provide</h3>
<p><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-15229" title="roadside assistance" src="http://www.carinsurancecomparison.com/Images/esarearse.jpeg" alt="roadside assistance" width="300" height="193" />Although roadside assistance does pay for some items that seem like they cross with your regular insurance policy; they don’t. Your primary car insurance will automatically take precedence in any regular insurance related type incident. For instance, if you have an accident and your car becomes inoperable, then your car insurance will take care of it first and foremost (provided your policy covers it).</p>
<p>Roadside assistance is not liability insurance, collision insurance, or comprehensive insurance; it is peace of mind insurance that if you ever run into car trouble while you’re out and about you won’t be left deserted. This means that it is also not mechanical insurance.</p>
<p>Roadside assistance will help your car become operable if it can or tow it if it can’t, but it does not pay for any mechanical repairs to your car.</p>
<blockquote><p>If your car gets a flat tire, roadside assistance will come out and put on your spare tire for you so that you can safely drive to a tire repair and retail shop.</p></blockquote>
<p>They will not buy your new tire for you. It is only the roadside assistance that is actually insured. There are other options for <a href="http://www.carinsurancecomparison.com/car-insurance-repair/" target="_blank">car repair</a> insurance.</p>
<p>Enter your ZIP code in our comparison tool for FREE quotes.</p>
<h3>Buying Roadside Assistance Car Insurance</h3>
<p><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-18668" title="roadside-assistance-insurance" src="http://www.carinsurancecomparison.com/Images/auto-insurance-for-teens4.jpg" alt="roadside assistance insurance" width="270" height="300" />Insurance providing roadside assistance is available from almost every car insurance provider and can be added on to your car insurance policy as an option. It is also available from many other providers, such as auto clubs, discount clubs, various groups and organizations, and even cell phone providers.</p>
<p>While there are many options available, not every policy or membership will have the same benefits, so it is important to review what your actual benefits are. It is also a good idea to determine which benefits are actually of value to you. If your primary concern is emergency breakdowns and you don’t really need maps, hotel discounts, and car discounts, then maybe you can find a membership plan with scaled down benefits and scaled down prices as well.</p>
<p>Also, most roadside assistance insurance plans have limitations. For example, you may only have coverage for up to four emergency calls per membership year, or towing may only be covered for up to a certain mileage or dollar amount of the expense.</p>
<p>Before buying roadside assistance insurance you need to make sure you understand exactly what you are getting and what the limitations are.  Keep these points in mind as you shop around for different roadside assistance insurance so that you know the prices you are comparing are a just comparison.</p>
<h3>Should I buy roadside assistance insurance?</h3>
<p><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-18667" title="buying-roadside-assistance-insurance" src="http://www.carinsurancecomparison.com/Images/car-insurance-companies2-269x300.gif" alt="buying roadside assistance insurance" width="269" height="300" />While roadside assistance insurance can be an excellent addition to your insurance policy, you do need to decide whether you actually need it based on other coverages that you may have.</p>
<p>For example, many credit card companies offer roadside assistance as a benefit for choosing their card. Of course, before you decide that this is an adequate option, you need to know the terms of your credit card agreement.</p>
<p>Many have a very low cap on how much they will pay for a tow, such as $30 to $50. If, however, you are going further than a couple of miles, a tow can cost a lot more money, even over $100.</p>
<p>In addition, some have a limited amount of tows that they offer per vehicle per year, such as two to four tows. For most people this is great, but for some this won&#8217;t be enough.</p>
<p>Another thing is that, if you have <a href="http://www.carinsurancecomparison.com/aaa-car-insurance-review">AAA</a>, then you won&#8217;t need to purchase roadside assistance. Now, if you are using AAA <a href="http://autoclubsouth.aaa.com/?zip=33610&amp;referer=www.aaa.com" target="_blank">only for their roadside assistance</a> program then you need to a comparison of services with your insurance company. Typically, an insurance rider is far less expensive than AAA&#8217;s membership costs.</p>
