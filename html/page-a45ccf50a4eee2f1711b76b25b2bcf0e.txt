        <h1>Basic Do-It-Yourself Diesel Engine Maintenance</h1>
        
                <div class="product-info"><span class="product-authors">By <a href="/search.html?query=Deanna+Sclar">Deanna Sclar</a></span> <span class="product-title">from <span class="product-title"><a href="/store/product/Auto-Repair-For-Dummies-2nd-Edition.productCd-076459902X.html" title="Information about this product: Auto Repair For Dummies, 2nd Edition" target="_blank">Auto Repair For Dummies, 2nd Edition</a></span></span></div>
        
	
        
    

    

    <p class="ReviewDate">Regular maintenance is absolutely imperative if you want a <a class="tooltip-link" rel="tooltip" href="#glossary-diesel_engine">diesel</a> <a class="tooltip-link" rel="tooltip" href="#glossary-diesel_engine">engine</a> to last, and every diesel owner will probably encounter some pitfalls and problems. Although diesel engines require no ignition <a class="tooltip-link" rel="tooltip" href="#glossary-tune-up">tune</a>-<a class="tooltip-link" rel="tooltip" href="#glossary-tune-up">ups</a> and tend to last longer without major repairs than gasoline engines, they do require regular low-cost maintenance, mostly in the form of frequent oil and filter changes. The urea injection systems that reduce nitrogen oxide emissions also need refilling, which is usually done as part of regularly scheduled maintenance.</p>
<p class="Warning">If you own a conventional gas-powered vehicle and you get sloppy about maintenance and don&#8217;t change the oil often enough, you&#8217;ll probably end up with an engine that has aged prematurely. If you own a diesel and do the same thing, you may end up with an engine that&#8217;s prematurely dead. The same goes for changing filters: A dirty <a class="tooltip-link" rel="tooltip" href="#glossary-fuel_filter">fuel</a> <a class="tooltip-link" rel="tooltip" href="#glossary-fuel_filter">filter</a> can impair a conventional vehicle&#8217;s performance, but dirty fuel can clog a <a class="tooltip-link" rel="tooltip" href="#glossary-diesel_fuel;_diesel_oil">diesel</a>&#8217;s <a class="tooltip-link" rel="tooltip" href="#glossary-diesel_fuel;_diesel_oil"><a class="tooltip-link" rel="tooltip" href="#glossary-fuel_injection">fuel</a></a> <a class="tooltip-link" rel="tooltip" href="#glossary-fuel_injection">injection</a> system, and you may need expensive professional help to get back on the road again.</p>
<p class="Remember">As a rule, you shouldn&#8217;t try to clean or adjust a diesel&#8217;s fuel injectors yourself, but if you maintain your vehicle according to the directions in the owner&#8217;s manual, they can last 100,000 miles or more. After all, truckers have always preferred diesels because they find them to be tough, reliable, and cheap to run and maintain.</p>
<p>Most diesels are designed so that the owner can perform regular maintenance chores without an undue investment of time and money.</p>
<p>If your owner&#8217;s manual doesn&#8217;t advise you on a specific maintenance task, or if you have no manual, amble on over to your dealership&#8217;s parts department and ask to see a copy of the service manual for your vehicle (some bookstores and public libraries may also have them). A quick look at the proper sections should tell you whether you can do the job yourself. If you&#8217;re not sure, ask one of the service advisors at your dealership to show you where the oil, air, and fuel filters are located and what&#8217;s involved in changing or servicing them. Most service facilities are pretty nice about that kind of thing.</p>
<p>Typical maintenance tasks include:</p>
<ul class="level-one">
 <li><p class="first-para">Changing the lubricating oil</p>
 </li>
 <li><p class="first-para">Changing the <a class="tooltip-link" rel="tooltip" href="#glossary-air_filter">air</a> <a class="tooltip-link" rel="tooltip" href="#glossary-air_filter">filter</a></p>
 </li>
 <li><p class="first-para">Changing the fuel filter</p>
 </li>
 <li><p class="first-para">Bleeding the fuel system</p>
 </li>
 <li><p class="first-para">Draining the <a class="tooltip-link" rel="tooltip" href="#glossary-water_separators">water</a> <a class="tooltip-link" rel="tooltip" href="#glossary-water_separators">separators</a></p>
 </li>
</ul>

<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-accessory_belt;_drive_belt"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">accessory belt; drive belt</div><p class="definition">A V-shaped or flat serpentine belt that&#8217;s driven by a crankshaft pulley and transmits kinetic energy to various accessories, such as the alternator, air conditioning compressor, fan, power-steering pump, and water pump.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-air_filter"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">air filter</div><p class="definition">The element in the air cleaner that removes impurities from the air. Most air filters are disposable, although some aftermarket types can be cleaned and reused.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-alternator"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">alternator</div><p class="definition">An engine part that generates electric current that&#8217;s stored in the battery and used to start the car and run the electrical equipment.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-automatic_transmission"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">automatic transmission</div><p class="definition">A transmission that selects gears automatically, either by means of a hydraulic converter and a system of bands and clutches, or with an electronic transmission controller.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-battery"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">battery</div><p class="definition">A box filled with a solution of water and acid called electrolyte. The box contains metal plates that store current generated by the alternator and deliver it to the parts of the car that operate electrically.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-block_heaters"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">block heaters</div><p class="definition">Devices that keep the engine warm in very cold weather when a vehicle isn&#8217;t used. These are especially important for starting diesel engines at extremely low temperatures.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-brake_booster;_power_booster"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">brake booster; power booster</div><p class="definition">In a vehicle with power brakes, a brake booster is located between the brake pedal and the master cylinder to increase the force applied to the pistons in the master cylinder.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-brake_fluid"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">brake fluid</div><p class="definition">The liquid used in the hydraulic brake system to stop or slow the car.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-brake_lines"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">brake lines</div><p class="definition">A system of hoses and metal tubes through which the brake fluid flows from the master cylinder to the brakes at each wheel.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-brake_lining"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">brake lining</div><p class="definition">A high-friction material that&#8217;s attached to the brake pad or brake shoe. When the pad is pressed against the disc, or the shoe is pressed against the brake drum, the lining grabs the disc or the inside of the drum, which slows the wheel and thus the car.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-brake_shoes"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">brake shoes</div><p class="definition">Curved pieces of metal on which are bonded high-friction brake linings that are forced against the brake drums to slow or stop the car.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-carburetor"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">carburetor</div><p class="definition">A device that vaporizes fuel and mixes it with air in proper quantities to suit the varying needs of the engine. Carburetors have been replaced by fuel-injection systems on most vehicles built since 1990.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-catalytic_converter"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">catalytic converter</div><p class="definition">A pollution-control device that consumes unburned gas in the tailpipe and reduces nitrogen oxide emissions.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-clutch"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">clutch</div><p class="definition">In a manual transmission, a device that disconnects the engine from the transmission to allow the driver to change gears and then allows the engine and transmission to resume contact and turn together at a new speed. In an automatic transmission, a clutch performs a similar function.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-cold_air_collector_box"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">cold air collector box</div><p class="definition">A rectangular box that contains the air filter. It performs the same function as the air cleaner.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-compression_gauge"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">compression gauge</div><p class="definition">A device used to check the amount of pressure created in a cylinder when the piston is at its highest point and is squeezing the fuel/air mixture into the smallest possible space.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-coolant;_antifreeze"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">coolant; antifreeze</div><p class="definition">An ethylene glycol or propylene glycol solution that raises the boiling point and lowers the freezing point of the water in the cooling system, prevents rust and corrosion, and lubricates the water pump.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-cylinder"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">cylinder</div><p class="definition">A hollow, tube-shaped pipe in the engine block in which the piston rides up and down to compress the fuel/air mixture that drives the engine.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-cylinder_head"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">cylinder head</div><p class="definition">The part of the engine above the engine block that contains the combustion chambers and the valves. The spark plugs screw into the top or side of the cylinder head.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-diesel_engine"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">diesel engine</div><p class="definition">An engine that burns diesel fuel instead of gasoline.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-diesel_fuel;_diesel_oil"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">diesel fuel; diesel oil</div><p class="definition">Fuel for cars with diesel engines. It&#8217;s similar to home heating oil, kerosene, and jet fuel.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-dipstick"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">dipstick</div><p class="definition">A metal stick that&#8217;s inserted into a reservoir to check the level of the fluid in the reservoir by means of markings on the stick. The most common dipsticks check the levels of engine oil, transmission fluid, and power-steering fluid.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-disc_brakes"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">disc brakes</div><p class="definition">Brakes that have calipers with high-friction brake pads, which grab a brake disc (sometimes called a rotor) attached to the wheel and force it to stop turning, thus stopping the car.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-distributor"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">distributor</div><p class="definition">The part of the ignition system that distributes the proper amount of electrical voltage to each spark plug in the correct sequence. This task is now performed electronically on distributorless ignition systems.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-distributor_cap"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">distributor cap</div><p class="definition">A cap that over the distributor that has an outlet for each spark plug wire, plus an outlet where the wire from the ignition coil enters the cap to conduct high-voltage electrical current to the rotor.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-drive_train"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">drive train</div><p class="definition">The path of power from the engine to the drive wheels. Consists of the clutch, transmission, driveshaft, differential, and the axle on which the drive wheels are situated.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-drum_brakes"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">drum brakes</div><p class="definition">Brakes that use hydraulic pressure to force curved brake shoes against the inner walls of a hollow metal drum attached to each wheel.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-engine_block;_cylinder_block"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">engine block; cylinder block</div><p class="definition">The cast iron, aluminum, or ceramic block in which the cylinders and the crankshaft are located.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-engine_control_unit;_ECU"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">engine control unit; ECU</div><p class="definition">The most powerful computer onboard a modern vehicle, also called a powertrain control module (PCM). Controls most engine functions and processes signals from the various engine, emissions, and related sensors.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-feeler_gauge"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">feeler gauge</div><p class="definition">A device for measuring the distance, or gap, between two surfaces, such as between the center and side electrodes on a spark plug.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-firing_order"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">firing order</div><p class="definition">The sequence in which the cylinders fire on a particular engine to distribute the shock of combustion evenly and to reduce engine vibrations.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-fuel_filter"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">fuel filter</div><p class="definition">A device that removes impurities from the fuel before it gets to the fuel injection system. In fuel-injected cars, the filter is found either in the fuel line under the car, or mounted on the firewall.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-fuel_injection"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">fuel injection</div><p class="definition">A fuel system without a carburetor that employs an electronic fuel management system to deliver a specific amount of fuel to each combustion chamber in response to changes in engine speed and driving conditions.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-fuses"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">fuses</div><p class="definition">Fuses protect the electrical components and wiring on your vehicle the same way they do in your home. They&#8217;re located in a fuse box (or boxes) that usually are found under or near the dashboard or under the hood.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-gap"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">gap</div><p class="definition">The space between the spark plug electrodes. Adjusting this space is called <i>gapping.</i>
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-head_gasket"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">head gasket</div><p class="definition">The seal between the cylinder head and the engine block. This gasket keeps the coolant out of the cylinders and free from contamination by exhaust gases.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-hybrid"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">hybrid</div><p class="definition">An alternatively fueled vehicle that combines a small internal combustion engine and an electric motor to get maximum power with minimum emissions and maximum fuel economy.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-internal_combustion_engine"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">internal combustion engine</div><p class="definition">An engine that works on power released by vaporized fuel and air burning inside the engine itself, rather than on an outside source of combustion as, for example, a steam engine does.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-jumper_cables"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">jumper cables</div><p class="definition">Cables used start a car with a dead battery by conducting current from another battery.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-manual_transmission;_standard_transmission"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">manual transmission; standard transmission</div><p class="definition">A vehicle transmission system in which gears are selected by the driver by means of a hand-operated gearshift and a foot-operated clutch.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-master_cylinder"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">master cylinder</div><p class="definition">A device that stores brake fluid and hydraulically forces it through the brake lines to the brakes when you step on the brake pedal.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-oil_filter"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">oil filter</div><p class="definition">A can-shaped device that screws onto the outside of the crankcase and cleans the oil as it circulates through a vehicle's lubrication system.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-power_brakes"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">power brakes</div><p class="definition">A brake system that uses a brake booster (or power booster) to make braking easier.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-power_steering"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">power steering</div><p class="definition">A device that uses hydraulic power to help the driver steer more easily.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-pressure_cap"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">pressure cap</div><p class="definition">A radiator cap on a coolant recovery system or radiator that allows the cooling system to operate under pressure at higher temperatures for greater efficiency.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-radiator"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">radiator</div><p class="definition">A device that cools the liquid in the cooling system by allowing it to circulate through a series of water channels that are exposed to air ducts.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-shock_absorbers"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">shock absorbers</div><p class="definition">Devices located near each wheel to cut down the vertical bouncing of the passenger compartment on the springs after the wheels go over a bump or the vehicle stops short.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-spark_plug"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">spark plug</div><p class="definition">A device that delivers an electrical spark to an engine's combustion chamber, igniting the fuel/air mixture that produces the power that drives the engine.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-spark_plug_gap"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">spark plug gap</div><p class="definition">The space between the center and side spark plug electrodes, across which the spark must jump to ignite the fuel/air mixture in the engine's combustion chamber.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-starter"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">starter</div><p class="definition">A small electrical motor that causes the engine crankshaft to begin to turn, which starts the engine running and so starts the car.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-starter_solenoid"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">starter solenoid</div><p class="definition">A device that uses electrical current to start and engage the starter.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-thermostat"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">thermostat</div><p class="definition">A device that keeps the hot coolant confined to the engine cooling passages to help the engine warm up more quickly. After the engine has warmed up, the thermostat allows the coolant to flow to the radiator, where it&#8217;s cooled and recirculated through the engine to prevent overheating.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-throw-out_bearing;_clutch_release_bearing"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">throw-out bearing; clutch release bearing</div><p class="definition">A part of the clutch, activated by the clutch pedal, that allows the clutch to disengage. If you allow the car to idle in gear with the clutch pedal pressed instead of shifting to Neutral, you can wear out the throw-out bearing.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-transmission_fluid"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">transmission fluid</div><p class="definition">A thin oil that fills the automatic transmission so that it can run on hydraulic pressure. It&#8217;s also found in many power-steering pumps.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-tune-up"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">tune-up</div><p class="definition">The process of replacing a vehickle's fuel filters, air filters, and spark plugs to ensure that air, fuel, and spark are available in good condition to obtain maximum engine efficiency.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-water_pump"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">water pump</div><p class="definition">A device that circulates liquid through the cooling system by pumping it from the engine water jackets to the radiator.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-water_separators"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">water separators</div><p class="definition">A device found on diesel vehicles that removes any water that may have contaminated the diesel fuel.
</p></div>
</div>
<div style="display:none;" id="glossary-wheel_bearings"><div class="titlebar"><img alt="" class="icon" src="http://media.wiley.com/spa_assets/R16B056/site/dummies2/include/images/icons/tooltip-glossary.gif" />Auto Repair &amp; Maintenance Glossary</div>
<div class="glossary"><div class="term">wheel bearings</div><p class="definition">The inner and outer bearings found at each wheel that cushion the contact between the wheel and the spindle it sits on.
</p></div>
</div>
