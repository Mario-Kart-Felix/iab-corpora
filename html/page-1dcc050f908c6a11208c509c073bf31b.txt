 Memorizing what these symbols stand for can speed up your understanding
 of the given circuit which intern leads to faster detection of system 
malfunctions.

<p>In the electrical schematics to follow you will see that symbols are 
arranged in a circuit to reflect the function, power state, and relative
 location of various components. </p><p>An electrical symbol may have a 
slight resemblance to the actual component or its main function. Be 
aware that solid and dotted lines represent energy that flows in and out
 of component connection points.</p><p>Abbreviated text labels or codes 
placed next to the symbols and lines aid in providing descriptions. Both 
All-Data and Mitchel 1 provide a depository of electronic auto repair schematics
 related to specific model electrical repair. Each section has links to the DIY automotive wiring diagram that help decipher the controls of that
 system.
</p>
<h2>Automotive wiring basic symbols<br/></h2>
<div class="ImageBlock ImageBlockCenter"><img src="http://www.auto-facts.org/images/repairdiagrams2.jpg" width="420" alt="electrical symbols defined" title="electrical symbols defined"></div>
<p>Automotive electrical diagrams provide symbols that represent circuit 
component functions. For example, a few basic symbols common to 
electrical schematics are shown as: (1) Switch, (2) Battery, (3) 
Resistor and (4) Ground.
</p>

<p>Note that the switch symbol displays an open or closed circuit path, 
which is what an actual switch performs. The battery symbol appears to 
be made of layers or plates, common to internal battery construction. 
The resistor symbol appears to impede energy flow, which is a resistor 
function. </p><p>The G100 ground symbol indicates a connection 
pointing downward (ground) that dissipates energy. The G100 designation 
is to help you find this location on the automobile. Factory auto repair
 manuals provide component location diagrams that also include grounding
 locations.</p><p>Variations of symbols will exist depending on function
 or other characteristics. Examples are: Variable Battery symbol (5) and
 the Case Ground symbol (6). Electrical repair diagrams are mostly 
standardized for the above symbols.
</p>
<h3>Auto wiring diagram advanced symbols<br/></h3>
<div class="ImageBlock ImageBlockRight"><img src="http://www.auto-facts.org/images/repairdiagram3.jpg" width="342" alt="electronic symbols" title="electronic symbols"></div>
<p>Abbreviated codes on the diagrams provide circuit path and part or 
component information. The codes or labels may be used to show circuit 
connector pin numbers, circuit values or component polarities that will 
add clarity to the diagnostic drawing. </p><p>These sets of label 
examples are: (1)A for Amperage or Amps. (2) ORN for wire diameter and 
color (3)1340 for Circuit Path ID (4)P for pass through grommet (5)A for
 Pin ID & Location (6)C1 for Terminal Connector ID (7)B+ for battery
 positive.
</p>

<p>At first glance the repair diagram may not convey how wires are of 
many colors and diameters. The diameter of each wire symbolized is 
indicated by a label placed at some point along side its drawn line 
(1)(0.8).</p><p>Being aware of the color of a wire is important.(2) Wire
 color information is provided as a color code label in this case 
black/white. (3)Also shown are common schematic illustrations of wires, 
(4)wires connected and (5) wires crossed but not connected.</p><p>For 
consistency most electrical schematics have signal or energy flow from 
top to bottom. Component inputs on the left and outputs on the right. 
Providing an understanding of how a system would function makes the 
wiring repair diagram one of the most valuable resources a technician 
can use in repairing a vehicle.
</p>
