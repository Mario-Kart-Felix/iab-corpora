<p>It&#8217;s been awhile since we&#8217;ve heard anything regarding the Audi Q5 Hybrid Quattro. The <a target="_blank" title="Audi Q5 Hybrid Quattro Unveiled" href="http://www.automobilemag.com/features/news/1011_audi_q5_hybrid_quattro/index.html">Q5 Hybrid Quattro made its debut at the 2010 Los Angeles Auto Show</a>, and if these spot shots are any indication, it could be coming with the Q5&#8242;s next face-lift at the end of the year.</p>
<p>What <em>Motor Trend&#8217;s</em> spy photographer did to deserve such a – well, rude – reaction likely relates to him catching Audi&#8217;s face-lifted 2013 Q5 wearing next-to-no camouflage. <a title="Caught! Refreshed Audi Q5 Sports New Grille" href="http://rumors.automobilemag.com/caught-refreshed-audi-q5-shows-off-revised-grille-116681.html" target="_blank">Unlike the last Q5 we spied</a>, this tester didn&#8217;t have any camouflage on its grille, allowing us to see Audi&#8217;s new corporate grille. Up front, this Q5 Hybrid Quattro also features Audi&#8217;s new headlight design, with its LED lighting element moving from the top to the bottom of the headlamp. This Q5 mule also sports an all-new front bumper, with a redesigned valance, and new fog light surrounds. Suffice it to say, those sweet-looking driving lamps mounted in the grille won&#8217;t make it into production.</p>
<p>Out back, the 2013 Q5 experiences a few more subtle changes than the front of the crossover. The taillights are redesigned, and the rear bumper is new. Also new are the exhaust tips, which are now D-shaped, as opposed to oval shaped.</p>
<p>The Q5&#8242;s profile remains unchanged, save for the &#8220;Hybrid&#8221; badge on the front-quarter panel. When it first debuted at the L.A. Auto Show, the Q5 Quattro Hybrid sported a 2.0-liter turbocharged I-4 and electric motor combo under the hood, producing 245 hp and 354 lb-ft of torque. Audi claimed the Q5 Quattro Hybrid could achieve 33.6 mpg. We&#8217;re not completely sure when we can expect the 2013 Q5 to make its debut, but it probably won&#8217;t happen until the third or fourth quarter of this year.</p>
<p>Head on over to <em>Motor Trend</em> to check out the full gallery, and see Audi&#8217;s engineer giving <em>Motor Trend </em>the bird.</p>
