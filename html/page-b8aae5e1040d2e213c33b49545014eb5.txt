			</div><div style="clear:both"></div><div style="padding-bottom:4px;"></div><p><a href="http://www.fiskerautomotive.com/" target="_self"><strong>Fisker Automotive</strong></a>, the Irvine developer of a $90,000 plug-in electric hybrid car, is kicking into high gear and will be doubling its local staff, adding up to 100 workers, says CEO Henrik Fisker.</p>
<div id="attachment_42808" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 280px"><a href="http://economy.ocregister.com/files/2010/10/fiskerkarma2.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-42808 " style="border: 0pt none;margin: 6px" title="fiskerkarma" src="http://economy.ocregister.com/files/2010/10/fiskerkarma2-300x199.jpg" alt="" width="270" height="179" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Fisker Karma</p></div>
<p>“We are growing at an incredible pace,&#8221; he said. &#8220;“We expect to hire 75 to 100 people over the next six to eight months, mostly on the engineering side.”</p>
<p>The company expects to roll out its long-awaited Karma plug-in electric hybrid luxury sedan in the first quarter of next year.</p>
<p>Most of the new workers will be be assigned to Fisker&#8217;s second model, dubbed the NINA project, which will be a $40,000  plug-in electric hybrid.</p>
<p>Fisker got a <a href="http://auto.ocregister.com/2010/04/23/irvine-automaker-fisker-wraps-up-529-million-federal-loan/32775/" target="_self"><strong>$529 million federal loan</strong></a> to back its plan, which will mostly go for the NINA project.  That model is expected to go into production in the third quarter of 2012.</p>
<p>The company also plans to begin hiring soon for the former GM plant in Delaware for the NINA project car.  An estimated 2,000 people will work there once it&#8217;s fully up and running.</p>
<p>Fisker reportedly is also looking at <a href="http://auto.ocregister.com/2010/10/04/irvines-fisker-automotive-eyes-possible-ipo/43352/" target="_self"><strong>a possible initial public offering</strong></a>.  Silicon Valley-rival Tesla, which has yet to turn a profit, went public in June.  The share price surged from an opening $17 to a peak of $30.42. It closed Friday at $20.54.</p>
<p>Last week Fisker was one of two Orange County companies that made the <a href="//economy.ocregister.com/2010/10/15/2-o-c-firms-make-cleantech-100-list/42650/" target="_self"><strong>2010 Cleantech 100</strong></a> list of up-and-coming firms expected to impact the cleantech market in the next five to 10 years. But the company also made the &#8220;Marmite&#8221; list — companies that investors and experts either love or hate.  They liked the concept but detractors weren&#8217;t so sure about mechanical and distribution issues.</p>
<p>Those interested in Fisker jobs, should email the company&#8217;s <a href="mailto:jobs@fiskerautomotive.com" target="_self"><strong>human resources department</strong>.</a></p>
