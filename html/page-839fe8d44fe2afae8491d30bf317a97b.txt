            <h1>Audi A3 Sedan will be built in China, arriving at Auto Shanghai next month</h1>
            
                <style type="text/css">
                    .topobject{ width:580px;float:right; text-align:center;}
                </style>
                
        <div class="topobject">
        
            <a href="http://content.worldcarfans.co/2013/3/12/medium/5409819221597602356.jpg"><img width="580" src="http://content.worldcarfans.co/2013/3/12/big/89478833286461126.jpg" alt=" Audi A3 Sedan will be built in China, arriving at Auto Shanghai next month" /></a>
            <div class="mainphotocredit">Audi A3 Sedan unibody structure / autohome.com.cn</div>
			
        </div>
        
                <div class="clear"></div>
            <div class="articlead2" id="Worldcarfans_ArticlePages_Middle_BTF_Center_300x250"></div><script type="text/javascript">googletag.cmd.push(function() { googletag.display('Worldcarfans_ArticlePages_Middle_BTF_Center_300x250'); });</script> <h2 class="abstract">Production kicks off in first half of 2013</h2>
            <div class="articlecontent">
               <!-- google_ad_section_start -->
               <p>The <a href="../113012453062/2014-audi-a3-sedan-spied-for-the-first-time" target="_blank">Audi A3 Sedan</a> has been photographed at the FAW-Volkswagen factory, confirming production will take place in China.</p>
<p>Audi's smallest sedan will make its public debut on April 20 when the 2013 Auto Shanghai opens its doors for the press. Set to tackle the Mercedes-Benz CLA, the A3 Sedan will take some of the styling cues seen on the 2011 <a href="../111030130920/audi-a3-sedan-concept-officially-revealed-for-geneva-video" target="_blank">concept</a> showcased in Geneva. It will ride on the MQB platform and is expected to be 4.44 meters long, 1.84 meters wide and 1.39 meters tall.</p>
<p>Engines will most likely be taken from the regular A3s, with gasoline mills varying from a 1.2 TFSI with 105 HP (77 kW) to a 1.8 TFSI with 180 HP (132 kW), while diesels should include two 2.0 TDIs with 150 HP (110 kW) and 184 HP (135 kW).</p>
<p>Based on a recent report, Audi will offer the new model in the United States in both four- and five-door versions with gasoline and diesel engines.</p>
