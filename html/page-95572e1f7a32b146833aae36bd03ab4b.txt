                <!--

~~~ -->	
				
            
                                  	  
<!--
The content itself 
~~~ -->
			<p>Most hybrids can be described as fuel-sipping commuter cars, but the ActiveHybrid 3 can be described more accurately as a hybrid-based performance machine.  In fact, the ActiveHybrid 3 actually offers 5.3 second 0-to-62-mpg acceleration which is actually .2 secs faster than the very capable BMW gasoline-powered 335i sedan.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Faster than the gasoline version?  It’s because of the hybrid drivetrain.  Some Hybrids are &#8220;electric powered automobiles with a small gasoline engine for backup&#8221;.  Full hybrids can thus be run on electric power or on the gasoline power when necessary.  The BMW, however, has a large gasoline engine and both the gas and electric engines can power the vehicle at the same time.  In fact, he engine in the ActiveHybrid 3 is actually the same engine as the BMW 335i.  This is a turbocharged 3.0 liter inline 6 cylinder delivering 302 hp and 295 lb-ft of torque.  Add to that power to an electric motor buried in the gearbox housing which by itself is rated as developing 55hp and 155 lb-ft torque and what you get is a lot of performance.</p>
