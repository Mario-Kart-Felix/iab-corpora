      <h1 class="postTitle"><a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/subarus-first-hybrid-could-appear-this-spring-possibly-at-new-york-auto-show/" rel="bookmark">Subaru&#8217;s First Hybrid Could Appear This Spring, Possibly at New York Auto Show</a></h1>

      
      
      <div id="creditsHolder" style="margin:10px 0 0 0;">

        <div id="fbLikeHolder1" style="display:inline-block;"></div>

        <small class="post-credits">
        January 28, 2013 at 3:56 pm by <a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/author/alexander-stoklosa/" title="Posts by Alexander Stoklosa" rel="author">Alexander Stoklosa</a>        </small>
      </div>

      <div class="post">

        <!-- Gigya Social Share Floater -->
        <div id="divButtons"></div>

        <div id="attachment_103454" class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 636px"><img class=" wp-image-103454" title="Subaru Advanced Tourer concept" src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" data-lazy-src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Subaru-Advanced-Tourer-concept-300-626x382.jpg" alt="Subaru Advanced Tourer concept" width="626" height="382" /><p class="wp-caption-text"><noscript><img class=" wp-image-103454" title="Subaru Advanced Tourer concept" src="http://blog.caranddriver.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Subaru-Advanced-Tourer-concept-300-626x382.jpg" alt="Subaru Advanced Tourer concept" width="626" height="382" /></noscript> Subaru Advanced Tourer concept</p></div>
<p>Nearly 18 months ago, Subaru <a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/subaru-working-on-1-6-and-2-0-l-turbo-fours-and-own-hybrid-seeks-30-percent-fuel-economy-gain-by-2015/">re-expressed its intent to roll out a hybrid system of its own</a>, and now <a href="http://www.thedetroitbureau.com/2013/01/subaru-readying-first-hybrid/" target="_blank"><em>The Detroit Bureau</em></a> reports that the new hybrid model will debut this spring. The timing makes sense for two reasons: First, the New York auto show opens in April, which would give the reveal a convenient venue; and second, the clock&#8217;s ticking—Subaru said its first hybrid would be introduced by 2013, which is, come to think of it, now. <span id="more-103448"></span></p>
<hr noshade="noshade" />
<ul>
<li><a href="http://www.caranddriver.com/reviews/2013-subaru-xv-crosstrek-20i-test-review" target="_blank">Instrumented Test: 2013 Subaru XV Crosstrek 2.0i</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.caranddriver.com/reviews/2014-subaru-forester-25-20xt-turbo-first-drive-review" target="_blank">First Drive: 2014 Subaru Forester 2.5i / 2.0XT Turbo</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.caranddriver.com/reviews/2013-subaru-brz-premium-long-term-test-review" target="_blank">Long-Term Test: 2013 Subaru BRZ Premium</a></li>
</ul>
<hr noshade="noshade" />
<p>Subaru officials wouldn&#8217;t comment on <em>The Detroit Bureau&#8217;</em>s report, but regardless, a Subaru hybrid is coming soon, whether it appears in New York or someplace else at a later date. That said, what form the car will take remains a mystery. Subaru has previewed its idea of a hybrid with a few concepts over the past few years, from the <a href="http://www.caranddriver.com/news/subaru-hybrid-tourer-concept-auto-shows">Hybrid Tourer</a> show car to the more recent <a href="http://www.caranddriver.com/news/subaru-advanced-tourer-concept-to-debut-in-tokyo-news">Advanced Tourer</a> wagon, neither of which directly previewed one of the brand&#8217;s mainstream models like the Impreza or Legacy. <span style="font-size: 13px;">We think it might be too early for even a concept-car take on Subaru&#8217;s first hybrid, if it, in fact, is a standalone model and not simply a hybrid iteration of the Impreza, Legacy/Outback, or Forester. Our bet is on Subaru first showing off the hybrid powertrain, then later giving an idea of what model that powertrain could go into. </span></p>
