<p><b>Roadside assistance programs don't really get the recognition they deserve, in my opinion.</b><br/><br/><br/>Auto
 insurance is a requirement for all drivers, and it sure comes in handy 
when you need it. But roadside assistance is an optional service than 
many drivers never consider until it's too late.<br/><br/><br/>I have to 
admit that I've been one of those drivers.  :)  In my younger years I 
never worried about being part of an Ohio auto club.  <br/><br/><br/>I know
 how to change a tire, and that was the only obstacle I really 
considered as a possible breakdown.  If anything more serious occurred, I
 knew I could call my dad or a friend if necessary.<br/><br/></p>

<p>I guess I really changed my mind about the value of Roadside Assistance Programs when I got married and moved away.<br/><br/><br/>My parents were getting older and my concern for <i>them</i> inspired me to take a good look at the roadside assistance companies servicing their region.<br/><br/><br/>It turned out that AAA had an Akron auto club that was perfect for my mom and dad's needs.  <br/><br/><br/>I
 purchased an annual package for them that covered either of them in any
 vehicle they happened to be driving or riding in as a passenger.<br/><br/><br/>Believe it or not, the cost for that peace of mind (for both me and them), was less than $100 per year. <b> Money well spent!<br/><br/></b></p>
<h2><span style="font-size: 24px;">Best Roadside Assistance Programs</span><br/></h2>
<p>If you are going to spend your money on a roadside assistant service, 
you want to make sure that you are dealing with a company that is 
reliable and will actually be there when you need them.<br/><br/></p>
<h1>The Good Sam Club<br/></h1>
<p><b>My #1 pick</b> of all the roadside assistance companies is most definitely the <a href="http://www.tkqlhce.com/click-2675033-10294062" rel="nofollow" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">Good Sam Club</a>.  Most people only think of the Good Sam club in relation to their <a href="http://www.auto-insurance-helper.com/rv-roadside-assistance.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">RV roadside assistance</a>, which is second to none, but Good Sam offers roadside assistance services to all drivers, not just RV owners.<br/><br/><br/>They
 give incredible value for money, in my view, with both their auto and 
RV programs.  They provide a lot of benefits for a small annual fee.  
Here are some of their many services included:<br/><br/></p><ul><li>Flat Tire Service<br/></li><li>Locksmith Service<br/></li><li>Flat Battery Service<br/></li><li>Emergency Fuel Service<br/></li><li>Unlimited Distance and Dollar Towing Service to the nearest network service facility<br/></li><li>Coverage for your spouse and children living at home<br/></li><li>Coverage all throughout the US, Canada, Puerto Rico, and US Virgin Islands<br/></li><li>Motorcycle and Sports Utility Trailers Covered<br/></li></ul><p><br/>...and
 more.  And that's just for the regular auto roadside assistant 
services.  For just a few dollars more per year, you can have your RV 
covered with all "the works" included.<br/><br/><br/>You really won't find a better roadside assistance program, so I would suggest you check out the <a href="http://www.tkqlhce.com/click-2675033-10294062" rel="nofollow" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">Good Sam Club</a> before you make your final choice.  They've got my tick of approval.<br/></p>
<h1>AAA Motor Club<br/></h1>
<p>Another great option among roadside assistance companies is the AAA auto
 club.  There are approximately 41 AAA Motor Clubs across the United 
States.  <br/><br/><br/>The AAA Roadside Assistance is probably the most 
well known roadside assistant services in the USA.  They've been around 
for decades and the phrase "call AAA" is almost a catch phrase for 
roadside service.<br/><br/><br/>As I mentioned earlier, I've purchased AAA 
membership for family members in the past because I wanted to be sure 
they had help should they need it.  It's a terrible feeling to be 
stranded on the side of the road and I particularly didn't want my aging
 parents to be in that predicament.<br/><br/><br/>Even though I said Good 
Sam is my favorite roadside assistance program, I think AAA motor club 
is excellent value for money, too.<br/><br/><br/>Read more about the available <a href="http://www.auto-insurance-helper.com/aaa-auto-insurance.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">AAA Motor Club services</a>.<br/><br/><br/>Before
 I move on to the next popular motor club, I wanted to mention a new 
alternative to AAA that I learned about recently.  It's called the <a href="http://www.betterworldclub.com/home.htm?join_code=BERG2121" rel="nofollow" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">Better World Club</a>, and they are an eco-friendly company.  So, if "going green" is dear to your heart, definitely check them out.</p>
<h1>AARP Roadside Assistance<br/></h1>
<p>The AARP Roadside Assistance service is really provided by Allstate 
roadside assistance.  From what I understand, AARP partnered up with 
Allstate so that they could provide their members with an affordable and
 reliable roadside assistant plan.  <br/><br/><br/>The only catch is that 
this particular service is only available to AARP members.  If you are 
over 50, definitely consider joining <a href="http://www.auto-insurance-helper.com/aarp-hartford-insurance.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">AARP Hartford</a> because of all the many benefits you'll receive besides being eligible for their Allstate roadside assistance partnership.</p>
<h1>Best Roadside Service<br/></h1>
<p>A perhaps "lesser known" roadside assistance program (when compared to the Big 3) is, believe it or not, really called <a href="http://www.bestroadside.com/amember/go.php?r=5388&amp;i=10" rel="nofollow" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">Best Roadside Service</a>.  That was good thinking when it comes to marketing, wasn't it?  ;) <br/><br/><br/>Having
 had a good look at this program to see what they offer and how they 
compare to the more well known auto clubs, they measure up pretty well.<br/><br/><br/>First
 of all, their price point is similar.  Secondly, they offer the same 
types of services so you can rely on them to come riding to your rescue 
in your time of need.<br/><br/><br/>They also offer emergency service in all 50 US States as well as all of Canada.  Another big check mark in the approval column. <br/><br/><br/>I
 did discover one interesting point of difference with Best Roadside 
that I didn't see among the other clubs.  Generally speaking, an auto 
club will cover an individual (or couple or family) regardless of what 
vehicle they are driving or riding in.  In other words, the coverage 
follows the person, not the vehicle.<br/><br/><br/>Yes, you can get that 
type of roadside assistance coverage with this program, but you ALSO 
have another option.  You can cover your vehicle instead.  <br/><br/><br/>What
 does that mean to you? Simply this.  No matter who is driving the car 
that is covered, if you've chosen the Vehicle Plan, you can call on your
 Best Roadside Service roadside assistant to come to your aid.</p><p><br/></p>
<hr style="width:40%;height:2px;">
<p><br/></p><p>Well, those are my top choices when it comes to roadside assistance 
companies in the US and Canada.  You really can't go wrong by choosing 
from the most reputable roadside assistance programs to rely on when 
you're in a tough spot.  <br/><br/><br/><b>Safe Driving!</b></p>
