    <p>
Please give us a moment to come down from our diesel-huffing high, we&rsquo;re still tingling from first laying eyes on the 2014 Volkswagen GTD. Yes, we know, the Golf-based GTD&mdash;essentially a diesel-powered GTI&mdash;has existed for decades, but the latest iteration <a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/vw-confirms-u-s-timeline-for-mkvii-golf-gti-next-gen-gtd-could-come-too/" target="_self">actually has a shot at making it to the U.S. market</a> for the first time. We were impressed with the outgoing GTD&mdash;Volkswagen teasingly lent us <a href="/reviews/2012-volkswagen-gtd-diesel-instrumented-test-review" target="_self">one to test here on American soil</a>&mdash;and the 2014 model brings even more power and refinement. Thus far, VW has only announced the GTD is headed for Europe but we&rsquo;ve got our fingers crossed. The model bows at the <a href="/2013-geneva-auto-show" target="_self">2013 Geneva auto show</a>.
</p>

<p>
<b>Gran Turismo Diesel</b>
</p>

<p>
Fans of Volkswagen&rsquo;s existing GTD and upcoming GTI will recognize the new GTD&rsquo;s basic formula, which largely starts and ends with dropping a high-output diesel engine into a Golf imbued with the GTI&rsquo;s handling gear. The 2014 GTD, based on the new <a href="/reviews/2015-volkswagen-golf-first-drive-review" target="_self">seventh-generation Golf</a> platform, gets a lowered sport suspension, 17-inch aluminum wheels, smoked LED taillights, a rear spoiler, and a body kit with a rear diffuser. 
</p>

<p>
In fact, the GTD looks nearly identical to the <a href="/news/2014-volkswagen-gti-concept-photos-and-info-news" target="_self">GTI concept</a> VW showed at last year&rsquo;s Paris auto show. (The latest, MkVII Golf&ndash;based GTI has yet to make its production-form debut.) The only differences between the two are the GTD&rsquo;s signature gray striping in its front fascia and its exhaust-outlet arrangement of two clustered tips on the left side of the bumper, as opposed to the GTI concept&rsquo;s split dual outlets. Just three exterior colors are available: Tornado Red, Black, and Pure White. 
</p>

<p>
The GTD&rsquo;s cabin is set apart by sport seats with a special tartan pattern, a black headliner, a sport steering wheel, stainless-steel pedals, a GTD shift knob, and unique instrument-cluster trim. The European model will come standard with ambient lighting, automatic climate control, park assist, and VW&rsquo;s new <a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/plug-and-play-vw-details-new-lineup-wide-modular-infotainment-system/" target="_self">modular touch-screen infotainment system</a>. 
</p>

<p>
<b>Chug, Chug, Diesel, Chug!</b>
</p>

<p>
The GTD&rsquo;s GTI bits are great, but its main party trick lives under its sleek hood, where you&rsquo;ll find Volkswagen&rsquo;s <a href="http://blog.caranddriver.com/detailed-vw%E2%80%99s-new-2-0-liter-ea288-four-cylinder-tdi-diesel/" target="_self">new EA288 TDI</a> direct-injected, 2.0-liter turbocharged diesel four-cylinder. The new engine pumps out 184 horsepower and a Ford Focus ST&ndash;shaming 280 lb-ft of torque, the latter peaking at just 1750 rpm. Those figures mark increases of 16 ponies and 22 lb-ft over the last-gen GTD&rsquo;s 2.0-liter four. European buyers can choose between a six-speed manual and a six-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. 
</p>

<p>
Volkswagen claims the manual car can scoot from 0&ndash;62 mph in 7.5 seconds. That figure is probably somewhat conservative&mdash;this one is more powerful and likely lighter, and we clocked the outgoing GTD with a dual-clutch auto at 7.1 seconds to 60. Top speed should also improve. The old GTD was drag-limited in our testing to 135 mph, but the 2014 model can hit 143, according to VW. 
</p>

<p>
Volkswagen of America has indicated to us that it would love to bring this GTD to the States, but an inquiry made for this story indicates that a final decision has yet to be made. Besides the fact that the GTD is just plain cool, VWoA&rsquo;s rationale for wanting the car here is simple: TDI models are immensely popular, so offering up a sporty yet efficient diesel halo model (the last-gen GTD returned 31 mpg under our heavy right feet) is fairly low-risk. If enthusiasts ask nicely&mdash;and loudly&mdash;enough, the diesel fairy might just drop this gem under some American pillows. For now, the GTD heads for European showrooms in June. 
</p>
