Just over 1000 of these wood bodied wagons were made in 1932, it's estimated that there are less than 10 remaining stock examples left. This car was the subject of a full nut and bolt restoration in 1989 by Hyannis Restorations in Hyannis, MA. (a renown shop specializing in Pebble Beach Cars). The car has traveled less than 1000 miles and remains in show room condition.

The car is finished in factory correct Winterleaf Brown with black fenders and correct red spoked wheels. Being a late '32, this Woody has Passenger Car headlights, and radiator cap, as well as a very desirable 20-louver hood. The fit and finish of this wagon is truly excellent, the car is absolutely factory correct. All of the car's bright-work is excellent with no pitting. The woodwork is exceptional. Fenders are perfect. Body welting is crisp and clean. Panel fit is excellent throughout. The side curtains and windows are in as new condition. The original rebuilt, 200 Cubic inch motor runs beautifully, and the rebuilt manual transmission shifts well. The underside of the car is finished to the same exacting standards as the top.
