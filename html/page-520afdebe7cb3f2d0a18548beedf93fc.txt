<h3>The All New 2012 Ford Focus Hatchback Review &#8211; Change Has Done Them Good</h3>
<p>The 2012 Ford Focus hatchback is all new for 2012 with new interior details and updated styling that offer exceptional visual appeal and elegant refinements in three trim levels designed to appeal to value-oriented buyers. Upgrades to the technological elements of the Ford Focus are designed to improve driver and passenger comfort, while the available sport-tuned suspension, rear spoiler and sports seating options can make even the Focus feel like a performance vehicle inside.</p>
<p><em>Under the hood</em><br />
The 2.0-liter inline four-cylinder engine offers surprisingly good acceleration and speed for a compact vehicle, producing 160 horsepower and still managing a healthy 36 mpg on the highway. Only available on the top-end Ford Focus Titanium, the Titanium Handling Package includes 18-inch alloy wheels, the aforementioned sport-tuned suspension and, best of all, a full-size spare tire to ensure improved handling even after a flat. The Ford Focus SE hatchback features a five-speed manual transmission, while the other two trim levels offer a six-speed PowerShift dual-clutch automatic transmission.<span id="more-4857"></span></p>
<p><em>Inside the cabin</em><br />
Gadget lovers will be delighted by the SYNC voice-activated entertainment and communications system, offered standard on both the SE and the SEL models. The Titanium trim level adds a number of luxurious extras including keyless ignition, a ten-speaker Sony stereo system and the touchscreen convenience and Wi-Fi capabilities of the MyFord Touch technology interface. HD radio is also available, along with MP3 and iPod interfaces to let drivers and passengers enjoy their preferred music anywhere they go.</p>
<p><em>On the road</em><br />
The 2012 Ford Focus hatchback has been reimagined and rebuilt from the ground up. Nowhere is this more obvious than in the way it handles; minimal body roll and electrically-assisted steering ensure that the Focus hatchback takes the corners and curves with ease. This is especially true with the upgraded sport-tuned suspension, available only on the Titanium but well worth the extra cost for the enhanced ride and comfort.</p>
<p><em>Conclusions</em><br />
The 2012 redesign has done a world of good for the Ford Focus hatchback line. Added technological features, updated exteriors and luxurious options ensure that the Focus can compete with the leaders in the compact car marketplace. With a base price for the SE just under $20,000, many buyers will be willing to put their money down on the new and improved Ford Focus hatchbacks in preference to more expensive competitors with fewer features overall.</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline">SE 5-Door model Details</span>:<br />
Engine: 2.0-liter direct-injection four-cylinder<br />
Horsepower &amp; Torque: 160 hp/145 lb-ft<br />
Transmission: five-speed manual transmission<br />
Fuel Economy: 26/36<br />
Base MSRP: $18,300</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline">SEL 5-Door model Details</span>:<br />
Engine: 2.0-liter direct-injection four-cylinder<br />
Horsepower &amp; Torque: 160 hp/146 lb-ft<br />
Transmission: six-speed shiftable automatic transmission<br />
Fuel Economy: 27/37<br />
Base MSRP: $21,300</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline">Titanium 5-Door model Details</span>:<br />
Engine: 2.0-liter direct-injection four-cylinder<br />
Horsepower &amp; Torque: 160 hp/146 lb-ft<br />
Transmission: six-speed shiftable automatic transmission<br />
Fuel Economy: 27/37<br />
0 to 60: 8.5 seconds<br />
Base MSRP: $22,700</p>
