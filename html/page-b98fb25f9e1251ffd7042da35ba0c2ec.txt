<h1>Electric Car Conversion Kit (for beginners)</h1>

<div class= "img-float-left"><img src="http://www.electric-cars-are-for-girls.com/images/canev-beetle-conversion-kit.jpg" width="215" height="185" alt= "VW Beetle conversion kit"></div>

<p><b>If you're new to conversions,</b> you may ask, "What's in an electric car conversion kit?"  The answer is, "that depends on where you get it".</p>  

The simplest conversion kits have only a motor and a compatible controller, <b>so they might seem to cost less.</b>  You'll need a few more items before you're ready to drive your conversion down the road, items you may or may not have laying around the garage already.</p>

<p>I do have a favorite place to get an electric car conversion kit: <b>Canadian Electric Vehicles.</b>  Why?</p>

<h2 class= "hbg">What About Universal Electric Car Conversion Kits?</h2>

<div class= "img-float-right"><img src="http://www.electric-cars-are-for-girls.com/images/canev-universal-conversion-kit.jpg" width="290" height="140" alt= "Universal conversion kit"></div>

<p><b>Canadian Electric Vehicles has universal kits,</b> too, that can be customized to fit ANY car, but Randy doesn't recommend:</p>
<ul>
<li class= "custom"><b>Heavy vehicles.</b>  Like that giant Hummer or full-size pickup that costs 150 bucks to fill the gas tank now, for example.</li>
<li class= "custom"><b>Automatic transmissions.</b>  Because they're range-wasters, and you won't have to shift (much) anyway.</li>
<li class= "custom"><b>A 4x4.</b>  Because of the added weight and drag of the 4x4 system.</li>
</ul>

<p>They will also provide pre-wired control boxes for a plug and drive system for an additional $1,200US.</p>

<h3>So should you get an AC or DC conversion kit?</h3>

<div class= "img-float-left"><img src="http://www.electric-cars-are-for-girls.com/images/canev-geo-conversion-kit.jpg" width="255" height="160" alt= "Geo Metro conversion kit"></div>

<p>Answer: it doesn't matter anymore, really.  You can get either one, and they're both equally good in a car, both simple to use and install.  The AC electric car conversion kit costs about a thousand dollars more than the DC, so not a lot of difference there.  I still like AC better, but they both have their virtues.</p>

<p>Canadian Electric Vehicles has <b>both AC and DC conversion kits available in stock.</b></p>
