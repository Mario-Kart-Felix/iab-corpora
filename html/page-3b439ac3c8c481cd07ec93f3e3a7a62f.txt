<p>Hybrid cars are coming down in price and just at the right time with the downturn in economy and high gas prices.  The fall of the auto industry also has an upside as they focus on on hybrid and electric cars during their restructure. </p>
<p>Low emissions, great milage, great savings at the pump and moving towards being more ecologically conscious are just some of the reasons for moving towards a hybrid electric vehicle.  Hybrid vehicles are the middle step between fossil fuel gas and diesel cars and electric vehicles.  As the name states, they are a hybrid and run off both energy sources.  This is good news for those of us who want to make a green difference, save some money and still have longer commutes that are not viable in an all electric vehicle at this time. </p>
<p>Save even more money by repairing a damaged hybrid electric car. </p>

<p>When shopping and researching a damaged repairable hybrid car you should take extra precautions in sourcing parts.  Because hybrid cars are newer, both in technology and market release, some parts may be harder to come by and it's for these reasons that insurance companies will write off cars with what seems to be minimal damage as the cost of OEM replacement parts are too expensive.  This can work to your advantage as you can source from salvage yards. </p>
<p>You can now buy a Hybrid vehicle in many makes and models.  From the modest and affordable Toyota Prius to the ultra luxury BMW X5 Hybrid, there is a hybrid car out there for everyone. </p>
